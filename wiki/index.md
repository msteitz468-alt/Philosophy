Emphasises transformation of "thinking cap," recovery of older systems being overthrown, "misfires" and hurdles, and science's later "directing role"; civilisational leadership shifts north. - [[Sources/Two Dogmas of Empiricism - W.V.O. Quine (1951)]] - W.V.O. Quine, "Two Dogmas of Empiricism" (1951 *Philosophical Review*; 1961 revisions in *From a Logical Point of View*). Foundational critique of the two dogmas of logical empiricism: the analytic/synthetic distinction (truth by meaning vs. fact) and reductionism (each meaningful statement reducible to experience constructs or having unique confirming sensory events). Yields **confirmation holism** (web of belief; unit of significance = whole of science), physical objects etc. as pragmatic "cultural posits" (epistemologically on a par with gods), and a shift to thorough **pragmatism** + naturalized epistemology. Directly attacks Hume's Fork background and Carnap's verificationism/state-descriptions; sets up post-positivist issues for Popper and Kuhn. New entity + 4 concepts. See dedicated source page + [[Thinkers/W.V.O. Quine]] + log.
- [[Sources/Language, Truth and Logic - A.J. Ayer (1936)]] - A.J. Ayer, *Language, Truth and Logic* (1936; Penguin 2001). The founding English-language manifesto of logical positivism: verification principle (meaningful = analytic or empirically verifiable; else literally senseless), elimination of metaphysics, all a priori = analytic = tautological (defeats Kant's synthetic a priori), emotive theory of ethics (moral statements express feeling, neither true nor false), philosophy as analysis / logic of science. The canonical statement of what Popper, Kuhn, and Quine are reacting against. New entity ([[Thinkers/A.J. Ayer]]) + 4 concepts. See source page + log.
- [[Sources/Against Method - Paul Feyerabend]] - Paul Feyerabend, *Against Method: Outline of an Anarchistic Theory of Knowledge* (1975; 3rd ed. 1993, incorporating *Science in a Free Society*; dedicated to Lakatos). The **radical pole** of the philosophy-of-science leg: there is **no fixed method** — every rule (including [[Concepts/Falsifiability as Criterion of Demarcation (Popper)|Popperian falsificationism]]) has been productively broken, so "[[Concepts/Epistemological Anarchism (Feyerabend)|anything goes]]." Built on the [[Concepts/Natural Interpretations and the Galileo Case (Feyerabend)|Galileo case]] (theory-laden "natural interpretations" replaced by rhetoric, an untrustworthy telescope, and [[Concepts/Counterinduction (Feyerabend)|counter-induction]]); argues for [[Concepts/Proliferation and Tenacity (Feyerabend)|proliferation + tenacity]] (Millian pluralism); concludes politically that science is one tradition among many and calls for the [[Concepts/Separation of Science and State (Feyerabend)|separation of science and state]]. - [[Sources/Representing and Intervening - Ian Hacking (1983)]] - Ian Hacking, *Representing and Intervening: Introductory Topics in the Philosophy of Natural Science* (1983). The experimental turn complementing the vault's rationality/representation cluster. Distinguishes entity realism from theory realism; "[[Concepts/If You Can Spray Them Then They Are Real (Hacking)|if you can spray them, then they are real]]" (use of electrons/positrons as tools in the Stanford niobium-ball experiment convinced Hacking). Experiment has a life of its own, often creates stable phenomena rather than recording pre-existing ones, and observation is a skill (pre-theoretical cases + convergence across independent instruments). Engages Kuhn (incommensurability as irrealist pressure on representation; Hacking wrote the intro to the ingested Chicago 4th ed.), Feyerabend, Lakatos (as surrogate for truth), and positivism. New entity + 5 concepts; extends the central [[Contradictions/Popper vs Kuhn - Falsification vs Paradigms]] page. See dedicated source page + [[Thinkers/Ian Hacking]] + log.
- [[Sources/Heidegger - A Very Short Introduction - Michael Inwood (1997)]] - Michael Inwood, *Heidegger: A Very Short Introduction* (OUP, 1997/2000). Compressed survey of Heidegger's entire philosophy centered on *Being and Time* (1927): the question of Being and the ontological difference; Dasein as being-in-the-world; ready-to-hand vs. present-at-hand; truth as unconcealment (*aletheia*); care (*Sorge*) as Dasein's being; being-toward-death, anxiety, conscience, resoluteness; ecstatic temporality (four levels of time); historicality and the Turn (*Kehre*); art and truth (*The Origin of the Work of Art*). Biography, Nazism, and legacy. The vault's first dedicated Heidegger source. New thinker + 10 concepts + 1 school (Phenomenology). See [[Thinkers/Martin Heidegger]] + log.

**Public Choice & Political Economy (Mancur Olson, Buchanan & Tullock, 2026-06-29 Ingest)**:
- [[Sources/The Rise and Decline of Nations - Mancur Olson (1982)]] - Mancur Olson, *The Rise and Decline of Nations: Economic Growth, Stagflation, and Social Rigidities* (1982). Public choice analysis extending the logic of collective action to the rise, decline, and macroeconomic performance of nations. See log.

**Pre-Socratic Philosophy (Burnet 1920, 2026-06-11 Ingest)**:
- [[Thinkers/Thales]] - Milesian philosopher and scientist (c. 624–546 BCE), recognized as the first Western philosopher; held water as the *arche* of all things.

- [[Thinkers/Anaximenes]] - Milesian philosopher (c. 586–526 BCE), associate of Anaximander; held *Air* to be the primary substance through condensation and rarefaction.
- [[Thinkers/Pythagoras]] - Greek philosopher and religious reformer (c. 570–495 BCE); held that number is the essence of all reality and taught metempsychosis. **Now contested**: Burkert (1972) argues the mathematician-scientist portrait is a later retrojection and the historical Pythagoras was a religious charismatic; Zhmud (2012) rebuts, defending him as both teacher and scientist — see [[Contradictions/Pythagoras the Scientist vs the Religious Charismatic]].
- [[Thinkers/Philolaus]] - Pythagorean of Croton (c. 470–385 BCE); earliest Pythagorean with surviving (partly genuine) fragments and, on Burkert's reconstruction, the true source of the "number cosmology" (limiters & unlimiteds bound by harmony) and the central-fire system credited to Pythagoras. Zhmud (2012) corrects: "to have number" = knowability not number-ontology, and the harmony of the spheres should be detached from his system. | thinker | metaphysics, cosmology, mathematics
- [[Thinkers/Archytas]] - Pythagorean mathematician, harmonic theorist, and statesman of Tarentum (c. 435–360 BC); "the first and only Pythagorean credibly associated with significant technical achievements in mathematics" (the Delian problem, the sibling sciences/quadrivium, the three means). Friend of Plato and the main channel of Pythagorean mathematics into the Academy; the last great figure of the early school. | thinker | mathematics, harmonics, politics
- [[Thinkers/Hippasus]] - Early Pythagorean of Metapontum (early–mid 5th c. BC); the figure to whom genuine mathematics is securely attached — the discovery of incommensurability, the bronze-disc acoustic experiment, the first proportions, the dodecahedron-in-sphere. The pivot of the Burkert–Zhmud dispute; his betrayal-legend springs from the *arrhētos* ("irrational"/"secret") pun. | thinker | mathematics, harmonics
- [[Thinkers/Alcmaeon of Croton]] - Crotonian physician-philosopher (late 6th–early 5th c. BC); Zhmud's case for the empirical heart of early Pythagoreanism — health as *isonomia*, the brain-centred theory of cognition, knowledge by inference (*tekmairesthai*), embryology, and soul-immortality (without transmigration). | thinker | medicine, epistemology, philosophy-of-mind
- [[Thinkers/Heraclitus]] - Greek philosopher (c. 535–475 BCE) from Ephesus; taught universal flux (*panta rhei*, fire), the unity of opposites governed by the *Logos*, and strife as creative force.
- [[Thinkers/Parmenides]] - Greek philosopher (c. 515–450 BCE) from Elea; founder of Eleatic monism, argued that Being is one, indivisible, and unchanging, introducing deductive logic to metaphysics.
- [[Thinkers/Zeno of Elea]] - Eleatic philosopher (c. 490–430 BCE), pupil of Parmenides; invented dialectic and proposed famous paradoxes of motion to defend Eleatic monism.
- [[Thinkers/Empedocles]] - Greek philosopher, poet, and statesman (c. 490–430 BCE) from Sicily; proposed the four elements (earth, air, fire, water) acted upon by Love and Strife.
- [[Thinkers/Anaxagoras]] - Greek philosopher (c. 500–428 BCE); brought philosophy to Athens, argued that a cosmic mind (*Nous*) orders infinite, qualitatively distinct seeds of matter.
- [[Thinkers/Democritus]] - Greek philosopher (c. 460–370 BCE) from Abdera; co-founder of ancient atomism (atoms and void in mechanical motion), presenting a materialist cosmology and cheerful ethics.

**Classical Philosophy**:
- [[Thinkers/Plato]] - Athenian philosopher (c. 427–347 BCE), founder of the Academy; author of dialogues spanning ethics, politics, and metaphysics, including *Timaeus*, *Critias*, *Republic*, *Symposium*, *Phaedrus*, *Sophist*, and *Statesman*. **Classical Greek Philosophy & History (Aristotle + Thucydides, 2026-06-04)**:
- [[Thinkers/Aristotle]] - Athenian philosopher (384–322 BCE), student of Plato, founder of the Lyceum. Works on modal logic/Organon (essentialism, modal copula, scientific demonstration), Nicomachean Ethics (eudaimonia as activity in accordance with virtue), Rhetoric (enthymeme, ethos/logos/pathos, branches of oratory), Metaphysics, and Politics (zōon politikon, natural slavery, classification of constitutions, polity/middle-class thesis, stasis, best state and education). Primary classical counterpart to Plato.
- [[Thinkers/Maimonides]] - Moses ben Maimon / Rambam (1138–1204), foremost philosopher of medieval Judaism; physician and jurist in Córdoba→Cairo. *Guide for the Perplexed* reconciles Torah and Aristotle: negative theology, rejection of Kalam occasionalism, creation-on-authority, naturalized prophecy, graded providence, rational law. Third corner of the Ghazali↔Averroes debate; major influence on Aquinas and (by inversion) Spinoza. | thinker | metaphysics, theology, ethics
- [[Thinkers/Al-Ghazali]] - Abu Hamid al-Ghazali (1058–1111), the most influential religious intellectual of Sunni Islam; Ash'arite theologian, jurist, and Sufi. *Tahafut al-Falasifa* refutes the Avicennan Aristotelians and condemns 3 doctrines as unbelief; Ash'arite occasionalism, the eternal-will cosmology, and the demonstrability criterion. The interlocutor whom [[Thinkers/Ibn Rushd]] answers. | thinker | metaphysics, theology, epistemology
- [[Thinkers/Ibn Rushd]] - Last great Islamic philosopher (1126–1198, Cordoba); “The Commentator” on Aristotle. *Tahafut al-Tahafut* defends eternity of the world and real natural causation against Ghazali’s occasionalist theology; nuanced on soul, resurrection, and the necessity of religious law for the masses. Primary source grounding Aquinas’ corrections on unicity of intellect and hylomorphism. New entity + concepts. Bridges Greek Aristotle → Latin scholasticism.
- [[Thinkers/Thucydides]] - Athenian historian/general (c. 460–400 BCE), author of the *History of the Peloponnesian War*. Empirical analysis of empire, subject revolt (Mytilene), power asymmetry in alliances, strategy, diplomacy, and the rhetoric of justification (honor, necessity, justice). “A possession for all time.”

**Stoicism (Roman, 2026-06-04 addition)**:
- [[Thinkers/Epictetus]] - Stoic teacher (c. AD 50–135, former slave). *Enchiridion*, Fragments, Golden Sayings: dichotomy of control (*prohairesis* – what is in our power vs externals), proper use of impressions, acceptance of role, equanimity, philosophy as daily discipline and service to divine order.
- [[Thinkers/Seneca]] - Roman Stoic (c. 4 BC–AD 65). *Letters to Lucilius*: practical ethics from daily occasions on time, death, friendship, virtue vs fortune, self-sufficiency, philosophy as medicine, endurance, critique of spectacle/wealth.
- [[Thinkers/Marcus Aurelius]] - Emperor and Stoic (AD 121–180). *Meditations* (full 12 books, primary journal during campaigns, George Long trans. from Stoic Six Pack); closest historical philosopher-king (per Plato ideal). Education under Stoics (Rusticus → Epictetus), governance with justice, examined life amid power and war. Now primary-sourced in his own words (daemon / ruling faculty, cosmic perspective, every act as if the last, co-operation, cheerful mortality).

**Hellenistic Philosophy (Epicurus, 2026-06-04 addition)**:
- [[Thinkers/Epicurus]] - Greek philosopher (341–271 BCE), founder of Epicureanism. Revived atomism (infinite atoms/void, multiple worlds, swerve for free will and collisions); empiricist epistemology (senses as criterion, verificationism); ethics of refined hedonism (ataraxia/tranquility as goal via prudent pleasure, "death is nothing," "live unnoticed"/lathe biosas, friendship; gods exist but uninvolved). Garden school as anti-elitist community. Materialist counter to Plato/Aristotle/Stoics; precursor to modern science/contractarianism.

**Roman Epicureanism (Lucretius, 2026-06-03 addition)**:
- [[Thinkers/Lucretius]] - Roman poet-philosopher (c. 99–55 BCE), author of *De Rerum Natura*. Primary elaborator of Epicurean atomism: secondary qualities doctrine (atoms have no color/smell — all emergent), four-component mind (heat/wind/air/unnamed 4th), 14 mortalism arguments (Book III), symmetry argument ("pre-birth = post-death nothingness"), mythological hells as earthly psychology. The Epicurean–Enlightenment transmission chain runs through him; rediscovered 1417 by Poggio Bracciolini. Direct philosophical counterpoint to Plato (Forms), Aristotle (final causes), and Plotinus (immortal soul/return).

**Neoplatonism (Plotinus, 2026-06-04 addition)**:
- [[Thinkers/Plotinus]] - Greek philosopher (c. 204–270 CE), founder of Neoplatonism. *Enneads* (Porphyry ed.): the One (transcendent ineffable source/Good) emanates hierarchically (One → Intellect/Nous with Forms → Soul → matter) without loss; soul's natural return (epistrophe) via purification, virtue ("likeness to God"), and contemplation to henosis (union). Synthesis of Plato (Forms, soul), Aristotle (Intellect), Stoicism (virtue, likeness) into mystical metaphysics of unity and ascent. Major influence on Christian, Islamic, and Renaissance thought. "Plato redivivus." Wallis (1972) adds: identification of metaphysical realities with states of consciousness; the undescended soul; cosmic sympathy's limits; the problems bequeathed to the later school.
- [[Thinkers/Porphyry]] - Plotinus's pupil, biographer, and editor (c. 232–c. 305): arranged the *Enneads*, wrote the *Isagoge*, "telescoped" the Hypostases, criticized theurgy rationally (*Letter to Anebo*), chief anti-Christian polemicist and conduit of Neoplatonism to the Latin West (Augustine's lens). | thinker | metaphysics, logic
- [[Thinkers/Iamblichus]] - Syrian Neoplatonist (d. c. 326) who fixed later Neoplatonism's framework: Ineffable beyond the One, triadic stratification (Law of Mean Terms, Unparticipated/Participated/Participant), fully descended soul, theurgy and divine grace over contemplation (*De Mysteriis*), the *skopos* rule of exegesis. | thinker | metaphysics, philosophy-of-religion
- [[Thinkers/Proclus]] - Head of the Athenian Academy (412–85), systematizer of late Neoplatonism: *Elements of Theology* (geometric deduction of the scale of being), the henads, monē–proodos–epistrophē, evil as parhypostasis, providence "by the gods' very existence," two vehicles of the soul; source of the *Liber de Causis* and Pseudo-Dionysius. | thinker | metaphysics, philosophy-of-religion
- [[Thinkers/Arthur O. Lovejoy]] - American philosopher/historian of ideas (1873–1962). *The Great Chain of Being* (1936): definitive tracing of the Chain (plenitude + continuity + gradation) as the pervasive presupposition from Plato/Aristotle/Neoplatonism through medieval/Enlightenment to temporalizing and "negative outcome" (rationalist hypothesis of complete intelligibility fails; world contingent). Initiates History of Ideas leg; method of unit-ideas. Cross to classical (the Chain as historical vehicle of emanation/gradation) and Phil of Science (background cosmology critiqued by Popper/Kuhn et al.). See new source + 4 concepts.

**Hellenistic Skepticism (Pyrrhonism, 2026-06-03 addition)**:
- [[Thinkers/Sextus Empiricus]] - Greek philosopher/physician (fl. late 2nd–early 3rd cent. CE), principal surviving source for Pyrrhonian Skepticism (*Outlines of Pyrrhonism*); method: equipollence (isosthenia) leading to epoché (suspension of judgment) and ataraxia (tranquility).

**Medieval Scholasticism (Anselm & Aquinas, 2026-06-11 Ingest)**:
- [[Thinkers/Anselm of Canterbury]] - Scholastic philosopher and theologian (1033–1109), Archbishop of Canterbury; father of Scholasticism and developer of the original ontological argument.
- [[Thinkers/Thomas Aquinas]] - Dominican theologian/philosopher (1225–1274, "Angelical Doctor" or "Angelic Doctor"). *Summa Theologica* I-II (Prima Secundae): last end/happiness as beatific vision of God (elevating Aristotle's eudaimonia); natural law (rational participation in eternal law); grace perfects nature; virtues (theological + cardinal); quaestio method. Major synthesis of Aristotle + Christian theology, natural law, and grace. New concepts: Beatific Vision/Last End, Natural Law. Extends classical ethics into medieval scholasticism.

**Patristics / Augustine (2026-06-04 addition)**:
- [[Thinkers/Augustine]] - Bishop of Hippo (354–430 CE, "Doctor Gratiae"). *Confessions* (13 books): pilgrimage of grace, "restless heart until it rests in you," divided will, garden conversion ("tolle lege"), memory as inner path to God/self-knowledge, time as distentio animi, evil as privation, Neo-Platonism as bridge (Book 7) to Christian resolution. Primary patristic source for Aquinas (Trinitarian mind analogy, grace/nature, beatific rest); explicit engagement with Plotinus. New concepts: Restless Heart, Memory-Interiority, Time-Eternity. Enormous influence on Western thought and the vault's classical-to-medieval thread.

**Early Modern / Rationalism and Empiricism**:
- [[Thinkers/Thomas Hobbes]] - English political theorist and materialist philosopher (1588–1679). Author of *Leviathan* (1651); developed a mechanistic view of human nature, a bleak concept of the State of Nature, and a contractarian defense of absolute sovereignty.
- [[Thinkers/Descartes]] - René Descartes (1596–1650). *Meditations on First Philosophy* (with Objections and Replies): hyperbolic doubt, cogito as first certainty, causal + ontological proofs of God as non-deceiver, clear and distinct criterion, real distinction of *res cogitans* (thinking substance) and *res extensa* (extended substance) with intimate union in the human composite. Foundational for modern epistemology, rationalism, mind-body dualism, and mechanistic science. Major hinge/rupture and synthesis point for the vault's classical cluster (engages Plato, Aristotle, Augustine, Aquinas, Sextus skepticism, Stoics, Plotinus). New concepts on method, cogito, dualism, God proofs, etc. See dedicated entity + source summary.
- [[Thinkers/Spinoza]] - Benedict de Spinoza (Baruch Espinoza, 1632–1677). *Ethics*: substance monism (*Deus sive Natura*), attribute parallelism (thought and extension as parallel expressions of the one substance), conatus as actual essence and striving to persevere, affects (active from adequate ideas vs. passive passions), geometrical method applied to metaphysics and ethics, freedom as self-determination through adequate ideas, blessedness as the intellectual love of God (*amor Dei intellectualis*) arising from intuitive knowledge. Radical naturalist monist counterpoint and synthesis to Descartes and the classical cluster. Exceptional methodological model for the vault. New concepts on monism, parallelism, conatus, affects, method, freedom/blessedness. See dedicated entity + source summary.
- [[Thinkers/Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz]] - German rationalist philosopher and mathematician (1646–1716). Developed the monadology (immaterial substance pluralism), Pre-Established Harmony, the Principle of Sufficient Reason, and optimistic theodicy.
- [[Thinkers/Locke]] - John Locke (1632–1704). *An Essay Concerning Human Understanding* (2nd ed. text): the mind at birth as empty cabinet / white paper / tabula rasa (no innate ideas or principles); all ideas and knowledge acquired via sensation and reflection (historical plain method). Book I: full refutation of innate speculative principles (no universal assent; children/idiots lack them; use-of-reason evasion fails) and innate practical/moral principles (conscience shaped by custom; no universal rules; actions > professions; cultural/historical counterexamples; moral rules require proof). Direct predecessor to Hume; primary empiricist counter to Descartes innatism. New entity + 2 concepts (this chunk). See dedicated entity + source summary.
- [[Thinkers/George Berkeley]] - Irish empiricist philosopher and Bishop of Cloyne (1685–1753); developed subjective idealism ("immaterialism") and the maxim *esse est percipi*.
- [[Thinkers/Hume]] - David Hume (1711–76). Scottish Enlightenment; culmination of British empiricism. *Enquiry concerning Human Understanding*: the Copy Principle (ideas copy impressions; the meaning-test "from what impression?"), Hume's Fork (relations of ideas vs. matters of fact), the problem of induction (no rational ground for past→future inference), causation as constant conjunction + the mind's customary transition (two definitions of cause), custom/belief as a lively feeling (the "sceptical solution"; reasoning as "a species of instinct"), compatibilism (liberty and necessity reconciled and both required for morality), the argument against miracles ("proportion belief to the evidence"), the proportionality critique of the design argument, and mitigated (Academical) scepticism ("commit it to the flames"). Third path on scepticism alongside Sextus (Pyrrhonian) and Descartes (hyperbolic). New entity + 8 concepts. See dedicated entity + source summary.
- [[Thinkers/Kant]] - Immanuel Kant (1724–1804). *Groundwork for the Metaphysics of Morals* (1785): good will only unconditional; duty from respect for law; CI system (universal law + Humanity as end + Kingdom of Ends); hyp vs cat imperatives; autonomy supreme vs heteronomy; freedom key to autonomy + two standpoints (sensible/heteronomy vs intelligible/autonomy/"as if"); incomprehensibility of pure practical reason. A priori pure metaphysics of morals. Pivotal synthesis/critique responding to Hume (full "dogmatic slumber" response primary via Groundwork + freedom/standpoints) while securing vs skepticism; contrasts Locke (experience), Aristotle (eudaimonia), Spinoza (conatus). New entity + 9 concepts total. See dedicated entity + source summary.

**German Idealism & Post-Kantian Voluntarism (2026-06-11 Ingest)**:

**18th & 19th-Century Political & Ethical Philosophy (2026-06-11 Ingest)**:

**Nietzsche (1844-1900)**:
- [[Thinkers/Nietzsche]] - Friedrich Nietzsche (1844–1900). *Beyond Good and Evil* (1886): Preface (Truth as woman, dogmatists, Plato's Pure Spirit/Good in Itself as worst error, Christianity as Platonism for the "people," tension of the bow we free spirits still possess); Chapter 1 (Will to Truth questioned, belief in antitheses of values as fundamental prejudice, philosophy as involuntary/unconscious autobiography and confession with moral purpose as vital germ, will to power as most spiritual tyrannical impulse/"creation of the world," "recognize untruth as condition of life" and place oneself "beyond good and evil," "philosophers of the dangerous 'Perhaps,'" "frog perspectives," explicit critiques of Kant ("Tartuffery" of categorical), Spinoza (hocus-pocus/mask for timidity), Stoics ("living according to Nature" as fraud), Plato). **American Founding (Publius, 2026-06-04)**:

**American Pragmatism (James, 2026-06-04)**:
- [[Thinkers/William James]] - American philosopher/psychologist (1842–1910), leading systematizer of pragmatism. *Pragmatism* (1907): the pragmatic method (trace practical consequences; theories as instruments); the genetic theory of truth ("truth *happens*… is made true by verification"; "the true is the expedient"; truth *in rebus*); tender-minded vs. tough-minded temperaments; the one and the many (→ pluralism; Absolute vs. Ultimate); common sense as inherited *denkmittel*; Schiller's humanism (malleable reality, "we carve the statue"); meliorism + pluralistic theism (God as *primus inter pares*). **20th-Century Analytic Philosophy (Russell, 2026-06-04)**:
- [[Thinkers/Bertrand Russell]] - British philosopher, logician, mathematician (1872–1970); co-founder of analytic philosophy, co-author of *Principia Mathematica*, Nobel Literature 1950. Author of the survey *A History of Western Philosophy* (1945). Empiricist heir (Locke/Hume) turned logical analyst: philosophy as the "No Man's Land" between theology and science; social cohesion vs. individual liberty + liberalism; logical analysis as piecemeal scientific method; the "cosmic impiety" critique of pragmatism's theory of truth. Opinionated verdicts on nearly every vault thinker (Spinoza highest, Aquinas/Plato/Nietzsche lowest). **Two ingested works**: the survey *History* (1945) and the primary epistemology *The Problems of Philosophy* (1912 — sense-data realism, acquaintance/description, correspondence theory of truth, the value of philosophy). Entity + 3 *History* concepts + 4 *Problems* concepts.

**Foundations of Machine Intelligence (Turing, 1950)**:
- [[Concepts/Imitation Game (Turing)]] - Turing's operational replacement for "Can machines think?": three-player text-only guessing game (man/woman/interrogator); machine takes the man's part; success = interrogator fooled at comparable rates. Behavioral/functional criterion; ancestor of the "Turing Test." Grounds all later evaluation of machine intelligence (including modern LLMs under sustained interrogation).
- [[Concepts/Universal Machine (Turing)]] - A digital computer, with adequate storage and speed and a suitable programme, can mimic any discrete-state machine. All digital computers are equivalent (speed aside); one general-purpose machine suffices for all computable processes. Babbage's Analytical Engine already contained the essential ideas (mechanical). Realized by every contemporary computer, VM, and LLM inference stack.
- [[Concepts/Discrete-State Machine (Turing)]] - Machines whose states are definite and distinct enough to be tabulated (next state = f(current state, input)); fully predictable from table + initial conditions + inputs (no avalanche from tiny errors, unlike continuous systems). Digital computers are discrete-state and therefore universal within the class. Key formal move that makes the imitation game well-defined and feasible for programmable machines.
- [[Concepts/Learning Machines (Turing)]] - Rather than hand-programme an adult mind, build "child machines" (minimal mechanism, "rather little mechanism, and lots of blank sheets") and educate them. Evolution analogy (structure=heredity, changes=mutation, experimenter judgment=selection); random element; punishments/rewards via efficient "unemotional" (symbolic) channels; imperatives encoded inside logical systems. The practical research programme Turing offered for reaching imitation-game performance.
- [[Concepts/Lady Lovelace's Objection]] - "The Analytical Engine has no pretensions to originate anything. It can do whatever we know how to order it to perform." Turing's replies: limited evidence available in 1842; machines frequently surprise their programmers; the "all consequences seen simultaneously" assumption is false for humans too. Direct ancestor of modern "stochastic parrot / remix vs. genuine novelty" debates; answered by the learning-machines programme.

**Information Theory & Communication (Shannon, 1948)**:
- [[Concepts/Entropy (Shannon)]] — H = −∑ p_i log_2 p_i (bits): measure of uncertainty/choice/information produced by a discrete source or set of probabilities. Properties: zero only on certainty; max when equiprobable; additive. English entropy rate implies ~50% redundancy. | concept | information-theory, ai
- [[Concepts/Channel Capacity (Shannon)]] — C = max rate (bits/sec) of reliable information transfer over a channel (noiseless: max entropy/sec; noisy: max mutual information H(x)−H(x|y)). Sharp limit. | concept | information-theory, ai, communication
- [[Concepts/Redundancy (Shannon)]] — 1 − (H / H_max): fraction of a source determined by statistical structure rather than free choice. English ~50% (local constraints). Enables compression and noise resistance. | concept | information-theory, language, ai
- [[Concepts/Equivocation (Shannon)]] — H(x|y): conditional entropy of input given output; remaining uncertainty after noisy reception (information lost to noise). Rate through channel = H(x) − H(x|y). | concept | information-theory, ai
- [[Concepts/Noiseless Channel Coding Theorem]] — Source of entropy rate H can be losslessly encoded for a noiseless channel of capacity C at rate approaching C/H (but not higher). Justifies entropy as the information rate; basis of compression. | concept | information-theory, computation
- [[Concepts/Noisy Channel Coding Theorem]] — If source rate H ≤ C, coding exists for arbitrarily low error/equivocation over noisy channel. If H > C, equivocation ≥ H−C. Reliable communication possible up to capacity. | concept | information-theory, ai, communication

**Causal Reasoning (Pearl, 2018)**:
- [[Concepts/Ladder of Causation (Pearl)]] — Three-rung hierarchy: Association/Seeing (P(Y|X), rung 1 — all statistics and deep learning), Intervention/Doing (P(Y|do(X)), rung 2 — requires diagram), Counterfactuals/Imagining (rung 3 — requires SCM). Moving up the ladder requires structural assumptions; data alone supply only rung 1. Pearl's mini-Turing test: causal reasoning as the AI criterion. | concept | causality, ai, philosophy-of-science
- [[Concepts/Do-Operator and Do-Calculus (Pearl)]] — do(X=x) = graph surgery (remove arrows into X, set X=x); separates "seeing" from "doing." Do-calculus: 3 rules for transforming interventional expressions; proven complete (Shpitser 2006). Back-door adjustment formula; front-door adjustment for unmeasured confounders. | concept | causality, statistics
- [[Concepts/Causal Diagrams and DAGs (Pearl)]] — Directed acyclic graphs: nodes = variables, directed edges = causal mechanisms. Three fundamental junctions: chain (A→B→C, mediation), fork (A←B→C, confounding), collider (A→B←C — conditioning *opens* the path; source of Berkson/birth-weight paradox). d-separation. Historical precursor: Sewall Wright's path diagrams (1920s). | concept | causality, statistics, ai
- [[Concepts/Confounding and Back-Door Criterion (Pearl)]] — Confounder = common cause of X and Y, creating spurious association. Back-door criterion: a set Z deconfounds if it blocks all back-door paths from X to Y without conditioning on any descendant of X or collider. Front-door adjustment (via mediator when confounder unmeasured). Instrumental variables. Simpson's paradox requires causal structure to resolve. | concept | causality, statistics, epidemiology
- [[Concepts/Counterfactuals and Structural Causal Models (Pearl)]] — SCMs: each variable Xᵢ = fᵢ(parents, Uᵢ); three-step counterfactual computation (abduction→action→prediction). Probability of necessity (PN / but-for causation) and probability of sufficiency (PS). Neyman-Rubin potential outcomes as rung-3 without diagrams (identifiability unclear). Climate attribution. | concept | causality, statistics, philosophy-of-science
- [[Concepts/Mediation and Direct-Indirect Effects (Pearl)]] — How X affects Y: direct (X→Y) vs indirect (X→M→Y). Controlled direct effect (CDE), natural direct effect (NDE), natural indirect effect (NIE) — NDE/NIE require counterfactuals. Mediation Formula. Cases: scurvy/vitamin C (wrong mediator), Barbara Burks (1926, collider bias pioneer), Berkeley admissions (Simpson's paradox as mediation problem). | concept | causality, statistics

**Early Modern / Rationalism (Descartes, 2026-06-04)**:
- [[Concepts/Method of Hyperbolic Doubt (Descartes)]] - Systematic, universal, methodical doubt (senses, dream argument, evil demon) as tool to demolish prejudices and reach bedrock certainty for reconstruction (not Pyrrhonian suspension/ataraxia). First Meditation; model for vault ingest/lint and reasoning scaffolds.
- [[Concepts/Cogito (Descartes)]] - "I am, I exist" necessarily true whenever conceived (even under maximal deception). The 'I' is a thinking thing (*res cogitans*). Archimedean point; performative self-certainty.
- [[Concepts/Clear and Distinct Perception (Descartes)]] - Criterion of truth: whatever is perceived very clearly and distinctly is true (secured by God as non-deceiver). Requires attention and withdrawal from senses/prejudices. Central epistemic standard; vault quality bar for claims and syntheses.
- [[Concepts/Res Cogitans and Res Extensa (Descartes)]] - Two really distinct substances (thinking/non-extended mind vs. extended/non-thinking body) yet closely conjoined/fused in the human composite (not pilot-in-ship). Primary vs. secondary qualities. Implications for vault layered architecture (raw vs. wiki, intellect vs. sensation).
- [[Concepts/Causal Argument for God's Existence from Objective Reality (Descartes)]] - Idea of God has more objective reality than any finite cause (including self) can supply; causal principle ("at least as much reality in cause as effect") requires God as cause. Plus conservation as continuous creation. Complements ontological; model for tracing sufficient causes.

**Early Modern / Rationalism (Spinoza, 2026-06-04)**:
- [[Concepts/Deus sive Natura - Substance Monism (Spinoza)]] - Only one substance (God or Nature); everything is in God and cannot be or be conceived without God. Finite things are modes, not separate substances or parts. Natura naturans (active attributes) vs. natura naturata (modes). Immanent cause; no transcendent creator.
- [[Concepts/Attributes and Parallelism (Spinoza)]] - God has infinite attributes (we know thought and extension), each expressing the essence of the one substance. "The order and connection of ideas is the same as the order and connection of things" (2p7). Mind and body (or sources and wiki representations) are one and the same thing expressed under different attributes; no cross-attribute causation.
- [[Concepts/Conatus (Spinoza)]] - Each thing, as far as it can by its own power, strives to persevere in its being; this striving is the actual essence of the thing (3p6–7). Foundation of affects (joy = increase in power; sadness = decrease) and ethics (virtue = power; highest good = understanding that most augments power).
- [[Concepts/Affects and Active vs Passive Emotions (Spinoza)]] - Affects are bodily affections (changes in power of acting) together with their ideas. Active affects (actions) arise from adequate ideas (we act); passive affects/passions arise from inadequate ideas (we are acted on). Ethical task: increase active affects and freedom through knowledge.
- [[Concepts/Geometrical Method - Rationalism (Spinoza)]] - *More geometrico*: definitions of essences → axioms → deductive chain of propositions. Because conceptual order mirrors causal order in nature (1a4 + parallelism), the method reveals the actual natures and relations of things. Rationalism as the thorough intelligibility of reality.
- [[Concepts/Freedom and Blessedness - Intellectual Love of God (Spinoza)]] - Freedom = acting from the necessity of one’s own nature (adequate ideas). Blessedness (*beatitudo*) is the highest joy accompanying the intellectual love of God (*amor Dei intellectualis*)—the joy of intuitive knowledge (*scientia intuitiva*) of God/Nature as cause of our joy. Eternal (sub specie aeternitatis); our greatest virtue and happiness. "All things excellent are as difficult as they are rare."

**Early Modern / Empiricist Foundations (Locke, 2026-06-04)**:
- [[Concepts/Tabula Rasa - Blank Slate or Empty Cabinet (Locke)]] - The mind at birth is an empty cabinet or white paper, void of all characters; no innate ideas or principles. All ideas and knowledge are acquired through experience (sensation and reflection). The constructive starting point of British empiricism and direct foundation for Hume's Copy Principle.
- [[Concepts/No Innate Principles or Ideas (Locke)]] - Systematic refutation (Book I): no universal assent to candidate speculative maxims ("what is, is"; law of non-contradiction) or practical/moral rules; children, idiots, and diverse cultures lack them; "when we come to the use of reason" and "assent on first hearing" fail as proofs; component ideas (identity, God, etc.) are themselves acquired; conscience and moral practice are shaped by custom and interest. Primary anti-innatist text; explicit contrast to Descartes and Plato.
- [[Concepts/Sensation and Reflection as the Two Fountains of Ideas (Locke)]] - All ideas originate in EXPERIENCE via two sources: SENSATION (external objects → sensible qualities) and REFLECTION (mind's own operations → perceiving, thinking, doubting, willing, etc.). Mind starts as white paper; no other originals. Direct basis for Hume's impressions/ideas.
- [[Concepts/Simple Ideas (Locke)]] - Uncompounded uniform appearances (e.g., coldness/hardness as distinct as smell/whiteness); the atomic materials of knowledge. Mind passive in receiving them from the two fountains; cannot invent or destroy simple ideas, only compound them.
- [[Concepts/Primary and Secondary Qualities (Locke)]] - Primary (solidity, extension, figure, number, motion/rest): real, inseparable from body, resemblances in ideas. Secondary (colors, sounds, tastes, etc.): powers in bodies (depending on primary) to produce sensations in us; no resemblance in the bodies. Ideas (mind) carefully distinguished from qualities (bodies). Key for perception, science, and contrasts with Lucretius/Descartes/Hume.
- [[Concepts/State of Nature, Property, and Revolution (Locke)]] - State of nature (perfect freedom/equality under law of preservation); property by labor mixing with commons (spoilage + "enough and as good" provisos); government by consent to protect rights; right of revolution on breach of trust.
- [[Concepts/Toleration (Locke)]] - True religion = inward persuasion; magistrate force limited to civil interests (life/liberty/property); church voluntary; limits for atheists (no oaths), foreign-loyalty religions, intolerant sects.
- [[Concepts/Nominal and Real Essences (Locke)]] - Nominal essence = abstract idea/qualities by which we name/classify sorts (conventional); real essence = underlying insensible constitution (largely unknown). Explains limits on substance knowledge.
- [[Concepts/Association of Ideas (Locke)]] — custom or chance creates "wrong connexion" of ideas not allied by nature; leads to obstinacy, antipathies, "a degree of madness" in most men | concept | epistemology
- [[Concepts/Complex Ideas, Modes, Substances, and Relations (Locke)]] — mind's voluntary combinations of simples: modes (affections), substances (particular things + idea of substratum), relations (comparing) | concept | epistemology
- [[Concepts/Faith and Reason - Enthusiasm (Locke)]] — reason as natural revelation; faith as assent on divine credit above but not contrary to reason; enthusiasm (private "revelation" without evidence) is dangerous madness; reason last judge | concept | epistemology, religion
- [[Concepts/Identity and Diversity - Personal Identity (Locke)]] — identity = same existence at determined time/place; for persons, consciousness (not same substance or soul); personal identity extends as far as consciousness of past actions | concept | metaphysics
- [[Concepts/Knowledge as Perception of Agreement or Disagreement of Ideas (Locke)]] — knowledge = perception of agreement/disagreement of ideas (4 sorts: identity, relation, coexistence, real existence); degrees intuitive/demonstrative/sensitive | concept | epistemology
- [[Concepts/Words as Signs of Ideas and Language (Locke)]] — words voluntary signs of ideas in speaker's mind (arbitrary, not natural); general terms stand for abstract ideas; abuse when used without clear ideas or for things directly | concept | philosophy of language

**Kantian Ethics (Kant, 2026-06-04)**:
- [[Concepts/Good Will (Kant)]] - The only thing good without limitation. Good in itself through its willing/principle (from duty/respect for law), not through effects or success in achieving ends. Even if it accomplishes nothing, it "shines like a jewel for itself." Nature's purpose for reason is a good will (the highest good and condition for all else), not happiness (which instinct could secure better).
- [[Concepts/Duty (Kant)]] - The necessity of an action from respect for the law. An action has moral worth only if done from duty (not merely in conformity with duty from inclination or prudence). Examples: preserving life or beneficence from duty when no inclination remains; promoting happiness from duty when inclinations conflict. Duty makes the good will visible under human hindrances.
- [[Concepts/Categorical Imperative - Universal Law Formulation (Kant)]] - "I ought never to conduct myself except so that I could also will that my maxim become a universal law." (Act only according to that maxim whereby you can at the same time will that it should become a universal law.) Test for duty: can the maxim be consistently willed as universal without self-contradiction or destroying the practice it presupposes? False promise example (universalized, promises lose all point). Recognized by common rational moral cognition; a priori formal law independent of consequences/inclination/happiness.
- [[Concepts/Hypothetical and Categorical Imperatives (Kant)]] - Hypothetical (conditional on ends willed: skill/technical or prudence for happiness) vs categorical (unconditional moral law, form/universal lawfulness only; apodictic, binding all rational wills). Only categorical yields practical law; all prior ethics reduce to hypothetical/heteronomous.
- [[Concepts/Formula of Humanity as End in Itself (Kant)]] - "Act so that you use humanity... always at the same time as end and never merely as means." Rational nature has absolute worth as objective end (not conditioned by inclination); persons limit arbitrary choice; grounds non-instrumentalization (examples: suicide, lying promise).
- [[Concepts/Kingdom of Ends (Kant)]] - Realm of rational beings as ends through their own universal self-legislation ("act as if... legislative member in a universal realm of ends"). "As if" the moral community were realized via maxims all could author; retains categorical force independent of others' compliance. Complements universal law + humanity.
- [[Concepts/Autonomy and Heteronomy of the Will (Kant)]] - Autonomy (will law to itself via maxims' suitability for universal legislation) = sole supreme principle of morality and ground of dignity. Heteronomy (law from any external object/inclination/"alien" source) yields only conditioned imperatives and cannot ground morality; diagnosis of all prior systems (happiness, feeling, perfection, divine will).
- [[Concepts/Freedom as the Key to Autonomy (Kant)]] - Freedom (independence of alien causes) flows into positive law-governed causality of special kind = autonomy = CI/moral law. "Free will and will under moral laws are the same." Must be presupposed for all rational beings under the *idea* of freedom (practical "as if"); synthetic a priori requires "third" (positive freedom) pointing beyond sense.
- [[Concepts/Two Standpoints: Sensible World and Intelligible World (Kant)]] - Same subject: as appearance in world of sense (natural laws, heteronomy, affected) vs as intelligence/thing in itself in intelligible world (free, autonomous, self-given moral law). Moral 'ought' = intelligible self's necessary volition for sensible self. "As if" member of intelligible makes CI possible; resolves freedom vs natural necessity dialectic without contradiction.

**Nietzsche and the Revaluation of Values (Nietzsche, 2026-06-04)**:
- [[Concepts/Will to Power (Nietzsche)]] - The most spiritual Will to Power—the tyrannical impulse of "creation of the world" and the "causa prima"—as the fundamental driver behind the "will to truth," logic, valuations, and all great philosophy. Philosophy as the attempt of imperious impulses to make themselves lord; "it always creates the world in its own image."
- [[Concepts/Perspectivism (Nietzsche)]] - All "truths," valuations, and claims are provisional perspectives ("frog perspectives" from some corner or "below"), often physiological or value-driven. No absolute antitheses of values or pure disinterested "Truth"; higher value may lie in what was called "evil" (pretense, will to delusion). "To recognize untruth as a condition of life."
- [[Concepts/Philosophy as Involuntary and Unconscious Autobiography (Nietzsche)]] - Every great philosophy is the "confession of its originator, and a species of involuntary and unconscious auto-biography"; the moral (or immoral) purpose is the "true vital germ." To understand abstruse claims, ask "What morality do they aim at?" Nothing impersonal in the philosopher; morality testifies to the order of his deepest impulses.
- [[Concepts/Beyond Good and Evil (Nietzsche)]] - To move beyond the traditional moral antitheses ("good/evil") and the dogmatist belief in value-antitheses (Plato's Good in Itself, Kant's categorical, etc.). "Recognize untruth as a condition of life" and operate with "dangerous 'Perhapses'" as prelude to revaluation by new "philosophers of the dangerous 'Perhaps'" and "free, very free spirits" (we still have the tension of the bow from the struggle against Platonism/Christianity).
- [[Concepts/Belief in Antitheses of Values (Nietzsche)]] - The deepest "prejudice of philosophers" (BGE aph. 2): the metaphysicians' unexamined faith that the highest-value things must have a separate, pure origin (Being, the Thing-in-itself, God) opposed to the low/"evil" — when perhaps the high and low are "insidiously related, knotted, and crocheted," even "essentially identical." A master lint heuristic (question the binary) and the root of the genealogical method.
- [[Concepts/Philosophers of the Dangerous "Perhaps" (Nietzsche)]] - Nietzsche's "new order of philosophers" / "free, very free spirits" of the future who dare the "dangerous 'Perhaps'," recognize untruth as a condition of life, keep "the tension of the bow," and *create* values (will to power) rather than receive truths. The constructive flip-side of the critique; charter for exploratory, non-dogmatic synthesis.

**Early Modern / Empiricism & Scepticism (Hume, 2026-06-04)**:
- [[Concepts/Impressions and Ideas - Copy Principle (Hume)]] - Perceptions divide into impressions (lively) and ideas (faint copies), differing in force and vivacity; every simple idea copies a prior simple impression. Meaning-test: "from what impression is that supposed idea derived?" (Hume's microscope; ancestor of verificationism). The missing-shade-of-blue counterexample. Anti-innatist engine; symbol-grounding parallels.
- [[Concepts/Hume's Fork (Relations of Ideas vs Matters of Fact)]] - Exhaustive division of inquiry: relations of ideas (a priori, demonstrative, contrary self-contradictory — math) vs. matters of fact (contingent, contrary always conceivable; known only by experience). Conceivability proves possibility, never existence. Detonates rationalist metaphysics; sets up induction; yields the "flames" meaning-filter. Ancestor of analytic/synthetic.
- [[Concepts/Problem of Induction (Hume)]] - All factual inference presupposes the future will resemble the past, a Uniformity Principle provable neither by demonstration (no contradiction in change) nor by experience (circular). So induction is not founded on reason. The defining problem of the epistemology of science; the foundational caveat on ML generalization / distribution shift. Answered (Sec. V) by custom, not reason.
- [[Concepts/Necessary Connexion and the Two Definitions of Cause (Hume)]] - No impression of power in objects ("conjoined, but never connected"); the idea of necessity copies the mind's customary transition after constant conjunction. Two definitions of cause: regularity (with counterfactual gloss) + psychological. Rejects occasionalism (reopens the Ghazali/Ibn Rushd debate empirically). The "New Hume" (regularity vs. sceptical realism) crux.
- [[Concepts/Custom and Belief (Hume)]] - The "sceptical solution": custom (habit) is "the great guide of human life"; belief is not a separate idea but a more vivid, involuntary manner of conceiving (vivacity) produced by a present impression. "Reasoning is a species of instinct." Naturalized epistemology; strong predictive-processing / connectionist resonance (confidence-as-vivacity, inference-as-trained-transition).
- [[Concepts/Compatibilism - Liberty and Necessity (Hume)]] - The dispute is verbal. Necessity = constant conjunction + inference (and it holds of human action — the uniformity of human nature; natural and moral evidence form one chain). Liberty = unconstrained action per the will (not absence of cause = chance). Both essential to morality. The classic compatibilist position; contrasts Spinoza's harder necessitarianism, Stoic fate, Epicurean swerve.
- [[Concepts/Of Miracles (Hume)]] - "A wise man proportions his belief to the evidence." A miracle violates a law of nature backed by uniform experience; no testimony suffices unless its falsehood would be more miraculous than the event — and (Part II) none ever has (witnesses, love of wonder, barbarous provenance, mutual cancellation of rival religions). Proto-Bayesian testimony calculus; high practical value for source reliability and risk judgment.
- [[Concepts/Mitigated Scepticism (Hume)]] - The vault's third path on scepticism: Pyrrhonism is powerless in life ("Nature is always too strong for principle"); its useful residue is durable modesty + limitation of inquiry to quantity/number (demonstration) and matters of fact (experience). "Commit it then to the flames." Includes Sec. XI's proportionality critique of the design argument. The ideal maintainer temperament (calibrated, scoped, anti-dogmatic).

**American Pragmatism (James, 2026-06-04)**:
- [[Concepts/Pragmatic Method (James)]] - Settle disputes / fix meaning by tracing each alternative's concrete practical consequences ("no difference anywhere that doesn't make a difference elsewhere"). Forward-looking, deflationary toward verbal disputes; theories as instruments, not "solving names." Peirce's principle generalized; the squirrel anecdote. A sharp lint heuristic.
- [[Concepts/Pragmatic Theory of Truth (James)]] - Truth is not static copying but a *verification-process*: "truth *happens* to an idea… is made true by events." Agreement = fruitful *leading*; truth lives "on a credit system"; "the true is only the expedient in the way of our thinking"; truth is *in rebus* (like health/wealth/strength), grows out of prior truths. Rebuts the "whatever is pleasant" charge. The vault's third path on truth.
- [[Concepts/Tender-Minded and Tough-Minded (James)]] - Philosophy's history as a clash of temperaments: tender-minded (rationalist/idealist/religious/monist/dogmatic) vs. tough-minded (empiricist/materialist/sceptical/pluralist). "The potentest of all our premises is never mentioned." Pragmatism as the mediator that lets a fact-lover keep a religion. Kin to Nietzsche's "philosophy as autobiography."
- [[Concepts/The One and the Many (James)]] - "The most central of all philosophic problems," handled pragmatically: oneness and manyness "absolutely coordinate," true in as many namable respects as fail. Absolute monism (Vedanta) rests on mystical, not intellectual, grounds; pragmatism sides with pluralism and may replace the "Absolute" with the "Ultimate" (oneness at the *end*). Mirrors the vault's link-graph architecture.
- [[Concepts/Meliorism (James)]] - Between optimism (salvation inevitable) and pessimism (impossible): salvation as a *grounded possibility* whose probability rises as actual conditions (incl. our own acts) accumulate. "Possible chicken means actual egg." The world's-author wager; pluralistic theism (God as *primus inter pares*). The vault's native theology of the unfinished, compounding wiki.
- [[Concepts/Humanism - Reality as Malleable (Schiller and James)]] - Schiller's humanism: "to an unascertainable extent our truths are man-made products"; reality is "malleable," "we carve the statue." "The *that* is reality's own; the *what* depends on the *which*… on us" (constellations, 27, the audience). Guarded against caprice by reality's "resisting" parts. Constructivist edge of pragmatism; mirror for the vault's carving of structure from raw sources (with provenance).

**20th-Century Analytic Philosophy (Russell & Wittgenstein, 2026-06-11 Ingest)**:
- [[Concepts/Philosophy as the No Man's Land between Theology and Science (Russell)]] - Russell's definition: philosophy is reasoned speculation on questions where "definite knowledge has so far been unascertainable" — between dogmatic theology and settled science. Its chief gift: "to teach how to live without certainty, and yet without being paralyzed by hesitation." A map for filing claims (science / dogma / philosophy) and the honest temperament of the Open-Questions sections.
- [[Concepts/Social Cohesion vs. Individual Liberty (Russell)]] - The master political dialectic from 600 BC to now: disciplinarians (tighten bonds; dogma, "nobility," irrationalist, anti-science) vs. libertarians (relax them; scientific, utilitarian, rationalist). Civilizations oscillate between ossification and dissolution; **liberalism** (Locke) is the attempt to escape — "stability without more restraints than necessary." Pairs directly with the Federalist cluster.
- [[Concepts/Philosophy of Logical Analysis (Russell)]] - Russell's own school and the book's conclusion: make logical analysis the main business of philosophy; renounce any "higher" knowing; proceed piecemeal toward "successive approximations — improvement, not rejection." Science as tentative/probabilistic authority; **scientific truthfulness** as an ethical virtue; **Occam's razor**; the **"cosmic impiety"** warning — the canonical realist critique of [[Concepts/Pragmatic Theory of Truth (James)]].
- [[Concepts/Sense-Data vs. Physical Objects (Russell)]] - *Problems* chs. I–IV: the appearance/reality distinction; **sense-data** (immediately known) vs. the **physical object** (inferred); structural realism (we know matter's *relations*, not its intrinsic nature); realism defended by *simplicity* + instinctive belief; idealism (Berkeley's *esse est percipi*) refuted via the act/object equivocation.
- [[Concepts/Knowledge by Acquaintance and Knowledge by Description (Russell)]] - *Problems* ch. V: direct **acquaintance** (sense-data, memory, introspection, the Self, universals) vs. **description** ("the so-and-so"). "Every proposition we can understand must be composed wholly of constituents with which we are acquainted." Description lets us pass beyond private experience. Mirrors the vault's provenance/grounding rule.
- [[Concepts/Correspondence Theory of Truth (Russell)]] - *Problems* ch. XII: truth = correspondence of belief to **fact**; "minds do not *create* truth or falsehood." Coherence rejected as the *definition* of truth (more than one coherent system; presupposes logic) but kept as a *test*. The realist counter-pole to [[Concepts/Pragmatic Theory of Truth (James)]]; anti-pragmatism already explicit in 1912.
- [[Concepts/The Value of Philosophy (Russell)]] - *Problems* ch. XV: philosophy "studied not for the sake of definite answers… but rather for the sake of the questions themselves"; enlargement of the Self via union with the **not-Self**; the **free intellect**; explicit rejection of "Man is the measure… truth is man-made." Charter for the Open-Questions discipline.
- [[Concepts/Picture Theory of Language (Wittgenstein)]] — The view that language represents reality by mirroring its logical structure; a proposition is a logical picture of facts, and any statement that does not map to possible facts in the world is meaningless. | concept | philosophy-of-language, logic
- [[Concepts/Language-Games (Wittgenstein)]] — Language is not one mirror of reality but countless rule-governed activities ("language-games") woven into action; "to imagine a language means to imagine a form of life" (§19). The later Wittgenstein's replacement for the picture theory. | concept | philosophy-of-language, philosophy-of-mind
- [[Concepts/Meaning as Use (Wittgenstein)]] — "The meaning of a word is its use in the language" (§43); meaning is neither a named object nor an inner idea — "a sign is dead; in use it is alive" (§432). | concept | philosophy-of-language
- [[Concepts/Critique of Ostensive Definition (Wittgenstein)]] — Pointing-and-naming cannot found meaning, since "an ostensive definition can be variously interpreted in every case" (§28); it works only where a place in the language is already prepared. Opens the *Investigations* against Augustine. | concept | philosophy-of-language
- [[Concepts/Family Resemblance (Wittgenstein)]] — General terms (e.g. "game") share no single essence but a web of overlapping, criss-crossing similarities (§§65–67). "Don't think, but look!" Directly denies Plato's Forms. | concept | philosophy-of-language, logic
- [[Concepts/Rule-Following (Wittgenstein)]] — The §201 paradox (every action can be made to accord with a rule) shows that following a rule is "a practice," not the grasp of an interpretation; "I obey the rule blindly" (§219); justification reaches bedrock — "this is simply what I do" (§217). | concept | philosophy-of-language, epistemology
- [[Concepts/Private Language Argument (Wittgenstein)]] — A language referring only to private inner sensations is incoherent: it has no criterion of correctness (§258), and the "beetle in a box" cancels out (§293). A frontal attack on the Cartesian/Lockean inner theatre. | concept | philosophy-of-mind, epistemology
- [[Concepts/Forms of Life (Wittgenstein)]] — Justification ends not in theory but in shared human practice and natural reaction: "What has to be accepted, the given, is — so one could say — forms of life" (II §xi); agreement "in judgments," not opinions (§242). | concept | epistemology, philosophy-of-language
- [[Concepts/Aspect Seeing (Wittgenstein)]] — The duck-rabbit: "I see that it has not changed; and yet I see it differently." Seeing-as is "half visual experience, half thought," presupposing mastery of a technique — undercutting any pure sensory given. | concept | philosophy-of-mind, philosophy-of-perception
- [[Concepts/Philosophy as Therapy (Wittgenstein)]] — Philosophical problems are confusions, not discoveries: "Philosophy is a battle against the bewitchment of our intelligence by means of language" (§109); its aim is "to shew the fly the way out of the fly-bottle" (§309); it "leaves everything as it is" (§124). | concept | metaphilosophy, philosophy-of-language

**Philosophy of Science / Critical Rationalism (Popper, 2026-06-04)** — *catalog block added 2026-06-04 (4 concepts from LSD + 4 from C&R); fixes prior index drift*:
- [[Concepts/Falsifiability as Criterion of Demarcation (Popper)]] - A system is empirical/scientific iff it is **falsifiable** (its logical form permits contradiction by possible "basic statements"). Not verifiability (impossible for universal laws). The more a theory prohibits, the more it says. Now includes the Marx/Freud/Adler-vs-Einstein illustration + seven conclusions + "conventionalist twist" from *C&R*. | concept | epistemology, philosophy-of-science
- [[Concepts/Corroboration (Popper)]] - A theory "proves its mettle" / is **corroborated** by surviving severe attempted refutations — a qualitative *report*, not probability or verification. Tracks (does not prove) [[Concepts/Verisimilitude (Truthlikeness) (Popper)|verisimilitude]]; tied to the three requirements for growth. | concept | epistemology, philosophy-of-science
- [[Concepts/Basic Statements and the Empirical Basis (Popper)]] - Singular falsifying statements are not incorrigible reports of "the given" but intersubjectively testable hypotheses accepted by **decision**; objectivity = intersubjective testability; testability goes on ad infinitum. | concept | epistemology, philosophy-of-science
- [[Concepts/Problem of Demarcation (Kant and Popper)]] - "Kant's problem" — the line between empirical science and metaphysics/logic. Solved by falsifiability (a *proposal*, not a discovery). Includes the Carnap critique (demarcation ≠ a criterion of *meaning*) and "false yet irrefutable" (pure existential statements; determinism vs. indeterminism). | concept | epistemology, philosophy-of-science
- [[Concepts/No Authoritative Sources of Knowledge (Popper)]] - Neither observation nor reason is an authoritative *source*; both empiricism and rationalism share the false "truth is manifest" doctrine (→ the conspiracy theory of ignorance). The real question: "How can we detect and eliminate error?" | concept | epistemology, philosophy-of-science
- [[Concepts/Three Views Concerning Human Knowledge (Popper)]] - Against **essentialism** (ultimate explanation by essences) and **instrumentalism** (theories as mere tools), Popper defends **conjecturalism/scientific realism**: theories are fallible conjectures about reality, true or false, capable of predicting genuinely new kinds of events. | concept | epistemology, philosophy-of-science, metaphysics
- [[Concepts/Verisimilitude (Truthlikeness) (Popper)]] - Nearness to truth = **truth-content minus falsity-content** (Tarski's objective truth as regulative idea). What science values is content/truthlikeness, the *opposite* of probability; a bold, refuted theory can be nearer the truth. (Note: the formal measure was shown flawed in 1974; the intuition survives.) | concept | epistemology, philosophy-of-science
- [[Concepts/Critique of Historicism and Dialectic (Popper)]] - **Prophecy vs. conditional prediction**: long-term historical prophecy is baseless (society is not isolated/stationary/recurrent); critique of Hegelian/Marxist **dialectic** (illegitimate toleration of contradiction); the open society + **piecemeal engineering** vs. utopian violence. The vault's first primary-source Popperian politics. | concept | philosophy-of-science, political-philosophy

**History & Sociology of Science (Kuhn, 2026-06-04)**:
- [[Concepts/Paradigm (Kuhn)]] - A concrete scientific achievement (exemplar) that gives a community its model problems, methods, and standards and so constitutes it as a community; later split into **disciplinary matrix** (broad) and **exemplar** (narrow). Exemplars over explicit rules ("priority of paradigms"). | concept | epistemology, philosophy-of-science, sociology-of-science
- [[Concepts/Normal Science and Puzzle-Solving (Kuhn)]] - Most science is conservative **puzzle-solving** within a paradigm that it *presupposes* (not tests); "how little [its problems] aim to produce major novelties." Cumulative and efficient — which is what eventually exposes the paradigm-breaking anomaly. | concept | epistemology, philosophy-of-science
- [[Concepts/Anomaly and Crisis (Kuhn)]] - Discovery begins when something violates paradigm expectations; anomalies are **not bare refutations** (must be recognized; always present) and provoke **crisis** only when central and intractable. No paradigm abandoned without a successor. | concept | epistemology, philosophy-of-science
- [[Concepts/Scientific Revolutions and Paradigm Shifts (Kuhn)]] - Non-cumulative **paradigm shift** resolving a crisis — a gestalt-switch/**conversion**, "not the sort of battle resolved by proofs." Afterward scientists "work in a different world"; textbooks hide the discontinuity; progress is "**evolution from**, not toward" a goal. | concept | epistemology, philosophy-of-science, history-of-science
- [[Concepts/Incommensurability (Kuhn)]] - Across a revolution, standards, problems, and term-meanings shift (mass, space, earth), so rival paradigms can't be strictly compared and choice isn't logic-forced — yet Kuhn denies this means irrationality/relativism (shared values: accuracy, consistency, scope, simplicity, fruitfulness). | concept | epistemology, philosophy-of-science, philosophy-of-language
- [[Concepts/Entity Realism vs Theory Realism (Hacking)]] - Hacking's key distinction: realism about entities (the objects really exist and have causal powers we use) is independent of realism about theories (theories aim at/literally describe truth). Experiment grounds the former even when the latter is troubled. | concept | epistemology, philosophy-of-science, metaphysics
- [[Concepts/If You Can Spray Them Then They Are Real (Hacking)]] - Hacking's pragmatic criterion: we count as real the entities (e.g. electrons) we can reliably use as tools to intervene and affect other things ("spray positrons/electrons to charge the niobium ball"). Use, not full theoretical belief, commits the experimenter to entity realism. | concept | epistemology, philosophy-of-science
- [[Concepts/Creation of Phenomena (Hacking)]] - Many experiments create stable, repeatable phenomena (by refining apparatus) that did not exist in pure form in nature beforehand; "repeating" is usually improving until the effect is on-demand. Supports entity realism via use. | concept | epistemology, philosophy-of-science, history-of-science
- [[Concepts/Experiment Has a Life of Its Own (Hacking)]] - Theory and experiment have no fixed priority or relation; experiment, instrumentation, and technology often develop substantial independence. "Which comes first?" has no single answer. The experimenter must often be realist about entities used as tools even if anti-realist about theories. | concept | epistemology, philosophy-of-science
- [[Concepts/Observation as a Skill (Hacking)]] - Observation is trained skill, not bare given or always strongly theory-laden. Pre-theoretical observations exist; we observe via instruments; convergence across independent physical systems (different microscopes) underwrites belief. | concept | epistemology, philosophy-of-science

**Logical Positivism (Ayer, 2026-06-04)**:
- [[Concepts/Verification Principle (Ayer)]] - A statement is literally meaningful iff it is analytic or empirically verifiable (some possible sense-experience is relevant to its truth). - [[Concepts/Metaphysics as Meaningless (Ayer)]] - Metaphysical sentences express neither tautologies nor empirical hypotheses; they are literally senseless, not false. Sources: linguistic/grammatical confusions (substance, Being, subsistent entities). Condemns transcendent theology, the soul, rationalist metaphysics. | concept | epistemology, philosophy-of-language, metaphysics
- [[Concepts/Analytic Propositions as Tautologies (Ayer)]] - All a priori truths (logic, mathematics, geometry) are analytic — they record linguistic conventions and say nothing about the world. Defeats Kant's synthetic a priori and Mill's inductivist account. Tautologies can still surprise us through computational complexity. | concept | epistemology, philosophy-of-language, logic
- [[Concepts/Emotive Theory of Ethics (Ayer)]] - Normative ethical (and aesthetic) symbols are pseudo-concepts; they express feeling, not propositions. Moral judgments are neither true nor false. Moral argument is always really about facts, not values. Distinct from subjectivism (which still asserts something). | concept | ethics, philosophy-of-language

**Analytic Philosophy & Post-Positivism (Quine, 2026-06-04)**:
- [[Concepts/Analytic-Synthetic Distinction (Quine)]] - The supposed fundamental cleavage between truths grounded in meanings independently of fact (analytic) and truths grounded in fact (synthetic). Quine argues it cannot be drawn sharply without circular appeal to synonymy or analyticity itself; the positivists' sharpened linguistic version (plus verification) fails internally. Direct attack on the ancestor [[Concepts/Hume's Fork (Relations of Ideas vs Matters of Fact)]]. | concept | epistemology, philosophy-of-language, philosophy-of-science
- [[Concepts/Reductionism and Verification Theory of Meaning (Quine)]] - Each meaningful statement is equivalent to a logical construct on immediate-experience terms (or has unique confirming sensory events). The verification theory (meaning = confirmation method) was meant to ground synonymy independently of analyticity. Quine shows radical and attenuated forms both fail (even Carnap's *Aufbau* leaves key connectives undefined; no isolated empirical content for statements). | concept | epistemology, philosophy-of-science, philosophy-of-language
- [[Concepts/Confirmation Holism (Duhem-Quine Thesis)]] - Statements about the external world face experience not individually but as a corporate body (the web of belief). The unit of empirical significance is the whole of science. Latitude in revision; no statement immune, none beyond pragmatic readjustment. Physical objects etc. as cultural posits. Underdetermination of theory by data. | concept | epistemology, philosophy-of-science
- [[Concepts/Naturalized Epistemology and the Web of Belief (Quine)]] - No first philosophy outside science that justifies or reconstructs it. Epistemology becomes a chapter of natural science (psychology, etc.). The conceptual scheme of science (including its posits and ontology) is a pragmatic tool for predicting experience; ontological questions are on a par with scientific hypotheses. | concept | epistemology, philosophy-of-science, metaphysics

**Epistemological Anarchism (Feyerabend, 2026-06-04)**:
- [[Concepts/Epistemological Anarchism (Feyerabend)]] - Science has no fixed method and needs none; every universal rule (incl. Popperian falsificationism) has been productively broken. - [[Concepts/Counterinduction (Feyerabend)]] - Advance/develop hypotheses that contradict well-confirmed theories *and* established facts; rival, refuted theories are often the only way to expose the theory-laden assumptions hidden in "the facts." Galileo advanced Copernicus by *replacing* them (relativity of motion; circular inertia), disguised as "reminiscence," plus an untrustworthy telescope and rhetoric — violating every empiricist rule. The vault's strongest theory-ladenness case. | concept | epistemology, philosophy-of-science, history-of-science
- [[Concepts/Proliferation and Tenacity (Feyerabend)]] - Theoretical pluralism: invent rival theories (proliferation) *and* hold promising ones through anomalies (tenacity); both rational. Uniformity impairs criticism and the free individual. Drawn from Mill's *On Liberty* and Bohr's practice. | concept | epistemology, philosophy-of-science
- [[Concepts/Separation of Science and State (Feyerabend)]] - Science is one tradition among many, not the one road to truth; "Reason" is not tradition-independent. So in a free society science should be separated from the state (as churches are), under democratic control, taught "as one view among many." | concept | philosophy-of-science, political-philosophy

### Schools

Movements and traditions (`type: school`, in `Schools/`).

- [[Schools/Neoplatonism]] — The Platonic tradition Plotinus inaugurated (204–70) through Porphyry, Iamblichus, and the Athenian School to the Academy's closure (529): three aims (systematic metaphysics of the One, the soul's return, defense of Hellenism against Christianity); later development as footnotes to the *Enneads*' open problems; vast influence via Augustine, Pseudo-Dionysius, Islamic falsafa, and the Renaissance. | school | metaphysics, philosophy-of-religion, history-of-philosophy
- [[Schools/Neopythagoreanism]] — Not the ancient school but its *reconstruction*: "a branch of Platonism that emphasizes the role of number" (One/Indefinite Dyad), from Speusippus and Xenocrates through Eudorus, Moderatus, Nicomachus, and Numenius, plus the Roman wonder-workers (Nigidius Figulus, Apollonius of Tyana). The image of Pythagoras the Middle Ages inherited. | school | metaphysics, religion, reception-history
- [[Schools/Pythagoreanism]] — The brotherhood founded by Pythagoras at Croton (c. 530 BC): on Burkert, metempsychosis + ritual *acusmata* at its core with a later, contested *mathematici* strand; on Zhmud (2012) a political *hetairia* (not a sect), with the acusmatici/mathematici split a late fiction and Aristoxenus' catalogue as its prosopographical backbone (Croton → Thebes/Phlius → Archytas of Tarentum). | school | metaphysics, mathematics, religion
- [[Schools/Phenomenology]] — Founded by Edmund Husserl (early 20th c.): systematic enquiry into conscious experience ("to the things themselves"). Husserl: intentionality, phenomenological reduction, transcendental ego, *Lebenswelt*. Heidegger radicalised it: from consciousness to Dasein, from epistemology to ontology, from timeless essences to historicality, added *Destruktion*. Extended by Sartre, Merleau-Ponty, Levinas, Gadamer, Derrida. | school | epistemology, ontology, philosophy-of-mind

### Contradictions

Major unresolved tensions between pages (`type: contradiction`, in `Contradictions/`). Each states both positions, names the pages/sources holding them, and is linked from both conflicting pages via `> [!warning]` callouts. Created in the 2026-06-04 schema migration.

- [[Contradictions/Sartre vs Freud - Lucidity vs the Unconscious]] - Both understand the human from subjectivity and self-formation, but split over whether the mind harbours an *unconscious* (Freud: repression, libido, the past) or is wholly *lucid* (Sartre: bad faith replaces repression; existential psychoanalysis recovers a free original choice, not a hidden past). Anchors: [[Thinkers/Jean-Paul Sartre]] / [[Concepts/Bad Faith (Mauvaise Foi) (Sartre)]] ↔ Freud (no dedicated page yet).
- [[Contradictions/Heraclitean Flux vs Parmenidean Stasis]] - Is change/becoming the fundamental nature of reality (all is flux), or is change logically impossible (stasis)? Anchors: [[Thinkers/Heraclitus]] ↔ [[Thinkers/Parmenides]].
- [[Contradictions/Pythagoras the Scientist vs the Religious Charismatic]] - Was Pythagoras the founder of mathematical natural science or a religious wonder-worker onto whom later ages projected a science he never had? A clash over historical *method* between two rigorous moderns: **Burkert (1972)** (deflation — religious charismatic; science is late/Academic) ↔ **Zhmud (2012)** (rehabilitation — both teacher and scientist; the *legend* grew, not the science). They converge on two negatives (smithy experiment fiction; tetractys late). Anchors: [[Sources/Lore and Science in Ancient Pythagoreanism - Walter Burkert (1972)]] ↔ [[Sources/Pythagoras and the Early Pythagoreans - Leonid Zhmud (2012)]] / [[Thinkers/Hippasus]].
- [[Contradictions/Atomist Materialism vs Platonic-Aristotelian Teleology]] - Is the order of the universe the result of blind, mechanical necessity (atoms/void) or intelligent design (teleology)? Anchors: [[Thinkers/Democritus]] ↔ [[Thinkers/Plato]] / [[Thinkers/Aristotle]].
- [[Contradictions/Dialectical Idealism vs. Atomist Materialism]] - Is the universe ultimately an organic, teleological self-unfolding of mind (Hegelian Idealism) or a blind, mechanical assembly of material particles (Atomist Materialism)? Anchors: [[Thinkers/Hegel]] ↔ [[Thinkers/Democritus]] / [[Thinkers/Lucretius]].
- [[Contradictions/Plato vs Aristotle on the Forms]] - Are universals separately existing, transcendent realities (Plato) or immanent forms of individual substances (Aristotle)? The foundational metaphysical split. Anchors: [[Thinkers/Plato]] ↔ [[Thinkers/Aristotle]].
- [[Contradictions/Plato vs Aristotle on the Ideal State]] - Should the ideal city maximize unity via guardian communism (Plato) or is the state essentially a self-sufficient plurality that maximal unity destroys, favoring private property with common use (Aristotle)? Anchors: [[Thinkers/Plato]] ↔ [[Thinkers/Aristotle]].
- [[Contradictions/Stoic Determinism vs Aristotelian Contingency]] - Is every event fixed by fate/*logos* (Stoics) or are some futures genuinely open (Aristotelian modality)? Anchors: [[Concepts/Dichotomy of Control (Epictetus)]] ↔ [[Concepts/Modal Copula (Aristotelian Modality)]]; the Epicurean swerve as a third option.
- [[Contradictions/Rationalism vs Empiricism on Innate Ideas]] - Does the mind hold innate ideas (Descartes) or is it a blank slate (Locke, Hume)? Anchors: [[Concepts/Clear and Distinct Perception (Descartes)]] ↔ [[Concepts/No Innate Principles or Ideas (Locke)]].
- [[Contradictions/Occasionalism vs Natural Causation]] - Is God the sole real cause (al-Ghazali) or do created things have real causal powers (Ibn Rushd)? With Hume's naturalist twist. Anchors: [[Concepts/Occasionalism vs Natural Causation (Ghazali and Ibn Rushd)]] ↔ [[Thinkers/Ibn Rushd]].
- [[Contradictions/Plotinus vs Iamblichus - Contemplation vs Theurgy]] - Can the soul return to the divine by philosophy alone (Plotinus, Porphyry — the undescended soul awakens through contemplation) or does salvation require ritual action and divine grace (Iamblichus, the Athenian School)? Prefigures Christian nature-vs-grace debates. Anchors: [[Thinkers/Plotinus]] ↔ [[Thinkers/Iamblichus]]. | contradiction | philosophy-of-religion, metaphysics
- [[Contradictions/Temporal Creation vs Eternal World]] - Did the world begin in time by God's free choice (al-Ghazali's eternal will) or is the cosmos beginningless (Avicenna/Aristotle, defended by Ibn Rushd)? Turns on whether "will" can select between indiscernibles without a reason. Aquinas & Kant agree the eternity side is undemonstrable. | contradiction | metaphysics, theology, cosmology
- [[Contradictions/The Highest Good - Pleasure vs Virtue]] - Is the highest good pleasure/ataraxia (Epicurus) or virtue alone (Stoics), with Plato's *Philebus* as a third position? Anchors: [[Thinkers/Epicurus]] ↔ [[Thinkers/Seneca]].
- [[Contradictions/Free Will - Spinozan Necessity vs Kantian Autonomy]] - Is the will an illusion of ignorance (Spinoza's necessitarianism) or autonomous self-legislation (Kant)? Anchors: [[Concepts/Deus sive Natura - Substance Monism (Spinoza)]] ↔ [[Concepts/Freedom as the Key to Autonomy (Kant)]].
- [[Contradictions/Hegelian Monism vs. Kantian Dualism]] - Does human reason possess the power to achieve absolute knowledge of ultimate reality (Hegelian Monism) or is it forever limited to sensory appearances (Kantian Dualism)? Anchors: [[Thinkers/Hegel]] ↔ [[Thinkers/Kant]].
- [[Contradictions/Popper vs Kuhn - Falsification vs Paradigms]] - The rationalism↔anarchism spectrum on method and progress: permanent rational **falsification** toward truth (Popper) ↔ paradigm-bound **normal science** + goal-less **revolutions** (Kuhn) ↔ Lakatos's research-programmes mediation ↔ **no method at all, "anything goes"** (Feyerabend) + Hacking's experimental pivot (entity realism via intervention/use grounds realism even when representation is troubled). The central tension of the Philosophy of Science leg. Anchors: [[Thinkers/Karl Popper]] ↔ [[Thinkers/Thomas Kuhn]] ↔ [[Thinkers/Paul Feyerabend]] ↔ [[Thinkers/Ian Hacking]].
- [[Contradictions/Hofstadter vs Searle - Strange Loops vs Chinese Room]] - Can formal symbol manipulation produce genuine meaning and consciousness? **Hofstadter**: yes — via isomorphism and strange loops (sufficient complexity + self-reference → semantics emerges). **Searle**: no — syntax is never sufficient for semantics; biological causation is required. The vault's central AI & Cognitive Science contradiction. Anchors: [[Thinkers/Douglas Hofstadter]] ↔ [[Thinkers/John Searle]], with [[Thinkers/Alan Turing]]'s Imitation Game at the origin.
- [[Contradictions/Wiener vs Shannon - Information and Meaning]] - Two founders share the same entropy mathematics (H = −∑ p log p) but reach opposite conclusions: **Shannon** brackets semantics — "the semantic aspects of communication are irrelevant to the engineering problem"; **Wiener** insists information is fundamentally social and semantic — "society can only be understood through a study of the messages." Secondary clash: Shannon treats information as a measurable engineering resource; Wiener argues information is not a commodity (decays, anti-additive, cannot be owned). Anchors: [[Thinkers/Norbert Wiener]] ↔ [[Thinkers/Claude Shannon]].
- [[Contradictions/Pearl vs. Statistics Orthodoxy - Causation Purged and Recovered]] - The deepest methodological rift in the AI & Cognitive Science leg: **Galton, Pearson, and Fisher** deliberately excluded causal language from statistics (correlation-only science; RCTs as the only legitimate causal method). **Pearl**: this was a century-long mistake that blocked science and crippled AI at rung 1. Causal Revolution (1990s–2010s) provides the formal tools (do-calculus, DAGs, SCMs) to answer causal and counterfactual questions from observational data. Modern deep learning repeats the statistics orthodoxy's error at scale. Relation to Hume's induction problem, Popper's conjecturalism, and Turing's imitation game. Anchors: [[Thinkers/Judea Pearl]] ↔ the Galton/Pearson/Fisher tradition.
- [[Contradictions/Dennett vs Nagel - Multiple Drafts vs Subjective Experience]] - Is consciousness irreducibly subjective? **Nagel** (1974): there is something it is like to be a bat; the phenomenal "what it's like" escapes any third-person objective account; physicalism cannot fully explain subjective experience. **Dennett** (1991): Nagel's pessimism is overstated; heterophenomenology provides a neutral third-person method; the Multiple Drafts Model explains consciousness without phenomenal residue; the intuition of inexplicability is generated by the Cartesian Theater illusion. The vault's phenomenology tension. Anchors: [[Thinkers/Daniel Dennett]] ↔ Thomas Nagel (not yet in vault).
- [[Contradictions/Hobbesian Absolutism vs. Lockean Constitutionalism]] - Is sovereign political authority absolute, undivided, and irrevocable (Hobbes), or is it a limited, divisible fiduciary trust subject to a right of revolution (Locke)? Anchors: [[Thinkers/Thomas Hobbes]] ↔ [[Thinkers/Locke]].
- [[Contradictions/Utilitarian Consequentialism vs. Kantian Deontology]] - Is the morality of an action determined solely by its consequences (utilitarianism) or by its conformity to duty and the categorical imperative (deontology)? Anchors: [[Thinkers/John Stuart Mill]] ↔ [[Thinkers/Kant]].
- [[Contradictions/Dialectical Idealism vs. Historical Materialism]] - Is human history driven by the developmental unfolding of ideas and consciousness (Hegel) or by the concrete development of material, economic forces of production (Marx)? Anchors: [[Thinkers/Hegel]] ↔ [[Thinkers/Karl Marx]].
- [[Contradictions/Subjective Idealism vs. Physical Realism]] - Is reality fundamentally mind-dependent ideas (Berkeley's subjective idealism) or mind-independent material substances (Cartesian/Lockean physical realism)? Anchors: [[Thinkers/George Berkeley]] ↔ [[Thinkers/Descartes]] / [[Thinkers/Locke]].
- [[Contradictions/Rousseau vs. Hobbes on the State of Nature]] - Is the state of nature a state of peace, pity, and freedom corrupted by society (Rousseau) or a war of all against all (Hobbes)? Anchors: [[Thinkers/Jean-Jacques Rousseau]] ↔ [[Thinkers/Thomas Hobbes]].
- [[Contradictions/Schopenhauer's Pessimism vs. Leibnizian Optimism]] - Is the universe the worst of all possible worlds driven by an insatiable, suffering Will (Schopenhauer) or the best of all possible worlds structured by sufficient reason (Leibniz)? Anchors: [[Thinkers/Arthur Schopenhauer]] ↔ [[Thinkers/Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz]].
- [[Contradictions/Early vs Later Wittgenstein]] - A philosopher refuting himself: does language have one hidden **logical form** with meaning as picturing/reference (*Tractatus*, 1922), or is it an open plurality of **language-games** with meaning as **use** and no shared essence (*Philosophical Investigations*, 1953)? Feeds two opposed movements — Vienna Circle/[[Thinkers/A.J. Ayer|Ayer]] (early) vs. ordinary-language philosophy/Austin/[[Thinkers/John Searle|Searle]] (late). Anchors: [[Concepts/Picture Theory of Language (Wittgenstein)]] ↔ [[Concepts/Meaning as Use (Wittgenstein)]] / [[Concepts/Language-Games (Wittgenstein)]].
