## Situation Assessment & Career Positioning

**Current Role (Account Manager)** vs **Target (Senior Account Executive)**:

AM services existing accounts, processes renewals/endorsements, responds to requests, supports AE, internal-facing, salary only.

Sr AE owns/grows book of business, drives retention + cross-sell revenue, proactively identifies gaps/solutions, primary client relationship owner, external-facing (strategy + new business), salary + commission/bonus on book revenue.

**The AM-to-AE Gap**: Common bottleneck. Not lack of skill, but lack of *visibility* into revenue impact. Fix by framing everything as "your book" with metrics (total premium under management, # accounts, retention rate, avg account size, largest account, proactive growth examples).

**Self-Assessment (Score 1-5 on competencies)**: New business development, book ownership mindset, C-suite relationship skills, market knowledge (carrier appetites, cycles, pricing), risk analysis & consulting (proactive exposures/coverage), negotiation skills (carriers/vendors), professional credentials (P&C + ARM + CPCU almost — strong here), industry specialization (deep in 1-2 verticals e.g. agribusiness/food processing — strong).

## The Internal Path: Making the Case at Alliant

- Build one-pager: current "book" metrics (frame as owned), growth examples (e.g. identified recall exposure, added coverage +$22K premium; consistent proactive enhancements), CPCU progress, value to Alliant (retention, cross-sell, ag expertise, local knowledge).
- Steps: Self-assessment → metrics framing → internal networking (managers, current AEs) → present one-pager + ask "What would it take for me to move into AE role in 6-12 months?" → listen/gather intel → set decision point (if vague after ~8 weeks, shift 80% effort external while performing strongly).
- Key: Never criticize Alliant ("great firm, 20-year run; now want to own relationships at executive level"). Never resign before signed offer.

## Resume & Personal Brand Overhaul

- Resume: Shift from task list (AM) to impact/ownership language (AE): "Managed $[X]M premium book of [N] commercial accounts... Achieved X% retention... Proactively generated $Y in additional premium via coverage gap identification...".
- Personal brand: LinkedIn, industry presence, thought leadership in agribusiness insurance, association involvement. Consistent messaging around "deep expertise + proven book growth + client strategy ownership".

## Networking & Relationship Strategy

- Internal: Cultivate sponsors/advocates at Alliant.
- External: Associations, events, warm referrals from current clients/carriers (ethically). Map relationships that could follow (non-solicit awareness).
- Scripts for "what business can you bring?" — honest about portable relationships + proven ability to generate new via network/expertise/associations.

---

## Job Search Execution Plan + Interview Prep & Negotiation

- Execution: Timeline of research, applications, outreach. Track in pipeline.
- Interviews: Common questions with strong answers:
  - "Tell me about your book": Specific numbers + proactive growth stories.
  - "How have you grown revenue?": Concrete examples (recall coverage +$22k, consistent enhancements).
  - "Why leaving Alliant?": Positive + desire for ownership/strategy level.
  - "What business can you bring?": Ethical, strategic (portable + generation ability).
  - "Where in 5 years?": Building significant book, leading practice (e.g. agribusiness), mentoring.
- Negotiation: Know comp benchmarks (salary + commission/bonus on book). Targets based on experience/book size.

## 12-Month Master Timeline + Risk Mitigation
