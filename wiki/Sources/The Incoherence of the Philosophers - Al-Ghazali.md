# The Incoherence of the Philosophers - Al-Ghazali

> **Tahafut al-Falasifa** (c. 1095 CE), by Abu Hamid Muhammad al-Ghazali (1058–1111). The primary text behind the vault's already-ingested rebuttal, Averroes' *Tahafut al-Tahafut* ([[Sources/Incoherence of the Incoherence - Ibn Rushd]]). A sustained logical refutation of twenty doctrines of the Islamic Aristotelians (chiefly **Avicenna**, with al-Farabi) — seventeen condemned as heretical innovation, three (world's eternity, God's ignorance of particulars, denial of bodily resurrection) as outright unbelief (*kufr*). Translated by Michael E. Marmura (BYU Press, 2nd ed. 2000).

## Argument Spine

Al-Ghazali's aim is **destructive, not constructive**: not to defend a theological system but to show that the *falasifa* have not *demonstrated* their metaphysical claims to the standard of proof set in their own logic. The unifying charge: the philosophers' God, who produces the world by the necessity of His essence (as the sun produces light), is denuded of will, power, life, and knowledge — He is not the God of the Qur'an. At issue is never God's *existence* (the philosophers affirm it) but the *nature of the godhead* — whether God acts by voluntary choice or by natural necessity. The decisive epistemic weapon is a **criterion of demonstrability**: scriptural assertions must be read literally unless their literal sense is shown — by strict demonstration, not mere plausibility — to be impossible. Since the philosophers fail that bar, their innovations carry no authority to override religion.

## Whole-Work Assessment

This ingest supplies the **primary half of a debate the vault previously held only from one side.** Averroes' *Tahafut al-Tahafut* and three concept pages had reconstructed al-Ghazali's positions through the rebuttal; the vault had no al-Ghazali thinker page and no source page for the original. That gap is now closed, and the two *Tahafut*s can be read against each other point by point.

Three corrections/deepenings to what the vault recorded second-hand. (1) The famous causality discussion is **not** a single occasionalist manifesto: al-Ghazali advances *two* incompatible theories — strict occasionalism and a "modified" theory granting things real dispositions — the second "for the sake of argument," to make miracles possible even on Aristotelian premises. (2) His positive cosmology, the **eternal will that specifies a temporal beginning**, is a substantive constructive doctrine despite the book's official "refute-only" posture — and its hinge is a definition of will as "the attribute that differentiates a thing from its similar," which is exactly what Averroes attacks as equivocation. (3) The book is governed by a single **criterion of demonstrability** (literal scripture stands unless its impossibility is *demonstrated*), which unifies the otherwise scattered twenty discussions and explains why al-Ghazali can grant the immaterial soul in Disc. 20 while refusing to prove it in Disc. 18.

New pages: [[Thinkers/Al-Ghazali]] (the missing thinker), and four concepts — [[Concepts/Criterion of Demonstrability (Ghazali)]], [[Concepts/Eternal Will and Temporal Creation (Ghazali)]], [[Concepts/God's Knowledge of Particulars (Ghazali)]], [[Concepts/Bodily Resurrection and the Immaterial Soul (Ghazali)]] — plus the contradiction [[Contradictions/Temporal Creation vs Eternal World]]. Updated: the two joint concept pages and the Occasionalism contradiction (now citing the primary text), and reciprocal links from [[Thinkers/Ibn Rushd]] / [[Sources/Incoherence of the Incoherence - Ibn Rushd]]. The work extends three vault threads at once — the Aristotle→Islamic→scholastic transmission ([[Thinkers/Thomas Aquinas]] inherits both the eternity-is-not-demonstrable move and the anti-occasionalist commitment to real natures), the causation thread that runs forward to [[Thinkers/Hume]]'s constant conjunction, and the faith-and-reason axis. Its natural next neighbor in `raw/` is Maimonides' *Guide for the Perplexed* (the parallel Jewish negotiation), not yet ingested.

The Argument Spine written in Step 1 held up against the text — the only refinement is that the "destructive only" framing is real but selectively honored (the eternal-will and resurrection sections do constructive work).
