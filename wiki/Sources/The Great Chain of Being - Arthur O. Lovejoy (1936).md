# The Great Chain of Being - Arthur O. Lovejoy (1936)

> **The Great Chain of Being: A Study of the History of an Idea** (1936; William James Lectures at Harvard). By Arthur O. Lovejoy (1873–1962), with new introduction by Peter J. Stanlis (2009/2017 ed.). The definitive modern history of the "Chain of Being" (or *scala naturae*) as the dominant presupposition of Western cosmology, theology, and reflective poetry from Plato through the late 18th century — and of its internal tensions, migrations across domains, temporalizing, and eventual breakdown. Lovejoy's method: tracking "unit-ideas" (recurrent conceptual components and their combinations) rather than systems or -isms; exposing implicit "endemic assumptions" and "dialectical motives" that shape thought far beyond explicit doctrine.

## Nature of the source (read this first)

This is the canonical primary source for the **History of Ideas** leg of the vault. It is not a survey of thinkers or a single philosophical system but a history of a *complex of ideas* — the fusion of three principles into the conception of the universe as a "Great Chain of Being." Lovejoy shows how this complex functioned as an often-unexamined presupposition ("the most widely familiar conception of the general scheme of things") that predetermined ideas in metaphysics, theology, science, ethics (optimism/problem of evil), biology, and poetry (Pope, etc.). The book ends with a philosophical "moral": the experiment in complete rational intelligibility of the cosmos (no arbitrariness) was a grandiose failure whose consequences became explicit over centuries. It supplies the root worldview presupposed (and later reacted against) by the vault's completed Philosophy of Science cluster (Popper, Kuhn, etc.) and classical/medieval sources (Plato through Aquinas, Plotinus, Augustine). Crosses into AI/CogSci leg via static hierarchy vs. later tangled/emergent/process models (e.g. [[Concepts/Strange Loops and Tangled Hierarchies (Hofstadter)]]).

## The three principles (the "unit-idea" complex)

Lovejoy isolates three principles whose combination generates the Chain:

- **Principle of Plenitude**: From Plato's *Timaeus* (and implicit in the Idea of the Good in *Republic*): the "good" (self-sufficient, non-envious) Source must produce the maximum diversity; "all things" possible must exist; no genuine potentiality of being can remain unfulfilled. The universe is (or ought to be) a *plenum formarum* — exhaustively full of every conceivable kind. This is not free choice but dialectical/ontological necessity (the Absolute would not be fully "good" or complete without it). Latent cosmical determinism.

- **Principle of Continuity**: From Aristotle (with direct deduction from plenitude): nature makes no leaps; between any two natural species there must be (and therefore is) an intermediate type; qualitative differences form continuous series with infinitesimal gradations; "the transition from plants to animals is continuous" (zoophytes as favorite example). No gaps in the series of forms.

- **Principle of Unilinear Gradation (scala naturae)**: All beings arranged in a single hierarchical order of excellence/perfection from the "meagerest kind of existents, which barely escape non-existence" up to the *ens perfectissimum* (or highest creature, with infinite disparity to the Absolute). Each link differs from neighbors by the "least possible" degree; higher grades possess all powers of lower + an additional differentiating one (e.g., Aristotle's hierarchy of soul powers: nutritive → sensitive → rational). Often fused with the prior two into the "Great Chain of Being" metaphor (chain, series of mirrors, etc.).

These are not always explicit; they operate as "endemic assumptions" shaping hypotheses in science and theology for ~2000 years.

## Historical sweep (per Lovejoy's chapters)

- **Greek genesis (Ch. II)**: Plato (*Timaeus* especially) supplies plenitude + the generative "good"; Aristotle supplies continuity + graded *scala* (powers of soul, zoological/psychological hierarchies) plus the recognition of classification limits. Neoplatonism (Plotinus, Macrobius, Pseudo-Dionysius) first fully fuses them into a coherent emanative scheme: the One overflows without loss or envy into Intellect (Nous/Forms), Soul, and the sensible manifold down to matter. "Non permisit manere Deum in seipso sine germine."

- **Medieval (Ch. III)**: Enters Christian theology via Augustine ("non essent omnia, si essent aequalia") and especially Pseudo-Dionysius (fecund "love"/goodness as creative energy). Aquinas synthesizes; the Chain justifies diversity (why not all equal?) and underpins natural law/beatific vision as apex for man. Internal conflicts: otherworldly self-sufficient Absolute vs. this-worldly generative God; static completeness vs. temporal process.

- **Early modern / new cosmography (Ch. IV)**: Copernican/Galilean/Newtonian shifts challenge but often retain the presuppositions (Chain theorems as "essential presuppositions in the framing of scientific hypotheses"). Leibniz (Ch. V) marries plenitude to sufficient reason (every detail has ultimate reason; this is the best of all possible worlds because it is the fullest compossible). Spinoza: absolute determinism from the nature of substance.

- **18th century (Chs. VI–VIII)**: Dominant popular expression in Pope's *Essay on Man* ("Vast chain of being! which from God began... / All that rises, rise in due degree"; "whatever is, is right"). Optimism as theodicy (partial evil = universal good; problem of evil solved by plenitude). Biology: pre-Darwinian efforts (Bonnet, Robinet, Monboddo, Erasmus Darwin) to fill gaps with missing links or "missing" species; Chain begins to temporalize.

- **Temporalizing and Romanticism (Chs. IX–X)**: The static, once-for-all complete Chain becomes a progressive Becoming (evolution avant la lettre). 18c biology + geology stretch time far beyond biblical; the "way down" (emanation) inverts toward ascent from below. Schelling/Oken: God not eternally complete but "coming to be" through nature/history; "the philosophy of Nature is the science of the eternal transformation of God into the world." Romantic plenitude: infinite striving, process, sensibility over abstract rational order. Primitivism as cultural extension.

- **Outcome and moral (Ch. XI)**: The long experiment presupposing complete rational intelligibility (sufficient reason for the world's magnitude, pattern, every link, no arbitrariness) ends in explicit failure. A temporal world of change cannot be deduced from or reconciled with a system of eternal necessary truths (arbitrary facts of beginning, span, why *this* selection from the infinite possibles). Plenitude + continuity generate infinities and contradictions (qualitative continuum is "a contradiction in terms"; maximal diversity requires "leaps"). Complete rationality (excluding arbitrariness) "becomes itself a kind of irrationality." World is contingent; "Will is prior to Intellect." Yet the idea had "curiously happy consequences" (stimulated classification, biology, the very science that helped undo its static form). The two Gods of the Platonic tradition cannot both be held; otherworldly self-sufficiency vs. this-worldly generativeness are incompatible.

## Key Claims / Positions

- The Chain was "probably the most widely familiar conception of the general scheme of things" until ~a century before 1936; it "necessarily predetermined current ideas on many other matters."
- Western philosophic/religious thought has been "profoundly at variance with itself" because of the latent opposition between the self-sufficient Absolute and the generative "good" that must overflow into all possible kinds.
- 18c optimism (Pope et al.) is the popular ethical application of plenitude + continuity; its shallowness on evil and human agency exposed by its own logic and by later temporalizing.
- The "static and constant" worldview of the Chain, based on unbounded confidence in discursive reason, proved unsatisfactory; the hypothesis of absolute rationality of the cosmos is "unbelievable."
- Methodological: history of ideas best done by isolating unit-ideas and their combinations/mutations across provinces (philosophy, theology, science, poetry), not by schools or individual systems. Many "great systems" are "more or less unstable compounds" of such components.

## Contradictions / Open Questions

- Internal to the idea: static completeness vs. generative fecundity; otherworldly God vs. this-worldly Nature-as-God; sufficient reason for *every* detail vs. the brute fact of temporality and the world's specific (arbitrary) character.
- With later thought: the breakdown feeds directly into 19c evolutionism (Darwin as temporalized Chain without the old rationalist grounding) and 20c philosophy of science (post-static-cosmos; see [[Thinkers/Karl Popper]], [[Thinkers/Thomas Kuhn]], [[Concepts/Scientific Revolutions and Paradigm Shifts (Kuhn)]]). No dedicated contradiction page yet (minor tensions flagged via cross-links; major recurring would warrant one).
- With AI/CogSci: static unilinear hierarchy vs. distributed/tangled/emergent models (Minsky society of agents, Hofstadter strange loops/tangled hierarchies, Dennett multiple drafts, Pearl causality). The Chain's "no leaps" and fixed gradation stand in tension with emergence, phase transitions, and non-unilinear causation. Further: the technium's exotropic becoming (Kelly) as the open-ended evolutionary/process successor that completes the temporalization the Chain underwent in the 18c–19c and the mental shift of the Scientific Revolution.
- With Kelly technium/exotropy: the static, finished, plenitudinous "diagram" (all possibles realized in graded hierarchy, no gaps, sufficient reason for every link) vs. the technium as seventh kingdom with inherent "wants" (self-perpetuation, increasing choices, exotropy/complexification, convergence on forms) that are life-like, choice-expanding, and open-ended. The Chain is the pre-modern presupposition overthrown by the technium-driven acceleration of becoming. See [[Sources/What Technology Wants - Kevin Kelly (2010)]], [[Concepts/Technium (Kelly)]], [[Concepts/Exotropy (Kelly)]].
