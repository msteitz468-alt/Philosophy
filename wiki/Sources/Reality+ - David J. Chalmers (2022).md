# Reality+ — David J. Chalmers (2022)
> Chalmers's "technophilosophy" treatise arguing that virtual reality is genuine reality — and using that thesis to dissolve Descartes's problem of the external world.

## Argument Spine
The central thesis is **virtual reality is genuine reality**, broken into three planks: (1) virtual worlds are not illusions or fictions — virtual objects are real (digital objects made of bits); (2) life in virtual worlds can be as good in principle as nonvirtual life; (3) we could be living in a virtual world now — the simulation hypothesis cannot be ruled out. Chalmers uses statistical simulation reasoning to sharpen Cartesian skepticism (we can't know we're not simulated), then dissolves it: since simulated worlds are genuine realities whose objects really exist (simulation realism / the it-from-bit hypothesis), even if we are in a simulation, most of our ordinary beliefs remain true. A fourth plank (Parts 5–6): virtual and augmented minds are genuine minds, and virtual lives carry genuine value. The method throughout is "technophilosophy": using technology (VR, AI, AR, deepfakes, simulation) to answer traditional philosophical questions and vice versa.

## Whole-Work Assessment

*Reality+* is the philosophical keystone of the vault's simulation cluster: it takes the hypothesis Virk popularized and Bostrom formalized and turns it from a skeptical threat into a metaphysical option, via the chain simulation hypothesis ≡ it-from-bit creation → structuralism about physics → simulation realism → the collapse of the Cartesian master argument (premise 2 denied, not premise 1). It supplies the arguments earlier ingests gestured at: fading qualia for Kurzweil's uploading optimism, a consciousness-based moral-status criterion for Bostrom's mind-crime worries, and the co-author's own update to the Clark–Chalmers extended mind. It also crystallizes the vault's deepest live contradiction — Chalmers's qualia realism against the Dennett/Clark deflationist bloc — and sets up the sharpest methodological contrast in the vault: Chalmers answering Descartes on Cartesian terms in the same season Heidegger's *Being and Time* was ingested calling the whole demand a scandal. The Argument Spine from Step 1 held up, with one correction: the anti-skeptical engine is structuralism, not semantics or it-from-bit as such ("a step ladder that we can kick away"), and the ch. 5 probability claim is more modest than the introduction suggests (≥25%, not "probably").
