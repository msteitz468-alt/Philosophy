# Aristotle - Metaphysics

> The founding treatise of Western metaphysics (W. D. Ross translation). Fourteen books, assembled by later editors (the "books after the *Physics*"), inquiring into **being qua being** — what can be said of anything at all simply insofar as it is. Develops the doctrine of the four causes, the analysis of substance (*ousia*) into form and matter (hylomorphism), the distinction of potentiality and actuality, the defense of the principle of non-contradiction, and the argument to a divine unmoved mover. A lifelong reckoning with — and partial rejection of — Plato's separate Forms.

## Argument Spine

There is a science of being qua being — first philosophy — distinct from the special sciences that carve off some genus of being. Because "being is said in many ways" but all with reference to one central case (*pros hen*), the science of being is really the science of **substance**: what it is to be a *this*. Substance turns out to be primarily **form** (the actuality that makes a composite the kind of thing it is), analyzed through the correlative pairs matter/form and potentiality/actuality. Since actuality is prior to potentiality and motion must be eternal, there must be a first **unmoved mover** — pure actuality, thought thinking itself, the divine — as the final cause of all motion. Throughout, Aristotle argues against Plato's separately existing Forms and against the Pythagorean/Platonic reduction of substance to number.

## Whole-Work Assessment

The *Metaphysics* is the keystone the vault's classical cluster had been missing: it converts the scattered Aristotle material (logic, ethics, rhetoric) into a unified metaphysical system and supplies the primary text for tensions previously held only on the Plato/secondary side. Its single most consequential contribution is the **substance → form → actuality → unmoved mover** chain, which is the direct ancestor of the cosmological argument and act/potency metaphysics in [[Thinkers/Thomas Aquinas]] and [[Thinkers/Ibn Rushd]] (and the foil for [[Thinkers/Spinoza]]'s one-substance monism and [[Thinkers/Leibniz]]'s monads). It also gives the **anti-Forms polemic** its canonical statement, decisively deepening [[Contradictions/Plato vs Aristotle on the Forms]], and grounds the essence/accident distinction underlying [[Concepts/Modal Copula (Aristotelian Modality)]]. The *pros hen* doctrine of being and the elenctic defense of non-contradiction are load-bearing for the whole later tradition (analogy of being; transcendental arguments). A cross-book thread visible only at the whole-work level: the priority of **actuality** announced in Book IX is what makes the argument of Book XII work — the eternal mover must be pure *energeia* — so Theta and Lambda must be read together. Note the major discontinuity flagged on [[Concepts/The Unmoved Mover (Aristotle)]]: Aristotle's god neither creates nor knows particulars, so the monotheist inheritance is a transformation, not a smooth descent.

The Argument Spine written in Step 1 held up against the text — the introduction's framing (reconciling Platonic permanence with observed change via being-qua-being → substance → form → actuality → divine mover) is borne out, with the qualification that the "reconciliation of Plato" framing understates how *adversarial* Books I and XIII–XIV are toward the Forms.
