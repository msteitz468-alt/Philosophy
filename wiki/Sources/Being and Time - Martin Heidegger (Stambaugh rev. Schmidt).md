# Being and Time — Martin Heidegger (1927; Stambaugh trans., rev. Dennis J. Schmidt)

> The vault's first primary Heidegger text: the unfinished masterwork that re-poses the forgotten question of the meaning of Being through an existential analytic of Dasein, culminating in temporality as the horizon of any understanding of Being.

## Argument Spine

The question of the meaning of Being (*Sein*) has been forgotten by the Western tradition, which treats "being" as the emptiest and most self-evident of concepts. Heidegger re-poses it by interrogating the one being for whom Being is an issue — Dasein — via a phenomenological-hermeneutical "existential analytic." Division One shows Dasein's fundamental constitution to be being-in-the-world, whose structural whole is care (*Sorge*). Division Two shows that the ontological meaning of care is ecstatic temporality (*Zeitlichkeit*): Dasein becomes a whole authentically in anticipatory resoluteness toward death, attested by conscience, and its temporality grounds everydayness, historicity, and the vulgar (clock) concept of time. The published book breaks off before Part One Division Three ("Time and Being") and all of Part Two (the destruction of the history of ontology: Kant, Descartes, Aristotle) — the horizon of Being itself is announced but never delivered.

## Translation Notes (Schmidt Foreword / Stambaugh Preface)

Schmidt reverses Stambaugh's hyphenated "Da-sein" (Heidegger kept "Dasein" in the Gesamtausgabe; the *Beiträge* distinguishes Da-sein from Dasein; English scholarship never adopted the hyphen). "being" is lowercased to avoid a "Super Thing." Signature Stambaugh choices: *Befindlichkeit* = "attunement"; *Verfallen* = "entanglement"/falling prey; *Bewandtnis* = "relevance"; *Zuhandenheit/Vorhandenheit* = "handiness"/"objective presence"; *Sorge/Besorgen/Fürsorge* = care / taking care / concern; *Wiederholung* = "retrieval"; *Augenblick* = "moment"; *Angst* untranslated; *Vorlaufen* = "anticipation — perhaps too weak." The edition prints Heidegger's later marginalia from his own copy (*Hüttenexemplar*, 157 remarks) as endmatter — *Beiträge*-era vocabulary (*Seyn*, *Ereignis*, resonance/play/leap) annotating the 1927 text.

## Whole-Work Assessment

The vault's first primary Heidegger text converts the Inwood-derived Heidegger cluster from reported doctrine into quoted argument: every major concept page now carries the actual definitions (care §41, temporality §65, death §§50–52, resoluteness §60, world-time §80) with section numbers. The primary text sharpened three things the survey blurred: (1) the *derivation architecture* — understanding→interpretation→statement, temporality→world-time→now-time, temporality→historicity — is the book's real skeleton; (2) the finitude of primordial time ("only because primordial time is finite can 'derivative' time temporalize itself as infinite") is the single thesis on which death, conscience, history, and the Aristotle/Hegel critiques all hang; (3) the unfinishedness is internal and admitted — §83 ends in questions, and the printed marginalia show Heidegger himself re-reading the torso from the far side of the Kehre. Contradictions opened or deepened: truth/reality dependence on Dasein vs. the vault's scientific realism (Hacking, Popper); the normative vacuum of resoluteness (now confirmed as structural: the call "says nothing"); Heidegger's denial of eternal truths vs. every Platonist and theological position in the vault. The Argument Spine of Step 1 held up, with one correction of emphasis: Division Two is less an "application" than a re-derivation — every Division One structure is explicitly taken back into temporality (§§67–71).
