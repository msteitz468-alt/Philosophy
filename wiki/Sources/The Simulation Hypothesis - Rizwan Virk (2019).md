# The Simulation Hypothesis — Rizwan Virk (2019)

> An MIT-trained video game designer argues that the convergence of video game technology (toward a "Simulation Point"), quantum physics anomalies, and Eastern/Western mystical traditions makes it plausible that our reality is a computed, video-game-like simulation.

## Argument Spine

Virk's central argument runs in three movements. (I) **Computer science**: video game technology is progressing through eleven identifiable stages (from text adventures to fully immersive, AI-populated, mind-interfaced simulations); reaching Stage 11 — the "Simulation Point" where a simulated world is indistinguishable from reality — is an engineering extrapolation, not fantasy, and via [[Thinkers/Nick Bostrom]]'s simulation argument, any civilization that reaches it will likely produce many ancestor simulations, making it statistically likely we are inside one. (II) **Physics**: the quantum weirdness of our world (indeterminacy, observer effects, delayed choice, quantized space-time, speed-of-light limits) is exactly what one would expect of an optimized simulation using conditional rendering — render only what is observed. (III) **Mysticism**: the world's religious traditions (maya, dream yoga, karma, reincarnation, afterlife records) read naturally as descriptions of a multiplayer video game with quests, respawns, and persistent player state — information outside the rendered world. Part IV weighs skeptical and supporting evidence of computation and distinguishes the NPC (simulated consciousness) vs RPG (player-based consciousness) versions of the hypothesis.

## Whole-Work Assessment

Virk's book gives the vault its first sustained treatment of the simulation hypothesis, and its chief structural contribution is the **PC-vs-NPC fork**: the same statistical argument (Bostrom's trilemma, now filed at [[Arguments/Simulation Argument (Bostrom)]]) yields either a deflationary computational monism (NPC version) or a modern dualism with consciousness outside the rendered world (Virk's RPG version). This fork wires the hypothesis directly into the vault's existing consciousness contradictions (Searle vs Hofstadter/Dennett) rather than standing apart from them. The physics chapters extend the Berkeley–Wheeler observer-dependence lineage into computational vocabulary (conditional rendering), and the mysticism chapters give the vault its first substantive maya/karma/dream-yoga material — though built on a persistent-soul model that quietly contradicts the Buddhist anattā doctrine it cites. The Argument Spine written at Step 1 held up against the sectional reads; the one correction worth recording is that Virk's popular rendering of Bostrom's probability ("more than 1!") garbles the math, flagged on the argument page. As a popularizer-synthesist source, its contested physics readings (consciousness-causes-collapse, retrocausality) are flagged as such on the concept pages.
