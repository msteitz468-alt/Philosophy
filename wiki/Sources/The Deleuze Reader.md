# The Deleuze Reader

> Anthology edited by Constantin V. Boundas (1993): 29 excerpts from Deleuze's major works, organized to show the internal logic of a philosophy of difference, becoming, and productive desire.

## Argument Spine

Deleuze's project is to replace Being with **difference** and linear time with **difference-making repetition**, in order to articulate a theory of pure becoming adequate to transformation rather than identity. This requires overturning Platonism (which grounds ethics and politics in imitation of stable Forms), dismantling representationalism, and reconceiving desire as productive (not as lack). The anthology moves from ontological groundwork (difference, multiplicity, event) through the theory of productive desire and schizoanalysis, into aesthetics (minor literature, cinema, painting, music), and finally into nomadic politics — each domain showing the same anti-identitarian logic at work.

## Whole-Work Assessment

*The Deleuze Reader* is the most philosophically dense anthology the vault has received. It contributes a coherent alternative ontology (difference/becoming/event) that stands in systematic tension with the vault's existing rationalist and empiricist clusters. Nietzsche, Spinoza, and Plato are the three thinkers most substantially transformed by this ingest: Nietzsche's eternal return is reread as selective ontology, Spinoza's *Ethics* becomes a manual for ethics-without-morality, and Platonism becomes the primary target of Deleuze's whole philosophical project. The vault now has its first major poststructuralist source, opening the possibility of a Contradictions page on Deleuze vs. analytic philosophy (Ayer, Russell, Wittgenstein) — the anti-representationalist challenge lands differently across these traditions. The political concepts (molar/molecular, schizoanalysis, war machine) create genuine cross-domain tensions with Olson and Buchanan-Tullock's rational-choice political economy.
