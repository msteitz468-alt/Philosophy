# Lore and Science in Ancient Pythagoreanism — Walter Burkert (1972)
> The definitive source-critical study (German *Weisheit und Wissenschaft*, 1962) that dismantles the picture of Pythagoras-the-scientist and reconstructs him instead as a religious charismatic, arguing that "Pythagorean" number-science is largely a retrojection from Plato's Academy.

## Argument Spine
The traditional image of Pythagoras as founder of mathematics, astronomy, and quantitative natural science is a historical illusion produced by the tradition's tendency to grow richer the further it stands from the events (Zeller's law: *recentiores deteriores*). Reading the **earliest** evidence strictly, Pythagoras emerges as a religious reformer and wonder-worker — a teacher of metempsychosis and ritual taboo (the **acusmata**), comparable to a "shaman" — with no demonstrable scientific or mathematical doctrine. The cosmology and number-metaphysics later called "Pythagorean" derive from two distinct, later strata: (1) **Philolaus** (late 5th c.), who recast Pythagorean lore in the idiom of Ionian *physiologia*, and (2) **Plato and the early Academy** (Speusippus, Xenocrates, Heraclides), who projected their own ideal-number metaphysics back onto Pythagoras. The genuine internal division within the early school — between **acusmatici** (followers of ritual sayings) and **mathematici** (those who pursued *mathēmata*/learning) — is the historical seed later mistaken for "Pythagoras the mathematician." Burkert thus inverts the developmental story (Burnet/Cornford: religion → science): the science was read *back into* the religion.

II (Earliest Tradition)** — metempsychosis fixed by Xenophanes/Aristotle/Dicaearchus; *acusmata* are ritual passwords (Aristotle the only early user of the term); the *acusmatici / mathematici* split, with Iamblichus caught reversing his source to put science first. → created Concepts Metempsychosis, Acusmata; School Pythagoreanism; the "shaman not scientist" verdict.

- **Ch. IV (Astronomy)** — central-fire system is *not* heliocentric (fire, not Sun, central; counter-earth pads the number to 10); fifth-century Ionian parallels throughout; celestial "harmony" (7 planets) doesn't even fit the 10-body cosmos. → created [[Concepts/Harmony of the Spheres]].

V (Musical Theory)** — the smithy/hammer "experiment" is physically false (pitch ∝ neither hammer-weight nor string-tension) → later fiction rationalizing the myth of Pythagoras' magical music; the genuine numerical work (bronze discs) belongs to Hippasus. → fed [[Concepts/Number as the Essence of Reality (Pythagoras)]], Pythagoras caveat.

VI (Number Theory & Greek Mathematics)** — "did the Pythagoreans found Greek mathematics?" → no; Eva Sachs' demolition of "Pythagoras the mathematician"; incommensurability re-dated to the late 5th c. and shown irrelevant to "all things are number"; deductive method is Eleatic; the tetractys is a religious oath-formula. → Pythagoras caveat, Number concept, contradiction page.

## Whole-Work Assessment
Burkert is the hinge text that converts the vault's inherited, Burnet/Russell portrait of Pythagoras-the-scientist into a *contested* one. Its contribution is less a new fact than a new **method**: read the Pythagorean evidence by date, distrust the rich late tradition, and the mathematician dissolves, leaving a religious charismatic (metempsychosis + acusmata) whose "science" was supplied posthumously by Philolaus and, decisively, by Plato's Academy. This opens the vault's single highest-value tension here — [[Contradictions/Pythagoras the Scientist vs the Religious Charismatic]] — and relocates the number-cosmology onto a new [[Thinkers/Philolaus]] page and a [[Schools/Pythagoreanism]] page. It also reframes the wiki's existing "discovery of the irrational shattered Pythagoreanism" story as itself a piece of the legend. It deepens rather than overturns the Plato pages: Platonism is shown to be *upstream* of "ancient Pythagoreanism," half-inverting Russell's famous dictum.
