# Philosophical Investigations — Ludwig Wittgenstein (1953)
> The posthumous masterwork of the "later Wittgenstein," which dismantles the picture theory of his own *Tractatus* and reconceives meaning as use within rule-governed social practices ("language-games") rooted in shared "forms of life."

## Argument Spine

The *Investigations* attacks one deep picture: that language works by names standing for objects and that meaning is a mental or abstract item correlated with a word. Against this, Wittgenstein argues that the meaning of a word is its **use** in the stream of human activity; that words are tools whose functions are as varied as the "language-games" we play; that the concepts we share are held together not by a common essence but by **family resemblances**; that **following a rule** is a practice, not an act of interpretation or an inner mental mechanism, and so is essentially public and communal; that a logically **private language** (referring to inner sensations no one else could understand) is incoherent because it lacks any criterion of correctness; and that philosophical problems are not discoveries to be solved by theory but confusions to be dissolved — knots produced when "language goes on holiday" and is wrenched from its ordinary use. Philosophy becomes therapy: "to shew the fly the way out of the fly-bottle." Bedrock is reached not in justification but in shared practice — "This is simply what I do."

## Whole-Work Assessment

The *Investigations* is the founding text of the "later Wittgenstein" and of ordinary-language philosophy, and its arrival reshapes the vault's philosophy-of-language and philosophy-of-mind clusters. Its largest single contribution is a self-contained reversal: a major philosopher dismantling his own prior system, which the vault now holds as [[Contradictions/Early vs Later Wittgenstein]] — a contradiction page of unusually high value because both sides are authored by the same mind and feed two opposed downstream movements (the [[Concepts/Picture Theory of Language (Wittgenstein)|picture theory]] → Vienna Circle / [[Thinkers/A.J. Ayer|Ayer]]; [[Concepts/Meaning as Use (Wittgenstein)|meaning as use]] → Austin / [[Thinkers/John Searle|Searle]]). It deepens existing pages by giving the vault a non-referential theory of meaning ([[Concepts/Meaning as Use (Wittgenstein)]]), a non-essentialist theory of concepts ([[Concepts/Family Resemblance (Wittgenstein)]]) that directly answers [[Thinkers/Plato|Plato]]'s theory of Forms, and an anti-Cartesian theory of mind ([[Concepts/Private Language Argument (Wittgenstein)]]) that engages [[Thinkers/Descartes|Descartes]] and [[Thinkers/Locke|Locke]] head-on. The [[Concepts/Rule-Following (Wittgenstein)|rule-following]] considerations and [[Concepts/Forms of Life (Wittgenstein)|forms of life]] supply a practice-based account of normativity and justification that resonates forward with [[Concepts/Paradigm (Kuhn)|Kuhn]]'s theory-laden perception and with usage-based/distributional semantics in AI. The Argument Spine written at Step 1 held up against the text: the central target throughout is the single picture that meaning is a name-object (or word-idea) correlation, and the positive replacement is meaning-in-use grounded in shared form of life.
