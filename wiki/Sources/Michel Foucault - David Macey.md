# Michel Foucault (David Macey, 2004)

> Intellectual biography tracing Foucault's life and the emergence of each major work, from his bourgeois provincial upbringing to his death in 1984.

## Argument Spine

Macey argues that Foucault's philosophy cannot be separated from his biography — his sexuality, his experience of psychiatric institutions, his political engagements, and his peripatetic academic life all directly generated the problematics each major work addresses. The book presents Foucault not as a systematic thinker but as a *diagnostician* who repeatedly invented new methods (archaeology, genealogy, ethics of the self) in response to concrete historical problems. Each phase of Foucault's career is shown to dissolve and reconstitute the previous one rather than building a cumulative system.
