# Pythagoras and the Early Pythagoreans — Leonid Zhmud (2012)
> The major modern counter-statement to [[Sources/Lore and Science in Ancient Pythagoreanism - Walter Burkert (1972)|Burkert]]: a source-critical study that rehabilitates Pythagoras and the early Pythagoreans as genuine contributors to mathematics, harmonics, and astronomy — arguing the "religious charismatic only" portrait is itself a modern over-correction.

## Argument Spine
Against Burkert, Zhmud argues that the early Pythagorean tradition does contain a recoverable scientific core, and that Pythagoras was both a religious teacher *and* a pioneer of the *mathēmata*. His method: privilege the **fourth-century Peripatetic evidence** (Aristotle's lost monographs, and especially **Aristoxenus, Dicaearchus, Eudemus**, who wrote the first histories of geometry, arithmetic, astronomy, and harmonics) over both the late Neoplatonic accretions *and* over Burkert's hypercritical deflation. On this reading: the acusmatici/mathematici split is not the key to the "Pythagorean question" (Zhmud doubts the acusmatici were ever a real philosophical "sect"); metempsychosis is genuinely Pythagoras' but does not exhaust him; and the discoveries in harmonic theory, the foundations of deductive mathematics, and the planetary-order astronomy belong, in good part, to Pythagoras, **Hippasus**, and their immediate successors down to **Philolaus** and **Archytas** — not to a retrojecting Academy. Zhmud thus reverses Burkert's central inference: the science is early and the *over-religious* portrait is the artifact.

## Section Notes
- **Ch. 1 (The Pythagorean Question)** — Methodological core: rejects the "either wonder-worker *or* scientist" alternative as a "contrived alternative"; the supernatural element is *thinner* in 5th-c. testimony and grows later, so the legend grew rather than the science being added. Heraclitus B129/B40/B81 attack a *thinker*; Isocrates' *Busiris* (non-Academic) already credits philosophy + the mathematical sciences. → contradiction page (Position A modern form), [[Thinkers/Pythagoras]].
- **Ch. 2 (Life)** — Accepts Samos/Mnesarchus(merchant)/Polycrates-flight(~532)/Croton/Sybaris/Cylonian-conflict/Metapontum-death(~490s) and an attested family; *rejects* the Oriental travels ("no grounds to believe they ever happened") as *prōtoi heuretai*/diffusionist conjecture. Disagrees with Burkert (112: "links with the Orient are real"). → [[Thinkers/Pythagoras]].
- **Ch. 3–4 (After Pythagoras / Community)** — The group was a political *hetairia*, **not a sect/cult** (attacks Burkert's use of Bryan Wilson); no orthodox religion to dissent from; Aristoxenus' 218-name catalogue as backbone; "family resemblance" criterion; demolishes communal property, 5-yr silence, oral-only-teaching-until-Philolaus. → [[Schools/Pythagoreanism]].
- **Ch. 5 (Acusmatici/symbols)** — The mathematici/acusmatici split is a **late fiction** (Nicomachus, 1st–2nd c. AD); "acusmata" is Iamblichus' coinage (real word *symbola*); symbols are folklore for allegorical *exēgēsis* (Anaximander the Younger), not a rule of life or password. Frontal rebuttal of Burkert's central thesis. → [[Concepts/Acusmata and the Two Sects (Acusmatici and Mathematici)]].
- **Ch. 6 (Metempsychosis/shamanism)** — Rejects "Greek shamanism" wholesale (Meuli–Dodds "on sand"); accepts metempsychosis as genuinely Pythagoras' but **borrowed from Orphism** (reversing Burkert) and stripped of salvation-eschatology (*sōma-sēma* is Orphic, not Pythagorean); vegetarianism inconsistent (sacrificial-animal loophole). → [[Concepts/Metempsychosis (Transmigration of Souls)]].
- **Ch. 7–8 (Mathematics/Harmonics)** — The core counter-case: deductive proof is *not* Eleatic (rejects Szabó/Burkert) but internal to the even/odd theory; the "Master attribution custom" is Iamblichus' conjecture; Pythagoras genuinely founded the *beginnings* (concord ratios via Xenocrates fr. 87, three proportions, even/odd, the theorem); incommensurability and the disc experiment belong to **[[Thinkers/Hippasus]]** (early–mid 5th c.). Concedes the smithy legend is fiction and the tetractys/decad late. → [[Concepts/Number as the Essence of Reality (Pythagoras)]], [[Thinkers/Hippasus]].
- **Ch. 9–10 (Astronomy/Life sciences)** — Planet-*order* is a real pre-Philolaus discovery (Eudemus fr. 146); the harmony of the spheres is a *physical* model older than Philolaus and must be **detached** from his central-fire system (against Burkert *and* Huffman); counter-earth explains lunar eclipses, not arithmology. Medicine was central (Croton): **[[Thinkers/Alcmaeon of Croton]]** founds physiology, brain-cognition, *tekmairesthai*, embryology — secular from the start, against Burkert's "magic+medicine." → [[Concepts/Harmony of the Spheres]], [[Concepts/Pythagorean Medicine and the Life Sciences]].
- **Ch. 11–12 (Soul / "All is number" / the Academy)** — There was **no early number-ontology to retroject**: Philolaus' principles are limit/unlimited/harmonia, "to have number" = to be knowable; Aristotle's "Pythagoreans" are his own polemical construct (named individuals vs anonymous bearers "non-intersecting"). Burkert's "two-lines" theory declared "incorrect"; the One+indefinite-dyad is 1st-c.-BC neo-Pythagorean. Number-ontology "was born in the Academy." → [[Concepts/Number as the Essence of Reality (Pythagoras)]], [[Thinkers/Plato]], [[Thinkers/Aristotle]].

## Whole-Work Assessment
Zhmud is the vault's counterweight to [[Sources/Lore and Science in Ancient Pythagoreanism - Walter Burkert (1972)|Burkert]]: the same source-critical discipline turned to the opposite conclusion. Its central contribution is to convert the vault's [[Contradictions/Pythagoras the Scientist vs the Religious Charismatic|Scientist-vs-Charismatic]] tension from a "old tradition vs. modern consensus" matter into a *live dispute between two rigorous moderns*, by (1) reversing the developmental story — the *supernatural* element, not the science, is what the tradition added over time; (2) privileging the fourth-century Peripatetic historians of science (Eudemus, Aristoxenus) over both late Neoplatonism and Burkert's deflation; (3) recovering genuine early Pythagorean achievement in mathematics, harmonics, planetary astronomy, and — newly for the vault — medicine and the life sciences ([[Thinkers/Alcmaeon of Croton]], [[Thinkers/Hippasus]], [[Concepts/Pythagorean Medicine and the Life Sciences]]). Strikingly, Zhmud parts from the *traditional* Position A as much as from Burkert: he denies any early "all things are number" doctrine at all, dissolving Burkert's retrojection thesis from the other side — both agree the slogan is not Pythagoras', for opposite reasons. He also *converges* with Burkert on two negatives (the smithy experiment is fiction; the decad/tetractys is late), which narrows the genuine dispute to whether real *mathēmata* reach back to Pythagoras' own circle. Net effect on the vault: the Pythagoras cluster is rebalanced from a Burkert-dominant reading to a two-sided one, two new thinker pages and a new medicine concept are added, and the contradiction page becomes the vault's exemplar of an unresolved scholarly method-dispute.
