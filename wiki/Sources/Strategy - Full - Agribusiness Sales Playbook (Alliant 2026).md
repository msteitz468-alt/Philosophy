# Strategy - Full - Agribusiness Sales Playbook (Alliant 2026)

> Cradle-to-Grave Playbook: From Prospecting to Renewal for Alliant Insurance Services Agribusiness Division – Central Valley / Western US. Q2 2026 – Q1 2027. Prepared for Mark Steitz (Agribusiness Division). CONFIDENTIAL.

**Source**: `raw/Strategy - Full.docx` (detailed operational sales strategy document, March 2026 timeframe).

**Mission**: Build Alliant’s agribusiness book in the Central Valley and Western US by converting trigger-based prospects into long-term, multi-line clients through a disciplined sales lifecycle—from first touch to renewal and expansion.

**12-Month Targets (Q2 2026 – Q1 2027)**:
- Discovery meetings booked: 40–60
- Proposals presented: 20–30
- New accounts bound: 10–15
- Average new-account premium: $75K–$150K
- First-year retention rate: 90%+
- Cross-sell ratio (lines per client): 2.5+

**Alliant Differentiators** (anchor every outreach/proposal/renewal; pick 1-2 most relevant):
- Largest dedicated ag team in the US: 55+ specialists (crop, livestock, dairy, food processing, agtech).
- Fresno + Bakersfield offices: Local claims advocacy, same-day site visits ("30 minutes away, not 3 hours").
- Captive & alternative risk programs: Proprietary group captives for nuts/dairy/vegetables/poultry — 15–25% total-cost-of-risk savings at scale.
- Field-to-fork expertise: Food safety, FSMA/LGMA compliance, product recall, supply-chain interruption, environmental liability (SGMA, CEQA, water curtailment).

## 1. Executive Summary & Alliant Value Proposition

Detailed above (mission + targets + differentiators to reference everywhere).

## 2. Market Intelligence & Target Identification

- Vertical deep-dives: Agribusiness specifics (crop, livestock, dairy, nuts, vegetables, food processing, agtech).
- Trigger identification: Events that create insurance needs/opportunities (expansions, regulatory changes, weather events, supply chain shifts, new facilities, leadership changes, etc.).
- Target list building: By premium potential, fit with Alliant capabilities/captives, accessibility via associations/network.
- Competitive landscape: Other brokers' presence, carrier relationships in region.

## 3. Prospecting Playbook: Triggers, Associations & Outreach

- Trigger-based: Monitor for signals (news, associations, client intel, public records) → immediate tailored outreach.
- Associations & events: Key ag/food processing groups, trade shows, local chambers in Central Valley/Western US. Plan presence, speaking, sponsorships.
- Outreach sequences: Multi-touch (email, call, in-person, value-add content like risk assessments or captive info). Scripts and templates for first touch, follow-up, warm intro requests.
- Integrates/updates prior research (Sessions 1-3).

## 4. Discovery & Needs Analysis Framework

- Structured discovery: Questions that uncover full risk picture beyond stated needs (field-to-fork exposures, compliance gaps, supply chain, environmental, recall, etc.).
- Needs analysis: Map client operations → Alliant differentiators/captives → multi-line opportunities (property, liability, workers comp, auto, product recall, environmental, etc.).
- Tools: Question frameworks, exposure checklists, captive qualification criteria.

## 5. Proposal Development & Presentation

- Tailored proposals anchored on 1-2 differentiators + specific client data from discovery.
- Visuals, captive illustrations (15-25% savings examples), local service proof points.
- Presentation: C-suite ready, focus on total cost of risk + coverage gaps closed + partnership (not just price).

## 6. Closing & Binding Strategy

- Objection handling: Price, incumbent relationships, "we're too small", captive concerns.
- Urgency/decision frameworks: Use triggers, renewal timing, regulatory deadlines.
- Binding process: Smooth handoff to service/ops, clear next steps.

## 7. Onboarding & Implementation

- Post-bind: Kickoff with client stakeholders, Alliant team intro, systems setup, first 30/60/90 day plan.
- Set expectations for claims advocacy, site visits, ongoing risk management.

## 8. Retention, Renewal & Cross-Sell

- Proactive: Annual risk reviews, coverage gap identification (like the +$22k recall example in personal plan), market updates.
- Renewal strategy: Data-driven (retention metrics, loss runs), relationship deepening, cross-sell to 2.5+ lines.
- Expansion: Leverage captives, new exposures, referrals.

## 9. Master Action Calendar (Q2 2026 – Q1 2027)

Quarterly/monthly/weekly actions: Association events, trigger monitoring cadence, prospecting volume targets, proposal prep windows, renewal clusters, networking goals, CPCU/study if aligned, personal brand activities.

## 10. Pipeline Tracking & Success Metrics

- CRM or simple tracker: Stages (target → trigger identified → outreach → discovery meeting → proposal → bound → onboarded → renewed).
- KPIs: Activity (meetings/outreach), conversion rates, premium booked vs target, retention, cross-sell, time-to-close.
- Review rhythm: Weekly pipeline, monthly metrics vs calendar, quarterly strategy adjustment.
