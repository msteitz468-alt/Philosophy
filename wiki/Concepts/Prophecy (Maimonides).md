# Prophecy (Maimonides)

> Maimonides' naturalized theory of prophecy (*Guide* II.32–48): prophecy is an **emanation from the Active Intellect**, flowing first into the prophet's rational faculty and then into his imaginative faculty. It is the highest natural human perfection, attainable only by one who has perfected intellect, imagination, and morals — not an arbitrary divine fiat. Moses alone is the homonymous exception, prophesying through pure intellect without the imaginative medium.

## Three Views — Parallel to the Creation Debate

Maimonides frames three positions, deliberately mirroring the [[Concepts/Creation vs Eternity (Maimonides)|creation]] trichotomy:
1. **The vulgar view** — God arbitrarily makes anyone morally decent a prophet.
2. **The philosophers' view** — prophecy is a *natural* perfection that the fully qualified person inevitably attains, like any other faculty maturing.
3. **Scripture's view (his own)** — identical to the philosophers' *except* that God may miraculously *withhold* prophecy from one otherwise qualified. The supernatural enters only as a veto, not as the positive cause.

## The Emanation Theory

> "Prophecy is, in truth and reality, an emanation sent forth by the Divine Being through the medium of the Active Intellect, in the first instance to man's rational faculty, and then to his imaginative faculty; it is the highest degree and greatest perfection man can attain."

The two faculties sort the recipients: when the overflow reaches **only the rational** faculty → philosophers; **only the imaginative** → "statesmen, lawgivers, diviners, charmers"; **both** → prophets. Prophecy thus unites theoretical insight with the imaginative power to clothe truth in vivid figures, parables, and visions that can move a people. "Dream is the nobelet (unripe fruit) of prophecy... the difference being one of quantity, not of quality" — ordinary dreaming and prophecy lie on one continuum.

## The Three Prerequisites

1. **Intellectual perfection** — acquired by study; one must already be a philosopher.
2. **Perfection of the imaginative faculty** — innate and constitutional; it "cannot be supplied or remedied by training." This is why not every philosopher is a prophet.
3. **Moral perfection** — the desires of touch (eating, sex) and the appetite for ambition/honor must be subdued.

Degrees of prophecy track these endowments. Emotional disturbance blocks the overflow: sadness, anger, and mourning suspended prophecy in Jacob and Moses — which also explains, for Maimonides, why prophecy lapsed in exile.

## Moses the Exception

Maimonides refuses to subsume Moses under the general theory: "the term prophet is applied to Moses and other men homonymously." Moses alone prophesied through the **intellect without the imaginative medium**, received words directly rather than through an angel, prophesied at will without preparation, and worked public miracles "in the sight of all Israel." This protects the unique, legislative authority of the Mosaic Law while still naturalizing prophecy in general.

## How Different Thinkers Use This

- **Within the *Guide*:** prophecy is the hinge between Maimonides' metaphysics and his politics/ethics — the prophet's perfected intellect grounds his [[Concepts/Reasons for the Commandments (Maimonides)|rational legislation]], and prophecy is the very "authority" by which the undecidable [[Concepts/Creation vs Eternity (Maimonides)|creation question]] is settled. The faculties at work presuppose the Aristotelian Active Intellect / soul psychology.
- **vs. [[Thinkers/Al-Ghazali]]:** for Ghazali, prophetic miracle is the centerpiece, secured by [[Concepts/Occasionalism vs Natural Causation (Ghazali and Ibn Rushd)|denying fixed natures]] so that anything is possible at God's decree; Maimonides instead *naturalizes* prophecy as the lawful unfolding of human faculties, reserving the supernatural to God's withholding. A clean instance of the two thinkers' opposite instincts about nature and miracle.
- **vs. [[Thinkers/Ibn Rushd]]:** both keep prophecy within an Aristotelian Active-Intellect framework and treat the prophet-legislator as supplying for the masses what demonstration supplies for the few — close kin to Averroes' philosophy/religion division.
- **vs. [[Sources/Ethics - Spinoza (Cambridge, Kisner ed. 2018)|Spinoza]]:** Spinoza's *Theological-Political Treatise* takes Maimonides' move further — prophecy is *entirely* a matter of vivid imagination, carrying moral certainty but no special speculative knowledge; the rationalist naturalization becomes a deflation.

## Contradictions / Open Questions

- If prophecy is a natural perfection, in what sense is revelation *authoritative* over reason — the very authority Maimonides invokes to settle the creation question?
- The Moses-exception protects the Law but strains the naturalism: is "homonymous" prophecy a genuine theory or a placeholder for a miracle?
