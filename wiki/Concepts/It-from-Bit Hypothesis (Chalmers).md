# It-from-Bit Hypothesis (Chalmers)
> "Everything in the physical world… is made of patterns of bits" — Wheeler's slogan, stripped of its idealism and read as a viable digital metaphysics that could realize current physics.

## Summary

*Reality+* ch. 8. The lineage runs from Leibniz's invention of binary (1679; the 1703 essay inspired by the I Ching's hexagrams) through Conway's Game of Life (a universal computer from simple cell rules) to John Wheeler's 1989 coinage: "every physical quantity, every it, derives its ultimate significance from bits, binary yes-or-no indications… it from bit." Chalmers strips Wheeler's participatory/observer (idealist-leaning) reading: the hypothesis is that physics is grounded in digital structures, a "distinctive form of materialism" and "an exciting newcomer" alongside Thales's water, Pythagoras's number, and Democritus's atoms.

Key distinctions:
- **Three kinds of information**: semantic (facts), structural (bits), symbolic (bits encoding facts). Wittgenstein's *Tractatus* ("The world is the totality of facts, not of things") is "it-from-fact," not it-from-bit.
- Information is physical (Landauer) and **substrate-neutral**; Bateson's "a difference which makes a difference" characterizes structural information.
- **Digital physics** (Zuse's *Calculating Space*; Fredkin, Wolfram): bit-level physics could realize current physics as statistical mechanics realizes thermodynamics; space and time may be emergent. Not favored by physicists — Chalmers needs only that it **could** be true.
- **Versions**: perfect it-from-bit (undiscoverable), **it-from-qubit** (Deutsch, Lloyd, Zizzi — better fit for a quantum world), it-from-trit/it-from-real; **it-from-bit-from-it** (bits realized by deeper "its" — including it-from-bit-from-consciousness, Berkeley or panpsychism) vs. **pure it-from-bit** ("a world of pure differences").

The hypothesis is ultimately "a step ladder that we can kick away" for the deeper **structuralism** ([[Concepts/Structuralism and the Garden of Eden (Chalmers)]]); its work in the book is the ch. 9 equivalence with the simulation hypothesis that yields [[Concepts/Simulation Realism (Chalmers)]].

## How This Relates to Prior Thinkers

- **[[Thinkers/Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz]]**: invented binary yet preferred the panpsychist idealism of monads ([[Sources/Monadology - Leibniz (1714)]]) — the road not taken.
- **[[Thinkers/Claude Shannon]]** ([[Sources/A Mathematical Theory of Communication - Claude Shannon (1948)]]): supplies the structural-information measure the hypothesis is built from.
- **[[Thinkers/Pythagoras]]** ([[Concepts/Number as the Essence of Reality (Pythagoras)]]): the deepest ancestor — reality as mathematical structure; it-from-bit is Pythagoreanism in binary.
- **[[Concepts/Evidence of Computation (Virk)]]**: Virk reads pixelation/Planck limits as evidence *for* computation; Chalmers keeps digital physics as a live metaphysical possibility without endorsing the physics.
- **[[Thinkers/Ludwig Wittgenstein]]**: *Tractatus* it-from-fact contrasted ([[Sources/Tractatus Logico-Philosophicus - Ludwig Wittgenstein (1922)]]).

**1996 ancestor**: *The Conscious Mind* ch. 8 already engaged Wheeler/Fredkin's "pure informational flux" and there *favored* the grounding *Reality+* leaves optional — phenomenal/protophenomenal properties as the intrinsic natures realizing physical information ("Experience is information from the inside; physics is information from the outside").
