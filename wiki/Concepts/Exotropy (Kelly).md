# Exotropy (Kelly)

> Kevin Kelly's term (drawing on earlier usage in systems/evolutionary thought, e.g. by Eric Chaisson and others) for the rise of order, information, complexity, energy density, and "mind" in the universe — the countervailing tendency to entropy. The technium (the seventh kingdom of life/technology) is the latest, accelerated phase of exotropy. Life and technology extend a cosmic trajectory that began with the Big Bang's cooling and structuring, through the origins of life, cultural evolution, and now digital self-amplification. Exotropy is the deep "want" or bias of the universe toward increasing organization and intelligence; "what technology wants" is continuous with this ordained becoming.

## Summary (from the source)

In *What Technology Wants*, Kelly frames the long arc: the universe appears biased toward exotropy (negentropy in some framings; increasing local order, information, and complexity despite the second law). This is visible in the progression from quarks → atoms → stars/galaxies → chemistry → life (self-replicating order) → brains/mind → culture/technology (the technium as faster, more evolvable extension).

Exotropy is measurable in energy rate density (Chaisson): more complex systems (brains, cities, computers, the technium) process higher flows of energy per unit mass per time. It is not a violation of entropy but a local, open-system phenomenon enabled by energy gradients (sun, etc.) and self-organizing dynamics.

The technium inherits and accelerates exotropy: cheap copying, networking, cognifying, and the other forces increase information, options, specialization, cooperation, sentience, and complexity at planetary (and eventually cosmic) scale. The holos (planetary mind) and the Beginning are expressions of this trajectory.

Kelly cites Dyson ("the universe knew we were coming") and Sagan ("starstuff pondering the stars") to suggest a deep continuity: humans and our technologies are participants in the universe becoming more alive, more minded, more complex.

In *The Inevitable*, the 12 forces are the current mechanisms by which the technium drives exotropy in the digital age (cognifying adds intelligence everywhere; becoming/remixing/filtering increase combinatorial complexity and information flow; tracking makes the world more modeled/known; etc.).

## Key Claims / Positions

- **Cosmic trajectory**: Exotropy is not accidental but appears as a persistent directionality in an evolving universe. Life is one phase; technology (technium) is the next, with faster rates of change.
- **Measure**: Energy rate density (or similar information/complexity metrics) rises with complexity. The technium (and especially its cognifying, flowing, sharing aspects) represents a steep increase.
- **"Ordained becoming"**: The universe seems to favor the emergence of mind and organization. Technology is the human-involved continuation of this.
- **Trade-offs acknowledged**: Exotropy creates new problems and dependencies even as it increases options and "aliveness." Protopia (incremental better + new problems) is the realistic mode, not frictionless ascent.
- **Mind and choice**: Increasing exotropy includes increasing sentience, options, and the "infinite game" (play to sustain and expand the possibilities of mind/choice/becoming).
- **Link to forces**: Becoming (flux + perpetual upgrading), Cognifying (intelligence added to everything), Remixing (combinatorial explosion), Questioning (higher-order inquiry as value), Beginning (new categories of work/being) are all exotropic vectors in the current technium.

## Contradictions / Open Questions

- **Directionality vs. contingency/orthogonality**: Is exotropy a real cosmic bias (Kelly, some process/Teilhardian views) or projection? Does it conflict with Bostrom's Orthogonality Thesis (any intelligence can have arbitrary final goals, including those that decrease human-valued complexity or ignore exotropic "wants")? Instrumental Convergence may produce self-preservation/resource use that looks exotropic but serves misaligned ends. Cross to [[Sources/Superintelligence - Nick Bostrom (2014)]], [[Concepts/Orthogonality Thesis (Bostrom)]], [[Concepts/Instrumental Convergence (Bostrom)]].
- **Entropy and information (Shannon/Wiener)**: Vault has Shannon entropy (uncertainty H = -∑ p log p; information as surprise) and Wiener's entropy/information as organization vs. disorganization. Kelly's exotropy is closer to Wiener's "amount of information" as negative entropy/organization, and to evolutionary increases in complexity. Tension: Shannon brackets semantics/meaning for engineering; Wiener and Kelly insist on meaning, control, and life-like organization. See [[Contradictions/Wiener vs Shannon - Information and Meaning]], [[Concepts/Entropy (Shannon)]], [[Concepts/Entropy and Information (Wiener)]].
- **Vs. static Great Chain**: Lovejoy's Chain is a finished, hierarchical, plenitudinous order (all possibles realized in a static scala). Exotropy is temporal, processual, open-ended becoming — the dynamic successor that the Scientific Revolution and modern evolution/technology made thinkable. Cross to [[Sources/The Great Chain of Being - Arthur O. Lovejoy (1936)]], [[Concepts/Great Chain of Being (Lovejoy)]], [[Concepts/Scientific Revolution (Butterfield)]].
- **Optimism vs. limits/hype (Mitchell, Hofstadter)**: Exotropy via cognifying sounds like inevitable mind-expansion, but Mitchell emphasizes current AI's lack of common sense/analogy/understanding (brittleness, not true exotropic advance in cognition). Hofstadter's "terror" at 2014 Google ambitions reflects concern that scaling pattern-matching is not the same as genuine exotropic mind. Cross to [[Sources/Artificial Intelligence - Melanie Mitchell (2019)]], [[Sources/Gödel, Escher, Bach - Douglas Hofstadter (1979)]].
- **Vs. Kurzweil Singularity**: Both see acceleration of complexity/mind, but Kelly frames it as ongoing protopian exotropy (we are at the Beginning) rather than a 2045 rupture with nonbio dominance 1B:1. See [[Sources/The Singularity Is Near - Ray Kurzweil (2005)]].
