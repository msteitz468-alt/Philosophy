# Attributes and Parallelism (Spinoza)

> God (the one substance) has infinite attributes, each expressing an eternal and infinite essence. We know two: thought and extension. The modes of each attribute are parallel: “The order and connection of ideas is the same as the order and connection of things” (2p7). A body (mode of extension) and its mind/idea (mode of thought) are one and the same thing expressed under different attributes. There is no causal interaction between mind and body; the same causal order appears under each attribute.

## Core Description

An attribute is “that which an intellect perceives of a substance as constituting its essence” (1def4). Since the essence of a substance is the basis for conceiving and causing all its properties (Spinoza’s rationalism), an attribute is the essence through which all the properties and modes of a substance under that aspect are conceived and caused.

God, as absolutely infinite substance, has infinite attributes (1def6). Each attribute is conceived through itself (1p10) and expresses the reality or being of the substance. We have knowledge of only two: thought (God is a thinking thing; particular thoughts are modes of thought) and extension (God is an extended thing; particular bodies are modes of extension).

**Parallelism** (primarily 2p7 and 2p7s): The order and connection of ideas (under the attribute of thought) is the same as the order and connection of things (under the attribute of extension). This has three main implications:

1. **Representation**: The idea of a body represents that body.
2. **Causal mirroring**: If body A causes an effect in body B, then the idea of A causes a corresponding effect in the idea of B (and vice versa under other attributes).
3. **Identity**: The mode of extension and the idea of that mode are “one and the same thing” expressed in two ways (under different attributes). Mind and body are not two substances (contra Descartes) but two aspects of the same finite mode of the one substance.

Consequently, “whether we conceive nature under the attribute of extension or under the attribute of thought or under any other attribute, we will find one and the same order or one and the same connection of causes” (2p7s). God is the cause of things as they are in themselves insofar as he consists of infinite attributes.

The mind is the idea of the body (2p11–13 and following). The human mind is the idea of the human body; its power of thinking equals the body’s power of acting (under their respective attributes).

## Key Supporting Propositions

- Thought is an attribute of God (2p1); extension is an attribute of God (2p2).
- There is in God an idea both of his essence and of everything that necessarily follows from it (2p3).
- The formal being of ideas recognizes God as cause only insofar as he is considered as a thinking thing (2p5).
- The modes of any attribute have God as their cause only insofar as he is considered under that attribute (2p6).
- The order and connection of ideas is the same as the order and connection of things (2p7).
- God’s power of thought is equal to his actual power of action (2p7c).
- The mind and the body are one and the same thing, conceived now under thought, now under extension (2p7s, 2p13s).
- The human mind is the idea of the human body (2p11–13 and corollaries).

## Relevance to Vault

Parallelism is one of the vault’s richest structural metaphors.

- **Sources and representations**: Raw source files (extension) and their wiki counterparts (ideas/summaries, analyses, concepts, syntheses) are one and the same knowledge expressed under different attributes. The “order and connection” of the sources (their internal logic, chronology, intertextuality) should be mirrored in the order and connections of the wiki (cross-references, concept clusters, index structure, log history).
- **No interaction problem**: We do not need a special mechanism for how “mind” (agent reasoning, schema application) acts on “body” (files, raw data) or vice versa. The same causal/maintenance order appears under both. When the agent edits a page on the basis of a clear reading of a source, it is not mind acting on body; it is the same activity expressed in two registers.
- **Adequate ideas and power**: The power of the “mind” (agent/wiki) under the attribute of thought equals the power of the “body” (the accumulated, well-maintained sources and structures) under the attribute of extension. Increasing the clarity and connectedness of the wiki increases the effective power of the whole system.
- **Multiple attributes**: The vault may be conceived under other “attributes” beyond files and concepts (e.g., temporal/historical via log and version history; social/collaborative via user direction and review; multimodal via images and other media). Each attribute expresses the same underlying “substance” (the knowledge project) in its own way, with the same order.
- **Contrast with Descartes**: Real distinction and causal interaction between two substances vs. one substance expressed under parallel attributes with no cross-attribute causation. This is the vault’s core paired study of early modern rationalism.
- **Implications for method**: Because the orders are the same, work done in one register (e.g., careful source reading) is automatically reflected in the other (better wiki structure), and vice versa. Maintenance under one attribute strengthens the whole.

**Open questions**:
- What other “attributes” of the vault are worth distinguishing and cultivating (e.g., a “social” or “temporal” attribute)?
- How strictly should we maintain parallelism in practice (e.g., should every major source change immediately trigger corresponding wiki updates, and how visible should that mirroring be)?
- Does the agent itself have a “body” (its tool use, session state, underlying model weights) that is parallel to its “mind” (reasoning, schema application)?
