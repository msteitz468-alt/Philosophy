# Faith and Reason - Enthusiasm (Locke)

> Reason = "natural revelation" (God speaking through the natural faculties). Faith = "assent to any proposition... upon the credit of the proposer, as coming from God." Where reason ends (things above reason but not contrary to it), faith may begin on divine authority. But revelation must be judged by reason (evidence that it is from God; content not contradicting clear knowledge).

**Enthusiasm**: "A strong and firm persuasion" of immediate supernatural revelation without reason or evidence. "It is a revelation, because they firmly believe it, and they believe it, because it is a revelation." Often from "the conceits of a warmed or overweening brain." Dangerous: sets up private opinion as infallible rule; leads to error, division, "madness." "Reason must be our last judge and guide in every thing."
