# Class Struggle (Marx)

> The central social and historical dynamic in Marxism. Formulated in *The Communist Manifesto* (1848) with the famous declaration: *"The history of all hitherto existing society is the history of class struggles."* It asserts that human societies are divided into antagonistic classes based on their relation to the means of production, and that the conflict between these classes is the primary driver of political and social change.

## Bourgeoisie and Proletariat

Under the capitalist mode of production, society is increasingly polarized into two great hostile camps:

1.  **The Bourgeoisie**:
    *   The modern capitalists, owners of the means of social production, and employers of wage labor.
    *   They control the state apparatus (which Marx calls "a committee for managing the common affairs of the whole bourgeoisie") and use it to protect private property.
2.  **The Proletariat**:
    *   The class of modern wage laborers who, having no means of production of their own, are reduced to selling their labor power in order to live.
    *   They are treated as a commodity, exposed to all the vicissitudes of competition and fluctuations of the market.

**Exploitation and Crisis**:
- Capitalism inherently exploits the proletariat by extracting "surplus value" (the wealth created by workers beyond what they are paid in wages).
- This leads to systemic crises of overproduction, growing inequality, and the concentration of capital into fewer hands.
- In doing so, the bourgeoisie acts as its own "grave-diggers" by organizing and unifying the working class in factories, fostering **Class Consciousness**—the workers' realization of their shared class interests and revolutionary power.

**Revolutionary Resolution**:
- The conflict must culminate in a proletarian revolution. Unlike prior historical revolutions (which replaced one ruling class with another), the proletarian revolution represents the interest of the immense majority and leads to the abolition of private property and class divisions altogether, establishing a classless, communist society.

## In the Vault

- **Master-Slave Inversion**: The class struggle is a direct materialist adaptation of Hegel's [Master-Slave Dialectic](file:///home/mark/Documents/Philosophy/wiki/Concepts/Master-Slave%20Dialectic%20(Hegel).md). Just as the master's dependence on the slave's labor eventually leads to the slave's liberation, the bourgeoisie's dependence on the proletariat's labor leads to the working class's revolution.
- **Realist Power Dynamics**: Matches Thucydides' realist focus on structural imbalances and conflict ([Power, Empire, and Revolt](file:///home/mark/Documents/Philosophy/wiki/Concepts/Power,%20Empire,%20and%20Revolt%20(Thucydides).md)), but shifts the unit of analysis from nation-states to economic classes.
- **Social Cohesion vs. Individual Liberty**: Connects to Bertrand Russell's master political dialectic: the absolute collective discipline required for class struggle vs. the individual liberty defended by liberals like Mill.
