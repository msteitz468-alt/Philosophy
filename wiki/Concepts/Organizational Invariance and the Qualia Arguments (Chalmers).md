# Organizational Invariance and the Qualia Arguments (Chalmers)
> Any system with the same fine-grained functional organization has qualitatively identical experiences — "whether realized in silicon chips, in the population of China, or in beer cans and ping-pong balls."

## Summary

*The Conscious Mind* ch. 7. The **principle of organizational invariance** is Chalmers's second psychophysical law, defended not against logical possibility (he grants zombies are logically possible) but against the *natural* possibility of absent or inverted qualia in functional isomorphs.

**Fading qualia**: replace neurons one by one with functionally identical silicon chips. If absent qualia were naturally possible, some intermediate "Joe" would have faded experience while remaining functionally identical and rational — "systematically wrong about everything that he is experiencing," claiming bright red while experiencing "tepid pink." Either that massive dissociation between cognition and consciousness, or "brute discontinuities in the laws of nature" (suddenly disappearing qualia). Both implausible. Against Searle's shrinking-consciousness variant: "There is simply no room in the system for any new beliefs to be formed."

**Dancing qualia**: install a functionally isomorphic silicon backup circuit with a switch. If inverted qualia were possible, flipping it would make qualia "dance" red/blue "before my eyes" while "I will simply go about my business, noticing nothing unusual" — but "when one's experiences change significantly, one can notice the change."

Conclusion: **nonreductive functionalism** — "functional organization suffices for conscious experience with natural necessity," though experience is not *reducible* to organization. Consciousness supervenes on the organizational, which "gives strong support to the ambitions of researchers in artificial intelligence."

## How Different Thinkers Use This

- **[[Thinkers/John Searle]]**: his silicon-brain "shrinking consciousness" scenario (1992) is the direct target; the argument also powers Chalmers's [[Concepts/Chinese Room Argument (Searle)]] reply in ch. 9.
- **Block**: absent-qualia intuitions (China-nation) are what the fading argument is built to defeat at the natural-possibility level.
- **[[Thinkers/John Locke]]** ([[Sources/An Essay Concerning Human Understanding - Locke]]): the inverted spectrum traces to the *Essay* — "one man's mind could not pass into another man's body, to perceive what appearances were produced by those organs."
- Grounds [[Concepts/Machine Consciousness and Mind Uploading (Chalmers)]]: *Reality+*'s and the uploading literature's gradual-replacement optimism is this argument redeployed (cf. [[Concepts/Mind Uploading (Kurzweil)]]).
