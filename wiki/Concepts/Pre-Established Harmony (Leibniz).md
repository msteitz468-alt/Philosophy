# Pre-Established Harmony (Leibniz)

> Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz's solution to the mind-body problem and the question of inter-substance causality. The theory of **Pre-Established Harmony** asserts that no two substances (monads) interact causally with one another; instead, God has pre-programmed every substance at creation so that their internal activities run in perfect, pre-coordinated synchronization.

## Core Principles

**Resolution of the Mind-Body Problem**:
- Descartes' dualism left an unresolved gap: how can an immaterial mind causally interact with a material body?
- Occasionalists (like Nicolas Malebranche) argued that God intervenes directly on every single occasion to coordinate mind and body (e.g., when you decide to move your arm, God moves it).
- Leibniz rejected occasionalism as requiring constant, perpetual miracles. Instead, he proposed **Pre-Established Harmony**: God, at the moment of creation, designed the soul and body with such perfect skill that they naturally keep time together, though they function on entirely different laws (the soul on laws of final causes/desires, the body on laws of efficient causes/motion).

**The Twin Clocks Analogy**:
- Leibniz illustrated this theory using the analogy of two clocks that keep perfect time with one another. This coordination could happen in three ways:
  1. **Direct Influence**: One clock moves the other (Descartes' physical interaction - rejected by Leibniz as physically impossible for mind and body).
  2. **Constant Supervision**: A clockmaker sits by the clocks and constantly adjusts them (Malebranche's occasionalism - rejected by Leibniz as unworthy of God).
  3. **Perfect Artistry**: The clocks are built so perfectly by a master craftsman that they naturally agree forever (Leibniz's pre-established harmony).

**Universal Harmony**:
- The harmony is not just between mind and body, but among all monads in the universe. Every single monad, being "windowless" and without external causal influence, develops purely from its own internal state. Yet, because of God's pre-established harmony, every change in one monad corresponds perfectly to changes in all others, making each monad a mirror of the entire universe.

## In the Vault

- **The Mind-Body Debate**: Pre-established harmony sits alongside Cartesian interactionism, Spinoza's double-aspect monism, occasionalism, and modern physicalism as a primary model for mind-body relation.
- **The Critique of Causality**: It represents a total rejection of transient causation (one substance acting on another), anticipating David Hume's skepticism about causal connection, though resolving it through divine rationalism rather than habit and association.
