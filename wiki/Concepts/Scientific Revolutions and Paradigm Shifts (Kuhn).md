# Scientific Revolutions and Paradigm Shifts (Kuhn)

> A **scientific revolution** is the non-cumulative episode in which a community abandons one [[Concepts/Paradigm (Kuhn)|paradigm]] in [[Concepts/Anomaly and Crisis (Kuhn)|crisis]] for an incompatible new one — a **paradigm shift**. It is a discontinuous "**gestalt switch**" or **conversion**, not a step-by-step deduction from neutral evidence; afterward scientists "work in a different world," and the resulting picture of progress is **evolution *from* primitive beginnings, not evolution *toward* a fixed truth**.

## The Shape of a Revolution

- **Necessity**: revolutions are how science gets fundamentally new theories; without the anomaly→crisis→revolution pattern the field would stagnate. "To reject one paradigm without simultaneously substituting another is to reject science itself."
- **Not compelled by proof**: "**The competition between paradigms is not the sort of battle that can be resolved by proofs.**" Because rivals are [[Concepts/Incommensurability (Kuhn)|incommensurable]] — disagreeing about standards, about which problems count, and about the meanings of shared terms — the transition "cannot be made a step at a time, forced by logic and neutral experience. Like the gestalt switch, it must occur all at once (though not necessarily in an instant) or not at all."
- **Conversion**: "The transfer of allegiance from paradigm to paradigm is a **conversion experience** that cannot be forced." Resistance, especially by older scientists committed to the old tradition, is "not a violation of scientific standards but an index to the nature of scientific research itself." Often the old guard never converts (Priestley, Kelvin); as Planck observed, a new truth wins "because its opponents eventually die." What actually happens is "an increasing shift in the distribution of professional allegiances."
- **What persuades** (short of proof): the new paradigm's claim to **solve the crisis-provoking problems**; striking **quantitative precision**; the **prediction of unexpected new phenomena** (Copernicus → phases of Venus; Fresnel's wave theory → Poisson's white spot; general relativity → Mercury's perihelion); and **aesthetic** appeal ("neater," "simpler") — decisive for the early few who develop a still-crude theory enough to attract the rest. Such a commitment "can only be made on faith."

## Changes of World View, and Invisibility

- **§X "Revolutions as Changes of World View"**: Kuhn argues — in deliberately hedged "try-out" language ("we may want to say") — that after a revolution "scientists work in a different world" (post-Copernicus astronomers, post-Lavoisier chemists). Hacking flags this as Kuhn's most metaphor-laden, contested claim; Kuhn never flatly asserts in "cold print" that the world itself changed.
- **§XI The invisibility of revolutions**: textbooks systematically **rewrite history to look linear and cumulative**, hiding the discontinuities (and even leading scientists, like Dalton, to misremember their own path). This manufactured continuity is why the cumulative myth is so persistent.

## Progress Without a Goal (§XIII)

Kuhn's most radical thesis, and the deepest break with Popper:

- Science is **cumulative *within* normal science but not *across* revolutions** — a revolution can *lose* problems and solutions (Newton banished "what causes gravity?"; later relativity reclaimed an analogous question), so it is not pure accumulation toward truth.
- "The developmental process described in this essay has been a process of **evolution *from* primitive beginnings**… But nothing that has been or will be said makes it a process of **evolution *toward* anything**." Like **Darwinian natural selection** — selection of the fittest practice by competition within the community, "without benefit of a set goal, a permanent fixed scientific truth." Kuhn explicitly questions "whether it really helps to imagine that there is some one full, objective, true account of nature" that science approaches, and suggests substituting *evolution-from-what-we-know* for *evolution-toward-what-we-wish-to-know* — adding that "somewhere in this maze… must lie the problem of induction."

## Relation to Other Vault Content

- **Vs. Popper's verisimilitude** (headline contrast): Popper holds that bold conjectures, surviving severe tests, carry science *toward* the truth — rising [[Concepts/Verisimilitude (Truthlikeness) (Popper)|verisimilitude]] with truth as a Tarskian regulative ideal. Kuhn denies any such goal "set by nature in advance." This is the core of [[Contradictions/Popper vs Kuhn - Falsification vs Paradigms]].
- **Historiographical background (Butterfield)**: Kuhn's model is in significant part a revision of the earlier "Scientific Revolution" historiography exemplified by Herbert Butterfield's *The Origins of Modern Science* (see [[Sources/The Origins of Modern Science 1300-1800 - Herbert Butterfield (1949)]] and [[Concepts/Scientific Revolution (Butterfield)]]). Butterfield framed the 16th–17th-c. transformation as a fundamental break that changed "habitual mental operations" and created the modern world and mentality, outshining Renaissance and Reformation. Kuhn complicates this with normal science (most science is not revolutionary), revolutions as community conversions rather than simple accumulation or single "thinking cap" change, and incommensurability (rival paradigms do not simply correct one another toward a shared truth). Hacking's introductory essay to the 4th ed. of Kuhn situates this shift.
- **Vs. Popper on method**: Popper makes revolution *permanent and rational* (constant attempted refutation); Kuhn makes it *rare, traumatic, and conversion-like*.
- **Anti-teleology** connects to Nietzsche's suspicion of fixed truth ([[Concepts/Perspectivism (Nietzsche)]]) and the constructivist pole of [[Concepts/Pragmatic Theory of Truth (James)]] — but Kuhn (per Hacking) is "a fact lover and a truth seeker," not a relativist; he keeps shared theory-choice **values** (accuracy, consistency, scope, simplicity, fruitfulness).
- The **political-revolution analogy** (institutions that "have ceased adequately to meet the problems… they have in part created") is explicit in §IX; note this is *not* the law-governed historical inevitability Popper attacks in [[Concepts/Critique of Historicism and Dialectic (Popper)]].

## Vault Meta / AI

A **paradigm shift** in the wiki = a restructuring of the SCHEMA itself, forced by accumulated anomalies that normal-science maintenance cannot absorb — discontinuous, not a lint tweak, and "invisible" afterward once the index/pages are rewritten to fit.
