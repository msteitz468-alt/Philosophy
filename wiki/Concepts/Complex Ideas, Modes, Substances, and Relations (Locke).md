# Complex Ideas, Modes, Substances, and Relations (Locke)

> Once the mind has simple ideas from the two fountains (sensation and reflection), it has power to repeat, compare, and unite them into COMPLEX ideas (voluntarily, beyond what direct experience gave). These are "made up of several simple ones put together." Reduced under three heads: 1. MODES (dependences or affections of substances; simple modes = variations of one simple idea, e.g. dozen/score; mixed modes = of several kinds, e.g. beauty, theft, murder). 2. SUBSTANCES (combinations of simple ideas representing particular things subsisting by themselves; include the "supposed or confused idea of substance" + qualities/powers, e.g. lead or man; single or collective, e.g. army or flock). 3. RELATIONS (the consideration and comparing of one idea with another).

Even the most abstruse ideas (space, time, infinity, etc.) are derived from repeating and joining simple ideas from sensation or reflection.

## Vault Relevance

- **With Hume**: Complex ideas and relations feed directly into Hume's Fork (relations of ideas vs. matters of fact from experience/co-existence) and the problem of induction (co-existence in substances not rationally necessary).
- **With Descartes**: Complex ideas of substances (with idea of substance) vs. clear/distinct simple natures; personal identity (consciousness) vs. substantial self.
- **AI / compounding**: Simple ideas = features/embeddings; complex = higher-level abstractions, objects, relations, categories built by the model. Modes/substances/relations map to attributes, entities, graphs. "Voluntarily" compounding = reasoning/synthesis layers.
