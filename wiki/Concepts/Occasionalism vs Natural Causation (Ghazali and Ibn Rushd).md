# Occasionalism vs Natural Causation (Ghazali and Ibn Rushd)

> The radical theological position (Ghazali, drawing on Ash‘arite theology) that there are no necessary causal connections or inherent powers in created things — fire does not burn cotton “by nature”; God creates the effect (burning, satiety, death, etc.) directly at each moment, usually following a customary “habit” but able to interrupt it at will (miracles). Opposed by Ibn Rushd (Averroes), following Aristotle: things possess real specific natures, essences, and active/passive powers from which their characteristic acts follow; observed regularities reflect genuine secondary causation sustained by a First Cause, not arbitrary divine intervention at every instant.

## Ghazali / Ash‘arite Occasionalism (Natural Sciences, First Discussion)

- **Core Denial**: The connection between what is believed to be a cause and its effect is not necessary. Each thing has its own individuality. Neither the existence nor non-existence of one is implied by the other. Satisfaction does not *imply* drinking, burning does not *imply* contact with fire, etc.
- **Example (Cotton and Fire)**: It is possible (in God’s power) for cotton to contact fire without burning, or to turn to ash without contact. The agent of burning is God (directly or via angels), who creates the blackness and separation of parts. Fire is a “dead body” with no real action.
- **Epistemological Ground**: Observation shows only simultaneity/concomitance (“habit”), never causation. We infer causation only because we do not see the true (divine) cause.
- **Broader Implications**:
  - No real secondary causes or “natures” in things.
  - All events are fresh creations by God; the world is re-created moment by moment.
  - Miracles pose no special problem (no fixed nature is violated).
  - Human acts are likewise not truly ours in the causal sense (God creates them).
  - Leads to the famous “scandalous” consequences Ghazali himself raises and tries to mitigate: at any moment a book could turn into a horse, a man into a dog, etc., consistent with God’s absolute power. Ghazali’s defense: God has created in us the knowledge that He will not do these things except at special times (prophetic miracles); habit fixes expectation.

Ghazali concedes in later replies that things can have qualities/dispositions, but still insists these need not produce their usual effects without God’s concurrent creation of the effect.

## Averroes’ (Aristotelian) Defense of Natural Causation

- **Denial of Causation = Sophistry and Destruction of Intellect**: Whoever denies observed efficient causes in sensible things either denies with his tongue what he knows in his mind or is misled by sophistry. If there are no causes, there is no agent for any act, no intellect (which perceives things *with their causes*), no logic, no definitions, no essences, no science, and ultimately no knowledge — only opinion.
- **Real Natures and Four Causes**: Things have specific natures/essences/attributes that determine their special functions and differentiate them (otherwise everything would be one, or nothing). All events have four causes (agent, form, matter, end) — necessary for the effect. The theologians themselves recognize conditions, signs, and “psychological qualities” when it suits them.
- **Habit and Nature**: “Habit” is ambiguous. If in the agent (God), it contradicts the Qur’anic “no alteration in the course of God.” If in things, it *is* nature. If only in our judgments, it is still an act of the soul determined by its nature. The philosophers locate the regularity in the existents themselves (nature) while affirming an immaterial First Agent whose act is the condition for the existence and operation of secondary causes.
- **Miracles and Impediments**: It is possible for fire to be brought near cotton without burning if an external impediment (e.g., talc) is present — this does not deny fire’s power to burn when conditions are normal. Miracles can occur through God (or angels) conferring a special quality on the fire (confining its heat) or on the body (resistance), or through rapid transformations of matter that are possible in principle but unusual in habit. The philosophers do not deny the possibility of marvels; they deny that they require the total abolition of causation.
- **On Abraham in the Fire**: Only heretical Muslims claim such things are impossible for God. The learned philosophers do not publicly dispute religious principles; they are obligatory for moral and social order. Religious truths surpass full human understanding but must be acknowledged.
- **Knowledge and Wisdom**: True knowledge is of a thing as it is in reality. If opposites are always in perfect equilibrium with no preponderating nature or cause, there can be no permanent knowledge of anything. The eternal knowledge and will of God are the ultimate causes of the natures in existents; our knowledge tracks those natures.

Averroes insists that the apparent action of things on each other (preparing dispositions) is real, even if the bestowal of substantial forms ultimately traces to higher principles (active intellect / bestower of forms / heavenly souls). He rejects the idea that all causation is illusory projection or pure sequence (Hume-like).

## Relation to Aquinas and the Vault’s Synthesis

Aquinas repeatedly refutes Averroes (and Avicenna) on the *soul and intellect* (unicity, individuation, active intellect as power of the soul rather than separate), but on **causation and nature** he is far closer to the Aristotelian–Averroist side than to Ghazali:

- **Natural Law (I-II, QQ. 90ff)**: Eternal law (God’s governance) → natural law as the rational creature’s *participation* via “the light of natural reason whereby we discern what is good and what is evil.” First principle: “good is to be done and pursued, and evil avoided.” This presupposes stable, knowable natures in things and real secondary causation. Human law derives from it for the common good.
- **Grace Perfects Nature**: Foundational maxim. If there were no real nature or causal powers, there would be nothing for grace to perfect.
- **Hylomorphism and Soul (Part I, QQ. 75-76)**: The soul is the substantial *form* of the body (Aristotelian). This grounds personal identity (“Socrates understands”), individuation, and the possibility of genuine knowledge by abstraction from phantasms. Directly anti-Averroist on the intellect, but the underlying commitment to real forms and causal powers in nature aligns against occasionalism.
- **Last End / Beatific Vision**: The composite human being (body + soul) is the subject; natural happiness (imperfect contemplation / virtuous activity) is real but incomplete; elevated by grace to supernatural vision. All of this requires a stable natural order.

The vault’s existing Aquinas pages already note the anti-Averroist polemics on the soul; the *Tahafut* now supplies the primary text being engaged and shows why the stakes (personal immortality, moral responsibility, possibility of science and natural ethics) were so high.

## Broader Vault Connections and Methodological Value

- **Epistemology**: Occasionalism threatens the very conditions of intellect and demonstration that the wiki’s reasoning scaffolds, agentic workflows, and cross-model routing rely upon. Averroes’ defense (“denial of causes implies denial of intellect”) is a powerful ancient statement of why reliable knowledge requires real regularities and causal structure in the world.
- **Contrasts with Other Traditions**:
  - Stoic determinism / providence (Cleanthes’ hymn appears across Epictetus, Seneca, Marcus) vs. radical contingency of occasionalism.
  - Epicurean / Lucretian atomism: blind collisions + swerve (no divine micromanagement, but also no final causes or natural law in the teleological sense).
  - Pyrrhonian skepticism: equipollence on causes (God as cause is “inconceivable or impious”; total disagreement on material sources) leads to *epoché* rather than resolution in either naturalism or voluntarism.
  - Platonic / Neoplatonic: demiurge or emanation still involves structured procession and return, not pure arbitrary re-creation.
- **Faith, Reason, and the Masses**: Ghazali uses occasionalism to safeguard absolute divine power and the possibility of miracles/prophecy. Averroes protects demonstrative philosophy while conceding religion’s indispensable role for mass virtue. Echoes the vault’s own distinction between the disciplined “few” (GROK schema, precise ingest/lint) and the broader utility of the resulting wiki for the user.
- **Modern Parallels**: Humean constant conjunction (no necessary connection) has occasionalist echoes. Scientific realism, natural kinds, and causal powers in contemporary metaphysics often side with the Aristotelian–Averroist intuition. AI/agent reliability assumes stable environmental regularities that can be modeled.

## Open Questions

- Can a consistent occasionalist account of “habit,” prophecy, and moral responsibility be given without smuggling back real natures or conditions?
- How much “nature” can be conceded (as Ghazali does in replies) before occasionalism collapses into a more Aristotelian concurrentism?
- Does the Aristotelian–Aquinian synthesis successfully preserve both real secondary causation *and* genuine divine sovereignty and freedom?
- Relevance for the LLM Wiki: the “nature” of sources, tools, and reasoning processes must be respected (not treated as infinitely malleable by prompt “will”) if compounding knowledge is to be possible.
