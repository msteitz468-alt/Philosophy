# Three Views Concerning Human Knowledge (Popper)

> From *Conjectures and Refutations* ch. 3. Popper distinguishes three accounts of what a scientific theory *is* and rejects the first two in favour of the third: scientific **realism / conjecturalism** — theories are genuine, fallible conjectures about the structure of reality, true or false.

## The Three Views

**(1) Essentialism — ultimate explanation by essences** (Aristotle; the "Galilean" view as later distorted). Science aims at *final*, ultimate explanations that reveal the **essences** or true natures of things; a good theory describes the essence behind appearances and stands in no further need of, or capacity for, explanation. Popper rejects this as **obscurantist**: by treating a theory (e.g. Newton's gravitation) as an ultimate essential truth it *forbids further questions* ("What is the cause of gravity? Can we explain Newton's law by a deeper, independently testable theory?"). Essentialism mistakes definitions for factual knowledge; definitions never tell us about "the nature of things." Science neither reaches nor needs ultimate essences.

**(2) Instrumentalism — theories as instruments** (Berkeley, Mach, Hertz, Duhem, Poincaré, Bridgman, Eddington; the Copenhagen/operationalist turn). Theories are mere **calculating tools** for deriving predictions from observations — neither true nor false, only convenient or inconvenient. This appears to dissolve the problem of how theories can be certain (they make no claims) and is attractive via Ockham's razor. Popper's **criticism**: instrumentalism cannot account for the difference between (a) predicting events of a *known kind* (eclipses, storms) and (b) the prediction of **new kinds of events** — *new effects* such as radio waves or new elements — which is the hallmark of a *discovery* about reality, not the working of a tool. Instrumentalism also cannot explain why theories are *refuted*: a mere instrument is never "false," only more or less handy, so the central event of scientific growth (refutation) becomes unintelligible. Tools are used within a theory but do not replace the theory's claim to describe the world.

**(3) Conjecturalism / Critical Realism — conjectures, truth, and reality** (Popper's own view). Theories are **genuine conjectures about the real world** — bold guesses that are true or false (corresponding or failing to correspond to the facts), aiming at ever better **structural** descriptions and at growing [[Concepts/Verisimilitude (Truthlikeness) (Popper)|verisimilitude]]. We can never *know* a theory is true, and our best theories are probably false in some respect, but they are real attempts to grasp reality, testable by their consequences and improvable by refutation. Science *discovers* (e.g. predicts genuinely new effects); it does not merely systematize observations or compute. This is a **realism** about the aim and content of theories combined with **fallibilism** about their attainment.

## Why It Matters

- Conjecturalism is the metaphysical complement of [[Concepts/Falsifiability as Criterion of Demarcation (Popper)|falsifiability]] and [[Concepts/Corroboration (Popper)|corroboration]]: only if theories *make claims about reality* can they be refuted, corroborated, or come nearer the truth. Instrumentalism would make falsification meaningless.
- It rescues the **realist, explanatory ambition** of science without the dogmatism of essentialism. Science can aim at truth and deep structure while never claiming final or essential knowledge.

## Relation to Other Vault Content

- **Vs. Aristotelian essentialism**: directly opposes the view in [[Concepts/Modal Copula (Aristotelian Modality)]] and [[Sources/Aristotle - Modal Logic (Organon, Patterson)]] that definitions state real essences grounding demonstration; connects to Locke's distinction in [[Concepts/Nominal and Real Essences (Locke)]] (Popper, like Locke, denies we grasp real essences — but goes further: science does not seek them at all).

- **Vs. Berkeley/Mach instrumentalism**: see Popper's "A Note on Berkeley as Precursor of Mach and Einstein" (ch. 6). Berkeley's [[Concepts/Primary and Secondary Qualities (Locke)|critique of matter]] and *esse est percipi* feed instrumentalism; Popper credits Berkeley's acuity while rejecting the anti-realist conclusion.

- **Truth**: conjecturalism presupposes the objective [[Concepts/Correspondence Theory of Truth (Russell)|correspondence theory of truth]] (Tarski), against the [[Concepts/Pragmatic Theory of Truth (James)|pragmatic theory]] — and indeed pragmatism/operationalism is a close cousin of instrumentalism that Popper opposes.

- **With Descartes/Spinoza**: shares their realism about an external, intelligible world while rejecting the rationalist claim to certain, intuitive ([[Concepts/Clear and Distinct Perception (Descartes)|clear and distinct]]) knowledge of it.
