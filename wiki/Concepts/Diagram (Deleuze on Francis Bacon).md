# Diagram (Deleuze on Francis Bacon)
> The diagram is the set of asignifying, involuntary marks that interrupt figurative data on a canvas — a localized chaos that opens "possibilities of fact" and enables the Figure to emerge from figuration.

## Summary

In *Francis Bacon: The Logic of Sensation* (1981), Deleuze develops a philosophy of painting via Bacon. Against both figuration (painting that illustrates or narrates) and pure abstraction (that eliminates sensation in favor of pure optical code), Deleuze identifies a **third path**: the **Figure** — a body distorted, deformed, vibrating with force — produced by the diagram.

**Sensation vs. figuration:** Sensation is neither the imitation of an object nor pure abstraction — it is the direct action of form on the nervous system. "Sensation is in the body, even if this is the body of an apple." Sensation is multi-level and synthetic, traversing multiple sensory registers through the underlying force of rhythm. The Figure embodies sensation; figuration illustrates something to be recognized.

**The diagram:** Every painter begins with figurative givens (clichés, photos, memories, perceptions) already filling the canvas before the first brushstroke. The painter's problem is to break through these givens. The diagram is the set of involuntary, asignifying marks — catastrophic brushstrokes, daubs, erasures — that interrupt the figurative data and open a "chaosmos" on the canvas. "The diagram is indeed a chaos, a catastrophe, but also a seed of order and of rhythm. It is a violent chaos in relation to the figurative data, but it is a seed of rhythm in relation to the new order of painting."

**Three paths from the diagram:**
1. **Abstraction** (Kandinsky, Mondrian): minimizes chaos, translates sensation into a pure optical code. Evacuates the Figure.
2. **Abstract Expressionism** (Pollock): the diagram engulfs the whole canvas — pure manual action, pure chaos. The Figure drowns.
3. **Bacon's path**: the diagram is **localized** — it interrupts only part of the canvas, leaving contours intact, enabling the Figure to emerge from chaos while retaining a recognizable body. "Between the involuntary made marks and the voluntary made marks there is a permanent interaction."

**The ritornello (Essay 24):** A parallel concept in music: the refrain (ritornello) is analyzed as having three simultaneous aspects: (1) a fragile point fixed against chaos (a child humming in the dark); (2) organization of a stable territory around that center; (3) opening outward toward cosmic forces. All three aspects are one — territorial assemblage — corresponding to the movement from chaos through territory to cosmos.

## Key Claims

- Sensation acts on the nervous system directly, without passing through the mind or representation.
- "The diagram is a chaos, a catastrophe, but also a seed of order and of rhythm."
- Painting's problem: how to break through clichés already occupying the canvas before you begin.
