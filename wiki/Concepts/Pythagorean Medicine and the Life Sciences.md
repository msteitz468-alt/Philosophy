# Pythagorean Medicine and the Life Sciences
> The rational, secular tradition of medicine, physiology, embryology, and botany among the early Pythagoreans — for [[Sources/Pythagoras and the Early Pythagoreans - Leonid Zhmud (2012)|Zhmud]], the clearest proof that early Pythagoreanism was a movement of working scientists, not only of mystics and ritualists.

## Summary
Croton was the foremost medical centre of the Greek world (Herodotus III.131), and on Zhmud's account medicine was *central* to early Pythagoreanism — "*medicina soror philosophiae*." The direction of influence runs from medicine *into* philosophy: the physicians' theories of balance, inference, and the body supplied early Pythagorean thought with some of its most durable ideas. This whole stratum is largely absent from [[Sources/Lore and Science in Ancient Pythagoreanism - Walter Burkert (1972)|Burkert]], whose Pythagoras is a religious figure; it is one of the main fronts on which Zhmud expands [[Contradictions/Pythagoras the Scientist vs the Religious Charismatic]].

## The Pythagorean Physicians
- **[[Thinkers/Alcmaeon of Croton]]** — the founder of the "physiological" turn: health as *isonomia* (balance of opposed *dynameis*), disease as *monarchia*; the brain as seat of cognition; the inference-programme (*tekmairesthai*, B1); probable dissection and the discovery of the optic nerves; embryology.
- **Democedes of Croton** — the most celebrated physician of his age (court of Polycrates, then Darius).
- **Iccus of Tarentum** — pioneer of *dietetics* and athletic training regimes.
- **Hippon of Samos** — extended Alcmaeon's embryology (semen from the spinal marrow, with an "experiment").
- **Menestor of Sybaris** — wrote the first known work on **botany** (plant life, with medical interest).
- **[[Thinkers/Philolaus]]** — also wrote on physiology (reason in the brain, life/sensation in the heart).

## Against the "Magic-and-Medicine" Reading
Zhmud explicitly rejects Burkert's "primeval unity of magic and medicine" (Empedocles as shaman-healer; the bean-taboo as proto-dietetics). On Zhmud's view Crotonian medicine was **rational and secular from the start**, descended from the practical Asclepiad army-physicians, not from soothsayers; the bean prohibition is irrelevant to Hippocratic diet. The life sciences are thus a counter-example to the claim that the early Pythagorean "science" is really disguised religion.

## Relevance to the Vault
- Supplies the empirical, experimental face of early Pythagoreanism (Alcmaeon's dissection, Hippon's and Iccus' experiments) alongside the mathematical face (see [[Thinkers/Hippasus]], [[Concepts/Number as the Essence of Reality (Pythagoras)]]).
- *Isonomia* feeds the later "soul as attunement/harmony" doctrine debated in the *Phaedo* — a medical idea, not a cosmological one.
