# The Idea Trap (Caplan)
> Economic shocks reduce voter economic literacy, which increases demand for bad policies, which further harms economic performance — a self-reinforcing downward spiral.

## Summary

The Idea Trap is Caplan's concept for a negative-feedback loop in which economic distress and irrational voter beliefs mutually reinforce each other. In *The Myth of the Rational Voter* (2007), Caplan uses it to explain why some democracies — particularly developing ones — can remain stuck in bad policy equilibria for extended periods.

## The Mechanism

1. **Economic shock** (recession, commodity price collapse, external crisis) reduces income, employment, and economic security for the median voter.
2. **Voter economic literacy falls** under stress: people have less time and cognitive bandwidth for dispassionate analysis; their [[Concepts/Systematic Economic Biases (Caplan)]] intensify as the emotional need for simple explanations grows.
3. **Bad policy demand rises**: less economically literate voters have stronger demand for protectionism, price controls, job-protection regulations, and scapegoating (of foreigners, corporations, "speculators").
4. **Politicians supply bad policies**: democratic governments responsive to voter demand implement the demanded policies.
5. **Economic performance worsens**: the bad policies reduce growth, investment, and dynamism, perpetuating or deepening the original shock.
6. **Cycle repeats**: continued poor performance feeds continued demand for bad policies.

## Why It's a "Trap"

Normal market logic suggests that bad policies correct themselves — the feedback of poor outcomes teaches voters to abandon them. The Idea Trap explains why this correction may not occur in politics:
- Voters attribute bad outcomes to the wrong causes (blaming foreigners, corporations, markets) rather than to the policies themselves.
- The very distress that should trigger policy revision instead triggers deeper demand for the policies causing the distress.
- There is no political profit in teaching voters that their preferred policies are the cause of their suffering.

## Relationship to Olson's Institutional Sclerosis

The Idea Trap is an epistemological mechanism where [[Thinkers/Mancur Olson]]'s [[Concepts/Institutional Sclerosis (Olson)]] is an organizational one. Both explain why stable bad equilibria persist: Olson through the accumulation of distributional coalitions that block reform, Caplan through the self-reinforcing cycle of economic distress and irrational belief. The two mechanisms can coexist and reinforce each other.
