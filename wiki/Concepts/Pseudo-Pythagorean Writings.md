# Pseudo-Pythagorean Writings
> The vast body of forged treatises composed under the names of Pythagoras and early Pythagoreans, mostly in artificial Doric — the literary form in which "Pythagoreanism" survived its own extinction, and a major hidden source of later Platonism.

## Summary
On Bruno Centrone's chapter in [[Sources/A History of Pythagoreanism - Carl A. Huffman (ed.) (2014)]], after the school's fourth-century demise Pythagoreanism survived mainly as *cultural interest*, evidenced by an apocryphal literature that "far outnumbers the genuine early fragments." There is no single origin or date (Centrone rejects Zeller's unified theory). A key driver was the **Academy's belief in a deep continuity between Pythagoreanism and Platonism**, which led Academics and their heirs to **project their own doctrines back onto Pythagoras** (see [[Schools/Neopythagoreanism]] and [[Sources/Aristotle - Metaphysics]] on the One/Indefinite Dyad as Platonic).

## Two Bodies of Texts
1. **Under "Pythagoras" / his family** — scanty and heterogeneous: the *Tripartitum*, the *Hypomnemata* ("Pythagorean Notes"), the *Hieros Logos*, the *Golden Verses*.
2. **Doric treatises under other Pythagoreans' names** — a homogeneous corpus in artificial Doric (ps.-Archytas, ps.-Timaeus, ps.-Ocellus, ps.-Ecphantus, ps.-Diotogenes, and women authors Phyntis, Periktione). On these see also [[Thinkers/Archytas]] (the spurious *On the Whole System / Categories* and *On Law and Justice*).

## The Doric "System"
The Doric treatises form a coherent doctrine: reality as nested ***systamata*** (world, city, family, individual, soul) governed by the rule that "**the better ought to rule, the worse obey**," with a microcosm–macrocosm analogy and a musical-harmonization metaphor. Their **doctrine of principles is Academic in origin**: two opposed *systoichiai* (rows of opposites) *plus a third, supreme God above the pair* — the monistic turn that parallels **Eudorus of Alexandria**, blending Platonic hylomorphism with Aristotelian *Categories*.

> "There are two principles of reality, the one containing the series [*systoichia*] of ordered and determined things, the other containing the series of unordered and undetermined things." (ps.-Archytas)

## Politics and the Jewish Parallel
- **Politics**: kingship as *imitation of God*; the king as ***nomos empsychos*** ("living law") — ps.-Diotogenes. The ideal is monarchy, with the mixed constitution a "second-best."
- **Theology/ethics** show a Plato–Aristotle fusion plus striking parallels with **Philo** and Jewish tradition (the bodyguard metaphor, God molding man, divine breath); ethics upholds *metriopatheia* against Stoic *apatheia*.

Centrone's verdict: the authors are "**Pythagoreanizing Platonists**" who believed in the continuity of Pythagoras, Plato, and Aristotle; the most likely date is the **1st c. BC – 1st c. AD**, close to the Alexandrian milieu of Eudorus and Philo. Notably, [[Concepts/Metempsychosis (Transmigration of Souls)|metempsychosis]] — the doctrine most securely Pythagorean — is *absent* from these treatises, confirming that they reflect later school philosophy, not the archaic religion.
