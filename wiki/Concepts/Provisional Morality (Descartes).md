# Provisional Morality (Descartes)

> The "provisory code of morals" of three or four maxims Descartes adopts in Part III of the *Discourse* so that he "might not remain irresolute" in action while his reason is busy demolishing and rebuilding his opinions. A house being rebuilt still needs somewhere to live: while judgment is suspended in theory, life must go on in practice.

## The Maxims

1. **Conform to custom and religion.** Obey the laws and customs of his country, hold firmly to the faith he was raised in, and in everything else follow "the most moderate opinions, and the farthest removed from extremes" held by the most judicious of those he lives among — judging people by what they *practice* rather than what they *say*. (He notes that the act of believing differs from knowing that one believes, so many are unaware of what they really believe.) He especially avoids binding promises that curtail future freedom, since he hopes to improve his judgment over time.

2. **Be firm and resolute in action.** Once a course is adopted — even on doubtful opinions — follow it steadfastly, as travelers lost in a forest should walk straight in one direction rather than wander. In action, where delay is not permitted, one must act on the *most probable*; and having chosen, treat the choice as certain. This frees one from the "repentings and pangs of remorse" that torment "feeble and uncertain minds."

3. **Master yourself rather than fortune.** "Endeavor always to conquer myself rather than fortune, and change my desires rather than the order of the world" — accustoming himself to the persuasion that "except our own thoughts, there is nothing absolutely in our power." When we have done our best with external things, what we fail to obtain is, for us, simply impossible; so we should no more regret lacking it than lacking "the kingdoms of China or Mexico." He explicitly credits this to "such philosophers as in former times were enabled to rise superior to the influence of fortune" — i.e. the Stoics.

The "fourth," concluding the code, is less a maxim than a resolution: to review the occupations of men and devote his own life to cultivating reason and seeking truth by his method.

## The Stoic Core

The third maxim is one of the vault's clearest rationalist restatements of the Stoic [[Concepts/Dichotomy of Control (Epictetus)]]: the sharp division between what is "in our power" (only our own thoughts / assent / desires) and what is not (the order of the world, fortune, externals), with the therapeutic conclusion that we should govern desire rather than fight the world. Descartes's "make a virtue of necessity" and "conquer myself rather than fortune" track Epictetus's *ta eph' hēmin* and Marcus's inner citadel closely. Compare [[Thinkers/Epictetus]], [[Thinkers/Marcus Aurelius]], [[Thinkers/Seneca]].

The crucial difference is *foundation and status*: for the Stoics this is the whole of ethics, grounded in the rational structure of nature (logos); for Descartes it is explicitly **provisional** — a temporary scaffold held only until the method yields a definitive morality grounded in metaphysics (which he never fully delivered, though the later *Passions of the Soul* and the correspondence with Princess Elizabeth move toward it). Where Stoic control of assent is the destination, Cartesian control of assent is a placeholder that keeps life livable during the epistemic demolition.

## Why "Provisional"

The morality is deliberately conservative (obey custom, avoid extremes, keep resolutions) precisely *because* Descartes's theoretical project is radical. Hyperbolic doubt applies to *knowledge*, not conduct: "no danger or error will ensue" from doubting in theory, but in action one cannot suspend judgment. The provisional code insulates practical life from the corrosive doubt turned loose on belief — a division of labor between the demolition site (theory) and the temporary dwelling (practice).

## Relevance to Vault

A model for acting under uncertainty while a knowledge base is being rebuilt: maxim 2 (act on the most probable, then commit) is exactly the posture of making a defensible editorial call during ingest rather than freezing; maxim 1 (follow the moderate consensus of the judicious, judge by practice not profession) resembles deferring to established schema conventions and observed exemplars over stated intentions; maxim 3 (govern what is in your power — the reasoning step, the page written — not outcomes) is the agentic analogue of the dichotomy of control. The whole device — a stable provisional layer so radical revision elsewhere doesn't paralyze action — mirrors keeping the wiki usable while any one section is under reconstruction.
