# Incommensurability (Kuhn)

> **Incommensurability** is the thesis that successive paradigms (pre- and post-revolution) cannot be strictly compared by a common measure: their proponents disagree about standards and about which problems matter, the *meanings* of shared terms change, and they "in a sense… practice their trades in different worlds." Hence paradigm choice "cannot be made a step at a time, forced by logic and neutral experience" — but, Kuhn insists, this does **not** make science irrational or paradigms simply incomparable.

## The Three Aspects (Kuhn, §XII)

Kuhn distinguishes three ways rival paradigms "fail to make complete contact":

1. **Incommensurability of standards / problems**: rivals "disagree about the list of problems that any candidate for paradigm must resolve" and about what counts as a legitimate problem or a good solution. (Newton's dynamics *banished* the question "what causes the attractive forces?" that Aristotle and Descartes demanded answered — a question general relativity later "proudly claim[ed] to have solved." Lavoisier's chemistry made the once-answerable "why are the metals alike?" illegitimate.) So you cannot settle the contest just by "counting the number of problems solved by each."

2. **Meaning change (semantic incommensurability)**: new paradigms reuse old vocabulary in new relationships. The classic example is **mass** — central to both Newton (*f = ma*) and Einstein (*E = mc²*), but Einsteinian mass "makes no sense in classical mechanics." Likewise '**earth**' meant *fixed position* to Copernicus's critics; '**space**' meant *flat, homogeneous, unaffected by matter* before general relativity. "To make the transition to Einstein's universe, the whole conceptual web… had to be shifted and laid down again on nature whole." So a sentence true in one paradigm and false in another may not be a straight contradiction — the words have changed meaning. Communication across the divide is "**inevitably partial**."

3. **"Different worlds" (perceptual/world incommensurability)**: "the proponents of competing paradigms practice their trades in different worlds… [they] see different things when they look from the same point in the same direction." This is tied to the [[Concepts/Scientific Revolutions and Paradigm Shifts (Kuhn)|gestalt switch]] and to theory-ladenness ("there can be no scientifically or empirically neutral system of language or concepts"). It is the most metaphysical and most contested aspect (Kuhn: "in a sense that I am unable to explicate further").

## Not Relativism, Not Irrationality

Kuhn was read (and attacked, and celebrated) as denying the rationality of science or as "the prophet of the new relativism." He rejected both:

- **Shared values of theory choice** persist across paradigms: theories should be **accurate, consistent, broad in scope, simple, and fruitful**. These are real, communal criteria of rational choice — but they are *values that require judgment*, not an algorithm that mechanically dictates a unique winner (different scientists weigh them differently; "an individual's transfer of allegiance… is often better described as conversion than as choice").
- Incommensurability ≠ incomparability: rivals can still "compare technical results"; "the exhibit of impressive concrete results will persuade at least a few." Newtonian mechanics is even taught as a limiting case of relativity.
- **[[Thinkers/Paul Feyerabend]]** (with whom Kuhn jointly "put the word incommensurable on the table" at Berkeley) developed it independently — and more radically — in [[Sources/Against Method - Paul Feyerabend|*Against Method*]] (now primary-sourced): for Feyerabend incommensurability reaches beyond theory into perception, [[Concepts/Natural Interpretations and the Galileo Case (Feyerabend)|"natural interpretations"]], child development, and art-historical styles, and it feeds his [[Concepts/Epistemological Anarchism (Feyerabend)|"anything goes"]]. Hacking argues Feyerabend's flamboyance, more than Kuhn's text, fueled the relativism furor. Kuhn pursued incommensurability to the end of his life via a theory of scientific **language** (a "logical syntax of the language of science").
- **Ian Hacking** (*Representing and Intervening*, 1983; also intro to this edition of *Structure*): treats (especially meaning-)incommensurability as a major source of pressure toward irrealism about *representations* and theory-comparison. Rather than resolving the issue at the level of paradigms and meanings, Hacking pivots the realism question to experimental *intervention*: even if representations are incommensurable, the ability to use entities as tools to create and observe stable phenomena across setups provides a more direct ground for entity realism. See [[Sources/Representing and Intervening - Ian Hacking (1983)]], [[Concepts/Entity Realism vs Theory Realism (Hacking)]], and [[Concepts/If You Can Spray Them Then They Are Real (Hacking)]]. This does not refute incommensurability but relocates where realism can be secured.
- **Productive sense**: Kuhn later found incommensurability illuminating for **specialization** — like Darwinian *speciation*, sciences branch into subdisciplines that become "to some extent mutually incomprehensible" (bridged by Galison-style "**trading zones**"). "Just as new species… do not interbreed, so new disciplines [are] to some extent mutually incomprehensible."

## Relation to Other Vault Content

- **Vs. Popper**: incommensurability undercuts Popper's picture of rival theories competing on a shared logical/empirical field where one is decisively falsified or shown more truthlike. If standards and meanings shift, there is no neutral arbiter — a direct challenge to [[Concepts/Falsifiability as Criterion of Demarcation (Popper)]] and [[Concepts/Verisimilitude (Truthlikeness) (Popper)]]. See [[Contradictions/Popper vs Kuhn - Falsification vs Paradigms]].
- **Theory-ladenness** is shared ground with Popper ([[Concepts/Basic Statements and the Empirical Basis (Popper)]]) against the positivist neutral "given."
- **Meaning-change** connects to philosophy of language: Locke's [[Concepts/Words as Signs of Ideas and Language (Locke)|words as signs of ideas]] and the realist [[Concepts/Correspondence Theory of Truth (Russell)|correspondence theory]] (if meanings shift with theory, cross-paradigm "correspondence" is harder to state).
- **Perspectivism**: resonates with [[Concepts/Perspectivism (Nietzsche)]] and the constructivist pole of [[Concepts/Pragmatic Theory of Truth (James)]] — but Kuhn keeps objective shared values and resists the relativist reading.
