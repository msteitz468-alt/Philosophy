# Kardashev Scale (Kaku)

> Michio Kaku's (2011) use of Nikolai Kardashev's 1964 quantitative classification of civilizations by energy consumption as the lens for humanity's destiny by 2100 and beyond. Type I: planetary (consume sliver of sunlight on planet ~10^17 watts; control weather, harness hurricanes, cities on oceans). Type II: stellar (consume all star output ~10^27 watts; Dyson sphere, colonize ~100 nearby stars, immortal vs. supernovae/ice ages/meteors). Type III: galactic (consume billions of stars ~10^37 watts; von Neumann self-replicating probes map Milky Way in 100k years at near-c). Current: Type 0.7 (fossil "dead plants"/oil/coal; Carl Sagan generalization). Transition Type 0 → I ~100 years at 1–2% GDP growth (or 2,500 years at 1%, 1,200 at 2%). "A Day in the Life in 2100" as lived Type I (mind interfaces, nanotech abundance, democracy spread, diseases planetary, internet as Type I phone). Resistance from terrorists/dictatorships preferring pre-modern theocracy/ignorance. Physics (4 forces, thermodynamics) as objective basis for ranking, unlike historian views (kings/queens, social movements).

## Summary (from the source)

Kaku (theoretical physicist, string theory co-founder) ranks civilizations quantitatively by energy, following Kardashev (who sought objective way to classify ET signals for SETI; culture/government vary but all obey physics; observable from earth: energy use). Each type separated by 10 billion (galaxy has ~10B+ stars).

- **Type 0 (now)**: Get energy from dead plants (oil, coal, wood). Nomadic/hunter-gatherer ancestors: 1/5 horsepower (bare hands), life expectancy 18–20, no written records, total wealth what you carry. Ice Age end ~10k years ago enabled agriculture (domesticate horses/oxen → 1 hp, surplus for cities, math/writing/calendars, armies/empires/slavery). Industrial Revolution ~300 years ago: steam/internal combustion → 100s hp, factories/mills/mines, working class, life expectancy rising (49 by 1900 US). Information age: electrons on fiber/satellites (Wall Street etc.). Carl Sagan: we are ~Type 0.7.
- **Type I (planetary, ~100 years)**: Consume planetary sunlight sliver (~10^17 W). Control/modify weather at will, harness hurricanes, ocean cities. Internet as beginning of Type I planetary telephone (cross-continent unlimited info; people feel more in common with distant strangers). Democracy spread (wars mostly between nondemocracies or democracy vs. nondemocracy; vibrant press/opposition/middle class with everything to lose makes war fever hard; smaller families mean no surplus children for military/priesthood). Diseases controlled planetary (H1N1 example: sequenced/vaccine in months via jet travel; ancient Ebola limited by low population). Nations weaken (economic power to regional trade blocs/global) but persist for local laws/taxes. Terrorism/dictatorships resist (prefer 11th century theocracy or keep people ignorant of outside wealth/progress; "chip mightier than sword"; North Korea example). "A Day in the Life in 2100": mind-over-matter BCI, ubiquitous sensors/nanotech/replicators/robots, abundance, health monitoring, entertainment, work/leisure shift.
- **Type II (stellar, thousands of years)**: Consume all star output (~10^27 W). Dyson sphere (gigantic shell around star absorbing all sunlight). Immortal: master weather (avoid ice ages), deflect meteors/comets, flee or prevent sun supernova (e.g., swing asteroids to move planet outward if red giant). Colonize ~100s light-years (still speed-of-light limited). At peace (space travel hard; centuries as Type I to resolve internal divisions).
- **Type III (galactic, tens of thousands+ years)**: Consume billions of stars (~10^37 W). Explore most of galaxy via self-replicating von Neumann probes (land on moon, make factory from lunar dirt, copy thousands, each copies thousands more; exponential sphere at near-c maps Milky Way in 100k years; universe 13.7B years old, plenty of time for rise/fall). Type IV (extragalactic): dark energy (73% universe; stars/galaxies only 4%).
- **Transition math**: 1% annual GDP growth → ~2,500 years per type jump; 2% → 1,200 years. Type 0→I in ~100 years realistic. Evidence of birth pangs: internet (Type I phone), globalization, democracy spread, H1N1 planetary response.

Kaku's string theory/cosmology background informs (Planck energy 10^19 GeV for space-time portals/wormholes in Type III; dark energy Type IV). Thermodynamics (1st: no free lunch; 2nd: entropy/disorder always increases — all things rot/decay/rust/age; advanced civ manages but can't escape) constrain even Type III.

## Key Claims / How Different Thinkers Use This

- **Kaku**: Energy ranking objective (physics laws universal; observable from earth). Type I inevitable unless natural catastrophe or "calamitous act of folly." "People living today most important ever" (5k generations since Africa ~100k ya; determine trajectory). Science (not kings/queens/folly) drives history (Verne/Leonardo consulted scientists but lacked 4 forces/quantum). Optimistic abundance (replicators, robots, mind interfaces) with cautions (resistance, entropy, AI limits from Ch2).
- **Kelly (forces driving transition)**: 12 forces (Cognifying, Becoming, Sharing, Accessing, etc.) as the "wants"/biases of the technium (seventh kingdom, exotropy, convergence) accelerating energy/tech to Type I planetary (internet as Type I comms, democracy/wealth flux, cognifying intelligence everywhere). "Inevitable" momentum from bits/networks/AI matches Kaku's 1–2% growth to Type I ~100 years. Cross to [[Sources/The Inevitable - Kevin Kelly (2016)]], [[Concepts/Cognifying (Kelly)]], [[Concepts/Becoming (Kelly)]], [[Concepts/Technium (Kelly)]] (technium as engine of Kardashev becoming).
- **Kurzweil (acceleration to Type I)**: Moore's law/exponential (Kaku notes slowing post-2020–25 post-silicon but assumes continued) + GNR revolutions as path to planetary abundance. Singularity ~2045 (merger, nonbio > human) overlaps Type I transition but Kaku more measured (planetary civ, no full rupture/immortality emphasis). Cross to [[Sources/The Singularity Is Near - Ray Kurzweil (2005)]], [[Concepts/Singularity (Kurzweil)]], [[Concepts/Law of Accelerating Returns (Kurzweil)]].
- **Bostrom (risks in transition)**: AI chapter (Asilomar, "rapture of nerds," superintelligence explosion potential) + Type III von Neumann probes align with orthogonality (arbitrary goals), instrumental convergence (self-preservation/resources for most goals), control problem. Kaku near-term narrow AI + cautious consciousness; Bostrom long-term. Cross to [[Sources/Superintelligence - Nick Bostrom (2014)]], [[Concepts/Superintelligence (Bostrom)]], [[Concepts/Orthogonality Thesis (Bostrom)]], [[Concepts/Instrumental Convergence (Bostrom)]].
- **Mitchell (AI limits in Type I rise)**: Ch2 explicit: pattern recognition/common sense unsolved (robots see dots/lines but don't understand; hear but no context; Deep Blue brute force ≠ intelligence; ASIMO "insect IQ" scripted). Matches Mitchell (DL brittleness, no robust common sense/analogy/understanding; hype vs. reality). Cross to [[Sources/Artificial Intelligence - Melanie Mitchell (2019)]], [[Concepts/Understanding vs Pattern Matching in AI (Mitchell)]], [[Concepts/Brittleness of Deep Learning (Mitchell)]].
- **Ford (economic risks en route)**: Kaku long-term Type I abundance (replicators, energy from stars) vs. Ford near-term technological unemployment/demand collapse ("machines do not consume," predictable jobs displacement, basic income as adaptation). Same cognifying tech; different horizons (2100 planetary vs. transition crisis). Cross to [[Sources/Rise of the Robots - Martin Ford (2015)]], [[Concepts/Technological Unemployment (Ford)]], [[Concepts/Basic Income Guarantee (Ford)]].
- **Wiener (cybernetics roots)**: AI/automation (Ch2) updates Wiener Second IR (machines displacing judgment); Kaku optimistic abundance vs. Wiener emphasis on human meaning/control/"know-what." Cross to [[Sources/The Human Use of Human Beings - Norbert Wiener (1950)]], [[Concepts/Second Industrial Revolution (Wiener)]].
- **History of Ideas (static vs. dynamic)**: Kardashev energy/becoming as evolutionary successor to Lovejoy's static Great Chain (finished plenitude/continuity/gradation hierarchy; "all possibles realized"). Science as engine of progress (not social movements) parallels Butterfield Scientific Revolution (mental/"thinking cap" shift, directing role). Cross to [[Sources/The Great Chain of Being - Arthur O. Lovejoy (1936)]], [[Concepts/Great Chain of Being (Lovejoy)]], [[Concepts/Scientific Revolution (Butterfield)]].
- **Mind/consciousness (Hofstadter, Dennett, Turing)**: Brain neural net (Hebb, parallel, distributed, resilient) vs. digital (Turing machine, CPU, subroutines, brittle). Pattern/common sense limits resonate with Hofstadter strange loops/tangled hierarchies, Dennett multiple drafts/no Cartesian Theater. Cross to [[Sources/Gödel, Escher, Bach - Douglas Hofstadter (1979)]], [[Sources/Consciousness Explained - Daniel C. Dennett (1991)]], [[Sources/Computing Machinery and Intelligence - Alan Turing (1950)]].

## Contradictions / Open Questions

- **Optimism (Type I inevitable/abundance) vs. risks (Bostrom, Ford, Mitchell)**: Kaku "inevitable" at 1–2% growth unless folly/catastrophe; Type I controls weather/disease, democracy reduces wars. But AI chapter echoes Bostrom (explosion, outsmarting/manipulating); Ford near-term demand collapse from same tech; Mitchell limits on actual intelligence (pattern/common sense unsolved). Transition costs (inequality, resistance, entropy) vs. long-term. Cross to relevant sources/concepts.
- **Kardashev vs. static Chain/Lovejoy**: Dynamic energy ranking (Type 0→I→II→III, von Neumann exponential) vs. finished, hierarchical, plenitudinous scala (no gaps, all possibles realized). Kaku "becoming" (science drives, entropy managed but inevitable) overthrows static presuppositions. Cross to Lovejoy/Butterfield.
- **Timelines/acceleration (Kurzweil/Kelly vs. Kaku measured)**: Kaku 2100 Type I (Moore slowing noted); Kurzweil 2045 Singularity (merger/1B:1); Kelly forces "inevitable" for decades but specifics choosable. Overlap on cognifying/Becoming as drivers; difference on rupture vs. protopian planetary.
- **Consciousness in Type I/II/III**: Kaku cautious (AI Ch2 limits; neural nets vs. digital; no robot maids yet). Ties to vault debates (Hofstadter/Dennett/Turing/Minsky on mechanisms; Bostrom orthogonality — superintelligence may have arbitrary goals unrelated to human flourishing).
- **Entropy/2nd law ultimate limit**: Even Type III manages but "all things must pass" (rot/decay/rust/age). Ties to Wiener/Shannon entropy (organization vs. uncertainty/disorder); no escape.
- **Resistance vs. progress**: Terrorists/dictatorships (pre-modern theocracy/ignorance) vs. Type I (education, chip > sword, democracy). "People today most important" — agency in transition.
