# Criterion of Demonstrability (Ghazali)

> Al-Ghazali's governing rule of method in the *Tahafut*: a scriptural assertion must be read in its literal sense **unless** its literal sense is shown — by strict demonstration (*burhan*), in the rigorous sense the philosophers themselves define in their logic — to be impossible. Mere plausibility, likelihood, or dialectical argument never licenses metaphorical reinterpretation. The burden of proof falls entirely on the philosophers, and on the contested metaphysical doctrines they fail to meet it.

## The Rule

Metaphorical interpretation of revelation "is incumbent if and only if scriptural assertions are demonstrated — in the strictest sense of demonstration — to be impossible. Otherwise they must be accepted in their literal sense." This single criterion underlies the whole argument of the book. Its force is asymmetric: the literal text holds by default, so the philosopher who would overturn it must produce a genuine demonstration, not just a persuasive theory. Where they offer only "arbitrary assertions which, when truly ascertained, constitute [nothing but] darkness atop darkness," religion is untouched.

## The Purely Destructive Stance

Al-Ghazali's task is to refute, not to build: "We have named the book *The Incoherence of the Philosophers*, not *The Introduction to Truth*." He argues, in his own words, only "as one who demands and denies, not as one who claims [and] affirms." To that end he will press on the philosophers the consequences drawn by *any* available theological school — "now Mu'tazilite, now Karramiyya, now Waqifiyya" — without himself defending any one of them: "I will not rise to the defense of any one doctrine but will make all the sects as one group against them." (The constructive sequel, he promises, will be a separate book — realized as *Al-Iqtisad fi al-I'tiqad*.) This is why the *Tahafut* can defend non-Ash'arite positions (an immaterial soul, a modified causal efficacy) purely *for the sake of argument*.

## Turning the Philosophers' Own Logic Against Them

In the Fourth Introduction he sets aside the theologians' vocabulary and argues "in the idiom of the logicians... in their language." His logic (he held) was a religiously neutral instrument, no different in substance from the theologians' reasoning. The strategy: the philosophers laid down, in their own works on demonstration, the conditions a proof must satisfy — and "none of which have they been able to fulfill in their metaphysical sciences." Their physics, mathematics, and astronomy *do* meet the bar (astronomy "rests on demonstrations — geometrical and arithmetical — that leave no room for doubt"), and al-Ghazali explicitly refuses to contest them; indeed "the harm inflicted on religion by those who defend it in a way not proper to it is greater than [the harm done by] those who attack it." It is only in *metaphysics* that the *falasifa* trade demonstration for dialectic while claiming the certainty of proof.

## How Different Thinkers Use This

- **vs. [[Thinkers/Ibn Rushd]]:** Averroes accepts the demonstration-criterion but flips its application — for him many religious truths *are* the proper domain of demonstrative philosophy (for the qualified few), and al-Ghazali's own arguments are the ones that fail rigor, dissolving into equivocation. Where al-Ghazali uses the criterion to *quarantine* metaphysics from reason's reach, Averroes uses it to *license* the philosopher's reading of scripture (*Fasl al-Maqal*).
- **vs. [[Thinkers/Thomas Aquinas]]:** A near-structural parallel in Latin scholasticism — Aquinas holds the world's temporal beginning to be known by faith, not demonstrable by reason, precisely because the philosophers' eternity-proofs are not conclusive. Both deploy "the philosophers have not demonstrated it" to protect a revealed claim, while differing sharply on how much natural theology *can* demonstrate.
- **vs. the verificationists ([[Concepts/Verification Principle (Ayer)|Ayer]], [[Thinkers/Popper|Popper]]):** A distant mirror-image. Al-Ghazali raises the *bar* for overturning revelation to near-impossibility; modern critics of metaphysics raise the bar for *asserting* it. Both turn on a demand that metaphysical claims meet a standard (demonstration / verification / falsifiability) that their proponents cannot satisfy.

## Contradictions / Open Questions

- Does the criterion prove too much? If only strict demonstration can dislodge the literal text, almost no scientific or philosophical revision of a scriptural cosmology could ever qualify — yet al-Ghazali concedes astronomy against literalist readings of eclipse verses. Where exactly is the line?
- Is the "refute-only" posture sincere or a rhetorical shield? Averroes charges that al-Ghazali smuggles in a positive Ash'arite metaphysics (occasionalism, atomism) throughout.

> [!warning] Averroes ([[Thinkers/Ibn Rushd]]) holds that it is al-Ghazali's *own* arguments that fail the demonstration test, dissolving into equivocation — see [[Sources/Incoherence of the Incoherence - Ibn Rushd]]
