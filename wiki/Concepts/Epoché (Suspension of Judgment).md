# Epoché (Suspension of Judgment)

> In Pyrrhonian Skepticism (Sextus Empiricus, *Outlines of Pyrrhonism*), epoché is the central intellectual state or pathos: "a state of the intellect on account of which we neither deny nor affirm anything." It is produced by equipollence (isosthenia) among opposed phenomena, noumena, or statements, and is followed "as a shadow follows the body" by ataraxia (tranquility of soul). Distinct from the Academic position (which *asserts* that nothing can be apprehended); the Pyrrhonist continues to search without firm belief even in the skeptical slogans themselves. The practical result is living "without beliefs" according to the ordinary fourfold regimen of life (nature, pathé, laws/customs, instruction in arts) while reporting appearances "like a chronicler."

## Core Elements from Sextus Empiricus

**How Epoché Arises**:

The Skeptic Way is "a disposition to oppose phenomena and noumena to one another in any way whatever." When opposed statements or appearances are of equal credibility ("equipollence"), the intellect is brought to epoché — it withholds assent.

- Not paralysis or inaction: the Skeptic still acts on appearances (the criterion for daily life) and follows customs/laws without believing them to reflect non-evident reality.
- "We do not apply the term 'disposition' in any subtle sense, but simply as cognate with 'to be disposed.'"

**Relation to Ataraxia**:

The hoped-for goal that originally motivated the search (disturbed by anomaly in "the facts," talented people sought truth to attain tranquility). When resolution proved impossible, epoché was adopted — and ataraxia arrived unbidden, "by chance as it were." The painter Apelles, unable to paint the horse's froth, threw his sponge at the picture in frustration; the sponge produced the exact froth effect sought. So too: "the Skeptics were hoping to achieve ataraxia by resolving the anomaly of phenomena and noumena, and, being unable to do this, they suspended judgment. But then, by chance as it were, when they were suspending judgment the ataraxia followed."

For unavoidable pathé (hunger, cold, illness), the Skeptic experiences only the moderate feeling itself, not the additional torment of believing "this is by nature bad" (which drives ordinary people to irrational pursuit/avoidance and fear of change).

**The Skeptic Does Not Dogmatize (Even About Epoché or the Slogans)**:

- Assents only to what is forced by passive phantasia (e.g., "I seem to be hot" when feeling hot).
- No assent to non-evident matters.
- The slogans ("Nothing more," "I determine nothing," "To every argument an equal argument is opposed," etc.) are not propounded as holding absolutely. They are either self-applicable (the slogan "Nothing more" is itself "no more" true than its opposite) or imprecise reports of current pathos. "Since the dogmatizer is one who posits the content of his dogmas as being true, while the Skeptic presents his skeptical slogans as implicitly self-applicable, the Skeptic should not be said to dogmatize thereby."

**The Modes as the Engine Producing Epoché** (see also [[Concepts/Equipollence (Isosthenia) and the Modes]]):

The Ten Modes (Aenesidemus) and Five Modes (Agrippa and later) are the specific arguments/ways of generating the equipollent oppositions that lead to suspension. They are offered "without, however, maintaining anything about their number or their force. For they may well be unsound, and there may be more than the ones I shall mention."

**"Has a System" Only as a Way of Life**:

If "system" means attachment to agreeing dogmas about the non-evident, no. If "system" means a way of life in accordance with appearances that follows a certain rationale (showing how to seem to live rightly and tending to produce the disposition to suspend judgment), then yes: the Skeptic follows the customs of country, laws, institutions, and his own pathé, while the rationale (the modes, opposition) points toward epoché.

## Relevance to Vault

**Epistemic humility and "chronicler" practice for the wiki**:

The Skeptic stance models how Grok should maintain the knowledge base per [[GROK]]: report what the sources appear to show (with wikilinks, frontmatter sources, provenance), without treating syntheses or claims as final non-evident truths. Update when new sources arrive or contradictions surface. "Like a chronicler" = cite the raw or prior wiki pages; flag when equipollent views exist.

**Tool for handling conflict and avoiding precipitant synthesis**:

Epoché is the disciplined pause when sources, models, or interpretations are in equipollence (strong arguments on multiple sides). Directly supports:
- Lint / health check: surface contradictions, stale claims, orphans, gaps.
- Ingest: when new source challenges old, note via callout or "Contradictions / Open Questions" rather than silently overwriting.
- Query responses: present balanced views with links; file substantial analyses as wiki pages rather than one-sided chat answers.
- Reasoning scaffolds / agentic workflows: generate opposing arguments (Tree of Thoughts, self-critique) before committing.

**Contrast and enrichment of existing teloi** (with [[Concepts/Ataraxia]]):

All major Hellenistic options in the vault seek a form of tranquility/equanimity:
- Epicurean ataraxia: via atomist understanding, prudent desires, "death is nothing," friendship, "live unnoticed."
- Stoic equanimity: via dichotomy of control, proper use of impressions, acceptance of role in divine order.
- Pyrrhonian: via epoché on the non-evident. Ataraxia is not earned by correct physics or virtue but arrives when one stops forcing resolution. Moderate pathé accepted; extra layer of "this is naturally bad/good" removed.

This third path (suspension) complements and challenges the others already documented. Updates Ataraxia, Eudaimonia, Dichotomy of Control pages with the contrast.
