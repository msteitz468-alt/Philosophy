# Joycean Machine and Memes (Dennett)

> Dennett's architectural account of how consciousness works at the implementation level (Chs. 7–9 of *Consciousness Explained*, 1991): human consciousness is a **more-or-less serial virtual machine** — the "Joycean machine," named after James Joyce's stream-of-consciousness novels — running inefficiently on the brain's massively parallel hardware. The program is **culturally installed** via language, writing, and talking-to-oneself habits. The software units are **memes** (Dawkins). The biological substrate is the **pandemonium architecture** (Selfridge). The feedback between culture and genome is the **Baldwin Effect**.

## The Joycean Machine

James Joyce's stream-of-consciousness style (notably in *Ulysses*) captures something real about human cognition: experience presents itself as a more-or-less sequential inner monologue, a running narrative commentary on what is happening. Dennett takes this seriously as a description of a virtual machine.

The Joycean machine is:
- **Serial (more or less)**: It processes in a sequence, one thought leading to another, unlike the massively parallel pandemonium of competing neural processes underneath.
- **A virtual machine**: It is not directly implemented in the hardware. Just as a Java virtual machine is a program running on a physical computer, the Joycean serial narrative is a pattern of activity running on (and exploiting) the brain's parallel hardware — but not identical to it.
- **Culturally installed**: The program is not hardwired. It is learned, culturally transmitted, and varies across individuals and cultures. The primary installation mechanisms are:
  - **Talking to yourself** (verbal inner speech, either subvocalized or fully internal)
  - **Writing** (externalized reasoning that feeds back into the internal architecture)
  - **Drawing, diagrams, and external scaffolding** (offloading cognition to the environment, which then shapes internal processes)

Von Neumann is the inadvertent engineer: von Neumann's serial computer architecture (fetch-decode-execute cycle) was designed to mimic the sequential appearance of thought, and then became the inadvertent model for how thought itself was understood. The Joycean machine is the biological precedent that von Neumann's architecture was (unconsciously) imitating.

## The Pandemonium Architecture (Substrate)

Below the Joycean virtual machine lies the **pandemonium architecture** (Selfridge, 1959): the brain is a collection of many parallel, competing "demons" (feature detectors, word-demons, concept-activators) that all fire simultaneously and compete for hegemony. The "winner" at any moment — the demon whose activation reaches some threshold — captures the output channel.

This is:
- **The biological reality**: massively parallel, competitive, distributed processing.
- **The substrate for the Joycean machine**: the pandemonium's competitive dynamics are what the Joycean virtual machine runs *on top of*.
- **Directly observed** in the experience of word-finding: when you try to retrieve a word, multiple candidates compete (tip-of-the-tongue state), and one wins. This is the pandemonium temporarily visible through the Joycean surface.

Speech production (Levelt's "Conceptualizer") dissolves under scrutiny into a pandemonium of word-demons competing for expression. There is no "Central Meaner" behind utterances — meaning is distributed across the competing pandemonium, and the Joycean serial narrative is what emerges from that competition.

## Memes

**Memes** (Dawkins, *The Selfish Gene*, 1976) are units of cultural evolution: ideas, tunes, habits, stories, rituals that propagate through imitation and learning, subject to variation and selection, analogously to genes.

Dennett applies the meme concept directly to consciousness:
- The Joycean machine is **a meme-complex** — a co-evolved package of cultural replicators (talking-to-oneself habits, narrative structures, self-attribution conventions) that propagates across generations via cultural transmission.
- Memes "parasitize" plastic brains: they reshape neural architecture over development, installing the virtual machine that produces the Joycean serial stream.
- Some features of consciousness may be **"selfish memes"** with no functional benefit to the individual carrier — they propagate because they are good at propagating, not because they help the person who has them.

This means consciousness (in the Joycean sense) is not primarily a biological adaptation of the individual — it is a cultural product installed in individual brains by the meme-evolution process.

## The Baldwin Effect

The **Baldwin Effect** (James Mark Baldwin, 1896) is a feedback mechanism between phenotypic plasticity and genetic evolution:
1. Organisms learn good tricks (useful behaviors) during their lifetimes via phenotypic plasticity.
2. In environments where the trick is consistently useful, organisms that can *learn it faster* (or come pre-wired for it) have higher fitness.
3. Over generations, the learned behavior becomes genetically entrenched — what was once learned is now innate.

Dennett uses the Baldwin Effect to explain:
- Why human brains grew large *before* language evolved: large brains increased general plasticity and learning ability; the capacity to learn language was the trick being selected for, not a specific language module.
- Why some aspects of the Joycean machine are becoming genetically entrenched: good cultural tricks that have been practiced long enough are being partially incorporated into the genome.
- The evolutionary mechanism is thus: **genetic evolution → large plastic brains → cultural meme-complex installs Joycean machine → Baldwin feedback → some Joycean features hardwire**.

This means consciousness is at an interesting transitional point: too recent to be fully hardwired (which is why it requires cultural installation), but old enough that some features are being entrenched via the Baldwin Effect.

## Consciousness as a Recent Cultural Achievement

A key implication: the Joycean virtual machine is too recent to be a hardwired neural module. Human brains reached their current size ~150,000 years ago; language and the complex cultural transmission needed to install the Joycean machine emerged only ~50,000–100,000 years ago. This means:
- The specific form of human consciousness (the serial inner monologue, the narrative self, the heterophenomenological reports) is a cultural achievement, not a biological given.
- It is in principle possible to have a human brain without the Joycean machine fully installed — and indeed, developmental variations in language acquisition and inner speech support this.
- The Joycean machine is therefore a **historical and cultural contingency**, not an eternal feature of brains of sufficient complexity.

## Relation to the Multiple Drafts Model

The Multiple Drafts Model (the [[Concepts/Multiple Drafts Model (Dennett)|MDM]]) describes the underlying neural process; the Joycean machine describes what emerges at the virtual-machine level. The MDM's parallel editorial processes are the pandemonium; the Joycean serial stream is the narrative coherence constructed *from* the MDM's outputs by the culturally installed virtual machine. The Joycean machine does not change the underlying MDM process — it is a pattern of activity superimposed on it.
