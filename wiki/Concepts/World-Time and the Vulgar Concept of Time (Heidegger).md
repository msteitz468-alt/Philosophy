# World-Time and the Vulgar Concept of Time (Heidegger)
> Clock-time — the endless, irreversible "sequence of nows" from Aristotle to Hegel — is a leveled-down derivative of world-time, which temporality itself makes public.

## Summary

*Being and Time* §§78–82: the derivation chain **temporality → world-time → now-time (vulgar time)**.

### Reckoning with Time and Datability (§79)

Before any clock, heedful taking-care already *reckons with* time, speaking it in the "now," "then," and "on that former occasion" — expressed making-present, awaiting, and retaining. Every such expression is essentially **datable**: "every 'now' is a 'now that …'. We shall call this seemingly self-evident relational structure … datability" — "the reflex of the ecstatic constitution of temporality." Expressed time is also *spanned* (the "during," "meanwhile") and *public*. Resolute existence "never loses time and 'always has time'"; the irresolute they-self, lost in what it takes care of, "has no time."

### World-Time (§80)

Public time is dated first from the environing world (dawn, sunrise — the sun as "natural clock"); "temporality is the reason for the clock." Time taken care of always has the character of *time for…* (appropriateness, significance): "we shall call the time making itself public in the temporalizing of temporality **world time**" — datable, spanned, public, significant, belonging to the world itself. World-time is "more objective than any object and more subjective than any subject" — like world, it is transcendent (cf. [[Concepts/Being-in-the-World (Heidegger)]]). Beings unlike Dasein are **within-time** (*innerzeitig*); strictly, they are "atemporal."

### The Vulgar Concept and Aristotle (§81)

Reading time off the moving clock-pointer yields time as "what is counted" — precisely [[Thinkers/Aristotle]]'s definition ("that which is counted in the motion encountered in the horizon of the earlier and the later," *Physics* IV), to which "all subsequent time-concepts — Plato, Augustine, Hegel — hold." World-time so caught sight of is **now-time**: "In the vulgar interpretation of time as a succession of nows, both datability and significance are lacking … The nows are cut off from these relations." The vulgar theses — infinity, irreversibility, "passing away" — are explicable only *from* finite, futural temporality and the they's flight from death: "The they never dies." "Only because primordial time is finite can 'derivative' time temporalize itself as infinite" (§65).

### Against Hegel (§82)

Hegel's time (space "is" time; time as "intuited becoming," negation of negation) is "the most radical way in which the vulgar understanding of time has been given form conceptually"; his time–spirit connection rests on a merely formal sameness of structure. Counter-thesis: "'Spirit' does not first fall into time, but *exists* as the primordial temporalizing of temporality." "Spirit does not fall into time, but factical existence 'falls,' in falling prey, out of primordial, authentic temporality."

### The Unfinished End (§83)

The book closes with questions, not answers: "Is there a way leading from primordial time to the meaning of being? Does time itself reveal itself as the horizon of being?" Division Three ("Time and Being") never appeared — see [[Concepts/Historicality and the Turn (Heidegger)]].

## Key Claims / Positions

### How This Compares to Other Thinkers in the Wiki

- **[[Thinkers/Aristotle]]**: his *Physics* IV account is honored as the first detailed interpretation of time — and diagnosed as the source of two millennia of "now-time," including Bergson.
- **[[Thinkers/Augustine]]**: the *distentio animi* is closer to Dasein's temporality than the cosmological account, but still, per §81, within the vulgar horizon.
- **[[Thinkers/Kant]]**: time as pure form of intuition inherits now-time while placing it "in the subject" — half right, by Heidegger's lights, for the wrong reasons.
- **[[Thinkers/Hegel]]**: explicit target of §82 (above).
- **[[Concepts/Eternal Will and Temporal Creation (Ghazali)]] / eternalism debates**: Heidegger's denial of "eternal truths" and of any time without Dasein sits athwart both sides — time is neither created nor eternal but temporalized.

## Contradictions / Open Questions

- > [!warning] Physics treats time as mind-independent (relativity was among Heidegger's own §3 examples of foundational crisis); the claim that clock-time is *derivative* of Dasein's temporality conflicts with scientific realism in the vault — same fault line as [[Concepts/Reality and the External World (Heidegger)]] and the Newton passage in [[Concepts/Truth as Unconcealment (Heidegger)]].
- Whether the derivation temporality → world-time → now-time is an argument or a redescription remains contested in the literature.
