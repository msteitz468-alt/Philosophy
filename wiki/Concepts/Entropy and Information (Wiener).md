# Entropy and Information (Wiener)

> Wiener's thermodynamic grounding of information theory: the universe trends toward maximum entropy (disorder, uniformity, heat death); life and information are local counter-currents — "islands here and now in a dying world." Personal identity = the continuity of a **pattern**, not of matter: "We are but whirlpools in a river of ever-flowing water." Information is the measure of local order / anti-entropy; information and entropy are "not conserved" and for this reason information cannot be treated as a commodity. This ontology connects the Boltzmann-Gibbs physics of thermodynamics to the meaning-bearing messages of social life.

## Summary

### The Entropic Universe

Wiener begins from Gibbsian thermodynamics: the physical universe is a probabilistic system tending toward maximum entropy — maximum disorder, uniformity, "heat death." The second law of thermodynamics says entropy (disorder) cannot decrease in a closed system. The universe is running down.

**Maxwell's demon**: James Clerk Maxwell's thought experiment showed that a sufficiently knowledgeable demon could sort fast and slow molecules, decreasing entropy locally — but only at the cost of the demon's own information processing (Szilard showed this in 1929). The demon cannot cheat the second law; information acquisition itself has thermodynamic cost. This argument connects information directly to physical thermodynamics.

### Life as Local Anti-Entropy

Against the universal entropic trend, life creates and maintains **local enclaves of decreasing entropy**:

- A living organism maintains a stable body temperature, chemical composition, and structure against the constant thermodynamic pressure toward dissolution.
- This maintenance is **homeostasis** — continuous negative-feedback correction of deviations from the target state.
- **"Life is an island here and now in a dying world."** The process by which living beings resist the general stream of increasing entropy is homeostasis.

When homeostasis fails faster than the organism can reconstitute itself, the organism dies — the pattern dissolves back into the entropic background.

### Organism as Message / Identity as Pattern

The most radical extension: **personal identity = pattern continuity, not material continuity.**

"We have already seen that certain organisms, such as man, tend for a time to maintain and often even to increase the level of their organization... The tissues change as we live: the food we eat and the air we breathe become flesh of our flesh and bone of our bone, and the momentary elements of our flesh and bone pass out of our body every day with our excreta. **We are but whirlpools in a river of ever-flowing water. We are not stuff that abides, but patterns that perpetuate themselves.**"

The corollary: **a pattern is a message**, and can in principle be transmitted as a message:

"It is amusing as well as instructive to consider what would happen if we were to transmit the whole pattern of the human body, of the human brain with its memories and cross connections, so that a hypothetical receiving instrument could re-embody these messages in appropriate matter."

Wiener does not think this is currently feasible (the amount of information in a germ cell alone exceeds the entire Encyclopedia Britannica), but holds it is not **theoretically** impossible. The distinction between matter-transport and information-transport is practical, not ontological. This is an early statement of what philosophers now call **substrate independence**.

### Information as Anti-Entropy

Shannon's entropy H = −∑ p_i log p_i measures uncertainty/disorder. Wiener approaches from the thermodynamic direction and arrives at the same formula, identifying **information as the measure of order** (negative entropy):

- Entropy measures disorder; information measures order. They have opposite signs.
- "Just as entropy tends to increase spontaneously in a closed system, so information tends to decrease; just as entropy is a measure of disorder, so information is a measure of order."
- Maintaining an organized pattern (a living body, a message, a society's institutions) requires continuous work against the entropic background.

### Information Is Not a Commodity

The anti-commodity argument follows directly from the thermodynamics:

- Good commodities are **conserved** (gold, kilowatt-hours of electricity transfer with minimal loss).
- Information is **not conserved**: it is anti-additive (like entropy), degrades over time, cannot be stored without depreciation.
- "The idea that information can be stored in a changing world without an overwhelming depreciation in its value is false."
- Patent law, secrecy classification, and the American "know-how" ideology treat information as storable, ownable property — but this is a category error. A country's scientific security is not a stockpile of facts; it is the living, dynamic capacity for continued discovery.
- "Information is more a matter of process than of storage."

### The Philosophical Contrast with Shannon

Shannon's 1948 paper explicitly brackets semantics: "the semantic aspects of communication are irrelevant to the engineering problem." Shannon's information theory treats H as a purely mathematical quantity describing statistical selection.

Wiener uses the same mathematics but insists on its thermodynamic meaning — information is not just a formal quantity but a measure of physical anti-entropy in a world tending toward disorder. For Wiener, information always connects to the world (to the messages society depends on, to the patterns that ARE living beings). The engineering and the metaphysical cannot be cleanly separated.

See [[Contradictions/Wiener vs Shannon - Information and Meaning]].

## Key Claims / Positions

- The universe trends toward maximum entropy; life and information are local counter-currents.
- Personal identity = pattern continuity, not material continuity ("We are but whirlpools").
- Information = measure of order / anti-entropy (same mathematics as Shannon's H but with physical/social grounding).
- Information is not conserved → information is not a commodity; it cannot be stored, owned, or traded like physical goods.
- The organism-as-message thesis makes teleportation (informational person-transport) logically possible, practically impossible.

## Contradictions / Open Questions

- > [!warning] The contrast with Shannon is the vault's central information-theory tension. Shannon brackets meaning and treats H as a purely engineering quantity. Wiener insists information's thermodynamic and social dimensions are inseparable from the mathematics. See [[Contradictions/Wiener vs Shannon - Information and Meaning]] and [[Thinkers/Claude Shannon]].
- "Organism as message / identity as pattern" is a form of functionalism or substrate-independence that allies with [[Thinkers/Douglas Hofstadter]]'s strange-loop thesis (consciousness = pattern = implementable on any substrate). Both challenge Cartesian dualism. But Wiener does not draw Hofstadter's strong-AI conclusion — he is alarmed by what machine autonomy might do.
- Wiener's claim that information decays and cannot be owned anticipates modern debates about AI training data, copyright, and intellectual property — all of which treat information as a commodity in precisely the way Wiener argues is conceptually confused.
