# Anxiety (Angst) (Heidegger)
> The objectless fundamental attunement in which the world as such — "nothing and nowhere" — discloses itself, individualizing Dasein and freeing it for authenticity.

## Summary

*Being and Time* §40 (deepened in §§50, 53, 68b). To grasp Dasein's structural whole, a "most far-reaching and most primordial" disclosive attunement is needed. Everyday entangled absorption in the they is a *flight* of Dasein from itself — and this flight rests not on fear of any innerworldly being but on **anxiety**, "which in turn first makes fear possible." "Fear is anxiety which has fallen prey to the 'world.' It is inauthentic and concealed from itself as such" (§40).

Unlike fear, anxiety has no innerworldly object: "That about which one has anxiety is being-in-the-world as such." In anxiety the totality of relevance collapses; the world shows "complete insignificance"; the threat is "nothing and nowhere." Precisely thereby the world *as world* — and Dasein as free being-possible — is disclosed. Anxiety **individualizes** Dasein (an existential, not epistemic, *solus ipse*), revealing **uncanniness** (*Unheimlichkeit*, not-being-at-home) as more primordial than the tranquillized familiarity of the they. From this analysis Heidegger wins the definition of Dasein's being as [[Concepts/Care (Sorge) (Heidegger)|care]] (§41).

In Division Two anxiety returns as the attunement of [[Concepts/Being-toward-Death and Authenticity (Heidegger)|being-toward-death]]: thrownness into death is revealed in anxiety, and "the they does not permit the courage to have anxiety about death" (§51); in anticipation Dasein stands in "passionate anxious freedom toward death" (§53). Anxiety is also the readiness demanded by [[Concepts/Conscience, Guilt, and Resoluteness (Heidegger)|resoluteness]]. Temporally, anxiety brings Dasein back to its thrownness "as something to be possibly repeated" and "holds the Moment in readiness" — it temporalizes out of the future of resoluteness, whereas fear arises from the lost present (§68b).

## Key Claims / Positions

### How This Compares to Other Thinkers in the Wiki

- **[[Thinkers/Søren Kierkegaard]]**: the acknowledged ancestor (anxiety as the dizziness of freedom before possibility); Heidegger ontologizes it, stripping the theological frame.
- **[[Thinkers/Jean-Paul Sartre]]**: adopts *angoisse* as consciousness of one's own [[Concepts/Radical Freedom and Situation (Sartre)|radical freedom]] (vertigo at the cliff: nothing prevents me from jumping); for Heidegger the accent falls on world-collapse and individualization before death, not on the spontaneity of consciousness.
- **[[Thinkers/Epicurus]] / the Stoics**: treat fear of death as an error to be dissolved by argument; Heidegger treats anxiety before death as a *disclosure* to be endured — suppressing it is the they's tranquillization, not wisdom.

## Contradictions / Open Questions

- > [!warning] Anxiety carries enormous methodological weight (it certifies the wholeness of care and the possibility of authenticity), yet it is a rare, fleeting mood — can an ontology rest on an exceptional experience?
- Relation to empirical psychology of anxiety deliberately unaddressed: Heidegger claims priority over psychology; cf. [[Concepts/Heterophenomenology (Dennett)]] for the opposed methodological instinct.
