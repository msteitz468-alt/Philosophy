# Modal Copula (Aristotelian Modality)

> In Aristotle’s modal logic (primarily *Prior Analytics*, part of the *Organon*), as analyzed by Richard Patterson, modality is expressed through a **modalized copula** (“A necessarily applies to all B”, “A possibly applies to all B”) rather than as operators on whole propositions (de dicto) or modifications of predicates (de re). This reflects real metaphysical distinctions between essential and accidental properties and underpins scientific demonstration.

## Core Elements (from Patterson analysis)
- Four kinds of propositions: assertoric (plain), necessary, possible, two-way possible (contingent).
- Modality concerns the *kind of connection* holding between subject and predicate.
- Two readings of copula necessity:
  - Weak: extensional/accidental (the predicate happens to apply necessarily to the things under the subject).
  - Strong: essential/per se (the predicate is essentially connected to the subject term itself).
- The system is grounded in Aristotle’s essentialism (genus, species, differentia, proprium, accident). Scientific demonstration proceeds from *per se* predications.
- Rejects purely formal modern reconstructions that sever logic from metaphysics.
