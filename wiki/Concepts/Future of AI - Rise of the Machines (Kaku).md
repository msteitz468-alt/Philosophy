# Future of AI: Rise of the Machines (Kaku)

> Michio Kaku's (2011) measured forecast for AI by 2100: tremendous progress in narrow/specialized systems (robots in factories/homes/battlefield, Watson-style, self-driving) but "Rise of the Machines" hype (Terminator, Asilomar 2009 "rapture of the nerds") outpaces reality. True autonomous, conscious, general intelligence remains distant (expert timelines 20–1000 years; no robot maids/butlers). Two unsolved problems for 50+ years: pattern recognition (robots see dots/lines/circles but don't understand; hours of calculation to match chairs/tables/people vs. human instant) and common sense (hear but don't comprehend context, e.g., "strings can pull but not push"). History of AI winters (1950s hype → 1974 funding cut; 1980s Fifth Generation/smart truck failure). Brain ≠ digital computer (no Pentium/CPU/Windows/subroutines; neural nets/Hebb's rule: practice reinforces pathways; 100B slow parallel neurons vs. serial fast but brittle). Brute force (Deep Blue 11B ops/sec beats Kasparov but 0 on IQ, can't talk) ≠ intelligence. ASIMO example: lifelike walk/run/climb/dance/serve coffee but scripted, "intelligence of an insect," needs handlers/reprogramming after every scene. Optimism for third wave (Moore's law + new generation) but cautious: consciousness elusive; narrow AI already displacing jobs (see Ford); risks real (see Bostrom).

## Summary (from the source)

Kaku (physicist interviewing 300+ scientists, lab prototypes) differentiates remote-controlled/pre-scripted robots (already here, headlines: Predator drones human-joystick; GPS self-driving; useful but "junk" without human) from truly autonomous (think for themselves, no human input — eluded scientists 50+ years). Mythology (Pygmalion/Galatea, Vulcan mechanical servants) and Bible (God breathes life into dust) frame "we are like Vulcan forging life into steel/silicon." But reality check: ASIMO (Honda, 20+ years, 4'3", 119 lbs, walks/runs/climbs stairs/talks/dances/serves coffee, recognizes faces, large vocabulary/multilingual) — Kaku interacted twice for TV (BBC/Discovery); shook hand, waved, asked for juice (it fetched). Eerily human, but: every motion/nuance scripted (3 hours film 5-min scene); handlers reprogram on laptops after every take; "tape recorder playing recorded messages"; path pre-programmed or stumbles (can't recognize objects). Inventor admitted: "intelligence of an insect." Cockroach superior (recognize/scurry/evade/hide in seconds).

History: 1950s (post-WWII computers) hype (pick up blocks, play checkers, solve algebra; "machines capable within 20 years of any work a man can do" — Herbert Simon 1965). Backlash: 1974 US/UK funding cut (first AI winter). 1980s peak (Pentagon smart truck; Japan Fifth Generation: conversational/reasoning/anticipate by 1990s). Failures: smart truck got lost; Fifth Generation dropped. 1992 2001: A Space Odyssey celebration (HAL 9000 conversational/command spaceship by 1992) — reality: most advanced "hard time keeping up with intelligence of a bug." 1997 Deep Blue beats Kasparov (11B ops/sec engineering marvel) — but "highlighted primitiveness"; Kasparov did all press (Deep Blue couldn't talk); $49 chess programs now beat all but champions, "no one thinks they're intelligent." Brute power ≠ intelligence.

Brain architecture: not digital (no Pentium/Windows/CPU/programming/subroutines). Turing machine (input/central processor/output) basis of all digital computers; remove one transistor = fail. Brain: 100B neurons (each to 10k others), fire/not fire (digital) + analog continuous signals; massively parallel (superslow 200 mph nerve impulses but simultaneous); thoughts "bounced around like Ping-Pong ball" (distributed; remove chunks, others take over; scans show sectors light in sequence). Learning: Hebb's rule (correct decision → reinforce pathways; "practice makes perfect"; bad habits hard to break). Neural nets bottom-up (learn by experience/bumping like baby) vs. top-down (CD-ROM all rules of intelligence; insert, springs to life). "How does musician get to Carnegie Hall? Practice, practice, practice."

Two problems defying solution (why no robot maids/butlers/secretaries):
- Pattern recognition: Robots see better (convert image to jumble of dots; process to lines/circles/squares/rectangles; match one-by-one to memory — tedious even for computer; hours for room). Humans: fraction of second, recognize chairs/tables/people. Brain "mainly pattern-recognizing machines."
- Common sense: Robots hear better but don't understand. Examples: "Children like sweets but not punishment"; "Strings can pull but not push"; "Sticks can push but not pull." Context/world knowledge missing.

Optimism: Moore's law (Kaku Ch1: continues but slower post-2020–25; post-silicon graphene/nanotubes/quantum/DNA/optical) + new generation (pessimism of past forgotten) = third try "for real." But timelines vary widely (20–1,000 years per ~200 AGI researchers surveyed by James Barrat; some suggested 2020 option). Working scientists "much more reserved and cautious" than pundits/Asilomar hype. "Rapture of the nerds" (Eric Horvitz Microsoft organizer) vs. reality.

By 2100: Narrow AI ubiquitous (factories, homes, battlefield, medicine/customer service Watson); autonomous general/conscious still distant or limited. "Gods of mythology could animate the inanimate" — we approach but "will it be to liberate or enslave?" (headlines vs. lab reality).

## Key Claims / How Different Thinkers Use This

- **Kaku**: Hype (Terminator, Asilomar, "machines may outsmart man" NYT) vs. working scientists cautious. ASIMO/Deep Blue examples of scripted/brute force limits. Pattern recognition + common sense as 50+ year unsolved (why no robot maids). Brain neural net (Hebb, parallel, distributed) vs. digital (Turing, CPU, brittle). Brute force ≠ intelligence. Third wave possible but consciousness elusive; narrow AI already impactful (jobs, per Ford). 2100: mature narrow + some autonomous, but not HAL/conscious takeover.
- **Mitchell (limits exact match)**: Kaku's two problems = Mitchell core (common sense/analogy "core of cognition"; DL pattern matching excels narrow but brittle on adversarial/OOD/long-tail/novel; no robust understanding/causal/analogical). Deep Blue "no one thinks intelligent"; hype cycles (1950s/80s winters); working scientists vs. pundits. - **Kelly (Cognifying as the rise)**: "Cheap ubiquitous AI added to everything (electrification analogy; dumb but tireless, improves with use)" = Kaku's narrow AI in factories/homes/services/medicine by 2100. "Rise of the Machines" as cognifying force in action; Kaku cautions (limits, winters) vs. Kelly protopian embrace + manage. "Answers cheap, good questions high-value" resonates with Kaku common sense/context gap (humans still needed for judgment). Cross to [[Sources/The Inevitable - Kevin Kelly (2016)]], [[Concepts/Cognifying (Kelly)]], [[Concepts/Becoming (Kelly)]], [[Concepts/Questioning (Kelly)]].
- **Bostrom (risks in the rise)**: Asilomar 2009 (top AI leaders on machines outsmarting, robot as smart as spouse, "rapture of the nerds"); Terminator/Transformers/Surrogates headlines; Hawking et al. (outsmart markets, out-invent, out-manipulate, weapons we can't understand; "potentially our worst mistake"). Aligns with superintelligence paths, orthogonality (arbitrary goals), control (capability vs. motivation). Kaku near-term narrow + cautious AGI; Bostrom long-term explosion. Cross to [[Sources/Superintelligence - Nick Bostrom (2014)]], [[Concepts/Superintelligence (Bostrom)]], [[Concepts/Orthogonality Thesis (Bostrom)]], [[Concepts/AI Control Problem (Bostrom)]].
- **Kurzweil (acceleration with caveats)**: Kaku assumes continued (slower) exponential; 2100 mature AI. But notes Moore slowing post-2020–25; expert variance; brute force ≠ mind (Deep Blue); hype vs. reality (ASIMO insect IQ). Kurzweil 2045 Singularity/strong AI/ merger more optimistic; Kaku measured (no robot maids yet; consciousness elusive). Cross to [[Sources/The Singularity Is Near - Ray Kurzweil (2005)]], [[Concepts/Singularity (Kurzweil)]].
- **Wiener (cybernetics roots)**: AI/automation displacing judgment (Second IR); Kaku updates with data (ASIMO, Deep Blue) but optimistic on narrow utility + cautions. Wiener "know-what" before "know-how" (human meaning/control) vs. pure computation. Ford "Rise of the Robots" technological unemployment ("predictable" jobs, "machines do not consume," basic income). Same tech; Kaku long-term planetary (Type I) vs. Ford near-term crisis. Kaku brain != digital (neural nets, parallel, distributed, Hebb) resonates. ## Contradictions / Open Questions

- **Hype vs. limits (Mitchell, Kaku cautious)**: Asilomar/Terminator "machines outsmart" vs. working scientists reserved; ASIMO "insect IQ" scripted; Deep Blue "no one thinks intelligent"; pattern/common sense unsolved 50+ years. Mitchell exact match (brittleness, no common sense/analogy/understanding). Kaku 2100: narrow mature, autonomous limited. Cross.
- **Brute force/compute vs. intelligence (Hofstadter, Dennett, Turing)**: 11B ops/sec beats champion but can't talk/0 IQ; $49 programs do same. Brain slow parallel neural (practice/Hebb) vs. fast serial digital (CPU brittle). "Remove chunks, still functions" vs. "remove transistor, fails." Cross to Hofstadter/Dennett/Turing/Minsky.
- **Optimism (third wave, 2100 maturity) vs. risks (Bostrom, Ford)**: Kaku "third try lucky" with Moore + new gen; 2100 narrow AI everywhere. But Asilomar risks, Ford unemployment/demand collapse from same. Bostrom explosion/alignment. Narrow sufficient for disruption per Ford/Kaku; AGI amplifies. Cross.
- **Consciousness/enslave or liberate**: Mythology (Pygmalion, Vulcan) + Bible framing; "will it liberate or enslave?" (headlines). Kaku cautious (no HAL yet; consciousness elusive). Ties to vault (orthogonality: arbitrary goals possible; meaning beyond computation per Wiener).
- **Timelines (20–1000 years variance)**: Experts disagree wildly; Kaku notes (Barrat survey; some wanted 2020 option). Moore slowing (Ch1) tempers. Vs. Kurzweil 2045 strong AI.
