# Conscience, Guilt, and Resoluteness (Heidegger)
> The silent call of care summons the they-self to its ownmost being-guilty; answering it — resoluteness — is the attested authentic disclosedness of Dasein.

## Summary

*Being and Time* §§54–62, 64. Anticipation of death (§53) showed an authentic being-whole to be ontologically *possible*; conscience supplies the existentiell *attestation* that Dasein actually demands it of itself.

### The Call (§§55–57)

Conscience (*Gewissen*), analyzed existentially rather than psychologically or theologically, is a mode of disclosedness — a **call** (*Ruf*), a mode of discourse. It summons the they-self away from lostness in [[Concepts/The They (das Man) and Falling Prey (Heidegger)|the they]] to its ownmost potentiality-of-being-a-self. "What does conscience call to the one summoned? Strictly speaking — nothing. The call does not say anything… Conscience speaks solely and constantly in the mode of silence" (§56). The caller is Dasein itself in its uncanniness — thrown, anxious, not-at-home: the call "comes from me, and yet over me." "Conscience is the call of care from the uncanniness of being-in-the-world that summons Dasein to its ownmost potentiality-for-being-guilty" (§59).

### Being-Guilty (§58)

Existential **guilt** (*Schuldigsein*) precedes all indebtedness and moral law: formally, "being-the-ground of a nullity." As thrown, Dasein never lays its own ground; as projecting, choosing one possibility means not-choosing others. "Care, the being of Dasein, thus means, as thrown project: being the (null) ground of a nullity. And that means that Dasein is as such guilty." This primordial guilt is the *condition of possibility* of morality, not a moral verdict. Understanding the call = *wanting-to-have-a-conscience*: readiness to be summoned, being free for one's ownmost being-guilty.

### Against the Vulgar Interpretation (§59)

Bad/good/warning/reprimanding conscience, conscience as inner court calculating debts — all interpret Dasein as objectively present and life as a "business." "Good" conscience is Pharisaical and "not a phenomenon of conscience at all"; the call gives "the most positive thing of all" — the ownmost potentiality-of-being-a-self.

### Resoluteness and Anticipatory Resoluteness (§§60, 62, 64)

"The eminent, authentic disclosedness attested in Dasein itself by its conscience — the reticent projecting oneself upon one's ownmost being-guilty which is ready for anxiety — we call **resoluteness** [*Entschlossenheit*]" (§60). Resoluteness does not detach Dasein from world or others; it first discloses the concrete **Situation** (the they knows only the "general situation") and can even authentically free others. Thought through to its end, resoluteness becomes **anticipatory resoluteness** (*vorlaufende Entschlossenheit*): only anticipating death gives resoluteness its wholeness, constancy, and certainty — death and guilt are equiprimordial in care. Selfhood is thereby redefined: self-constancy (*Selbst-ständigkeit*) "means nothing other than anticipatory resoluteness" (§64) — against [[Thinkers/Kant]]'s "I think," which rightly refuted the substantial soul but "slips back into the same inappropriate ontology of the substantial"; Kant missed that "I think" is "I think *something*" and "did not see the phenomenon of world."

Anticipatory resoluteness is then shown to be possible only as [[Concepts/Temporality (Heidegger)|temporality]] (§65), and its concrete historical enactment is fate and repetition (§74; see [[Concepts/Historicality and the Turn (Heidegger)]]).

## Key Claims / Positions

### How This Compares to Other Thinkers in the Wiki

- **[[Thinkers/Kant]]**: conscience as the voice of practical reason under universal law vs. Heidegger's contentless call prior to any law; the categorical imperative presupposes, on Heidegger's account, the existential guilt it cannot explain.
- **[[Thinkers/Jean-Paul Sartre]]**: inherits the structure (facticity + nihilating freedom = [[Concepts/Being-for-itself and Being-in-itself (Sartre)|the for-itself]]) but replaces the call of conscience with translucid consciousness; [[Concepts/Bad Faith (Mauvaise Foi) (Sartre)|bad faith]] plays the role of fleeing the call.
- **[[Thinkers/Nietzsche]]**: genealogy debunks guilt as internalized cruelty; Heidegger de-moralizes guilt in the opposite direction — into ontological structure.
- **[[Thinkers/Augustine]]**: the inner voice and restless not-at-homeness are secularized here; Heidegger's uncanniness is Augustinian *inquietum cor* without God.

## Contradictions / Open Questions

- > [!warning] The normative vacuum (flagged already in [[Concepts/Being-toward-Death and Authenticity (Heidegger)]]): resoluteness specifies *no criteria* for what to resolve upon — Inwood's "Hitler was no less resolute" objection. The primary text confirms it: the call is contentless by design; the Situation is disclosed, not evaluated.
- The "violence" Heidegger concedes to his own interpretation (§63) — existential analysis must wrest Dasein from its self-covering — raises the methodological question of falsifiability; cf. [[Thinkers/Karl Popper]].
