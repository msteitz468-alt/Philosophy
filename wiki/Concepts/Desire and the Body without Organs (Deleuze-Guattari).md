# Desire and the Body without Organs (Deleuze-Guattari)
> Desire is not lack, law, or castration — it is a positive productive process that assembles a plane of immanence (the Body without Organs) crisscrossed by particles and fluxes.

## Summary

The central argument of *Anti-Oedipus* (1972), compressed in the *Reader*: psychoanalysis misunderstands desire by grounding it in **lack** (Freud), **law** (Lacan), and **castration** (the symbolic order). Deleuze and Guattari argue that this is not a theoretical error but a political operation — psychoanalysis destroys real assemblages of desire by Oedipalizing them, reducing indefinite intensive processes ("a tummy," "a Hitler," "a race") to the personal-possessive ("my daddy," "my castration"). The unconscious is not a theater (representing family dramas) but a factory (producing real).

**Desire as productive.** "Desire was a process and it unrolled a plane of consistence, a field of immanence, a 'body without organs' as Artaud put it, crisscrossed by particles and fluxes which break free from objects and subjects." Desire is *energeia* (activity in itself) rather than *kinesis* (movement toward an end); it doesn't "want" an object it lacks — it produces.

**Body without Organs (BwO).** The BwO is not the absence of organs but the absence of the *organization* of organs into a fixed form. It is the plane of consistency — flat, unorganized, intensive — across which desiring-machines make connections. The narrator of Proust's *Remembrance* is described as "an enormous BwO — like a spider at the center of its web, registering only involuntary signs."

**Assemblage.** Desire only exists when *assembled*: "desire only exists when assembled or machined. You cannot grasp or conceive of a desire outside a determinate assemblage, on a plane which is not preexistent but which must itself be constructed." There are no drives as structural invariants — only assemblages that a component enters.

**Against pleasure as norm.** "Pleasure interrupts the process of desire as reterritorialization." The Reichian/hedonist idea that pleasure is desire's aim confuses the reterritorialization of desire with desire itself — and often involves hatred or fear of desire ("the cult of pleasure").

**Schizophrenic delirium as world-historical.** When Deleuze reads schizophrenic delirium, it is not familial (daddy/mommy) but racial, historical, geopolitical — "all delirium is racial," designating zones of intensity on the BwO. The schizo is "closest to the beating heart of reality, to an intense point identical with the production of the real." Freud's analysis of Schreber erases the entire geopolitical content to reduce it to the father.

## Key Claims

- "Those who link desire to lack, the long column of crooners of castration, clearly indicate a long resentment, like an interminable bad conscience." (Anti-Oedipus)
- Desire is collective: "every desire is the affair of the people, or an affair of the masses, a molecular affair."
- Psychoanalysis "systematically destroys real desire by overcoding assemblages."
- Lack appears only when the plane of immanence has not been constructed — it is a deprivation of conditions, not a structural feature.

## How Different Thinkers Use This

- **Freud/Psychoanalysis (target):** Freud posits desire as fundamentally oriented toward an object by lack; the unconscious as a representational theater of family relations; castration as the universal operator. Deleuze/Guattari: the unconscious is production, not representation; desire is connection, not lack; Oedipus is an imposition.
- **[[Thinkers/Spinoza]]:** Conatus (the striving to persevere in being) and affects provide Deleuze's closest precursor: desire as power of acting, not as orientation toward what one lacks.
- **[[Thinkers/Nietzsche]]:** Will to power as positive expression of force — not wanting power one lacks but the expression of the kind of power a force is. Parallel to productive desire.
- **[[Concepts/Ethics Without Morality (Deleuze-Spinoza)]]:** The link between sad passions and psychoanalytic Oedipalization runs through both pages — both argue that resentment/lack are imposed deprivations, not natural conditions.

## Contradictions / Open Questions

- > [!warning] If desire is always already assembled on a plane that "must be constructed," who or what does the constructing? The anti-subjectivist frame seems to require a practical agent of construction, which risks smuggling back the subject Deleuze has expelled.
