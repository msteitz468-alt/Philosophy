# Cybernetics (Wiener)

> Norbert Wiener's definition: "the science of control and communication in the animal and the machine." Coined independently in 1948 from the Greek *kubernētēs* (steersman or governor). Core thesis: the same formal laws governing feedback in mechanical control systems (Watt governor, ship steering engines, anti-aircraft predictors) also govern biological control systems (nervous system, homeostasis, voluntary motion, reflexes). Control and communication are two aspects of the same phenomenon. "Society can only be understood through a study of the messages and the communication facilities which belong to it."

## Summary

Wiener coined "cybernetics" in *Cybernetics, or Control and Communication in the Animal and the Machine* (1948), naming it after the Greek *kubernētēs* — the helmsman who steers a ship by continuously correcting course in response to wind and current. (Ampère had earlier used the French *cybernétique* in a classification of sciences, but Wiener's coinage was independent.) The word's root also gives us "governor," which is significant: Watt's steam-engine governor is one of the canonical examples, and Maxwell provided the first mathematical analysis of its feedback dynamics in 1868.

### The Unifying Claim

Cybernetics rests on a single audacious unification: **the formal structure of purposive behavior is the same in animals and machines.** The anti-aircraft fire-control predictor (which Wiener designed with Rosenblueth and Bigelow during WWII) must:

1. Track the target's current position
2. Predict the target's future position
3. Aim the gun at the predicted position
4. Observe whether the shot connects (feedback)
5. Correct the prediction model for the next shot

A human reaching for a glass of water performs precisely this structure through the nervous system. The eye observes the hand's position; the cerebellum computes the error between actual and desired position; motor neurons adjust the movement; the cycle repeats at ~10Hz until the hand reaches the glass. **Control IS feedback IS communication.**

The same analysis extends to:
- **Biological homeostasis**: body temperature, blood oxygen, hormone levels — all maintained by negative-feedback loops (deviation from target → corrective action → return to target)
- **Social systems**: markets (price signals feedback supply/demand), legal systems (law as feedback on social deviation), language (speaker and listener co-correct toward shared meaning)
- **Machines**: thermostats, autopilots, industrial regulators, digital computers

### Society as Communication System

The boldest extension: **society itself is a cybernetic system.** "Society can only be understood through a study of the messages and the communication facilities which belong to it." Political institutions, economic structures, legal systems, cultural traditions — all are analyzable as networks of messages and feedback loops. This is not metaphor but the literal claim that the same mathematics of control theory applies.

Wiener uses this framework to analyze:
- **Law** (Chapter VI): legal language as the coding system; precedent as the feedback stabilizer; courts as adversarial communication channels
- **Secrecy** (Chapter VII): information classification as jamming one's own signal
- **Industrial revolution** (Chapter IX): the shift from power transmission to information transmission as the defining feature of the Second IR

### Wiener vs. Ampère / History of the Term

Although Ampère used a near-identical term in 1834, Wiener's coinage was independent. The significance of the term is the analogy it draws: a *governor* (in the political sense) steers society through feedback on social messages; a *steersman* maintains course by continuously correcting for drift. Both the political and the engineering connotations are intentional.

## Key Claims / Positions

- Control and communication are two aspects of the same formal phenomenon.
- The same feedback equations govern servomechanisms, homeostasis, nervous systems, and social institutions.
- Society is understandable only through its messages — cybernetics extends to social theory.
- Feedback is the mechanism of purposive behavior in all systems that exhibit it.

## Contradictions / Open Questions

- > [!warning] Wiener's insistence that cybernetics extends to social semantics and meaning directly conflicts with [[Thinkers/Claude Shannon]]'s explicit bracketing of semantics from information theory. Shannon: "the semantic aspects of communication are irrelevant to the engineering problem." Wiener: society is only understandable through its messages (which are inherently meaningful). See [[Contradictions/Wiener vs Shannon - Information and Meaning]].
- Does cybernetics apply to *all* social systems, or only those with clear feedback structures? Wiener's examples (law, language, markets) are suggestive but the extension to political institutions is contested.
- The control-communication unification was deeply influential across biology (regulatory biology), psychology (behaviorism, cognitive science), computer science (AI as engineering of feedback-driven systems), and social science (systems theory, operations research). Its breadth is also its weakness: "everything is feedback" can become unfalsifiable.
