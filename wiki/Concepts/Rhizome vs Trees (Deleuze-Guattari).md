# Rhizome vs Trees (Deleuze-Guattari)
> The rhizome — a botanical term for a root-system with no center, no beginning, no end — is Deleuze and Guattari's model for thought, knowledge, and reality as multiplicity rather than hierarchy.

## Summary

In *A Thousand Plateaus* (1980), Deleuze and Guattari oppose two models of thinking and organization: the **arborescent** (tree-like) and the **rhizomatic**. The tree model dominates Western thought: it works by binary branching from a central root or trunk, by tracing rather than mapping, by the law that the One becomes Two. The rhizome is the alternative: multiple entry points, no center, connection of any point to any other across heterogeneous registers, growth by the middle.

This is not merely a model — it is an ontological claim about the nature of reality, knowledge, and language. Arborescent structures are political structures: Chomsky's grammatical tree is "more fundamentally a marker of power than a syntactic marker."

## Key Claims

- **Two models:** Root-book (the classical text as image of the world) → Radicle-system (fascicular root, apparent multiplicity that still posits a compensatory unity in the subject, e.g., Joyce, misread Nietzsche) → Rhizome (true multiplicity: n − 1, always subtract the One).
- **Six principles of the rhizome:**
  1. **Connection:** any point can connect to any other point.
  2. **Heterogeneity:** connections cross registers (biological, semiotic, political, linguistic).
  3. **Multiplicity:** no subject or object unity; dimensions change in nature as connections increase.
  4. **Asignifying rupture:** a rhizome can be cut at any point and continue along new lines; it is anti-memory and anti-genealogy.
  5. **Cartography:** a rhizome is a map, not a tracing. "The map does not reproduce an unconscious closed in upon itself; it constructs the unconscious."
  6. **Decalcomania:** the rhizome has multiple entrances, never a fixed origin.
- **Political corollary:** "Chomsky's grammaticality, the categorical S symbol that dominates every sentence, is more fundamentally a marker of power than a syntactic marker."
- **Becoming vs. imitation:** The orchid/wasp example — not mimicry but "capture of code," aparallel evolution: "a becoming-wasp of the orchid and a becoming-orchid of the wasp."
- **The book-world relation:** "A book has neither object nor subject; it is made of variously formed matters, and very different dates and speeds. To attribute the book to a subject is to overlook this working of matters."

## Key Quotations

> "The multiple must be made, not by always adding a higher dimension, but rather in the simplest of ways, by dint of sobriety — always n − 1."

> "The rhizome is an acentered, nonhierarchical, nonsignifying system without a General and without an organizing memory or central automaton, defined solely by a circulation of states."

> "The rhizome is altogether different, a map and not a tracing... The map does not reproduce an unconscious closed in upon itself; it constructs the unconscious."

## How Different Thinkers Use This

- **[[Thinkers/Wittgenstein]] (Family Resemblance, Language-Games):** structural resonance — Wittgenstein's family resemblance also rejects essential unity and insists on lateral connections. But Wittgenstein remains descriptive (how language *is* used); Deleuze is constructive (what to *build*).
- **[[Thinkers/Plato]]:** the arborescent model is implicitly Platonic — hierarchy from the One. Deleuze's rhizome is the direct anti-Platonic ontological proposal.
- **[[Thinkers/Heidegger]]:** Heidegger's Being-question still privileges a foundational question as origin. Deleuze's rhizome begins in the middle.

## Contradictions / Open Questions

- > [!warning] The rhizome as *model* seems self-undermining: a model of non-modeled thought is still a representation. Deleuze is aware of this and frames the rhizome as a "practice" rather than a theory, but the tension remains.
