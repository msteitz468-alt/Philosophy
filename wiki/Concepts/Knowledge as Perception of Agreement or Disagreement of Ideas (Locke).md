# Knowledge as Perception of Agreement or Disagreement of Ideas (Locke)

> "Knowledge then seems to me to be nothing but THE PERCEPTION OF THE CONNEXION OF AND AGREEMENT, OR DISAGREEMENT AND REPUGNANCY OF ANY OF OUR IDEAS. In this alone it consists." Where this perception is, there is knowledge; else we fancy, guess, or believe but come short.

Four sorts:
1. IDENTITY or DIVERSITY (first act; white not black; what is, is).
2. RELATION (abstract relations; e.g., math triangles equal).
3. CO-EXISTENCE or NECESSARY CONNEXION (in same subject, esp. substances; e.g., gold fixed).
4. REAL EXISTENCE (God is; I exist; particular external things).

**Degrees**:
- Intuitive (immediate, without deduction; highest certainty, irresistible; e.g., self-evident, 1+2=3).
- Demonstrative (by intermediate ideas/proofs, as geometry; each step intuitive; certainty but requires memory/effort).
- Sensitive (of particular external existences via sensation; lower but practical assurance for life; "an assurance that deserves the name of knowledge").

Knowledge of existence: self (intuitive — "I think..."); God (demonstrative from own + idea of necessary being); other finite things (sensitive, with limits — not as certain as the others, but sufficient).

Knowledge limited to our ideas and their agreements (not full real essences of substances; not all of existence). Probability for the rest (conduct of life).

## Vault Relevance

- **With Hume**: Directly grounds Hume's Fork (relations of ideas = intuitive/demonstrative; matters of fact from experience/co-existence/probability) and the "flames" (sophistry beyond these).
- **Wiki / claims**: A claim is "known" only if we perceive the agreement/disagreement of the relevant ideas (traceable to sources). Intuitive = clear from index/GROK; demonstrative = step-by-step synthesis; sensitive = direct from raw. Limits = don't overclaim beyond ideas/sources.
- **AI**: Knowledge = perception of relations in representations. Degrees match (direct, reasoned, perceptual/grounded). Limits = uncertainty quantification, probability for action.
