# Creation of Phenomena (Hacking)

> Many experiments do not discover or record pre-existing natural regularities in a pure state; they *create* the phenomena that are then studied. "Talk of repeating experiments is misleading. Experiments are not repeated but improved until phenomena can be elicited regularly."

## Core Idea

Experimental work often involves constructing stable, reproducible effects that may not have existed in isolable form in nature prior to the apparatus and procedures. The "phenomenon" (e.g., a particular electromagnetic effect, a purified beam, a regular interference pattern) is the product of skilled intervention and refinement of equipment, materials, and conditions.

"Repeating" an experiment is usually a matter of successive improvements until the effect becomes a reliable, on-demand tool or object of study rather than a one-off curiosity.

## Examples (from Hacking)

- Electromagnetic effects: many require specific arrangements that do not occur "naturally" in pure form; the lab produces the clean version.
- The broader history of experiment (optics, thermodynamics, solid state, radio astronomy) shows phenomena being stabilized through technological iteration.
- Microscopes and detectors: the images or signals we trust are produced by the instrument + specimen preparation + environment; convergence across independent physical systems strengthens the reality claim.

## Philosophical Payoff

- Undermines simple "observation of nature" pictures (positivist or naive empiricist).
- Supports entity realism: once a phenomenon is stably created and manipulable, the entities used to produce and detect it (lenses, fields, particles, beams) are treated as real tools.
- Ties to "experiment has a life of its own": the creation and stabilization of phenomena can proceed with significant independence from theoretical prediction or explanation.
- Challenges overly strong theory-ladenness theses: the work of making the phenomenon regular is often practical and instrumental before (or alongside) full theoretical articulation.

## Relation to Other Vault Content

- Central to Hacking's contrast with purely representational or theory-first views (Kuhnian normal science under a paradigm can include measurement and phenomenon-creation that is not just puzzle-solving within a fixed theory).
- Complements [[Concepts/Observation as a Skill (Hacking)]]: creating the conditions for reliable observation is itself skilled work.
- Links to [[Concepts/If You Can Spray Them Then They Are Real (Hacking)]]: the created phenomena become further sites where entities are used as interveners.
- Historical resonance with Baconian topics (Hacking's Ch. 15): Bacon already emphasized active manipulation ("twist the lion's tail") to wrest secrets from nature; Hacking updates this with modern examples and anti-Lakatos cautions about how theory can warp the telling of experimental history (e.g., Michelson-Morley).

## Vault Meta / AI

In knowledge work and agentic systems, many valuable "phenomena" (reliable behaviors, benchmark results, stable capabilities) are *created* by careful scaffolding, prompt engineering, tool composition, and iterative refinement rather than merely observed in the wild. "Reproducing" a result is usually improving the apparatus (context, verifier, memory, retrieval) until the effect is regular and inspectable. The wiki grows by creating stable, linkable, intervenable conceptual phenomena.
