# Reality and the External World (Heidegger)
> "The 'scandal of philosophy' does not consist in the fact that this proof is still lacking up to now, but in the fact that such proofs are expected and attempted again and again."

## Summary

*Being and Time* §43. The "problem of the external world" is a pseudo-problem: cognition is a *founded mode* of being-in-the-world, so whoever raises the question is already amidst a disclosed world. [[Thinkers/Kant]] called it a scandal that no proof of the external world had been given (his Refutation of Idealism); Heidegger inverts the scandal — the demand for proof itself betrays the Cartesian starting point. Kant's refutation "takes the subject, the 'in me,' as the starting point," so "ontologically the position of Descartes would, after all, be retained"; at best it proves the co-presence of two objectively present things, never the connection of "in me" and "outside me." "Dasein defies such proofs, because it always already is in its being what the later proofs first deem necessary to demonstrate for it" (§43a).

**Against realism**: it agrees with Heidegger's result (innerworldly beings are disclosed) but wrongly holds the world's reality *needs and admits of* proof, and explains being ontically, by beings.

**For (and against) idealism**: "If idealism emphasizes the fact that being and reality are only 'in consciousness,' this expresses the understanding that being cannot be explained by beings" — then "Aristotle was no less of an idealist than Kant." But if idealism reduces beings to an undetermined subject, it is "methodologically no less naive than the grossest realism," having failed to analyze the being of consciousness itself.

**Reality dependent on care**: "Being (not beings) is dependent upon the understanding of being; that is, reality (not the real) is dependent upon care" (§43c). Beings are not created by Dasein; but *reality* — the intelligibility of beings as real — is possible only while Dasein exists. Dilthey's and Scheler's account of reality as *resistance* already presupposes the disclosed world. This is the exact structure of the truth claim of §44 (see [[Concepts/Truth as Unconcealment (Heidegger)]]: "Newton's laws … became true through Newton").

## Key Claims / Positions

### How This Compares to Other Thinkers in the Wiki

- **[[Thinkers/Descartes]]**: source of the pseudo-problem — the encapsulated *res cogitans* that must bridge to the world; Heidegger: Dasein "is always already 'outside' together with some being encountered" (§13).
- **[[Thinkers/Kant]]**: praised for seeing an ontological connection to time, condemned for accepting the demand for proof.
- **[[Thinkers/George Berkeley]] / idealism**: partially vindicated — being is never explicable by beings — but ontically naive about the being of mind.
- **[[Thinkers/G.E. Moore]] / common-sense realism** (where present): "Here is a hand" answers the skeptic ontically; Heidegger's dissolution is ontological — the question itself is malformed. Cf. also [[Thinkers/Edmund Husserl]]'s epoché, which *suspends* the world's being rather than showing the suspension impossible.

## Contradictions / Open Questions

- > [!warning] "Reality is dependent upon care" invites the idealist reading Heidegger disowns; the being/beings distinction does the work, and critics (and the later marginalia) doubted it can bear the load. Conflicts with scientific-realist positions in the vault (e.g. [[Sources/Representing and Intervening - Ian Hacking (1983)|Hacking's entity realism]]) — flagged, not reconciled.
- The skeptic "can no more be refuted than truth proved" (§44c) — Heidegger dissolves rather than answers skepticism; whether that satisfies is a live question against [[Thinkers/Hume]] and [[Thinkers/Karl Popper]].
- > [!warning] Direct methodological opposite of [[Arguments/Cartesian Skepticism and the Simulation Riposte (Chalmers)]]: where Heidegger calls the demand for a proof of the external world the real "scandal of philosophy" and dissolves the problem's premises (Dasein is already being-in-the-world), Chalmers stays inside the Cartesian problematic and answers it on its own terms via [[Concepts/Simulation Realism (Chalmers)]]. Two rival cures for the same disease, ingested the same season.
