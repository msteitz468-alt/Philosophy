# Phenomenological Method and Destruktion (Heidegger)
> "Ontology is possible only as phenomenology" — and the recovery of the question of Being requires a positive *Destruktion* of the history of ontology (Kant → Descartes → Aristotle).

## Summary

*Being and Time* opens (Introduction, §§1–8) by re-posing the forgotten question of the meaning of Being against three prejudices that sanction its neglect: that "being" is the most universal concept, that it is indefinable, and that it is self-evident. Heidegger answers: "The indefinability of being does not dispense with the question of its meaning but forces it upon us" (§1). Being is not a genus — its universality "surpasses" generic universality (the medieval *transcendens*; [[Thinkers/Aristotle]]'s unity of analogy "placed the problem of being on a fundamentally new basis").

Every question has a formal structure (§2): what is asked about (*Gefragtes* — being), what is interrogated (*Befragtes* — beings, specifically Dasein), and what is to be ascertained (*Erfragtes* — the meaning of being). Interrogating [[Concepts/Dasein (Heidegger)|Dasein]] is not circular: "presupposing" being is a preliminary look that lays "bare and exhibits the ground," not a deduction.

### The Phenomenological Method (§7)

Phenomenology is a *method*, not a standpoint or school. Heidegger dismantles the word into its Greek components:

- **Phenomenon** — "what shows itself in itself," distinguished from semblance (*Schein*) and mere appearance (*Erscheinung*).
- **Logos** — apophantic discourse, *letting something be seen*; truth as *aletheia*, un-concealment, not correspondence (see [[Concepts/Truth as Unconcealment (Heidegger)]]).
- **Phenomenology** — letting what shows itself be seen from itself. Its distinctive theme is precisely what does *not* show itself initially: the *being* of beings, which is covered over. Hence the phenomenology of Dasein is **hermeneutics** — description whose very meaning is interpretation.

"Higher than actuality stands possibility. We can understand phenomenology solely by seizing upon it as a possibility" (§7). Heidegger credits [[Thinkers/Edmund Husserl]]: the investigations "would not have been possible without the foundation laid by Edmund Husserl" — yet he transforms [[Schools/Phenomenology]] from a science of consciousness into fundamental ontology.

### Destruktion of the History of Ontology (§6)

Because Dasein's temporality grounds its historicity, tradition both transmits and *covers over* its own "wellsprings." A **destruction** (*Destruktion*) of the history of ontology is therefore required — explicitly *positive*, not a demolition: it stakes out the tradition's genuine possibilities and limits. The planned stages (never published as Part Two): [[Thinkers/Kant]] (schematism and time), [[Thinkers/Descartes]] (the *cogito sum* whose *sum* is left undetermined), [[Thinkers/Aristotle]] (the treatise on time that governed every later account "including Bergson"). The guiding discovery: Greek ontology understood being as *ousia/parousia* — constant **presence** — i.e., tacitly from one temporal mode, the present. Exposing this is the hinge between the analytic of Dasein and the interpretation of time as the horizon of Being.

### Priority of the Question (§§3–4)

- **Ontological priority**: sciences advance through crises in their basic concepts (foundations of mathematics, relativity, biology, theology); regional ontologies presuppose fundamental ontology.
- **Ontic priority**: Dasein alone is "ontically distinguished by the fact that in its being this being is concerned about its very being"; "the ontic distinction of Dasein lies in the fact that it is ontological" (§4). Hence fundamental ontology = the existential analytic of Dasein. Ontic self-understanding is *existentiell*; the ontological structure is *existential*.

## Key Claims / Positions

### How This Compares to Prior Thinkers in the Wiki

- **[[Thinkers/Edmund Husserl]]**: retains "to the things themselves" but relocates phenomenology from transcendental consciousness and the [[Concepts/Phenomenological Reduction and Epoché (Husserl)|epoché]] to the hermeneutics of existence; the phenomenon par excellence is Being, not the noema.
- **[[Thinkers/Descartes]]**: the methodological villain — the *cogito* investigated, the *sum* "left completely undiscussed"; medieval ontology (*ens creatum*) imported into the *res cogitans* as "an ill-fated prejudice."
- **[[Thinkers/Kant]]**: "the first and only one who traversed a stretch of the path toward investigating the dimension of temporality" (the schematism, "an art hidden in the depths of the human soul"), but blocked by Descartes' subject.
- **[[Thinkers/Aristotle]]**: both the high point (analogy of being) and origin of the covering-over (being as presence).

## Contradictions / Open Questions

- > [!warning] The *Destruktion* (Part Two) was never published — the method's historical half remains a promissory note; cf. [[Concepts/Historicality and the Turn (Heidegger)]].
- Hermeneutic phenomenology openly embraces the circle of interpretation that [[Thinkers/Edmund Husserl]]'s presuppositionless science sought to avoid.
