# Cinema - Time-Image and Movement-Image (Deleuze)
> Deleuze distinguishes two regimes of the cinematic image: the movement-image (pre-war cinema, organized by sensory-motor action) and the time-image (post-war cinema, organized by pure optical-sound situations that show time directly).

## Summary

In *Cinema 1: The Movement-Image* (1983) and *Cinema 2: The Time-Image* (1985), Deleuze develops a philosophy of cinema as a philosophy of the image and of time — not as a history of film but as a taxonomy of image-types.

**Movement-image regime:** Pre-war cinema (Hollywood and its contemporaries) operates via **sensory-motor schemata** — characters perceive situations, which provoke actions, which resolve situations. The movement-image is organized around the action-image: stimulus → response. It operates in a "living present" where the whole is always implied through action.

**The frame and the out-of-field:** The cinematic frame determines a relatively closed system; it tends toward saturation or rarefaction. The out-of-field has two dimensions: (a) extension to unseen sets (relative) and (b) opening onto duration and the whole (absolute). "The whole is the open, and relates back to time or even to spirit rather than to content and to space."

**Time-image regime:** Post-war cinema (Italian neorealism, French New Wave — Rossellini, Godard, Rivette) breaks with sensory-motor schemata. Characters encounter situations they can no longer respond to motorically — they become **seers** rather than agents. The result is the **pure optical-sound image**: "a cliché is a sensory-motor image of the thing... if our sensory-motor schemata jam or break, then a different type of image can appear: a pure optical-sound image, the whole image without metaphor, brings out the thing in itself, literally, in its excess of horror or beauty."

**What time-images show:** Time directly, rather than through movement. Three image-types in the time-image regime:
- **Chronosign:** image of time itself (crystal-images, sheets of past, peaks of present).
- **Lectosign:** readable image — requires interpretation, not just perception.
- **Noosign:** thinking image — image that makes you think rather than act.

## Key Claims

- The movement-image subordinates time to movement; the time-image subordinates movement to time (time becomes the primary category).
- Post-war historical trauma (WWII, the Holocaust) made the action-image untenable — the intolerable cannot be resolved by action.
- Cinema is a new form of philosophy — it creates concepts through images, not words.

## Contradictions / Open Questions

- > [!warning] Deleuze's taxonomy of image-types is philosophical, not empirical — many films do not fall cleanly into either regime. The historical periodization (pre-/post-war) is approximate, and Deleuze acknowledges overlaps.
