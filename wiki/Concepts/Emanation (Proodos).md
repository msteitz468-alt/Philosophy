# Emanation (Proodos)

> In Plotinus' Neoplatonism (*Enneads*), emanation (Greek *proodos*, "procession" or "going forth") is the necessary, non-temporal, undiminished "overflow" by which the One gives rise to the hierarchy of reality: One → Intellect (Nous) → Soul → Nature/Matter. It is not creation by will or fiat (no demiurge deciding to make the world), not production that depletes the source, and not a fall or accident. Each subsequent level is an "image" or likeness of the prior, participating in it while introducing greater multiplicity, distance from unity, and (at the lowest) privation. The process is eternal and spontaneous — like light radiating from the sun or a spring overflowing — preserving the perfection and simplicity of the One while accounting for the ordered multiplicity of the cosmos.

## Core Elements from the Source and Context

- **Undiminished Giving**: The One remains fully itself, simple, and perfect; emanation does not divide or lessen it. "The One is all things and no one of them" (Ennead V.2 etc.). Posterior hypostases are "less" only in the sense of greater multiplicity and dependence, not in a diminishment of the source.
- **Hierarchical Order (Hypostases)**: 
  - One (pure unity, beyond intellect/being).
  - Intellect (Nous): first emanation; thinks the One and thereby generates the Forms as its own thoughts; unity-in-multiplicity.
  - Soul: emanates from Intellect; introduces time, motion, and the capacity for descent into bodies while retaining orientation to the higher; World Soul + individual souls.
  - Matter/Nature: last, furthest image; the substrate of the sensible world, indeterminate, source of change and multiplicity (evil as absence of form/good, not a positive rival principle).
- **Reversion as Complement**: Emanation (going forth) is complemented by *epistrophe* (return/reversion). Everything naturally strives back toward its source; the soul's ascent is the reversal of its procession.
- **Not Creation Ex Nihilo or Demiurgic**: Contrasts with later Abrahamic creatio ex nihilo (will of a personal God) and Plato's Timaeus (demiurge crafts the world as a living image according to the Forms, with some voluntarism). Plotinus' version is more impersonal, necessary, and "generous" (overflow of plenitude).
- **Image and Participation**: Each level is an image (*eikon*) of the prior and participates (*methexis*) in it. The sensible world is "in the likeness" of the intelligible; matter is the extreme of otherness.

The reference file (raw/The Enneads - Plotinus.txt) signals the treatises on origins and order (e.g., V.1–2, VI.9) where the dynamics are worked out in detail.

- **Classical Contrasts**:
  - Vs. Plato (Timaeus): More impersonal and necessary (no demiurge deliberating); stronger emphasis on the dynamic outflow and the soul's natural reversion.
  - Vs. Aristotle: The highest is beyond the thinking Intellect (Aristotle's god as self-thinking thought is Plotinus' second hypostasis).
  - Vs. Stoics: Emanation is transcendent and hierarchical; Stoic logos/pneuma is immanent and providential within the world.
  - Vs. Epicurus: Opposite of blind atomic collisions + swerve in the void (no guiding hierarchy or purposeful return; multiple worlds are accidental, not images of a higher One).
  - Vs. Thucydides: The "real" generative order is intelligible/emanative, not the apparent flux of power and revolt (though the material image has its own reality and must be navigated).
