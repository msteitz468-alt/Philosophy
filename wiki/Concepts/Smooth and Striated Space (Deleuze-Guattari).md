# Smooth and Striated Space (Deleuze-Guattari)
> Smooth space is the open, intensive, directional space of nomads and haptic perception; striated space is the closed, extensive, metric space of sedentary institutions and optical perception.

## Summary

Drawing on the art historian Alois Riegl and his distinction between haptic (touch-like, close-range) and optical (distant, form-grasping) vision, and on the mathematician Riemann's distinction between smooth and metric manifolds, Deleuze and Guattari develop a topology of space that applies across aesthetics, politics, and ontology.

**Striated space** is organized by fixed and variable elements — a fabric where threads/warp are fixed and the thread/weft is variable but constrained. It is metric, optical, directed from point to point, bounded. The State apparatus inhabits and produces striated space; it "striates" smooth spaces.

**Smooth space** is directional rather than metric — occupied by intensive magnitudes, events, haecceities, winds, sounds, forces. It is nomadic, haptic, operated not by fixed coordinates but by trajectories. The war machine inhabits smooth space.

**Nomad art** (Essay 19): The abstract line of nomadic art is a variable, mutant line without contour — it positively expresses smooth space rather than delimiting a form against a background. "A line that delimits nothing, that describes no contour, that no longer goes from one point to another but instead passes between points... constantly changing direction, a mutant line of this kind... is truly an abstract line, and describes a smooth space."

**Cinema and space** (Essay 20): The cinematic frame determines a relatively closed system; the "out-of-field" has a relative dimension (extension to unseen sets) and an absolute dimension (opening onto duration and the whole). "The whole is the open, and relates back to time or even to spirit rather than to content and to space."

**Cinema and time** (Essay 21): Post-war cinema (Italian neorealism, French New Wave) breaks from the sensory-motor action-image to produce pure optical and sound situations — "pure optical-sound images" that confront reality directly without motor resolution. This produces the time-image (chronosign), which shows time directly rather than through movement. See [[Concepts/Cinema - Time-Image and Movement-Image (Deleuze)]].

## Key Claims

- Smooth space is occupied by intensities, forces, events; striated space by forms, subjects, functions.
- The nomadic abstract line: "constant change of direction... positively motivated by smooth space."
- The State constantly striates smooth space; the war machine constantly smooths striated space.
- There is no pure smooth space — smooth and striated spaces always mix and convert into each other.
