# Pythagorean Harmonics
> The mathematical theory of musical concord — the one domain where the Pythagorean program of "reality as number" delivered genuine, testable science. Andrew Barker's reconstruction of *kanonikos* harmonics.

## Summary
On Andrew Barker's chapter in [[Sources/A History of Pythagoreanism - Carl A. Huffman (ed.) (2014)]], the Pythagorean harmonic theorists are the ***kanonikoi*** — distinguished by Ptolemaïs of Cyrene from the Aristoxenian *mousikoi*: the *kanonikoi* "proceed on the basis of **reason** (*logos*)" and the monochord (*kanōn*), the *mousikoi* on **sense-perception**. Both are "musicians" generically, but the Pythagorean program grounds music in number rather than in the trained ear.

## The Core Doctrine
- **Concords as ratios.** The primary concords correspond to simple ratios: the **octave 2:1**, the **fifth 3:2**, the **fourth 4:3** — the famous numbers of the *tetractys* (1,2,3,4).
- **Physical acoustics.** Early Pythagoreans grounded pitch in a *quantitative* attribute — the speed or frequency of impacts on the air. **[[Thinkers/Archytas]]** (fr. 1) was the first to analyze the high/low pitch difference this way.
- **No separation of domains.** Early Pythagoreans did *not* split a mathematical realm from an audible one; that division was Plato's contribution (*Republic*), using tools the Pythagoreans themselves lacked.
- **Philolaus' integration.** [[Thinkers/Philolaus]] (frs. 6, 6a) fused the musical and the metaphysical: *harmonia* = the octave, which "locks together" limiters and unlimiteds — "all things that are known have number." This is the bridge to [[Concepts/Number as the Essence of Reality (Pythagoras)]].

## Mathematization: The *Sectio Canonis*
The principle that concordant intervals must have **multiple or epimoric (superparticular) ratios** was systematized in the *Sectio canonis* (c. 300 BC, the Euclidean tradition). This generated *mathematically-based refutations of empirical claims* — e.g. the octave-plus-fourth (8:3) cannot be a concord, and the tone (9:8) cannot be divided into two equal halves. The "Pythagorean diatonic," using the ***leimma*** (256:243), underlies the construction of the **World-Soul** in Plato's *Timaeus*.

## Who Actually Did It
Barker is firmly source-critical:
- **Pythagoras himself contributed nothing demonstrable** to harmonics; the "harmonious blacksmith" story is "pure fiction" (the physics is false — see [[Thinkers/Pythagoras]]).
- **[[Thinkers/Hippasus]]** was the real pioneer (the metal-disc demonstrations confirming the concord ratios — bronze discs of relative thicknesses 12:9:8:6).
- **[[Thinkers/Philolaus]]** integrated it metaphysically; **[[Thinkers/Archytas]]** perfected it (the three means) — and was Plato's principal *target* in the *Republic* for being insufficiently "rational" (relying too much on the ear). The later harmony-of-the-spheres construction (Nicomachus' planetary scale) extends this into cosmology — see [[Concepts/Harmony of the Spheres]].
