# Logical Supervenience and Reductive Explanation (Chalmers)
> "A natural phenomenon is reductively explainable in terms of some low-level properties precisely when it is logically supervenient on those properties."

## Summary

*The Conscious Mind* ch. 2 builds the book's engine. **Supervenience**: "B-properties supervene on A-properties if no two possible situations are identical with respect to their A-properties while differing in their B-properties." Distinctions: **local vs global** (individual vs whole-world determination — shape vs the Mona Lisa's value), and the load-bearing **logical vs natural**: logical supervenience holds across all conceivable worlds; natural (nomic) supervenience only across naturally possible ones. Kripke's image: with logical supervenience, once God fixes the physical facts "the B-facts come along for free"; with mere natural supervenience "God has to do more work... make sure there is a law."

**Materialism** is thereby defined: true iff all positive facts supervene globally-logically on the physical (matching Jackson's "every minimal physical duplicate of our world is a duplicate simpliciter"). Reductive explanation works by functional analysis (reproduction, learning, heat) and is possible exactly where logical supervenience holds.

The modal machinery is **two-dimensional semantics**: every concept has a **primary intension** (fixes actual-world reference — "watery stuff"; a priori; over centered worlds) and a **secondary intension** ("H₂O"; rigidified). Kripkean a posteriori necessity is a necessary secondary proposition with a contingent primary one — so Kripke's cases never rule out any conceivable *world*, only misdescriptions of it. Conceivability of a world entails its logical possibility.

Section 5's sweeping claim: "Conscious experience is almost unique in its failure to supervene logically." Biology, economics, sociology — all logically supervene (no skeptical "problem of other biologies"). Residual exceptions: consciousness, indexicality, negative facts (the "That's all" fact), and laws/causation (Hume).

## How Different Thinkers Use This

- **[[Thinkers/Saul Kripke]] / Putnam**: supply the a posteriori-necessity apparatus that Chalmers's 2-D framework domesticates; the intended blocker of zombie arguments becomes their enabler.
- **[[Thinkers/David Hume]]** ([[Sources/An Enquiry concerning Human Understanding - Hume (Oxford, Millican ed.)]]): laws and causation as the other great non-supervenient residue — Chalmers's list of the world's furniture is notably Humean.
- **[[Thinkers/Daniel Dennett]] / Lewis / Armstrong**: type-A materialists who accept the biconditional and so must claim consciousness *is* functionally analyzable.
- The framework returns in *Reality+*'s structuralism ([[Concepts/Structuralism and the Garden of Eden (Chalmers)]]) and underwrites [[Arguments/Zombie Argument (Chalmers)]].
