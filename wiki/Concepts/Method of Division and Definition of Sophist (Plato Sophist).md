# Method of Division and Definition of Sophist (Plato Sophist)

> The late Platonic logical method of definition (*diairesis*) by successive bifurcation of a genus, and the resolution of the Parmenidean problem of Non-Being by defining it as Otherness (Difference).

## Summary

This concept encompasses two load-bearing developments in late Platonic logic and metaphysics:
1. **The Method of Division** (*diairesis*): A formal, top-down deductive method for defining a concept by taking a broad genus and repeatedly dividing it into two mutually exclusive sub-species until the target concept is isolated.
2. **The Metaphysics of Otherness**: The resolution of Parmenides's ban on Non-Being. By demonstrating that "Non-Being" is simply **Difference (Otherness)** rather than absolute nothingness, Plato explains how falsehood, negative statements, and error are logically possible.

---

## The Method of Division (Diairesis)

In the *Sophist* and *Statesman*, Plato replaces the intuitive, dialogic search for definitions with a systematic, classification-tree method:

```
                  [ Genus: Art/Skill ]
                           |
            +--------------+--------------+
            |                             |
      [ Acquisitive ]               [ Productive ]
            |                             |
     (hunting/fishing)            (creation/manufacture)
            |                             |
      +-----+-----+                 +-----+-----+
      |           |                 |           |
 [ By Force ] [ By Consent ]   [ Images ]  [ Realities ]
```

- **Process**: Start with a broad genus (e.g., Art/Skill), split it into two sub-species (Acquisitive vs. Productive), select the branch containing the target, split it again, and repeat.
- **The Definition of the Sophist**: The interlocutors perform multiple divisions, eventually defining the sophist as a practitioner of **productive, human, image-making art**, specifically the sub-branch of **semblance-making** (creating deceptive copies that appear beautiful or true to the ignorant but lack the actual proportions of truth).

---

## Defining Non-Being as Otherness (Difference)

To maintain the definition of the sophist as an "image-maker who speaks falsehoods," the Eleatic Stranger must refute the absolute monism of Parmenides.

**The Eleatic Challenge**:
- Parmenides argued that "Non-Being is not." Since a false statement is speaking of "what is not," falsehood is a logical impossibility.

**The Solution: The Five Greatest Kinds**:
- Plato introduces the **Five Greatest Kinds** (*Megista Gene*): **Being, Motion, Rest, Sameness, and Otherness**.
- **Otherness** (*Difference*) is a universal Form that is distributed through all things.
- When we say something "is not," we are not asserting the physical existence of absolute nothingness (which is indeed impossible). We are simply asserting **Otherness**:
  - *"Motion is not Rest"* does not mean Motion is nothingness; it simply means Motion is *other than* Rest.
  - A false statement (e.g., "Theaetetus is flying") is not speaking of nothingness; it is speaking of things *other than* the facts that exist.
- Non-Being is thus rehabilitated as a positive logical category: **Difference**.

---

## Relevance to Later Philosophy

- **Aristotelian Logic**: The Method of Division directly evolved into Aristotle's method of definition by **genus and specific difference**, which became the standard definition model in classical and medieval logic (e.g., the Porphyrian Tree).
- **The Philosophy of Difference**: Prefigures modern and postmodern metaphysics (e.g., Hegel, Derrida, Deleuze) which treat "difference" or "otherness" as a primary metaphysical category rather than a mere negation of identity.
