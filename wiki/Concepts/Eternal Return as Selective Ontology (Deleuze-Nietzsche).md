# Eternal Return as Selective Ontology (Deleuze-Nietzsche)
> Deleuze reads the eternal return not as cosmological cyclicism but as a selective ontological test: only becoming-active has being; reactive forces do not return.

## Summary

The standard reading of Nietzsche's eternal return takes it as a cosmological thesis (everything recurs identically) or a practical test (act only as if you would will the act to recur eternally). Deleuze accepts the practical formulation — "whatever you will, will it in such a way that you also will its eternal return" — but finds it insufficient, and argues for a deeper **selective ontology**.

**Step 1 — Practical selection.** The thought of eternal return as imperative eliminates half-willing, provisional actions, and the "little poisons of revenge." This is its first effect.

**Step 2 — Why this isn't enough.** Reactive forces can also will their eternal return; a nihilism taken to its logical conclusion still passes the first test. So the eternal return, interpreted merely as a practical imperative, cannot by itself complete the transvaluation of values.

**Step 3 — Esoteric selection.** By completing nihilism (the will to nothingness turned against itself), the eternal return performs a second operation: becoming-reactive is expelled from the cycle. Only **becoming-active has being**. "The eternal return is the being of becoming. But becoming is double: becoming-active and becoming-reactive... only becoming-active has being."

**Step 4 — Transmutation.** The eternal return converts negation (will to nothingness) into affirmation — this is the Dionysian metamorphosis, not merely a reversal of values but a new mode of being. This is what Nietzsche names with "Overman": the form that arises when human forces encounter the forces of "unlimited finity" — the superfold. "The superman is much less than the disappearance of living men, and much more than a change of concept: it is the advent of a new form that is neither God nor man."

**Kierkegaard vs. Nietzsche.** Both oppose repetition to generality and make it the act of freedom. But Kierkegaard's theater is of faith (the leap); Nietzsche's is of cruelty and dance (unbelief). Kierkegaard restores an interior depth; Nietzsche affirms surface multiplicity.

## Key Claims

- "Only becoming-active has being."
- Eternal return is *selective* — reactive forces do not return. "The eternal return gives to the will the practical law of selection."
- The eternal return as the being of becoming: "difference and repetition" is the formula — difference is the content of becoming; repetition (eternal return) is its being.
- The Overman as superfold: the new form arising when forces within man meet silicon, the genetic code, agrammatical language — "unlimited finity" beyond both the infinite (God-form) and the finite (man-form).

## How Different Thinkers Use This

- **[[Thinkers/Nietzsche]]:** The thinker page carries the canonical readings; this page carries Deleuze's specific selective-ontological interpretation, which is a creative extension rather than strict exegesis.
- **[[Thinkers/Kant]]:** Deleuze draws an explicit structural parallel: the eternal return has the same formal role as Kant's categorical imperative (test of universalizability) but differs absolutely in content — not the form of the law but the selective power of becoming.
- **[[Thinkers/Heidegger]]:** Heidegger's Overman reading interprets Nietzsche as completing metaphysics (will to power as the essence of Being). Deleuze's Overman breaks with the man-form entirely — it is not a new metaphysics but a new ontological plane (superfold).

## Contradictions / Open Questions

- > [!warning] Conflicts with standard Nietzsche scholarship: most scholars read eternal return as cosmological or as a phenomenological test, not as selective ontology that literally expels reactive forces from being. Deleuze's reading is philosophically productive but historically contested.
