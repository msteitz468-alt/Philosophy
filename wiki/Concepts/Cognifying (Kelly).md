# Cognifying (Kelly)

> One of Kevin Kelly's 12 inevitable technological forces (2016): adding cheap, ubiquitous intelligence (AI) to everything—objects, jobs, processes, decisions—like electrification or industrialization before it. Not just humanoid robots replacing humans, but "dumb" but tireless and improving intelligences applied everywhere. AI as infrastructure. The "manufacturing cost" of cognition drops toward zero. This force is already underway and will intensify for decades as a strong bias of digital technology. It overlaps with and accelerates the other forces (tracking, questioning, flowing, becoming, etc.).

## Summary (from the source)

Kelly's analogy: electrification transformed every industry and aspect of life not by replacing human muscle with a single "humanoid electricity" but by adding cheap, reliable power to everything (lights, motors, appliances, factories, homes). Cognifying is the same for intelligence.

AI is currently "dumb" in many respects (narrow, brittle, lacking common sense), but it is:
- Tireless.
- Improving with use (data + feedback loops).
- Cheap to replicate and distribute at scale.
- Getting cheaper and better fast.

Applied to any job or object, it creates new value even before it reaches "human level." A truck doesn't need to be fully self-driving in every condition to be transformed by AI (better routing, predictive maintenance, driver assistance). A factory, a farm, a hospital, a classroom, a shoe, a search engine—everything gets a layer of cheap cognition.

Consequences Kelly highlights:
- AI as "dumb worker" that never gets tired, never needs benefits in the same way, and gets smarter the more it is used.
- New occupations and categories of work created faster than old ones are displaced (see [[Concepts/Beginning (Kelly)]]).
- The cost of answers and decisions drops dramatically (see [[Concepts/Questioning (Kelly)]]).
- Combined with tracking and flowing, it enables real-time optimization at scales previously impossible.
- Part of the larger shift: products become services (a car becomes a cognified transportation service that learns and adapts continuously).

Kelly is explicit that this is not (yet) about godlike general AI replacing humanity, but about the pervasive, infrastructural addition of intelligence to the technium. The force is "inevitable" because the underlying economics and physics of computation and data favor it.

## Relation to Other Vault Content

- **Core to the 12 forces**: Cognifying is one of the most powerful and cross-cutting (it makes tracking smarter, questioning deeper, flowing more adaptive, becoming faster, etc.). See [[Concepts/The Inevitable (Kelly)]].
- **Cross to capability/optimism (Kurzweil, 2026 AI sources)**: Direct realization of exponential progress in AI. Grok, Claude, Gemini, agentic workflows, etc., are partial but accelerating examples of cognifying in language, reasoning, tool use, and orchestration.
- **Cross to risks (Bostrom)**: The same force that creates value also creates the substrate for superintelligence, goal misalignment, and instrumental convergence. Cheap, ubiquitous, improving intelligence is exactly the capability that makes control/alignment the central problem.
- **Cross to limits (Mitchell, Hofstadter)**: Current cognifying is powerful at statistical pattern matching and narrow tasks but still lacks robust understanding, causal reasoning, common sense, and analogy (the "core of cognition" per Mitchell). Scaling + scaffolding gets us far but may not be sufficient for the full vision without new architectures. The "dumb but tireless" worker is real; the godlike replacement is not (yet).
- **Cross to mind (Hofstadter, Minsky, Dennett, Pearl)**: Cognifying is the substrate on which strange loops, societies of agents, multiple drafts, and causal models run. Pure pattern matching without structure or self-reference is limited (Pearl's rung 1 critique of deep learning).
- **Cross to earlier cybernetics (Wiener)**: Adding intelligence to machines at scale is the continuation of the "second industrial revolution" Wiener warned about—machines displacing not just muscle but judgment. The need for human "know-what" becomes more urgent, not less.
- **Vault meta**: The agent (Grok) and the wiki itself are instances of cognifying—using intelligence to filter, remix, question, track, and become. Ingest, lint, synthesis, and tool use are cognified processes layered on raw sources.

## Contradictions / Open Questions

- **"Dumb" AI still transformative?** Yes, per Kelly—electrification didn't require genius electricity to transform the world. Narrow but cheap and scalable intelligence is already reshaping economies and daily life.
- **Job creation vs. displacement**: Kelly (and the "Beginning" force) argues net creation of new work; critics argue the quality, distribution, and speed of transition matter enormously. Ties to existing vault debates on labor and meaning.
- **When does "cognifying" become the alignment problem?** The transition from useful narrow AI to systems capable of long-horizon, high-stakes agency is exactly the territory of Bostrom/Wiener concerns.
