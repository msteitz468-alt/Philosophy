# Argument from Consciousness (Jefferson)

> Professor Jefferson's Lister Oration (1949): "Not until a machine can write a sonnet or compose a concerto because of thoughts and emotions felt, and not by the chance fall of symbols, could we agree that machine equals brain — that is, not only write it but know that it had written it. No mechanism could feel (and not merely artificially signal, an easy contrivance) pleasure at its successes, grief when its valves fuse, be warmed by flattery, be made miserable by its mistakes, be charmed by sex, be angry or depressed when it cannot get what it wants."

## The Objection (§6, objection 4)

This is presented as one of the strongest and "very well expressed" contrary views. It denies the validity of the imitation game as a sufficient test: behavioral equivalence (fooling the interrogator) is not enough. The machine must have inner experience — thoughts and emotions *felt*, and meta-knowledge ("know that it had written it").

Extreme form: the only way to be sure a machine (or a man) thinks is to *be* that machine/person and feel oneself thinking. This is the solipsist position.

Turing notes that Jefferson would probably be willing to accept the imitation game (or viva voce) in practice, but the quoted statement sets a very high bar.

## Turing's Reply

1. **Solipsism**: The extreme view makes communication of ideas difficult or impossible. "A is liable to believe 'A thinks but B does not' whilst B believes 'B thinks but A does not.'" In ordinary life we adopt the polite convention that everyone thinks; we do not demand direct access to inner feelings.

2. **Practical tests already exist**: The game (with player B omitted) is used in *viva voce* examinations precisely to discover whether someone "really understands" or has "learnt it parrot fashion." Turing gives an extended specimen dialogue about a sonnet ("Shall I compare thee to a summer's day" → "It wouldn't scan"; "a winter's day" → "nobody wants to be compared to a winter's day"; Mr. Pickwick and Christmas). If a sonnet-writing machine could sustain answers of this quality and coherence under interrogation, "I do not think [Jefferson] would describe it as 'an easy contrivance.'"

3. **Mysteries of consciousness need not be solved first**: "There is, for instance, something of a paradox connected with any attempt to localise it." Turing does not claim to dispel the mystery; he claims only that it does not have to be solved before we can answer whether a machine can play the imitation game satisfactorily.

The consciousness objection is closely related to several of the "disabilities" (be the subject of its own thought, enjoy strawberries and cream, fall in love, etc.) and to the "no surprise / no originate" objection.

## Relation to Other Vault Content

- Direct engagement with [[Thinkers/Descartes]]: the *cogito* and the requirement that the thinking thing *know* its own thinking; the real distinction of *res cogitans* (whose essence is thought) from *res extensa*. Turing offers a thoroughgoing mechanistic account that treats thought as executable by a physical discrete-state machine without a separate non-mechanical substance.
- Tension with [[Concepts/Identity and Diversity - Personal Identity (Locke)]]: Locke makes consciousness ("same thinking thing") the criterion that makes a person the same over time. The imitation game tests attribution of "thinking" on behavioral grounds alone, without privileged access to the inner consciousness that Locke requires for identity.
- Ancestor to the entire qualia / inverted spectrum / Chinese Room (Searle) literature: the demand that "understanding" or "feeling" be present, not merely simulated or functionally equivalent. See [[Concepts/Chinese Room Argument (Searle)]] and [[Thinkers/John Searle]] for the later, sharper syntax/semantics version of the objection to computational/strong AI accounts of mind.
- The vault's behavioral criterion for its own success (can the Grok + tools + wiki system sustain interactions that a skilled interrogator cannot reliably distinguish from a careful human researcher?) is consciously Turingian and therefore inherits the same open tension with consciousness objections.

## Vault Meta / AI

When the agent produces a page, synthesis, or reply, the user/interrogator has no direct access to "whether it felt" the content or "knew that it had written it." Evaluation proceeds exactly as Turing recommends: by sustained, open-ended questioning (prompting, follow-ups, requests for revision, cross-checks against sources) that probes for real understanding vs. parroting. The SCHEMA, index, and prior pages function as the "store" and "instruction table" that make coherent, context-sensitive performance possible without requiring the system to possess (or the user to verify) inner phenomenal states. The "polite convention" is extended to the agent for the duration of productive work.
