# Household Management and the Ranking of Wealth-Getting (Pseudo-Aristotle)

> Household management (*oikonomia*) is the art prior to and component of statecraft, covering the natural relations of husband/wife and master/slave and the fourfold competence — acquiring, preserving, improving, using — over property. Modes of acquiring wealth are ranked ethically: agriculture (most honest, character-forming) above extraction, trade/wage-labor, and war.

## Summary

The *Economics* opens by deriving household management from the same acquire-then-conduct structure as statecraft, but locates it as prior: a nation is an assemblage of households sufficient for civilized life, and the household is therefore "older" than the nation it composes. The household has two parts, persons and property. Governing the human part begins with the most natural relation, husband and wife — framed not merely as biological procreation (as among lower animals) but as cooperation toward a distinctively human "happy existence," with man and woman assigned complementary (not identical) natural faculties by design. The master/slave relation is treated as an extension of property management to human chattels: good slave-management is itself mean-structured, avoiding both the insolence bred by unearned indulgence and the resentment/weakness bred by harsh deprivation, with the prospect of eventual freedom recommended as a just and expedient incentive. Governing the material part of the household requires four distinct competences — acquiring, preserving, improving, and using property — and a ranked hierarchy of acquisition modes: agriculture is most honest and character-forming (wealth not derived from other men, and physically toughening); mining/extraction is honest but non-generative; trade and wage-labor draw wealth from others by consent; war draws it by force. Personal, hands-on supervision by the master and mistress ("no one takes the same care of another's property as of his own") is repeatedly urged over full delegation to stewards.

## Key Claims / Positions

- Household management is prior to and structurally continuous with statecraft, differing mainly in that a household has a single ruler while a nation's rule is distributed.
- Gender roles are naturalized as complementary rather than identical: differing faculties assigned by "Divine Providence" to the same shared end (the household's preservation and flourishing).
- Slave management follows the doctrine-of-the-mean logic found in the *Nicomachean Ethics*: neither indulgence-without-discipline nor discipline-without-sustenance, but proportionate work, chastisement, and food, calibrated to desert.
- Property management requires four distinct skills (acquire, preserve, improve, use), and productive property should always exceed unproductive, with total risk never concentrated at once.
- A clear ethical ranking of wealth-getting modes: agriculture > mining > trade/wage-labor > war — anticipating the *Politics* Book I distinction between natural household wealth-getting and unnatural, unlimited *chrematistike* (money-making for its own sake).
- Personal supervision (the "master's eye," the "master's footprints") is held superior to full delegation, on both moral and practical-efficiency grounds.

## How Pseudo-Aristotle Uses This

Extends the *Nicomachean Ethics* doctrine of the mean (see the forthcoming [[Concepts/Doctrine of the Mean (Aristotle)]] from the same day's Nicomachean Ethics ingest) into concrete domestic administration, and anticipates the *Politics*' natural/unnatural wealth-getting distinction without yet developing the money-as-an-end critique that Book I of the *Politics* makes explicit.
