# Care (Sorge) (Heidegger)
> The being of Dasein is care — "ahead-of-itself-Being-already-in-(the-world) as Being-alongside (entities encountered within-the-world)."

## Summary

*Sorge* (care) is the **basic unifying structure** of [[Concepts/Dasein (Heidegger)|Dasein]]'s being. It is not a feeling, an attitude, or an ethical disposition but the ontological constitution that makes feelings, attitudes, and ethics possible. Heidegger's formal definition: care is "ahead-of-itself-Being-already-in-(the-world) as Being-alongside (entities encountered within-the-world)" (BT 192).

### The Threefold Structure

Care integrates Dasein's three equiprimordial dimensions:

1. **Ahead-of-itself** (*Sich-vorweg-sein*): Dasein always projects itself toward possibilities — it is oriented to the future, running ahead into what it can be. This is the existential moment of **existence** (*Existenz*).

2. **Already-in-the-world** (*Schon-sein-in-der-Welt*): Dasein finds itself always already *thrown* into a factical situation it did not choose — born into a language, a culture, a body, a historical moment. This is the moment of **facticity** (*Faktizität*), oriented to the past.

3. **Being-alongside entities** (*Sein-bei*): Dasein is absorbed in its everyday dealings with equipment and others — hammering, talking, computing. This is the moment of **falling** (*Verfallen*), oriented to the present.

These three moments correspond to the three **ecstases of temporality** (future, having-been-ness, present), which is why care is ultimately grounded in [[Concepts/Temporality (Heidegger)|temporality]].

### Concern and Solicitude

Care manifests in two modes:
- **Concern** (*Besorgen*): care directed toward ready-to-hand entities — using tools, managing tasks.
- **Solicitude** (*Fürsorge*): care directed toward other people — helping, guiding, competing.

Both are modifications of care, not separate phenomena. "Care" in Heidegger's sense is not "caring about" in the moral sense (though moral caring is one ontic expression of it); it is the structural fact that Dasein's being *matters to it*.

### Care and the Scandal of Philosophy

The unity of care dissolves the traditional "scandal of philosophy" — the supposed failure to prove the external world. If Dasein's being is care, then Dasein is always already *in* a world, dealing with entities, alongside others. The question "Does the external world exist?" presupposes a worldless subject who must somehow reach the world — precisely the Cartesian abstraction that care's threefold structure replaces. As Heidegger puts it, the real scandal is not the absence of a proof but that such a proof is thought necessary.

### Care and Philosophical Error

Even philosophical errors are rooted in care's structure. Dasein's everyday fallenness — its absorption in the present and the "they" — generates systematic distortions: idle talk (*Gerede*) passes on claims severed from their evidential basis; curiosity (*Neugier*) pursues novelty without depth; ambiguity (*Zweideutigkeit*) obscures who genuinely understands what. These are not failures of individual intellect but structural features of inauthentic Dasein.

## From Being and Time (Primary Text)

Stambaugh's rendering of §41: "the being of Dasein means *being-ahead-of-oneself-already-in* (the world) *as being-together-with* (innerworldly beings encountered). This being fills in the significance of the term care." Care is won methodologically from [[Concepts/Anxiety (Angst) (Heidegger)|anxiety]] (§40), the attunement that discloses the structural whole; care precedes will, wish, predilection, and urge, which are all founded on it, and is prior to any distinction of "theory" and "praxis." §42 offers a pre-ontological confirmation: the Latin *Cura* fable (Hyginus, via Burdach) — Care shapes *homo* from *humus*, and Saturn ("time") awards her possession of it "as long as it lives"; the double sense of *cura* (anxious effort / devoted dedication) mirrors thrown project.

Division Two deepens the structure twice: care proves to harbor **guilt** — "Care, the being of Dasein, thus means, as thrown project: being the (null) ground of a nullity" (§58, see [[Concepts/Conscience, Guilt, and Resoluteness (Heidegger)]]) — and its ontological *meaning* is temporality: "Temporality reveals itself as the meaning of authentic care" (§65). §43c states the strongest consequence: "Being (not beings) is dependent upon the understanding of being; that is, reality (not the real) is dependent upon care" — see [[Concepts/Reality and the External World (Heidegger)]].

## Key Claims / Positions

### How Different Thinkers Approach This

- **[[Thinkers/Aristotle]]**: The closest Aristotelian parallel is *orexis* (desire/striving) as the basic orientation of living beings. But for Aristotle, the highest human activity is *theoria* (contemplation) — disengaged beholding. Heidegger's care makes practical engagement, not contemplation, ontologically primary.
- **[[Thinkers/Descartes]]**: The *cogito* isolates a thinking substance; there is no structural moment of being-already-in-the-world. Heidegger: the cogito abstracts from care's full structure and generates the pseudo-problem of the external world.
- **[[Thinkers/Kant]]**: Kant's practical reason (the moral law as a fact of reason) captures something of care's futural dimension (ahead-of-itself), but Kant separates theoretical and practical reason in a way Heidegger's unified structure of care does not.
- **[[Thinkers/Spinoza]]**: *Conatus* (striving to persist in being) parallels care's "ahead-of-itself" moment, but conatus applies to all entities, whereas care is restricted to Dasein.

## Contradictions / Open Questions

- > [!warning] Care integrates existence, facticity, and fallenness as "equiprimordial" — but if fallenness is inauthentic, is it genuinely *equal* to the other moments? The charge of a hidden evaluative hierarchy persists.
- Heidegger claims care is not a feeling, yet the word's ordinary connotations (worry, concern) constantly pull interpretation toward the affective. The tension between technical and ordinary senses is permanent.
