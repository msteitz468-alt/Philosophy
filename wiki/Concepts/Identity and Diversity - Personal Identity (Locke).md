# Identity and Diversity - Personal Identity (Locke)

> Identity consists in a thing existing at a determined time and place, excluding all of the same kind. One thing cannot have two beginnings of existence; two things one beginning. PRINCIPIUM INDIVIDUATIONIS is existence itself.

For substances: God (eternal, everywhere); finite spirits (determined beginning); bodies (same particles/mass if no addition/subtraction; division creates distinct).

For living things (vegetables, animals, man): not the same mass of particles but the same continued LIFE communicated to different particles united to the same organized body (oak from plant still the same oak; colt to horse the same horse).

For man: same continued life in a fitly organized body (shape matters; not just soul — an embryo and adult are the same man; a prince's soul in a cobbler's body would be the same *person* but not the same *man* to others).

**Personal identity**: "a thinking intelligent being, that has reason and reflection, and can consider itself as itself, the same thinking thing, in different times and places" — by *consciousness* (inseparable from thinking; "it is impossible for any one to perceive without PERCEIVING that he does perceive"). Consciousness makes personal identity and extends as far as it reaches back to past actions/thoughts. Different substances can be united into one person by the same consciousness (analogous to different bodies united into one animal by life). Body goes to making the man in ordinary language. Prince/cobbler thought experiment illustrates: same person (accountable for prince's actions) but not same man. For resurrection: same consciousness (with possibly different body) makes the same person.

## Vault Relevance

- **With Hume**: Personal identity by consciousness (no impression of enduring self as substance) directly feeds Hume's bundle/self scepticism and naturalism.
- **With Descartes**: Consciousness as self vs. cogito as substantial res cogitans; prince/cobbler challenges pure substance identity.
- **AI / agents / self**: Persistent identity via "consciousness" (memory/context of past actions/states) rather than fixed substrate (different "bodies" = model versions or sessions). "Same person" across changes = long-term memory/continuity in agents. Prince/cobbler for role/persona shifts. Turing's Imitation Game tests attribution of "the same thinking thing" on purely behavioral/functional grounds (text outputs only), without direct access to the inner consciousness Locke requires for personal identity — exactly the tension flagged in the source-summary for the 1950 paper. See [[Thinkers/Alan Turing]], [[Concepts/Imitation Game (Turing)]], [[Concepts/Argument from Consciousness (Jefferson)]].
- **Personal KB / wiki**: Self-tracking in the vault (goals, progress, "book of business") relies on consciousness extending back (log, index, dated pages). Identity of "Mack Steitz" across career changes is continued "life"/organization of projects/habits, not same "particles" (roles).
