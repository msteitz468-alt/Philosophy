# Form and Matter - Hylomorphism (Aristotle)

> Every sensible substance is a composite of **matter** (*hylē*, what it is made of, the potential) and **form** (*morphē*/*eidos*, what makes it the kind of thing it is, the actual). They are not two things glued together but one thing under two aspects — "the proximate matter and the form are one and the same, the one potentially, the other actually." *Metaphysics* VII–VIII.

## Summary

Aristotle's answer to both Presocratic materialism and Platonic Forms is **hylomorphism** (from *hylē*, matter + *morphē*, form). A bronze sphere is bronze (matter) shaped into sphericity (form); a human being is a body (matter) informed by soul (form). Book VIII (Eta) lays out the three senses of substance correspondingly: "the matter (that which, not being a *this* actually, is potentially a *this*), the formula or shape (that which being a *this* can be separately formulated), and the complex of the two." In any material thing, **matter is potentiality and form is actuality** ([[Concepts/Potentiality and Actuality (Aristotle)]]).

The deep problem Aristotle sets himself is the **unity of the composite**: why is the syllable *ba* not merely *b* + *a*, why is flesh not merely fire + earth lying side by side? If you only ever have ingredients plus a relation, you need a further thing to unite them, and a regress threatens. His resolution: there is no gap to bridge, because **matter and form are not two independent items**. "The juxtaposition does not consist of those things of which it is the juxtaposition" — rather, the form *is* the actuality of *this* matter, and the matter *is* the potentiality for *this* form. They are one being seen as what-it-could-be and what-it-is. Unity is built in, not added.

A consequence (VIII): **form is neither generated nor destroyed.** When a bronze sphere is made, what comes to be is the *composite*; the sphericity is not separately manufactured. Likewise "health comes from health" — the form of health in the physician's art produces health in the body. Only composites enter and leave existence; forms (essences) do not. "It is a man that produces a man."

## Key Claims / Positions

- **Hylomorphic composite**: sensible substance = matter (potential) + form (actual), unified intrinsically.
- **Matter** = the indeterminate substratum, "potentially a *this*"; not itself a substance in the primary sense ([[Concepts/Substance (Ousia) (Aristotle)]]).
- **Form** = the essence/shape, the actuality, that which is definable ([[Concepts/Essence and Definition (Aristotle)]]); substance in the primary sense.
- **Unity solved by act/potency**: no third uniting element needed; form is the actuality of *that* matter.
- **Forms are ungenerated**: generation is of composites, not of form or essence.
- Hylomorphism grounds Aristotle's **psychology** (soul as the form of a living body) and **physics** (the four causes operate on form-matter composites).

## How This Sits Among Thinkers

- **Versus the atomists** ([[Thinkers/Democritus]], [[Concepts/Atomism - Atoms and Void (Leucippus and Democritus)]]): for the atomists only matter-in-motion (atoms + void) is real; form/arrangement is derivative and purposeless. Hylomorphism makes form a genuine, irreducible principle — the seat of essence and final cause. See [[Contradictions/Atomist Materialism vs Platonic-Aristotelian Teleology]].
- **Versus [[Thinkers/Plato]]**: form is real (Aristotle agrees) but **immanent**, not separate — the form exists only as the form of *this* composite, not in a transcendent realm ([[Contradictions/Plato vs Aristotle on the Forms]]).
- **Downstream — [[Thinkers/Thomas Aquinas]]** and **[[Thinkers/Ibn Rushd]]**: hylomorphism is the backbone of scholastic metaphysics (matter/form, substantial form, the soul as form of the body), and grounds debates on individuation and personal immortality.
- **Modern rupture — Descartes**: [[Concepts/Res Cogitans and Res Extensa (Descartes)]] replaces the body-soul hylomorphic unity with two *separate substances*, abolishing substantial form and final cause in nature — the decisive break the Scientific Revolution makes with Aristotle.
- **[[Thinkers/Locke]]**: [[Concepts/Primary and Secondary Qualities (Locke)]] and the corpuscularian "real constitution" replace form with micro-structure.
