# Equipollence (Isosthenia) and the Modes

> Equipollence (isosthenia): "equality as regards credibility and the lack of it, that is, that no one of the inconsistent statements takes precedence over any other as being more credible." The engine that produces epoché (suspension of judgment) in Pyrrhonian Skepticism (Sextus Empiricus). When any claim or appearance about a non-evident matter can be opposed by an equal and opposing claim/appearance, the intellect withholds assent. The "modes" or "arguments" (10 of Aenesidemus, 5 of the later Skeptics, plus others against causes) are the specific, systematic ways of generating such oppositions. The core practice of the Skeptic Way: "to every argument an equal argument is opposed." Not a set of dogmas but a method and a pathos (what appears to the practitioner now).

## Core Elements from the *Outlines of Pyrrhonism*

**Equipollence Defined**:

When phenomena are opposed to phenomena (e.g., tower round from afar, square up close), noumena to noumena (order of heavens suggests providence; good fare ill and bad fare well suggests no providence), or mixed, and the opposed sides are equally persuasive (or unpersuasive), equipollence holds. Result: first epoché, then ataraxia.

"By 'opposed' statements we simply mean inconsistent ones, not necessarily affirmative and negative."

**The Ten Modes (Aenesidemus; "older Skeptics")**:

Handed down as ten ways (also called arguments or points) through which suspension seems to be brought about. Order adopted without prejudice. Superordinate structure: three modes (what does the judging / what is judged / both), which reduce to relativity as the most generic; the ten are subordinate.

Detailed (with examples in text):

1. **Variety of animals**: Different origins, body structures, sense organs, and affections produce different phantasiai from the same external objects. (Long development: production without/with intercourse; viviparous/oviparous; eye differences — jaundice, bloodshot, animal pupils, mirrors, pressure on eyeball; other senses — touch (shell/flesh/scales), hearing (narrow/wide canal, plugged), smell (cold vs excess blood), taste (dry/moist tongues, fever). Preferences/aversions: perfume vs dung beetles, sea water, mud for pigs, hemlock for quails, etc. Even "non-rational" animals (esp. the dog) not inferior in sensation, internal reasoning (Stoic Chrysippus on dog's dialectic), justice, art, recognition (Homer's Argus). Conclusion: cannot prefer our phantasiai; suspend on how the external is in nature.)

2. **Differences among human beings**: Even granting humans more credible than animals, humans differ in body (Scythian vs Indian form/humors; tolerances e.g. Attic woman 30 drams hemlock, Lysis 4 drams opium; Demophon shivered in sun, Psyllaeans unharmed by asps) and soul (different choices/aversions per poets Pindar, Homer, Euripides, etc.). If all humans, contradiction; if some, which (Platonist says Plato, Epicurean Epicurus...)? Leads back to suspension. Dogmatists' egoistic preference for themselves begs the question (they are part of the dispute).

3. **Differences in constitution of the sense organs**: Senses disagree (paintings have relief to eye not touch; honey pleasant tongue unpleasant eye; perfume pleases smell displeases taste; spurge juice painful eyes painless elsewhere; rain water good for eyes rough on windpipe; sting-ray numbs extremities harmless elsewhere). Apple appears complex (smooth, fragrant, sweet, yellow) — but may have only one quality appearing differently, or more qualities our organs don't detect. "Nature" (Aristotle cited) is itself disputed among Dogmatists. Senses don't apprehend external; intellect can't either.

4. **Circumstances** ("conditions"): Natural vs unnatural, awake vs asleep (different phantasiai; neither absolute), age (cold air temperate to young, cold to old; different interests), motion vs rest (stationary things seem to move when sailing), liking/disliking (pork repels some, delights others; ugly mistresses thought beautiful), hunger/satiety (same food pleasant/unpleasant), drunk/sober (shameful things not shameful when drunk), predispositions (wine sour after dates/figs, sweet after nuts), fearful/courageous, distressed/cheerful. Anomaly unresolved → suspend. Judge is always in some condition (not "no condition at all" — absurd); contaminated by it.

5. **Positions, distances, and locations**: Stoa appears tapering from end, symmetrical from middle; boat small/stationary far, large/moving near; tower round far, square near. Lamplight dim in sunshine bright in dark; oar broken in water straight out; egg soft in bird hard in air; coral soft in sea hard in air; sound different in pipe vs flute vs air.

6. **Admixtures**: Nothing affects us by itself but always with something (complexion one hue in warm air, another in cold; sound in thin vs dense air; spices more pungent in bath/sun vs cold; body light in water heavy in air). Cannot say how the thing is by itself.

7. **Quantity and constitution of the external objects**: Moderate wine strengthens and gladdens; excess maddens and produces torpor. Small food nourishes, large disturbs. Same things produce opposite effects by quantity/strength.

8. **Relativity**: Everything appears in relation to something (the judging subject or things observed together with it). "Everything appears in relation to something." Most generic mode to which the others reduce.

9. **Frequency or infrequency of occurrence**: Sun more marvelous to those who see it rarely (e.g., Indians once a month); earthquakes astonishing to the inexperienced but not to those who live in seismic areas.

10. **Ways of life, customs and laws, mythic beliefs, and dogmatic opinions**: Enormous variation across peoples and schools in what counts as good/bad, pious/impious, beautiful/ugly, true/false. (Examples throughout the text and implied by the other modes.)

**The Five Modes (later/"more recent Skeptics")**:

1. Disagreement (ordinary life and philosophers reach unresolvable impasse on a topic).
2. Infinite regress (support needs support ad infinitum; no place to begin).
3. Relativity (as above).
4. Hypothesis (Dogmatists, in regress, start from something they deem worthy of acceptance without question or demonstration).
5. Circularity (what should support the matter in question needs support from the matter itself).

Applied to any proposed matter (sense or thought object): if disagreement not decidable → suspend; if decidable, leads to regress (by sense or thought), circularity, or hypothesis. All sense/thought objects are also relative. Thus everything leads to epoché.

**Two Modes** (additional): Everything apprehended is either through itself or through another; neither works (dispute on both; regress/circularity).

**Modes against causal explanations** (Aenesidemus' eight + the five suffice): Causes concern the non-evident (no agreed confirmation from appearances); plethora of explanations but Dogmatists pick one; orderly effects from unordered causes; from appearances to non-appearances; based on own hypotheses not common; accept what fits hypothesis reject what doesn't; causes conflict with appearances or own hypotheses; explain puzzling from equally puzzling.

**"To Every Argument an Equal Argument Is Opposed"**:

The fundamental practice and one of the slogans. The method itself, not a metaphysical claim.

## Relevance to Vault

**The practical engine of skeptical method for the wiki**:

Equipollence and the modes are tools for *generating* balanced opposition before synthesis or decision. Directly applicable to [[GROK]] workflows:
- Ingest: when new source appears to contradict prior pages, generate the equipollent view explicitly (wikilink both, note in contradictions section) rather than overwriting.
- Lint/health check: use modes diagnostically — disagreement? regress in sources? relativity to context/author? hypothesis in old claims? circular self-reference in the KB?
- Query/exploration: present equipollent sides with links; file analyses that show the opposition and resulting (provisional) synthesis.
- Index and Overview maintenance: keep summaries accurate to "what appears now" from current sources; update when equipollence shifts.

**Epistemic humility and "chronicler" reporting**:

The modes prevent premature closure on non-evident matters. The wiki reports "what now appears to us to be the case" from the sources read, with full provenance. New sources can (and should) produce new oppositions.

**Enrichment of ataraxia and other concepts** (cross with [[Concepts/Ataraxia]], [[Concepts/Epoché (Suspension of Judgment)]]):

Equipollence is the specific mechanism that makes the Pyrrhonian path to ataraxia work. It complements (and challenges) Epicurean atomist resolution and Stoic control/acceptance. Both Epicureans and Stoics appear in Sextus as Dogmatists; their systems are among those the modes target. The vault now has all three Hellenistic routes documented and contrasted.

**Classical domain integration**:

The modes are the "how" of the third philosophy. They explicitly engage and undermine the claims of the Dogmatists already in the vault (Aristotle's essentialism/demonstration/final causes, Epicurus' atoms as truth, Stoic providence and impressions, Platonic Forms, etc.). The relativity and animal/human/sense/circumstance modes are especially rich for epistemology, perception, and custom (ties to MasterClass craft as culturally/practically situated excellence; sales playbook as custom in a vertical).
