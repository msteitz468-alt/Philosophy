# Reductionism and Verification Theory of Meaning (Quine)

> The second dogma of empiricism: the belief that each meaningful statement is equivalent to some logical construct upon terms which refer to immediate experience (radical reductionism), or at least that each synthetic statement has a unique range of possible sensory events that would confirm or infirm it (attenuated form). Closely tied to the verification theory of meaning (the meaning of a statement is its method of empirical confirmation or infirmation).

## The Dogma in Its Forms

- **Radical reductionism** (Locke, Hume, and later): every idea must originate in sense experience or be compounded of such ideas. In semantic terms: a term is significant only if it is a name (or compound/abbreviation of names) of a sense datum; a statement is meaningful only if translatable into sense-datum language. Tooke is cited as an early semantic formulation.
- **Verification theory of meaning** (Peirce onward, prominent in logical empiricism): the meaning of a statement *is* the method of empirically confirming or infirming it. An analytic statement is the limiting case confirmed no matter what.
- **Attenuated reductionism** (still influential in Quine's time): even if full translation fails, each synthetic statement still has its own unique confirming and infirming sensory events. This is implicit in the verification theory.

From the verification theory + synonymy one could recover analyticity (a statement is analytic if synonymous with a logical truth) and thereby save the first dogma as well.

## Quine's Critique

- **Russell's "incomplete symbols defined in use" and Frege's statement-as-unit** were advances over term-by-term empiricism, but still drew the grid too finely.
- Carnap's *Der logische Aufbau der Welt* (1928) was the most serious attempt to carry out reduction: starting from a sense-datum language + the full resources of modern logic (including higher set theory), Carnap defined a wide array of sensory concepts by ingenious constructions. He was the first empiricist to take serious steps toward actually executing the program rather than merely asserting reducibility.
- Even so, the program fails in principle for statements about the physical world:
  - Carnap's suggestions (spatio-temporal point-instants as quadruples of reals; assignment of sense qualities according to "laziest world" canons compatible with experience) remain sketchy.
  - The connective "is at" (quality q is at point-instant x;y;z;t) is an added undefined primitive; the canons counsel its use but do not eliminate it.
  - No actual translation of physical-object statements into the initial sense-data + logic language is achieved or even clearly indicated.
- Carnap later abandoned the translatability of physical-object statements into experiential statements (reductionism in its radical form ceased to figure in his philosophy).
- The dogma survives in the subtler supposition that each statement (taken in isolation) can admit of confirmation or infirmation. This is what Quine rejects.

## The Verification Theory's Link to the First Dogma

The verification theory was meant to provide an independent account of cognitive synonymy (statements are synonymous iff they have the same confirmation method), from which analyticity could be defined without circularity. If it worked, the two dogmas would stand or fall together — and the second dogma's failure drags the first down with it.

## Relation to Vault Content

- **Background in Hume**: Hume's Copy Principle (every simple idea copies a prior impression) and the meaning-test ("from what impression is this idea derived?") are early versions of the reductionist/verificationist impulse. See [[Concepts/Impressions and Ideas - Copy Principle (Hume)]].
- **Carnap as target**: The *Aufbau* and Carnap's later semantical rules / state-descriptions are the direct objects of Quine's criticism (see also [[Concepts/Problem of Demarcation (Kant and Popper)]] where Popper critiques Carnap's use of verificationism for demarcation rather than meaning).
- **Popper connection**: Popper rejected the equation of demarcation with a criterion of *meaning* and rejected verification in favor of falsifiability. Quine supplies the deeper diagnosis of why the verificationist tools were broken from the start.
- **Holism as the alternative**: once reductionism is abandoned, statements (and even whole theories) do not have isolated empirical content. See [[Concepts/Confirmation Holism (Duhem-Quine Thesis)]].

## Relevance to Vault

- **Against statement-by-statement grounding**: The wiki does not (and cannot) reduce every claim to a unique set of raw "sense data" (source excerpts). Integration is holistic; a new source can require adjustments across many pages. Treating any single page or claim as having its own private confirming evidence is a form of the dogma.
- **AI / RAG / grounding**: Retrieval-augmented generation hopes to "reduce" answers to retrieved chunks. Quine warns that the relation is never that clean; the model's "web" (parameters + prompt + retrieved context + prior conversation) as a whole faces the test of usefulness or user correction. Over-claiming local verifiability for individual generated sentences repeats the reductionist error.
- **Lint / schema discipline**: when a claim in the wiki cannot be traced to specific sources or to explicit schema conventions, it is functioning as an unreduced posit. That is sometimes necessary and pragmatic (see the physical-objects-as-posits theme), but it should be acknowledged rather than disguised as direct verification.

**Open questions**:
- Can a workable, non-holistic confirmation theory be given for individual statements or for restricted languages (e.g., observation sentences in Quine's later work)?
- How much of the success of actual science is compatible with the failure of reductionism?
