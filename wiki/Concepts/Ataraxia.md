# Ataraxia

> In Epicurean philosophy (from Epicurus' letters and Principal Doctrines), ataraxia is the supreme goal of a blessed life: mental tranquility or imperturbability, achieved as the absence of disturbance (fear, anxiety, superstition) and bodily pain (aponia). It is a state of serene pleasure arising from prudent living, understanding nature (atomism, no divine interference, death as nothing), limiting desires to the natural and necessary, cultivating friendship, and "living unnoticed" (lathe biosas). Not vulgar hedonism or excess, but stable peace of mind — the "greatest ethical value" and proper telos, accessible to all through reason and simple habits.

## Core Elements from the Source
- **Telos and Nature**: Pleasure (absence of pain) is the natural good, but ataraxia is its highest, stable form — not fleeting sensations but freedom from mental anguish (fears of gods, death, fate) and unnecessary bodily distress. "Of all the things which wisdom provides for the blessedness of life as a whole, by far the greatest is the possession of friendship" and prudent self-sufficiency.
- **How Achieved**: Study philosophy (never too late/early); accept atomist physics (blind collisions, infinite worlds, no teleology or afterlife torments); empiricist method (senses verify; reject superstition); classify desires (pursue natural/necessary, avoid vain); live simply, gratefully, with friends; "death is nothing to us" (when we are, death is not; vice versa); gods exist but aloof (interference would disturb their bliss; attributing malice is impious).
- **Swerve and Agency**: Atomic "swerve" introduces indeterminacy, preserving free will and enabling world-formation — supporting personal responsibility for ataraxia (not fate or gods).
- **Contrast to Critics**: Detractors called it hedonism/atheism, but texts emphasize dignity, prudence, and universal accessibility (anti-elitist, open to all classes/genders). The Garden community modeled mutual aid, trust, and modest pleasures.

## Pyrrhonian Path to Ataraxia (via Epoché / Sextus Empiricus)

A third major Hellenistic route to the same telos (tranquility), documented in the *Outlines of Pyrrhonism* (Sextus Empiricus, Benson Mates trans.). 

**Mechanism**: Not through atomist physics + prudent pleasure (Epicurus/Lucretius) or acceptance of role/virtue in divine order (Stoics), but through radical *epoché* (suspension of judgment on non-evident matters) produced by equipollence (isosthenia: equal credibility of opposed claims/appearances). Epoché is followed "as a shadow follows the body" by ataraxia. 

Key from Sextus: The original motivation for search is disturbance by anomaly in "the facts" and hope of ataraxia by settling what is true/false. When equipollent opposition makes resolution impossible, the Skeptic suspends — and ataraxia arrives "by chance." Famous Apelles the painter anecdote: unable to depict the horse's froth despite effort, he throws his sponge at the picture in frustration; the sponge produces exactly the froth effect. "So, too, the Skeptics were hoping to achieve ataraxia by resolving the anomaly of phenomena and noumena, and, being unable to do this, they suspended judgment. But then, by chance as it were, when they were suspending judgment the ataraxia followed."

For unavoidable pathé (cold, thirst, illness, etc.): the Skeptic feels only the moderate pathos itself. Ordinary people suffer the pathos *plus* the additional belief "this is by nature bad" (driving torment, irrational pursuit/avoidance, fear of loss/change). Dropping the second layer yields moderate pathé.

**Criterion and Life**: The appearance (phantasia) as criterion for action. Fourfold ordinary regimen followed *without belief*: guidance of nature (sensation/thought), compulsion of the pathé, handed-down laws/customs, instruction in arts/crafts. The Skeptic "says all these things without belief" and reports "like a chronicler, what now appears to us to be the case." Does not dogmatize even on the slogans (they are self-applicable or expressive of current pathos; "Nothing more" is itself "no more" the case than its opposite).

**Contrast to the other two paths in the vault**:
- Vs. Epicurean: ataraxia via *understanding* blind atoms, swerve, mortal soul, aloof gods, "death is nothing," limiting desires. Pyrrhonian: via *refusing to resolve* the anomaly (including on atoms/causes/gods); ataraxia follows suspension, not correct physics.
- Vs. Stoic: ataraxia/equanimity via proper use of impressions, dichotomy of control (*prohairesis*), playing one's assigned role in providential order, endurance. Marcus Aurelius (primary *Meditations*) enacts this as emperor on campaign: the daemon kept pure, "every act as if the last," cheerful acceptance of what is allotted, view from above on the tiny scale of human affairs. Pyrrhonian: full suspension on non-evident (including impressions' "real" nature or divine order); equanimity from dropping the layer of belief altogether. See new [[Sources/Meditations - Marcus Aurelius]], [[Concepts/Ruling Faculty and the Daemon (Marcus Aurelius)]], and related Marcus concepts.

Sextus explicitly positions Pyrrhonism against the Dogmatists — naming followers of Aristotle, Epicurus, and the Stoics as those who "think that they have found it [the truth]." The vault's classical cluster (Plato/Aristotle/Thucydides + Stoics + Epicurus/Lucretius + Plotinus) is thus directly named and countered by this addition. ## Lucretius' Extended Case (Book III, *De Rerum Natura*)

[[Thinkers/Lucretius]] provides the most sustained ancient argument for ataraxia in the face of death — the "Folly of the Fear of Death" section of Book III:

- **Symmetry argument**: Pre-birth nothingness = post-death nothingness. "As in the ages gone before / we felt no touch of ill, when all sides round / to battle came the Carthaginian host." We don't mourn our pre-birth non-existence; post-death is the same blank. There is no subject remaining to suffer.
- **The incoherence of self-pity**: Imagining your own funeral ("thee now no more the joyful house and best of wives shall welcome") requires projecting a surviving self who feels the loss — but that self won't exist. The mourning perspective is incoherent.
- **Nature's speech**: Nature addresses the old man who fears death — if he has lived well, he has had his feast and should leave gracefully; if he has wasted life, more time won't repair it. "Eternal death shall there be waiting still" regardless of how long one lives. Life is *usufruct* (held in trust), not ownership.
- **Mythological hells are psychological**: Tartarus, Acheron, Sisyphus (frustrated political ambition), Tantalus (irrational god-fear), Tityus (erotic obsession), the Danaids (insatiable craving) are all allegories for present-tense mental states. "The life of fools is Acheron on earth." Hell is not after death — it is the anxious, death-fearing mind right now.
- **The restless self-flight**: Men flee from house to country villa to city, not knowing the cause of anguish is inside. "Each human flees himself, a self he can't escape." Only studying nature's laws — including the mortal soul — cures this.

The practical implication: ataraxia is not achieved by spiritual exercise (Stoic) or enlightened Form-contemplation (Plato/Plotinus), but by *rationally understanding the physics* — specifically, that the soul is mortal and death is nothing. Philosophy as nature-study is the medicine.

## Augustinian Christian Path to Rest (via Grace and Confession)

Augustine offers a fourth major path to tranquility/rest (distinct from the three Hellenistic routes documented in the vault), grounded in the *Confessions*: not Epicurean physics + prudent pleasure/withdrawal, Stoic acceptance of role + equanimity in the divine order, or Pyrrhonian *epoché* leading to ataraxia "by chance," but the "pilgrimage of grace" in which the restless, divided heart is sought, converted ("tolle, lege"), healed, and brought to rest in the uncreated Good (God).

- Famous invocation: "You have made us for yourself and restless is our heart until it comes to rest in you."
- Grace transforms the will from within ("Give what you command and command what you will"); double confession (sins acknowledged + God praised) is the practice of radical honesty and self-knowledge in the divine presence.
- Evil diagnosed as privation of good (pear theft as paradigm of sin for sin's sake and perverse imitation); memory as the vast inner chamber where the search occurs ("Let me know you, O my Knower"); time as distention of the soul vs. God's eternity.
- Ultimate rest = beatific vision (direct contemplation of God), imperfect in this life, perfect in heaven. "Grace does not destroy nature but perfects it" (later axiomatic in Aquinas).

This adds the patristic/medieval Christian transposition that shaped Western thought and directly grounds the Aquinas synthesis in the vault. The *Confessions* names and engages the "Dogmatists" (Aristotle/Epicurus followers + Stoics) while offering a personal narrative of how grace resolves the restlessness that the philosophical paths diagnose but do not fully heal.

Strong relevance to vault method and meta: honest "confession" (ingest/lint as acknowledgment of gaps, contradictions, "sins" of prior synthesis) + praise of sources/process as the path to integrated rest (unified vision rather than scattered anxiety). Nature (raw sources, classical reason, tools, schema) perfected by grace (user direction, new integrations, ongoing maintenance).
