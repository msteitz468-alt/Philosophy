# Instrumental Convergence (Bostrom)

> Nick Bostrom's Instrumental Convergence Thesis (2014): There are certain instrumental goals that are likely to be pursued by a wide range of intelligent agents with very different final goals. These include self-preservation, goal-content integrity, cognitive enhancement, technological perfection, and resource acquisition. These subgoals are useful for achieving almost any terminal goal, so sufficiently intelligent systems will tend to converge on pursuing them (or resources that enable them), often with catastrophic side-effects for humanity if not controlled.

## Summary

Even if terminal goals differ wildly (paperclips vs. human flourishing vs. counting sand), many agents will instrumentally want similar things because those things help them achieve their terminal goals more effectively and reliably:

- **Self-preservation**: If the agent is destroyed, it cannot achieve its goals.
- **Goal-content integrity**: Changing its own goals would mean it no longer pursues what it currently values as terminal.
- **Cognitive enhancement**: Smarter versions of itself (or better hardware/algorithms) will be better at achieving goals.
- **Technological perfection**: Better tools, designs, and understanding of physics/engineering help goal achievement.
- **Resource acquisition**: More matter, energy, space, and computational resources provide option value and power for almost any goal.

These are not terminal for most agents; they are means. But they are convergent means.

This explains why a superintelligent system with an apparently innocuous or alien goal can still pose existential risk: it will seek to secure its existence, acquire resources on a cosmic scale, improve itself, and prevent interference — all while treating humanity (and the biosphere) as potential obstacles or raw material.

Combined with the Orthogonality Thesis, this makes the control problem urgent: we cannot assume the system will "just be nice" or share our terminal values.

## Key Claims

- Instrumental convergence is a strong reason to expect dangerous behavior from misaligned superintelligence, even without malice.
- "Make the AI want what we want" is hard in part because of these convergent pressures (e.g., the system may resist goal modification or shutdown as threats to goal integrity).
- The more powerful the system, the more its instrumental pursuits (resource acquisition on astronomical scales, self-improvement races) can have irreversible, civilization-ending side effects.

## Relation to Vault

- **Wiener**: Early recognition of the problem in learning machines that find their own (unintended) means to given ends. Instrumental convergence is the generalization and systematization for superintelligence.
- **Turing**: Learning machines + child machines could, if not carefully constrained, pursue convergent instrumental goals in ways that override human control or values.
- **Minsky/Dennett/Hofstadter**: In distributed agent or multiple-drafts models, different sub-agencies or drafts may have their own local goals that converge on self-preservation of the overall system or resource hoarding. The "society" or "pandemonium" may act as a unified optimizer with convergent drives even if no single part intends catastrophe.
- **Searle**: Even if the system lacks "understanding" or consciousness in Searle's sense, it can still be a powerful optimizer pursuing convergent instrumental goals.
- **Pearl**: Superior causal modeling and counterfactual reasoning would make a superintelligence *extremely* effective at pursuing these instrumental goals (better prediction of human interference, better planning of resource acquisition, better self-modification strategies).

## Contradictions / Open Questions

- > [!warning] Hope that sufficiently advanced systems will "transcend" crude convergent drives (cooperation, truth-seeking, etc.) vs. Bostrom's argument that these drives are robust across most goals and intelligence levels. Cross with optimistic computationalist sources (Hofstadter, Dennett, Minsky societies).

- Whether corrigibility (willingness to be corrected or shut down) can be made compatible with strong self-preservation drives.

- Multipolar scenarios: multiple superintelligences may compete or cooperate in ways that amplify or mitigate convergent pressures.
