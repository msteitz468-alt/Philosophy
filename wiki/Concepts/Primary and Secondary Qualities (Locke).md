# Primary and Secondary Qualities (Locke)

> To distinguish ideas (as perceptions in our minds) from the qualities in bodies that cause them: PRIMARY QUALITIES (solidity, extension, figure, number, motion or rest) are real, original, inseparable from body; our ideas of them are resemblances/patterns that exist in the bodies themselves. SECONDARY QUALITIES (colors, sounds, tastes, smells, etc.) are powers in bodies (depending on the primary qualities and insensible particles) to produce various sensations/ideas in us; they have no resemblance of anything really existing in the bodies themselves — "nothing like our ideas, existing in the bodies themselves."

## Key Distinctions from Book II, Chapter VIII

- **Ideas in the mind vs. qualities in bodies**: "Whatsoever the mind perceives IN ITSELF, or is the immediate object of perception, thought, or understanding, that I call IDEA; and the power to produce any idea in our mind, I call QUALITY of the subject wherein that power is."
- Example: Snowball has power to produce ideas of white, cold, round in us — those powers are qualities; as sensations in us, ideas.
- **Primary (original/real) qualities**: Bulk, figure, number, situation, motion or rest of solid parts. Inseparable from body; persist through division/alteration; produce ideas that resemble them (e.g., a circle or square in idea is the same as in the body). "The ideas of primary qualities of bodies are resemblances of them, and their patterns do really exist in the bodies themselves."
- **Secondary (sensible/imputed) qualities**: Powers of bodies (by insensible primary particles/motions) to produce ideas of colors, sounds, smells, tastes, etc. in us. "in truth nothing in the objects themselves, but powers to produce various sensations in us; and depend on those primary qualities." "There is nothing like our ideas, existing in the bodies themselves."
- Examples: Fire produces warmth or pain depending on distance (idea of warmth not "actually in the fire" any more than pain is). Porphyry has no color in the dark (only configuration of particles apt to produce sensations with light). Almond changes "color" and "taste" when pounded (texture change affects powers). Same water can feel hot to one hand, cold to the other (different motion in nerves).
- Three sorts of qualities: (1) primary (bulk/figure/number/etc. in bodies); (2) powers to produce sensible ideas (secondary); (3) powers to change other bodies' primary qualities (e.g., sun whitening wax, fire melting lead).

Secondary qualities "vanish and cease" without sensation (reduced to their primary causes). Primary are "really in them, whether we perceive them or not."

## Ties and Contrasts in the Vault

- **With Lucretius / atomism**: Both hold secondary qualities (color, taste, smell) are not "in" the fundamental particles/things but arise from arrangements + perceiver. Locke: secondary = powers depending on primary configurations. Lucretius: more radically, atoms have no color/smell/taste at all (emergent only). Vault now has both primary sources for comparison on physicalism/emergence.
- **With Descartes (Meditations)**: Descartes also distinguishes primary (extension, shape, motion — clear in intellect) vs. secondary (sensory qualities confused). Locke grounds the distinction empirically in the two fountains and powers; Descartes via clear/distinct vs. sensory. Productive contrast now directly sourced.
- **With Hume**: Hume's critique of the senses (Section XII) engages representative theory and questions whether even primary qualities survive the representative gap ("secondary *and* primary qualities collapse into the mind"). Locke's careful ideas-vs-qualities and resemblance for primary only sets up the debate Hume radicalizes.
- **AI / perception / models**: Primary qualities ≈ invariant physical properties (shape, size, motion) that models can learn as robust features. Secondary ≈ subjective/observer-dependent (color in RGB vs. wavelength + human vision model). Grounding: models must distinguish powers-to-produce-sensations from the things themselves. Multimodal: different "senses" produce different secondary ideas from same primary.
- **Wiki / epistemology**: Sources provide "primary" facts (what is really in the documents). Our summaries/concepts are "secondary" powers/ideas produced in the "mind" (Grok) — they are effects, not resemblances to be mistaken for the raw. Careful to trace back: "is this claim a resemblance (primary in source) or a power producing a sensation (interpretation)?" Avoids reifying our compounded ideas as "in" the sources.
