# Pragmatic Theory of Truth (James)

> One-sentence hook: Truth is not a static "copying" of a ready-made reality but something that *happens* to an idea — "it *becomes* true, is *made* true by events," namely by the process of its verification; "the 'true' is only the expedient in the way of our thinking."

## Overview

The doctrine of Lecture VI ("Pragmatism's Conception of Truth"), credited to Dewey and Schiller and defended by James against "a hailstorm of contempt." Both pragmatists and intellectualists accept the dictionary definition — truth = "agreement of our ideas with reality" — but James **analyzes "agreement" pragmatically**. The intellectualist treats truth as "an inert static relation": once you have the true idea, "there's an end of the matter… you are in stable equilibrium." James asks his usual question: "Grant an idea to be true — what concrete difference will its being true make in anyone's actual life? … What is the truth's cash-value in experiential terms?"

The answer: **"True ideas are those that we can assimilate, validate, corroborate and verify. False ideas are those that we cannot."** Truth is a *verification-process*: "The truth of an idea is not a stagnant property inherent in it. Truth *happens* to an idea. It *becomes* true, is *made* true by events. Its verity is in fact an event, a process: the process namely of its verifying itself, its veri-fication."

## Key Arguments

- **Agreement = leading, not copying.** To "agree" with a reality is "to be guided either straight up to it or into its surroundings, or to be put into such working touch with it as to handle either it or something connected with it better than if we disagreed." Copying is "one very important way of agreeing… but far from being essential." Many realities ("past time," "power," "elasticity," the clock's "works") cannot be copied; the word or symbol "serves you truly" by leading without clashing.
- **Truth lives "on a credit system."** "Our thoughts and beliefs 'pass,' so long as nothing challenges them, just as banknotes pass so long as nobody refuses them." Indirect/potential verification "passes muster" (we assume the wall-object is a clock, that Japan exists) — but the system rests on "direct face-to-face verifications somewhere," "without which the fabric of truth collapses like a financial system with no cash-basis whatever."
- **Three kinds of reality to agree with**: (1) concrete sensible facts; (2) relations among ideas — the "eternal" truths of logic and mathematics, true "once true, always true," coercive; (3) "the whole body of other truths already in our possession." A new truth must mediate among all three "with a minimum of jolt, a maximum of continuity."
- **"The 'true'… is only the expedient in the way of our thinking, just as 'the right' is only the expedient in the way of our behaving."** Expedient "in the long run and on the whole," since experience "has ways of boiling over." The **"absolutely true"** ("what no farther experience will ever alter") is "that ideal vanishing-point towards which we imagine that all our temporary truths will some day converge."
- **Truth is *in rebus*, not *ante rem*.** Like "health," "wealth," and "strength" (all words "in *th*"), "truth" names *processes* (verifications) that "pay," not a prior excellence inhering in things and explaining them. To put the abstraction "Truth" behind the facts as their cause is "the stock rationalist trick" (Lessing's epigram on "wealth"; the doctor's "ether is a respiratory stimulant"). "Truth exists *ante rem* just as much and as little as the other things do."
- **Truth grows.** "Truth is made largely out of previous truths… funded." A "double influence" (a snowball's growth): facts → beliefs → acts → new facts → new truth, "and so on indefinitely." "The 'facts' themselves… are not true. They simply *are*. Truth is the function of the beliefs that start and terminate among them."
- **Why we *ought* to seek truth** is itself pragmatic: true beliefs are "invaluable instruments of action" (the cow-path that leads to the saving house). Our "obligation to agree with reality is… grounded in a perfect jungle of concrete expediencies"; the imperative "think the true" is "tremendously conditioned" (no duty to announce your address when asked the time).

## Rebuttal of the standing objection

James anticipates the charge (Russell-style) that this makes truth "whatever you find it pleasant to say" — "an impudent slander." The pragmatist, "pent in… between the whole body of funded truths squeezed from the past and the coercions of the world of sense," feels more "objective control" than anyone. Schiller's "works" and Dewey's "satisfaction" are misread "into the silliest of possible meanings." James's positive reply: the rationalist's own definitions of truth ("the system of propositions with an unconditional claim to be recognized," "judgments we are under obligation to make") are "unutterably trivial" until handled pragmatically; an "agreement" demanded "for no reasons" and ruling out all consequences is "an absolutely meaningless abstraction." "It is the pragmatists and not the rationalists who are the more genuine defenders of the universe's rationality."

## Contrasts / Lineage

- **Vs. adequation/correspondence (Aristotle, [[Thinkers/Thomas Aquinas]], [[Concepts/Knowledge as Perception of Agreement or Disagreement of Ideas (Locke)]], [[Concepts/Clear and Distinct Perception (Descartes)]])**: the classical/medieval/rationalist view James is overturning — truth as a mind-independent *match* (a copy, an intuited agreement, a God-guaranteed clear-and-distinct perception). James keeps the *word* "agreement" but redefines it as fruitful leading.
- **Vs. the Absolute's "eternal" ready-made truth (Royce, Bradley, [[Concepts/Deus sive Natura - Substance Monism (Spinoza)]], [[Concepts/The One (To Hen)]])**: rationalism "faces backward to a past eternity"; reality "stands complete and ready-made from all eternity." Pragmatism "faces forward to the future"; reality and truth are "everlastingly in process of mutation."
- **Builds on empiricism ([[Thinkers/Hume]], [[Thinkers/Locke]])**: extends the verification/meaning-test temper; [[Concepts/Custom and Belief (Hume)]] (belief as a *manner of conception*/feeling, "reasoning a species of instinct") is a near cousin of "truth as a working leading," and [[Concepts/Problem of Induction (Hume)]] underwrites the claim that even our "oldest truths" are revisable.
- **Kinship with [[Concepts/Perspectivism (Nietzsche)]]**: "to recognize untruth as a condition of life" ≈ "the true is the expedient"; both deny a pure, disinterested, mind-independent Truth.
