# Virtual Worlds and Cartesian Dualism (Chalmers)
> "Descartes may have been wrong about the physical world we live in. But he was right about many virtual worlds" — VR naturalizes mind-body dualism and answers Princess Elisabeth's interaction objection.

## Summary

*Reality+* ch. 14. Virtual worlds structurally separate "physics" (the physics engine) from "psychology" (the rules governing creatures). Intelligent creatures in Alan Kay's Vivarium "would almost certainly become dualists... Furthermore, they would be correct to be dualists." A human VR user's mind lies outside virtual space, governed by other laws — so whether dualism holds is **perspective-relative**: "Relative to the outer physics, René's situation is not dualistic. Relative to the inner physics, it is dualistic."

The simulation model "helps us to overcome Princess Elisabeth's objection" — her famous challenge to Descartes ("it would be easier for me to concede matter and extension to the soul than to concede that an immaterial thing could move and be moved by a body") is answered by exhibiting exactly how non-physical/physical interaction works in every biosim setup. The **impure** simulation hypothesis corresponds to a "Cartesian it-from-bit creation hypothesis," the **pure** version to a non-Cartesian one; statistical reasoning favors pure simulations (impure ones each need a biological brain), and physics as a closed causal network is evidence against the Cartesian version — "certainly not demonstrated." From the inner view the deep truth may be "a sort of neutral monism." NPCs plus players plus special-powers objects even yield "a sort of trialism" and a vindicated animism.

## How This Relates to Prior Thinkers

- **[[Thinkers/Descartes]]**: partial vindication — the *Meditations* dualism ([[Sources/Meditations on First Philosophy - Descartes (Oxford, 2008)]]) is contingently true of biosim worlds; his imagination argument ("I can imagine my mind without my body...") is reconstructed but not endorsed for our world.
- **Elisabeth of Bohemia**: her interaction objection, long the standard refutation of dualism, gets its first concrete constructive answer.
- **[[Thinkers/Daniel Dennett]]**: "Where Am I?" frames the Matrix two-brains puzzle ("Is there one person here or two?").
- **[[Concepts/Simulation Hypothesis (Virk)]]**: Virk's player/avatar (RPG) vs. NPC versions map exactly onto Chalmers's impure/pure — with Virk favoring the dualist-shaped RPG version, Chalmers the statistics of the pure one.
- **[[Thinkers/Baruch Spinoza]]/[[Thinkers/Hume]]**: the "neutral monism" gesture connects to the vault's substance-monist and bundle traditions ([[Sources/Ethics - Spinoza (Cambridge, Kisner ed. 2018)]]).
