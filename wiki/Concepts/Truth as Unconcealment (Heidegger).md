# Truth as Unconcealment (Heidegger)
> Truth is not the correspondence of assertions to facts but *aletheia* — the process by which beings emerge from concealment into the open.

## Summary

Heidegger's account of truth (*Wahrheit*) is one of his most radical departures from the Western philosophical tradition. He rejects the **correspondence theory** — truth as the agreement of a proposition with a state of affairs — and replaces it with truth as **unconcealment** (*Unverborgenheit*), recovering the Greek word *aletheia* (literally "un-concealment," from *a-* + *lethein*, to escape notice).

### Critique of Correspondence

The correspondence theory fails, Heidegger argues, on *both* sides:

1. **Assertions** (as sounds, ideas, or propositions) are already meaning-laden constructs; there is no "bare" assertion waiting to be matched with a "bare" fact.
2. **Facts** are always entwined in worldly involvements — the very identification of a "fact" presupposes a prior disclosure of the domain in which it shows up.

Neither pole is independent enough for genuine "correspondence." The theory smuggles in an unexamined conception of Dasein as a worldless knowing-subject facing a value-neutral reality — precisely the Cartesian picture Heidegger dismantles.

### Truth as Disclosure

For Heidegger, truth is primarily **Dasein's disclosedness** — the fact that Dasein is the entity in whose being entities can show up at all. "There is truth only in so far as Dasein is and as long as Dasein is" (BT 227). This does not mean truth is subjective or arbitrary; it means that without an entity that *has* a world — that understands, interprets, and is attuned — there is nothing for beings to be disclosed *to*.

Truth is a matter of **degree**: illumination is never complete, nor is it ever wholly absent. Every disclosure involves a simultaneous **concealment** — to see something as a hammer is to not-see it as a piece of metal, a weight, a gift. Falsehood is not the co-ordinate opposite of truth but **covering-up** or **distorting**, possible only against a background of truth. Concealment is not mere absence but an active dimension of the truth-process.

### Assertions and Pre-Predicative Truth

Assertions are *derivative* of a more primordial disclosure. Before one can state "the hammer is heavy," one must already be engaged in a practical context where hammers, heaviness, and projects are disclosed. The assertion narrows and fixes what was already open in pre-predicative understanding, transforming the [[Concepts/Readiness-to-Hand and Presence-at-Hand (Heidegger)|ready-to-hand]] into the [[Concepts/Readiness-to-Hand and Presence-at-Hand (Heidegger)|present-at-hand]]. In Inwood's account, assertions emerge by "progressively detaching language from concrete speech situations."

### Truth and Art (The Later Heidegger)

In *The Origin of the Work of Art*, truth-as-unconcealment becomes the central category of aesthetics: art is "the truth of beings setting itself to work." The Greek temple does not represent anything; it *opens* a world — the sacred, the political, the natural — by gathering earth and sky, mortals and divinities. See [[Concepts/Art and Truth (Heidegger)]].

### Newton's Laws Without Dasein

Heidegger's claim provokes a sharp question: were Newton's laws "true" before Newton? Heidegger answers that the laws describe beings that were already there, but the *uncovering* of them — their truth — required Dasein. Newton's laws "became true through Newton; before him they were neither true nor false." This does not make them arbitrary; it makes truth an *event* rather than an eternal property.

## From Being and Time (Primary Text)

§44's three-step argument: (a) the traditional concept — locus of truth is the judgment, essence is *adaequatio intellectus et rei* — is derivative; being-true is *discovering* (*entdeckend-sein*); (b) the primordial phenomenon is the **disclosedness of Dasein** itself — *aletheia* as unconcealment must always be "wrested" from beings, "a kind of robbery," and Dasein is equiprimordially "in the truth" and "in untruth"; (c) truth is relative to Dasein's being without being "subjective": "'There is' ['gibt es'] truth only insofar as Dasein is and as long as it is." Heidegger notes Aristotle never actually located truth primarily in the judgment. We do not presuppose truth — truth first makes presupposing possible; the skeptic "can no more be refuted than truth proved," and "eternal truths" are unproven "remnants of Christian theology."

The Newton passage in full force: "Newton's laws, the law of contradiction, and any truth whatsoever, are true only as long as Dasein is. Before there was any Dasein, there was no truth; nor will there be any after Dasein is no more. … Before Newton's laws were discovered, they were not 'true.' From this it does not follow that they were false… The laws became true through Newton, through them beings in themselves became accessible for Dasein." The pre-predicative ground of assertion-truth is worked out in §33's derivation chain (understanding → interpretation → statement); see [[Concepts/Disclosedness — Attunement, Understanding, Discourse (Heidegger)]].

## Key Claims / Positions

### How Different Thinkers Approach This

- **[[Thinkers/Aristotle]]**: "To say of what is that it is, and of what is not that it is not, is true" (*Metaphysics* Γ). This is the origin of the correspondence theory. Heidegger respects Aristotle but argues that *aletheia* was already at work in Greek thought prior to the correspondence formulation; Aristotle formalised what the Pre-Socratics experienced more primordially.
- **[[Thinkers/Plato]]**: The allegory of the cave in the *Republic* is Heidegger's paradigm of truth-as-unconcealment: the prisoner's ascent is a movement from concealment to disclosure. But Plato then reinterprets truth as correctness (*orthotes*) — the correct apprehension of the Forms — initiating the decline into correspondence.
- **[[Thinkers/Descartes]]**: Truth is the "clear and distinct" perception of a self-transparent subject. Heidegger: this presupposes the Cartesian subject-object split and makes truth a property of representations rather than a happening.
- **[[Thinkers/Kant]]**: "The nominal definition of truth, that it is the agreement of cognition with its object, is assumed as granted." Heidegger: Kant inherits the correspondence framework uncritically.
- **[[Thinkers/A.J. Ayer]]**: Logical positivism eliminates Heidegger's question as meaningless. Heidegger would respond that the verification principle itself rests on an unexamined disclosure of what counts as meaningful — a prior truth-event that positivism cannot account for.

## Contradictions / Open Questions

- > [!warning] If truth depends on Dasein, what is the status of truths about a world before (or after) Dasein? Heidegger's "Newton's laws" example is often criticised as leading to idealism despite his protests.
- The relationship between truth as *aletheia* in the early Heidegger (BT) and in the later Heidegger (OWA, the turn) is debated: does the later account correct or continue the earlier one?
