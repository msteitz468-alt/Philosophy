# Double-Aspect Theory of Information (Chalmers)
> "Experience is information from the inside; physics is information from the outside" — a speculative prototheory on which information has both a physical and a phenomenal aspect.

## Summary

*The Conscious Mind* ch. 8, flagged by Chalmers as "a skeleton around which a theory might be built" and "the most likely to be entirely wrong." Coherence and invariance principles underdetermine the psychophysical connection; a fundamental theory needs basic laws. Candidate construct: Shannon-style syntactic **information spaces** (states with difference structure), physically realized wherever there is Bateson's "difference that makes a difference," and phenomenally realized in the intrinsic similarity structure of experience (3-D color space, the visual field). The **double-aspect principle**: "information (in the actual world) has two aspects, a physical and a phenomenal aspect."

Consequences faced squarely:
- **Panpsychism**: information is everywhere causation is, so "Experience is ubiquitous too" — the sliding scale runs human → mouse → fish → slug → thermostat ("Certainly it will not be very interesting to be a thermostat"). Alternative: mere **protophenomenal** properties in simple systems. "I am much more confident of naturalistic dualism than I am of panpsychism."
- **Metaphysics**: Wheeler/Fredkin **it-from-bit** ("pure informational flux, without any further substance") meets the Russellian point that pure information needs grounding — solved together: phenomenal/protophenomenal properties are the intrinsic natures realizing physical information. "Two solutions for the price of one."
- Residue: Sellars's **grain problem** (how micro-experience composes smooth macro-experience), constraint problems (avoiding experiences for stomachs and CD players — perhaps require availability for global control), and six open questions. The defect is being "too unspecific in its predictions," not untestable.

## How Different Thinkers Use This

- **[[Thinkers/Claude Shannon]]** ([[Sources/A Mathematical Theory of Communication - Claude Shannon (1948)]]): supplies the syntactic information measure — pointedly *non*-semantic, sidestepping [[Contradictions/Wiener vs Shannon - Information and Meaning]].
- **[[Concepts/It-from-Bit Hypothesis (Chalmers)]]**: the 1996 treatment is the ancestor of *Reality+* ch. 8 — there the phenomenal grounding ("it-from-bit-from-consciousness") is one option among several; here it is the favored move.
- **[[Thinkers/Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz]]** ([[Sources/Monadology - Leibniz (1714)]]) and **[[Thinkers/Baruch Spinoza]]**: monadic panpsychism and dual-aspect monism are the classical precedents; Chalmers's Russellian version is their modern descendant.
- **[[Concepts/Entropy and Information (Wiener)]]**: Wiener's "information is information, not matter or energy" gets an unexpectedly literal metaphysical upgrade.

## Contradictions / Open Questions

- > [!warning] Sits uneasily with [[Concepts/Coherence Between Consciousness and Awareness (Chalmers)]]: unrestricted, the double-aspect principle gives experience to thermostats; restricted by global availability, it collapses toward the coherence principle. Chalmers leaves the tension open.
