# Hume's Fork (Relations of Ideas vs Matters of Fact)

> Hume's exhaustive division of "all the objects of human reason or enquiry" (*Enquiry* Section IV) into two mutually exclusive kinds: **Relations of Ideas** and **Matters of Fact**. The fork is the master classification that drives the problem of induction, the analysis of causation, and the incendiary conclusion of Section XII. Anything that is neither a relation of ideas nor a matter of fact is "sophistry and illusion."

## The Two Prongs

- **Relations of Ideas**: the propositions of geometry, algebra, and arithmetic — "every affirmation which is either intuitively or demonstratively certain." They are *a priori* (discoverable "by the mere operation of thought, without dependence on what is anywhere existent in the universe"), and their **denial implies a contradiction**. "Though there never were a circle or triangle in nature, the truths demonstrated by Euclid would forever retain their certainty." (≈ the later category of *analytic* / *necessary* truths.)

- **Matters of Fact**: everything about real existence. Their distinguishing mark: **the contrary is always possible** — "The contrary of every matter of fact is still possible, because it can never imply a contradiction, and is conceived by the mind with the same facility and distinctness as if ever so conformable to reality." *"That the sun will not rise tomorrow is no less intelligible a proposition, and implies no more contradiction, than the affirmation that it will rise."* (≈ *synthetic* / *contingent* truths.) Matters of fact beyond present sense and memory are known only through **cause and effect**, and thence only through **experience**.

The criterion separating the prongs is **conceivability**: a relation of ideas cannot be denied without contradiction; a matter of fact always can. Ayer's [[Concepts/Verification Principle (Ayer)|verification principle]] is a direct logical sharpening of the Fork — Ayer calls Hume's "commit it to the flames" passage "a rhetorical version of our own thesis." Quine's "Two Dogmas" then attacks the sharpness of the Fork itself: see [[Concepts/Analytic-Synthetic Distinction (Quine)]]. This makes *conceivability a test of (logical) possibility* — and crucially, **conceivability never establishes actual existence**. Whatever is distinctly conceivable is possible; what is possible is not thereby actual.

## What the Fork Does

1. **Detonates rationalist metaphysics**: any claim about existence (God, substance, causal necessity, the external world) must be a matter of fact, hence contingent and answerable only to experience — *not* demonstrable a priori. This is the standing refutation of the [[Concepts/Ontological Argument (Descartes)|ontological argument]] (existence is never part of an essence; "the non-existence of any being is as clear and distinct an idea as its existence") and of Spinoza's deduction of the world's features from the essence of substance.
2. **Sets up the problem of induction**: since causal relations are matters of fact, and the contrary of any predicted effect is conceivable, no causal inference is demonstrative. See [[Concepts/Problem of Induction (Hume)]].
3. **Yields a meaning/value filter** (Section XII): the only proper objects of inquiry are *quantity and number* (demonstration) and *matters of fact* (experience). A volume containing neither should be committed "to the flames." See [[Concepts/Mitigated Scepticism (Hume)]].

## Lineage and Legacy

The fork sharpens distinctions in Leibniz ("truths of reason" vs. "truths of fact") and anticipates Kant's *analytic/synthetic* and *a priori/a posteriori* axes (Kant's revolutionary question — are there *synthetic a priori* truths? — is precisely a challenge to the exhaustiveness of Hume's fork). It is the direct ancestor of the logical positivists' analytic/synthetic-plus-verification picture.

Quine's "Two Dogmas of Empiricism" (1951) launches the decisive internal critique: the fork (and its linguistic descendants) is not sharp or exhaustive in the way required. The analytic/synthetic distinction cannot be clarified without circular appeal to synonymy or analyticity itself; the verificationist reductionism that was meant to secure the synthetic side (each statement having its own unique confirming sensory events) also fails. See [[Concepts/Analytic-Synthetic Distinction (Quine)]], [[Concepts/Reductionism and Verification Theory of Meaning (Quine)]], and [[Sources/Two Dogmas of Empiricism - W.V.O. Quine (1951)]]. The result is confirmation holism (the web of belief) and naturalized epistemology: the unit of empirical significance is the whole of science, not isolated relations of ideas or matters of fact. A continuing reference point for post-positivist debates over the a priori, meaning, and the status of logic/mathematics.

## Relevance to Vault

- **Early-Modern hinge**: the decisive empiricist check on the vault's rationalists. Against [[Thinkers/Descartes]] and [[Thinkers/Spinoza]], existence claims cannot be spun from concepts alone. Conceivability (Descartes' "clear and distinct") survives — but only as a test of *possibility*, severed from existence.
- **Skeptical cluster**: a more surgical instrument than Pyrrhonian equipollence — instead of opposing every claim with an equal one ([[Concepts/Equipollence (Isosthenia) and the Modes]]), Hume *sorts* claims and shows where certainty is and isn't available.
- **AI / vault meta**: a clean *triage rubric* — for any assertion, ask: is this a formal/definitional truth (relations of ideas), an empirical claim answerable to data (matter of fact), or neither (flag as empty)? Mirrors the distinction between analytic checks (type systems, proofs, schema constraints) and empirical checks (tests, observations, source evidence). The "conceivability ≠ existence" point is a guard against hallucination: a fluent, coherent, *conceivable* description is not thereby *true of the world*.
- **Lint heuristic**: classify each substantive wiki claim as definitional or factual-with-provenance; anything that is neither is a candidate for the flames.

**Open questions**:
- Is the fork *exhaustive*? Kant's synthetic a priori (and mathematics' own status) presses here.
- Does Hume's own science of human nature (custom, belief, association) state matters of fact — and is it then as contingent and fallible as any other?
