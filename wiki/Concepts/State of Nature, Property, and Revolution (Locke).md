# State of Nature, Property, and Revolution (Locke)

> In the *Two Treatises of Government* (in the Complete Works volume): State of nature is perfect freedom and equality under the law of nature (reason, preservation of mankind). Property arises by mixing labor with the commons (with spoilage and "enough and as good" provisos). Government by consent of the governed to protect natural rights (life, liberty, property); legislative supreme but bound; executive with prerogative; dissolution and right of revolution when trust breached (tyranny, etc.).

## Key from Summaries in Source/Entity

First Treatise refutes patriarchal absolutism (Filmer); no hereditary divine right from Adam.

Second: State of nature (freedom/equality/law of preservation); property by labor; social contract; consent; right of revolution.
