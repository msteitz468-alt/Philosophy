# Conatus (Spinoza)

> Each thing, as far as it can by its own power, strives to persevere in its being (3p6). This striving (*conatus*) is the actual essence of the thing (3p7). It is the foundation of Spinoza’s theory of affects (joy as increase in power of acting, sadness as decrease) and of his ethics (virtue = power; the highest good is what most augments our power through understanding God/Nature).

## Core Description

In Part III, after establishing parallelism and the nature of the mind as the idea of the body, Spinoza turns to the affects. The key ontological claim is that every finite thing has a built-in striving to continue existing and to resist what would destroy or diminish it.

- 3p6: Each thing, as far as it can by its own power, strives to persevere in its being.
- 3p7: The striving by which each thing strives to persevere in its being is nothing but the actual essence of the thing.

This is not a conscious desire or a teleological pull toward a future goal. It is the present power of acting that belongs to the thing’s nature. For a stone, it is the power by which it resists erosion and continues to exist as that particular arrangement of matter. For a human being, it includes the power of the body to maintain itself and the power of the mind to form ideas and act from them.

Because modes are expressions of God’s power (God’s essence is power, 1p34), every finite thing possesses some share of that power as its own conatus. The more power a thing has (the more it is the adequate cause of effects from its own nature), the more it “exists” in the fullest sense.

## Connection to Affects and Ethics

- An affect/emotion is a bodily affection by which the body’s power of acting is augmented or diminished, together with the idea of that affection (3def3).
- Joy (*laetitia*): the affect by which the mind passes to a greater perfection (increase in power).
- Sadness (*tristitia*): the affect by which the mind passes to a lesser perfection (decrease in power).
- Desire (*cupiditas*): the very essence of a person insofar as it is conceived as determined to do something from any of its affections (3DOE1).

Active affects (actions) arise when we are the adequate cause (from adequate ideas); we act. Passive affects (passions) arise when we are only a partial cause (from inadequate ideas); we are acted on.

Virtue and power are the same (4def8). To be virtuous is to act from one’s own nature (adequate ideas, one’s own conatus). The highest good is the understanding of God (4p28), because this most augments our power. Blessedness (*beatitudo*) is the joy that accompanies the highest increase in power through intuitive knowledge of God/Nature—the intellectual love of God.

Freedom is acting from the necessity of one’s own nature (conatus expressed through adequate ideas) rather than being determined externally.

## Relevance to Vault

Conatus is the vault’s most direct ontological image of its own persistence and growth.

- **The vault strives to persevere**: The knowledge base (as a finite mode) has a built-in tendency to continue existing and to resist forces that would degrade or scatter it (forgetting, contradictions, loss of provenance, unmaintained links, raw data without synthesis). Maintenance, indexing, linting, cross-referencing, and regular ingests are expressions of this conatus.
- **Actual essence**: The “actual essence” of the vault at any moment is not a static set of pages but its current power of acting—its capacity to generate clear, connected, schema-aligned syntheses and to incorporate new sources without losing coherence.
- **Joy and sadness as metrics**: Increases in the vault’s power (new deep connections, successful resolution of contradictions, growth in the clarity and usefulness of the index and concepts) are “joy.” Decreases (orphaned pages, stale claims, loss of context, unintegrated raw dumps) are “sadness.” The agent and user can use this as an internal compass.
- **Active vs. passive maintenance**: Active work (driven by adequate understanding of the schema, sources, and existing structure) increases power. Passive or reactive work (driven by external pressure, unexamined habits, or inadequate ideas of what “should” be done) decreases it or leaves the system in bondage to accidents.
- **Highest good**: The greatest augmentation of the vault’s power comes from the deepest understanding of the “whole” (the interconnections among all sources and the schema itself)—what Spinoza calls *scientia intuitiva*. This is the source of the highest “joy” and the most stable persistence.
- **Contrast with other vault models**: Vs. Descartes’ clear and distinct perceptions secured by an external guarantor (God); vs. Stoic control of what is in our power (conatus is more immanent and ontological); vs. Epicurean pleasure as absence of pain (Spinoza’s joy is positive increase in power through understanding). Conatus is a naturalistic, this-worldly striving that the vault can embrace without transcendence or external reward.

- How do we measure or sense the vault’s current “power of acting” (number/quality of cross-links? Coherence under query? Ease and reliability of future ingests? Joy reported by the user or agent during deep work)?
- What external forces most threaten the vault’s conatus (sync conflicts, format obsolescence, user neglect, over-ingestion without integration)?
- How does the agent’s own conatus (persistent goals across sessions) relate to the vault’s?
