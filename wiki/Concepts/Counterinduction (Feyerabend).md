# Counterinduction (Feyerabend)

> The procedure of advancing and developing hypotheses that **contradict well-confirmed theories and/or well-established facts**. Against the rule that new theories must agree with the accepted view and with observation, Feyerabend argues that "we may advance science by proceeding counterinductively" — because rival hypotheses are often the *only* way to uncover the hidden assumptions buried in our "facts."

## The Two Faces of Counterinduction

Feyerabend (*Against Method*, chs. 2–3) distinguishes two counter-inductive moves:

1. **Against established theories** — *proliferate* views inconsistent with the reigning theory. The **consistency condition** (new hypotheses must agree with accepted ones) is unreasonable: it "preserves the older theory, and not the better theory." Theories contradicting a well-confirmed view "give us evidence that cannot be obtained in any other way," because some facts only become visible *through* an alternative. (See [[Concepts/Proliferation and Tenacity (Feyerabend)]].)

2. **Against the "facts"** — develop hypotheses inconsistent with observation. Since "no theory ever agrees with all the facts in its domain," and since **facts are constituted by older ideologies** ([[Concepts/Natural Interpretations and the Galileo Case (Feyerabend)|natural interpretations]]), a clash with the facts may be a *sign of progress*, not refutation. To find and test the hidden assumptions inside an observation, you must adopt a theory that conflicts with it and "turn the argument around," using the conflict as a **detecting device** for the buried "ideological ingredients of our knowledge."

## Why It Works

The point rests on **theory-ladenness**: an apparently decisive "observational refutation" smuggles in unexamined theories disguised as plain facts (Galileo's tower argument hid the assumption that perceived motion = real motion). You cannot expose these from inside the accepted framework, because the framework's own concepts are used to identify the percepts. The only escape from the circle is "an external measure of comparison" — a *contradicting* alternative. Counterinduction is thus the engine of discovery: "Ideological ingredients of our knowledge… are discovered with the help of theories which are refuted by them. They are discovered counterinductively." Feyerabend's historical example is the **Parmenides/Zeno** paradoxes of motion: Diogenes' "refutation" by simply walking missed the point; holding the counter-factual argument *open* led to far richer results.

## Relation to Other Vault Content

- **Inversion of induction**: a direct counter to the inductivist demand to generalize from confirming instances; relates to [[Concepts/Problem of Induction (Hume)]] and Popper's anti-inductivism (Popper too prized bold conjectures, but counterinduction goes further: keep theories that conflict with *the evidence itself*).
- **Vs. Popper's falsification**: where [[Concepts/Falsifiability as Criterion of Demarcation (Popper)|Popper]] says a refuted theory must (tentatively) go, Feyerabend says cultivate refuted/conflicting theories precisely *because* they expose what the consensus cannot see. See [[Contradictions/Popper vs Kuhn - Falsification vs Paradigms]].
- **With [[Concepts/Confirmation Holism (Duhem-Quine Thesis)]]**: underdetermination is what makes counterinduction viable — a theory can be held against the data by relocating the conflict elsewhere in the web.
- **With pluralism**: counterinduction is the methodological face of [[Concepts/Proliferation and Tenacity (Feyerabend)|proliferation/tenacity]] and of Mill's case for entertaining contrary opinions ([[Concepts/Social Cohesion vs. Individual Liberty (Russell)]]).

## Vault Meta / AI

A concrete heuristic: to stress-test a settled page or consensus reading, deliberately develop the **contradicting** interpretation and see what hidden assumptions it forces into view — a structured devil's-advocate / red-team move (the vault's contradiction pages are a mild institutional form of this). For AI/agentic reasoning: generate hypotheses *inconsistent* with the current best answer to surface unstated premises and brittle "facts"; consensus-only search is self-confirming. Bounded by Feyerabend's caution — counterinduction is a discovery tactic, not a standing licence to ignore evidence.
