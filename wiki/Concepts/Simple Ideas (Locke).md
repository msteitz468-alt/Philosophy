# Simple Ideas (Locke)

> Some of our ideas are SIMPLE and some COMPLEX. Simple ideas are uncompounded appearances: "each in itself uncompounded, contains in it nothing but ONE UNIFORM APPEARANCE, OR CONCEPTION IN THE MIND, and is not distinguishable into different ideas." They are the materials of all knowledge, furnished only by sensation and reflection. The mind is passive in receiving them and cannot invent or destroy any new simple idea; it can only repeat, compare, and compound those it has received.

## Key Distinction and Properties (Book II, Ch. II)

- Simple ideas enter the mind "simple; and unmixed" even if the qualities in the object are united/blended.
- Examples: coldness and hardness in ice are as distinct in the mind as smell and whiteness of a lily, or taste of sugar and smell of a rose.
- "The mind can neither make nor destroy them": "it is not in the power of the most exalted wit, or enlarged understanding, by any quickness or variety of thought, to INVENT or FRAME one new simple idea in the mind, not taken in by the ways before mentioned; nor can any force of the understanding DESTROY those that are there."
- The understanding is "merely passive" in their reception: "the objects of our senses do, many of them, obtrude their particular ideas upon our minds whether we will or not."
- Only qualities that affect the senses are imaginable as simple ideas. (Speculation on other possible senses in other creatures.)

Simple ideas are the "materials of all our knowledge." Once stored, the mind has power to compound them into complex ideas (see future [[Concepts/Complex Ideas, Modes, Substances, and Relations (Locke)]]).

## Connection to Primary/Secondary Qualities

Many simple ideas of sensation come from primary qualities (real resemblances: solidity, extension, figure, number, motion/rest) or secondary qualities (powers in bodies depending on primary to produce sensations like color, taste, sound — no resemblance in the body itself).

## Vault Relevance and Contrasts

- **Direct foundation for Hume**: The Copy Principle radicalizes this — simple ideas are faint copies of prior lively impressions (from the same two sources). The "materials" Locke describes become the building blocks Hume subjects to sceptical analysis (no simple impression of necessary connexion or self as substance).
- **Vs. Descartes / rationalists**: No simple innate ideas (e.g., idea of God as simple trademark). All simple ideas trace to experience; intellect alone (pure intellection) does not furnish new ones before sensation.
- **Vs. atomists (Lucretius)**: Simple ideas of secondary qualities (color, taste) are in the mind as uniform appearances, produced by primary configurations in bodies (or atoms). Locke systematizes the distinction; Lucretius emphasizes they are not "in" the atoms at all.
- **AI / models / grounding**: Simple ideas = atomic perceptual features or embeddings. Models cannot "invent" new grounded simple representations without data (sensation) or internal operations (reflection). Complex concepts (abstractions, substances, causes) are compounds built from them. Passive reception phase = training data ingestion; active compounding = reasoning/synthesis layers.
- **Wiki practice**: Raw sources provide the "simple ideas" (facts, claims, observations). Wiki pages and concepts are compounds. Maintenance requires attending to the simple materials (not over-compounding without clear simple foundations). The "cannot invent new simple ideas" is a caution against fabricating without source grounding.
