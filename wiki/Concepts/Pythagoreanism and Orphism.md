# Pythagoreanism and Orphism
> The relationship between two overlapping but distinct strands of Greek religious-philosophical life — and a case study in how *not* to treat ancient "movements" as systems with a single essence.

## Summary
On Gábor Betegh's chapter in [[Sources/A History of Pythagoreanism - Carl A. Huffman (ed.) (2014)]], late antiquity (Proclus, Iamblichus' *Hieros Logos*) forged a seamless **Orpheus → Pythagoras** chain — Pythagoras initiated by Aglaophamus at Libethra — a story *never attested before Iamblichus*. Classical sources already associate the two (Herodotus on the wool-burial taboo) but treat both as fringe; some (Ion of Chios, Epigenes, Aristotle via Cicero) even held that **Pythagoreans authored the poems passed off as "Orphic"** (by Cercops, Brontinus).

## The Non-Essentialist Picture
New material evidence — the **Olbia bone plates**, the gold tablets (Hipponion, Pelinna, Thurii), and the **Derveni papyrus** — has made Orphism tangible and discredited the old hypercritical view (Wilamowitz, Linforth) that "Orphism" barely existed. Betegh endorses a **pluralistic, non-essentialist** account: neither Orphism nor Pythagoreanism is a "system." Refining Burkert's image —

> "Bacchic, Orphic, and Pythagorean are circles each of which has its own center, and while these circles have areas that coincide, each preserves its own special sphere." (Burkert)

Crucially, **neither vegetarianism nor metempsychosis is a shared *essential* core**:
- [[Concepts/Metempsychosis (Transmigration of Souls)|Metempsychosis]] is among the **best-documented tenets of *Pythagoras*** himself, whereas evidence for it in early *Orphism* is "scarce and mostly indirect."
- Strict vegetarianism was *not* a core Pythagorean feature (see [[Concepts/Pythagorean Way of Life and Ethics]]); Orphic *orpheotelestai* clients show no lasting lifestyle change.

## Sociological and Methodological Contrast
- **Orphism** centers on one-off *teletai* (rites regaining purity for inherited/ancestral guilt); **Pythagoreanism** on everyday standing *praxis* and lifelong membership in a *hetairia*.
- Yet both share a deeper *method*: giving **new religious significance to natural-philosophy concepts** via an authority figure plus interpretable pronouncements (the number three / Zeus as "head, middle, end"; [[Thinkers/Philolaus]]' central **Hearth** ↔ the Derveni cosmogony's central ball of fire).
- A key *difference*: the Derveni poem has a demiurgic **Mind** ordering the cosmos, whereas Philolaus "never says *who* fitted the limiters and unlimiteds together" — "properly theological considerations are strikingly missing from early Pythagorean documents." This is significant for [[Concepts/Number as the Essence of Reality (Pythagoras)]] and [[Concepts/Harmony of the Spheres]]: the early Pythagorean cosmos is *ordered* but not obviously *created*.
