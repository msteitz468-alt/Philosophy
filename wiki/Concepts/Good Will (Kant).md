# Good Will (Kant)

> There is nothing it is possible to think of anywhere in the world, or indeed anything at all outside it, that can be held to be good without limitation, excepting only a **good will**. [...] The good will is good not through what it effects or accomplishes, not through its efficacy for attaining any intended end, but only through its willing, i.e., good in itself, and considered for itself, without comparison, it is to be estimated far higher than anything that could be brought about by it in favor of any inclination, or indeed, if you prefer, of the sum of all inclinations. Even if through the peculiar disfavor of fate, or through the meager endowment of a stepmotherly nature, this will were entirely lacking in the resources to carry out its aim, if with its greatest effort nothing of it were accomplished, and only the good will were left over (to be sure, not a mere wish, but as the summoning up of all the means insofar as they are in our control): then it would shine like a jewel for itself, as something that has its full worth in itself.

## Development in the *Groundwork* (Preface + First Section)

Talents of mind (understanding, wit, judgment), qualities of temperament (courage, resolution, self-control), and gifts of fortune (power, wealth, honor, health, happiness) are good and to be wished for *only if guided by a good will*. Without it, they can become extremely evil and harmful (e.g., cold-bloodedness makes a villain more dangerous and abominable). Moderation, self-control, etc., praised by the ancients, lack inner worth without good will—they can serve evil.

The good will's worth is *intrinsic*, not instrumental or consequential. It is good "not through what it effects or accomplishes... but only through its willing." Even if it achieves nothing (due to fate or "stepmotherly nature"), as long as it has willed with all available means, its worth remains undiminished—"it would shine like a jewel for itself." Utility, fruitlessness, or success/failure neither add to nor subtract from this worth (they are merely the "setting").

Nature's purpose in endowing a rational being with reason and will cannot be happiness (instinct would achieve that end more reliably and safely). Reason's authentic vocation is to produce a **will good in itself**, which is the highest good and the condition for everything else (including any claim to happiness). A good will has its own species of contentment from fulfilling an end set by reason itself, even if it conflicts with inclinations.

The good will is not the *only* or *entire* good, but the *supreme* good and indispensable condition for the worthiness to be happy or for any other goods to have moral value.

## Relevance to Vault

- **Contrast with Hume/Locke empiricism**: Moral worth grounded in the a priori principle of the will (good will/duty/respect for law), not in sentiment, custom, utility, experience, or consequences. Challenges the vault's strong empiricist thread (Hume's "custom as the great guide," Locke's ideas from sensation/reflection as the sole materials).
- **Contrast with Aristotle**: Good will as unconditional jewel vs eudaimonia (happiness/flourishing as the highest human good achieved through virtuous activity over a complete life). Duty/principle vs function, habituation, and the mean.
- **Contrast with Spinoza**: Good will as the condition for worth (even against inclinations) vs conatus (striving to persevere/increase power) and intellectual love of God/Nature as highest joy/good.
- **With Stoics**: Resonates with duty and rational control (good will as "summoning up of all the means"), but Kant's is a priori universal law rather than role or acceptance of fate.
- **AI/agents**: "Good will" as aligned intent and principle (the maxim or policy) rather than outcomes/rewards. Relevant to constitutional/aligned AI: an agent whose "willing" (decision procedure) has intrinsic worth because it is governed by universalizable, non-self-undermining rules, even if it "achieves nothing" in a particular instance or faces obstacles. Jewel-like robustness of principle over brittle optimization.
- **Wiki/personal**: Maintenance and synthesis from "good will"—i.e., from respect for the schema (GROK.md), accurate sourcing, and universal principles (e.g., "I ought never to edit in a way I could not will as a universal practice for all maintainers") rather than convenience, speed, or personal inclination. Integrity in the face of "adversities" (incomplete sources, ambiguity) shines independently of immediate "success" (perfect synthesis). Complements Lockean "book of business" (ownership through labor) with deontological guardrails.
- **Ethics cluster**: Provides the rational a priori foundation that tensions and enriches the vault's eudaimonia, ataraxia, dichotomy of control, natural law, and restless heart/rest in God.
