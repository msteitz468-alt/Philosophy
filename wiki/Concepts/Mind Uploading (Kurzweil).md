# Mind Uploading (Kurzweil)

> Ray Kurzweil's (2005) vision of scanning a human brain at sufficient detail (via nanobots by late 2020s) and reinstantiating its salient details (personality, memory, skills) into a powerful nonbiological computational substrate. This captures the "pattern" of a person, allowing transcendence of biology. By the 2030s, computational resources will suffice; gradual transfer occurs as nonbiological intelligence dominates. Confirmation via personalized "Jane Smith" Turing test. Part of the Singularity (~2045) where humans merge with machines.

## Summary

From *The Singularity Is Near* (Ch. 4, 6):

"Uploading a human brain means scanning all of its salient details and then reinstantiating those details into a suitably powerful computational substrate. This process would capture a person’s entire personality, memory, skills, and history."

Scanning: from within using nanobots (available late 2020s). Higher estimates: ~10^19 cps and 10^18 bits (vs. lower for functional simulation). Hybrid: standard modules for basic functions, sub-neuron detail for personality/skills.

Confirmation: convincing a judge the upload is indistinguishable from the original specific person (personalized Turing test). By then, nonbio will have passed original Turing test (~2029); complications with enhanced judges.

Gradual transfer: "We will have effectively uploaded ourselves, albeit gradually, never quite noticing the transfer. There will be no 'old Ray' and 'new Ray,' just an increasingly capable Ray."

Bodies: virtual in realistic environments, nanotech physical, etc. (Human Body 2.0/3.0).

## Key Claims

- Feasible in early 2030s (resources + scanning); perfected by end of 2030s.
- Captures "you" sufficiently for any purpose (within natural change over fraction of a second).
- Part of broader merger: nonbio portion dominates by 2040s (billions of times more capable).
- Addresses identity: gradual, so continuity preserved.

## Relation to Vault

- **Hofstadter (GEB), Mitchell (2019)**: Mitchell (Hofstadter student) engages Kurzweil's uploading/singularity in context of 2014 Google meeting (Hofstadter's terror at hype); her book as skeptical on current limits for "true" understanding needed in uploads. Cross to Hofstadter/GEB, Mitchell sources/thinkers.

- **Bostrom (Superintelligence)**: Uploading as one path; but orthogonality means uploaded minds could have arbitrary goals (not necessarily human-compatible). Cross to Bostrom.

- **Virk (The Simulation Hypothesis, 2019)**: Uploading is Stage 10 ("downloadable consciousness") of the [[Concepts/Road to the Simulation Point (Virk)]]; Virk also reads reincarnation doctrine as consciousness download/upload at birth/death — see [[Concepts/Karma and Reincarnation as Quest Engine (Virk)]] and [[Concepts/Simulation Hypothesis (Virk)]].

- **Turing, Minsky, Dennett**: Builds on Turing test (personalized version); Minsky's agent view of mind; Dennett on self as narrative/center of gravity (uploading as pattern transfer). Cross.

- **Wiener, Pearl**: Pattern (Wiener's "organism as message") vs. need for causal models (Pearl) in uploads.

## Contradictions / Open Questions

- > [!warning] Identity/continuity: Is the upload "you"? (Original may exist; quantum/embodiment issues; gradual vs. sudden.) Cross to Dennett/Hofstadter on self.
- Chalmers (*Reality+* ch. 15) now supplies the argument Kurzweil lacked — fading qualia for substrate-neutral consciousness — while splitting the identity verdict: nondestructive uploading creates a new person; "gradual uploading is the best bet for surviving." See [[Concepts/Machine Consciousness and Mind Uploading (Chalmers)]].

- Optimistic feasibility vs. Mitchell/Bostrom limits/risks (current AI lacks understanding; uploaded minds could be dangerous).
