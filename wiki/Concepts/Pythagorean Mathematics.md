# Pythagorean Mathematics
> What "Pythagorean mathematics" actually was — and the hard truth that almost none of it can be securely traced to Pythagoras himself. Reviel Netz's reframing of the discipline as a network, not a continuous tradition.

## Summary
On Reviel Netz's chapter in [[Sources/A History of Pythagoreanism - Carl A. Huffman (ed.) (2014)]], Greek mathematics is best understood as a series of short-lived **"networks"** rather than a continuous school tradition. The *first* network (the figures in Proclus' Eudemus-based list) was centered on Plato's Athens and specialized in **non-configurational classification** — numbers, means, ratios, proportions, irrationals, harmonies (as opposed to the later configurational, diagram-based geometry of Archimedes' network).

## Archytas as the Hinge
**[[Thinkers/Archytas]]** is the crucial figure: he belonged simultaneously to the south-Italian "Pythagoreans," to the mathematicians' network, and to Plato's circle — "the gear that engaged Pythagoreanism with mainstream mathematics." Netz quotes Huffman's striking paradox: Archytas "fits the popular conception of a Pythagorean better than anyone in the Pythagorean tradition," and yet **"Aristotle never refers to Archytas as a Pythagorean!"** Netz's epigram: *"Archytas was not a card-carrying Pythagorean: there were no cards."* Membership was recognition, not doctrine — echoing the "family resemblance" thesis (see [[Schools/Pythagoreanism]]).

## Demolishing the Myths
- **No special "pebble arithmetic."** The Becker/Burnet theory of a distinctively early Pythagorean arithmetic done with *psēphoi* (pebbles) "has no evidential support"; it rests on a misunderstanding of ancient numeracy. Read correctly, *psēphoi* means **abacus calculation**, not mosaic-pebbles.
- **Eurytus rehabilitated.** Eurytus' notorious practice of assigning numbers to "man" or "horse" is "wrong but intellectually meaningful," not the silly mosaic procedure that pseudo-Alexander imagined.
- **The number-doctrine is Aristotle's reading of Philolaus.** Aristotle's claim that the Pythagoreans made numbers the principles of all things "likely derives from Aristotle's *interpretation* of [[Thinkers/Philolaus]]," not from a literal Pythagorean doctrine. See [[Concepts/Number as the Essence of Reality (Pythagoras)]] and [[Sources/Aristotle - Metaphysics]].
- **The geometrical ascriptions are undecidable.** Proclus' two hardest pieces of evidence (Eudemus on *Elements* I.32 and on "application of areas") are simply *undecidable* — leaving two starkly opposed images of Pythagoreanism (the discovery of incommensurability, classically credited to [[Thinkers/Hippasus]], Netz treats with the same caution).

## Wider Significance
After Archimedes, "number-and-music metaphysics" became hard to sustain, forcing later thinkers either to **archaize** (the Neoplatonists, reviving Nicomachus) or to **invent a new mathematical metaphysics** (the Scientific Revolution). Pythagorean mathematics thus matters less as a body of theorems than as the recurrent *temptation* to read the cosmos as number — the through-line from Philolaus to Kepler.
