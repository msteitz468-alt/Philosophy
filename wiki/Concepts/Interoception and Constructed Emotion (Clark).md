# Interoception and Constructed Emotion (Clark)
> The prediction machine's first job is staying alive: hunger, mood, and emotion are anticipatory constructs built from predictions about the body, not passive readouts of present bodily states.

## Summary

Clark extends [[Concepts/Predictive Processing (Clark)]] inward. "The primary task of all the prediction machinery in our heads is to help us stay alive," keeping inner bodily states within tight bounds of viability. This resolves the **Dark Room puzzle** (if the goal is minimizing prediction error, why not seek a silent dark corner and die?): creatures *predict* foraging and feeding, and those predictions are self-fulfilling, so a living system's expected states are active ones, not the void.

**Interoception** is the inward-looking sensory system (heart, gut, viscera, blood sugar, temperature), distinct from exteroception and proprioception. Its prediction errors drive corrective action. The regulatory pillars are **homeostasis** (returning to a set point; term coined by Walter Cannon) and **allostasis** (preemptively *altering* the set points to meet anticipated demands). "Feelings of hunger and tiredness are for the most part signals not of present needs but of predicted, impending challenges."

## Key Claims / Positions

- **Body budgeting** (Lisa Feldman Barrett): the brain anticipatorily tracks and replenishes resources (water, salt, glucose). "Every prediction you make... is always in relation to the activity of your heart and lungs, your metabolism, your immune function." Every emotion-related brain region is a body-budgeting region.
- **Emotion is constructed, not fingerprinted.** Against a naive reading of William James ("our feelings and emotions are... nothing other than perceptions of our own varying physiological responses"), Clark holds there are "no neat, recurrent 'bodily fingerprints' for the different emotional states." Emotion is **interoceptive predictive processing**: bodily arousal integrated with higher-level predictions of its cause (the same racing heart read as fear, excitement, attraction, or a heart attack). Framing therefore alters feeling *itself* — "raw feelings" are already "highly informed guesses" (anterior insular cortex central).
- **Error dynamics** — how well error-minimization is going *relative to expectation* — drive curiosity, mood, and a "Goldilocks zone" of optimal learning (supported by "artificial curiosity" simulations and infant studies).
- **Depression as a disorder of allostasis** / "locked-in brain": inefficient energy regulation plus **cognitive immunization** (high precision on prior negative beliefs robs positive evidence of updating power).
- **Aesthetic chills** ("salience detection hypothesis"): frisson marks the sudden resolution of important uncertainty; art and music are "probability designs" (Karin Kukkonen) — artifacts engineered to interact with predictive brains.

## How This Relates to Prior Thinkers

- **[[Thinkers/Daniel Dennett]]**: constructed emotion parallels Dennett's deflation of qualia into reactive dispositions — feelings are informed guesses, not intrinsic given data.
- **[[Thinkers/Martin Heidegger]]**: Clark's interoceptive attunement is a naturalist cousin of Heideggerian *Befindlichkeit* / mood (see [[Concepts/Disclosedness — Attunement, Understanding, Discourse (Heidegger)]]) — mood as a way the world is disclosed, here recast as body-budgeting prediction.
- **[[Thinkers/Aristotle]]**: contrasts with [[Concepts/Theory of Perception (Aristotle)]] — perception (including of the body) is active construction, not reception of form.
