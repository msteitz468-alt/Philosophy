# Naturalistic Dualism (Chalmers)
> "If this is dualism, then we should learn to love dualism" — phenomenal properties as fundamental features alongside space-time, mass, and charge, connected to the physical by basic psychophysical laws.

## Summary

*The Conscious Mind* ch. 4. The master argument: conscious experiences exist; a physically identical zombie world is logically possible; so consciousness facts are "further facts about our world, over and above the physical facts"; so materialism is false. What follows is **property dualism**, not Cartesian substance dualism: experience is fundamental, governed by psychophysical (supervenience) laws that *supplement* physics rather than contradict it — analogous to Maxwell adding electromagnetism. Possibly the basic properties are **protophenomenal**; possibly the truth is a broader monism, "but it cannot be a materialist monism."

Objections met: Kripke-style a posteriori necessity is defused by the 2-D framework (and for consciousness "the primary and secondary intensions coincide"); "strong metaphysical necessity" (Loar's type-B move) is brute and ad hoc — "Why couldn't God have created a zombie world?" Jackson's Mary gains factual knowledge; the only coherent materialist reply, the Lewis–Nemirow ability analysis, is "deeply implausible."

The **epiphenomenalism worry** (causal closure + natural supervenience = idle experience) gets four responses, the favored one Russellian: physics characterizes entities only relationally, and phenomenal or protophenomenal properties may be the *intrinsic* natures the causal flux relates — "As Russell (1927) notes, this is a matter about which physical theory is silent." The view is "epiphenomenalistic to a first approximation," which Chalmers argues is livable; interactionist quantum dualism is rejected.

The **logical geography**: Type-A (functionalism/eliminativism — Dennett, Lewis, Ryle), Type-B (a posteriori materialism — Loar, Levine, Papineau), Type-C (property dualism — Jackson, Chalmers), Type-C′ (Russellian — Russell, Feigl, Lockwood, Maxwell). The A-vs-rest divide is the "Great Divide" over taking consciousness seriously.

## How Different Thinkers Use This

- **[[Thinkers/Descartes]]**: the ancestor disavowed — no second substance, no interaction, causal closure kept.
- **[[Thinkers/Baruch Spinoza]]** ([[Sources/Ethics - Spinoza (Cambridge, Kisner ed. 2018)]]) and **[[Thinkers/Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz]]**: the deep precedents for dual-aspect and panpsychist alternatives Chalmers's Type-C′ revives; see also [[Concepts/Double-Aspect Theory of Information (Chalmers)]].
- **[[Thinkers/Daniel Dennett]]**: "Given the way that dualism wallows in mystery, accepting dualism is giving up" — quoted and denied; see [[Contradictions/Dennett vs Nagel - Multiple Drafts vs Subjective Experience]].
- **[[Thinkers/Thomas Henry Huxley]]**: the historical epiphenomenalist position Chalmers approaches "to a first approximation."

## Contradictions / Open Questions

- > [!warning] Direct clash with [[Concepts/Qualia Disqualified (Dennett)]] and [[Concepts/Multiple Drafts Model (Dennett)]]: Dennett's type-A materialism holds that zombie conceivability is an illusion; Chalmers holds that denying it "denies the evidence of our own experience."
- > [!warning] [[Thinkers/John Searle]]'s biological naturalism agrees against reduction but rejects both dualism and organizational invariance — a three-way tension with [[Concepts/Chinese Room Argument (Searle)]].
