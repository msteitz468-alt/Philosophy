# Power-Knowledge (Foucault)
> Power and knowledge are not opposed — power produces knowledge, and knowledge exercises power; the hyphen in "power-knowledge" (*pouvoir-savoir*) marks their irreducible entanglement.

## Summary

The power-knowledge concept emerges from Foucault's genealogical phase, especially from the GIP (Groupe d'Information sur les Prisons) prison activism (1971–) and *Surveiller et punir* (1975). The key move is rejecting both the Enlightenment view (knowledge is achieved by escaping power, by "speaking truth to power") and the crude Marxist view (knowledge is determined by class interests).

For Foucault, power is not a possession or a force exercised top-down by a sovereign — it is relational, exercised in networks of relations, and **productive**: it produces subjects, bodies, and knowledge rather than merely suppressing them. Every major institution (prison, clinic, school, factory, army) is simultaneously a site of power and a site of the production of knowledge about the individuals it processes. The prison generates criminology; the clinic generates medicine; the school generates pedagogy. This knowledge feeds back into the exercise of power over the individuals it categorizes.

The "micro-physics of power" — Foucault's term — describes the capillary distribution of power through the tiniest practices, habits, and techniques of bodies, rather than concentrating it in the state or economy.

## Key Claims

- Power is not repressive but productive: it creates identities ("the criminal," "the homosexual," "the delinquent"), knowledge (criminology, psychiatry, sexology), and normativity.
- Every form of knowledge is embedded in power relations; there is no "view from nowhere" — but this is not relativism, since genealogy can distinguish better and worse uses of power-knowledge.
- The "specific intellectual" (who speaks from concrete knowledge of a specific institution) is politically more effective than the "universal intellectual" (who speaks in the name of universal humanity).
- Resistance is always already present wherever power is exercised — there is no position outside power, only forms of counter-conduct within it.

## Contradictions / Open Questions

- If there is no position outside power-knowledge, is Foucault's own genealogical analysis also a power-knowledge operation? The book cannot escape what it analyzes.
- Late Foucault's turn to "care of the self" suggests a space of ethical practice not entirely determined by power — in tension with the totalizing picture of power-knowledge.

> [!warning] Compare with [[Thinkers/Karl Marx]]: Marx grounds ideological distortion in class relations and the mode of production. Foucault's power-knowledge is more diffuse (no single source, no base/superstructure) and analyses the body rather than labor as the primary site of subjection.
