# Nominal and Real Essences (Locke)

> In Book III (substances): Nominal essence = the abstract idea or set of qualities by which we name and classify a sort/species (e.g., the observable properties we call "gold": yellow, heavy, fusible, etc.). Real essence = the underlying constitution of the thing (insensible primary qualities/particles) that causes those properties, which we do not fully know. We sort by nominal essences (our ideas), not real (which are unknown or partial); this explains why classifications are conventional and why we can't have demonstrative knowledge of co-existence in substances beyond observed.

## Vault Relevance

- **With primary/secondary**: Real essence ties to primary qualities; nominal to the ideas/powers we experience.
- **With Hume**: Limits on knowledge of necessary co-existence in substances; supports induction problems.
- **AI/classification**: Models learn nominal essences (feature clusters for labels) vs. real (underlying causal structure, hard to access). Good for ontology in knowledge bases: pages as nominal, sources as pointing to real.
- **Wiki**: Concepts/categories are nominal essences (our abstractions from sources); "real" is the raw data. Avoid mistaking our labels for the things.
