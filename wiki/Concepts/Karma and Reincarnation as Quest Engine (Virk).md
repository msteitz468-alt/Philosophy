# Karma and Reincarnation as Quest Engine (Virk)

> Virk's Part III thesis: the world's mystical traditions — maya, reincarnation, karma, recording angels, life reviews — read naturally as descriptions of a multiplayer video game with respawning characters, persistent player state, and an algorithmic quest generator.

## Summary

The mapping runs tradition-by-tradition:

- **Maya (Hindu/Buddhist)**: the physical world as a temporary, shared, dream-like construct; enlightenment is "waking up" inside the dream — the mystic's version of recognizing the rendered world as rendered. Yogananda: "Birth and death are doors through which you pass from one dream to another." Buddhist **dream yoga** (Six Yogas of Naropa) trains exactly this recognition.
- **Reincarnation as respawn**: the soul (player) persists across lives; the body (character/avatar) changes. Consciousness "downloads" at birth and "uploads" at death — Stage 10 technology ([[Concepts/Mind Uploading (Kurzweil)]]) as ancient doctrine.
- **Karma as persistent data**: action-consequence information stored outside the rendered world (subtle bodies, Akashic Records) and retrieved to shape future lives — precisely game state stored server-side.
- **The quest engine**: when players with karmic history share a scene, an engine scans their karmic rosters and generates contextual quests; completion resolves or creates karma. Virk: "Buddha's endless wheel is now a computer algorithm... The Lords of Karma have been reduced to a sophisticated AI that can track billions of players and create quests for them in each of their lifetimes in real time."
- **Western traditions**: recording angels (Islam's Kiraman Katibin, the Book of Life) as logging daemons; God as the omniscient process running the system; NDE "life reviews" (Moody, Brinkley) as gameplay playback; purgatory/Bardo as intermediate storage states.
- **Anomalies as glitches**: synchronicity (Jung's "acausal connecting principle"), déjà vu (Dick's reprogrammed variables), UFOs, and OBEs (virtual-camera repositioning) as artifacts of the simulation's information architecture.

## How Different Thinkers Use This

- This is the book's argument for the RPG version of the [[Concepts/Simulation Hypothesis (Virk)]]: "the Eastern mystics may be closer to the nature of the Great Simulation than many of the scientists."
- **Jung** and **Jacques Vallée** supply the associative-information-storage reading of coincidence and UFO phenomena.
- The maya doctrine parallels [[Thinkers/Plato]]'s cave and [[Thinkers/George Berkeley]]'s idealism — three traditions demoting the perceived world to appearance.

## Contradictions / Open Questions

- > [!warning] Buddhist doctrine explicitly denies a persistent soul (anattā — "bundle of cause and effect," per Ricard's own framing); Virk's persistent-player model is closer to Hindu ātman than to the Buddhist sources he cites. Unflagged tension inside the book's own evidence.
- The method is analogical throughout: that a doctrine *can* be described in game vocabulary does not evidence that it is *true* of our world — a concern the vault's falsifiability pages ([[Contradictions/Popper vs Kuhn - Falsification vs Paradigms]]) sharpen.
