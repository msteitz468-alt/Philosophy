# Words as Signs of Ideas and Language (Locke)

> Words are the great instrument and common tie of society. Man is fitted to frame articulate sounds (words). Use them as signs of internal conceptions/ideas to communicate thoughts (voluntary imposition; not by natural connexion — else one language). General signs (to avoid multiplication; stand for general ideas/abstract ideas). Negative/privative words signify absence. Ultimately derived from sensible ideas (even abstract words transferred from sensation; "spirit" from breath, etc.).

Words (except proper names) are general, standing for sorts/ranks/species/genera (by abstract ideas). Primary/immediate signification: ideas in the mind of the speaker. Secret references: (1) to ideas supposed in other men's minds (common acceptation for communication); (2) to the reality of things (especially substances). By use, readily excite ideas. Often used without clear/determinate signification (learned before ideas; focus on words not things; "parrots"). Signification perfectly arbitrary.

(Full treatment covers names of simple ideas, mixed modes, substances (nominal vs. real essences; species by abstract ideas), relations, particles; imperfections and abuse of words (equivocation, etc.); remedies like definitions and attention to ideas.)
