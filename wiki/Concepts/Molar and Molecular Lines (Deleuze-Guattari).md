# Molar and Molecular Lines (Deleuze-Guattari)
> Individuals and groups are composed of three kinds of lines — rigid molar, supple molecular, and lines of flight — which are always immanent to one another and define a politics of becoming.

## Summary

Against the Marxist claim that a society is defined by its contradictions, Deleuze and Guattari argue that a society is defined by its **lines of flight** — what escapes, flees, deterritorializes. Three kinds of line compose every social formation and every individual:

**1. Rigid segmentary (molar) lines** encode life into binary segments: family/profession, man/woman, adult/child, citizen/foreigner. They are overcoded by an abstract State machine that homogenizes the segments into a unified social body. These lines are arborescent, hierarchical, and anchored in the present.

**2. Supple molecular lines** generate fluxes of deterritorialization that shoot between molar segments — they cross thresholds, produce micro-becomings, introduce variation into the codes without yet rupturing them. May 1968 is cited as the eruption of a molecular line that the molar segments could not contain.

**3. Lines of flight/rupture** carry persons or groups away entirely — toward the imperceptible, the becoming-universal. They are the condition of possibility for social transformation but carry their own dangers.

**Three dangers corresponding to three lines:**
- Molar danger: overcoding, fascism at the macro level.
- Molecular danger: microfascisms and "black holes" of subjective implosion — the molecular line can produce "a most tender and intimate despotism."
- Line of flight danger: becoming a machine of destruction (fascism in the strict sense, where the line of flight turns into self-destruction).

**Politics as active experimentation:** "Politics is active experimentation, since we do not know in advance which way a line is going to turn." The schizoanalytic task is cartographic: trace the lines, identify the dangers, construct war-machines that maintain lines of flight without tipping into destruction.

**Against the State model:** The war machine is not the State's opposite (reactive anti-State) but a fundamentally different mode of social organization — nomadic, operating on lines of flight, without centralized command or molar overcoding. The question for revolutionary politics: can organization avoid becoming a model of the State?

## Key Claims

- "A society is defined by its lines of flight which affect masses of all kinds."
- "Politics is active experimentation, since we do not know in advance which way a line is going to turn."
- Three dangers: blowing up rigid segments too fast destroys the organism; molecular lines harbor microfascisms; lines of flight tip into destruction machines.
- Minority questions are contemporary revolutionary forms: "A new type of revolution is in the course of becoming possible."

## How Different Thinkers Use This

- **[[Thinkers/Karl Marx]]:** Marx defines society by contradiction (capital/labor) and envisions revolution as the capture of the State apparatus. Deleuze/Guattari define society by lines of flight and envision revolution as the construction of a war-machine that bypasses the State model.
- **[[Concepts/Collective Action and Free Riding (Olson)]] / [[Concepts/Constitutional Political Economy (Buchanan-Tullock)]]:** Both Olson and Buchanan-Tullock analyze politics through interest groups and rational choice. Deleuze/Guattari: the political is prior to interest — molecular lines and lines of flight compose interests rather than expressing them.
- **[[Concepts/Institutional Sclerosis (Olson)]]:** Olson's distributional coalitions slow societies by capturing rents through molar organizations. Deleuze/Guattari would recognize this as a form of overcoding by rigid segments — but would resist Olson's individualist remedy (Schumpeterian creative destruction) in favor of constructing molecular alternatives.
