# Master Morality and Slave Morality (Nietzsche)

## Overview

In the *First Essay* ("Good and Evil," "Good and Bad") of *On the Genealogy of Morals*, Nietzsche provides the famous distinction between two fundamental types of morality, rooted in different "pathos" and different creators.

**Master morality** (aristocratic, noble): "Good" originates in the self-affirmation of the powerful, high-minded, aristocratic — those who feel *themselves* good and their actions "of the first order." Born of the "pathos of distance" (the chronic, despotic esprit de corps and fundamental instinct of a higher dominant race). "Bad" is the pale, late-born foil for the low, vulgar, plebeian. Spontaneous "yes" to self; negative concept secondary. Etymological evidence across languages confirms "good" = noble/powerful/aristocratic (e.g., Greek, Latin *bonus* as warrior, "blond" vs. dark pre-Aryan, etc.). Masters' right of naming extends to language as expression of power.

**Slave morality** (priestly, herd, ressentiment-driven): The "revolt of the slaves in morals." When the weak/priestly/underdog, deprived of direct outlet for action, make *ressentiment* creative. Says "no" from the outset to everything "outside itself." Action is fundamentally *reaction*. Requires an external "evil enemy" (the aristocrat, distorted by the "venomous eye of resentfulness" into the "evil one") to create its "good" (the self as the poor, weak, suffering, meek, pious). The Jewish transvaluation ("the wretched are alone the good... you aristocrats... are to all eternity the evil") is the most profound and fateful; Christianity its popular form and world-historical victory. "Good and evil" replaces "good and bad."

The two have fought "Rome against Judaea, Judaea against Rome." Judaea (slave) has been provisionally victorious (Christianity, Reformation, French Revolution), but the fight continues at higher, more psychological levels. "Beyond Good and Evil" is explicitly *not* the same as "Beyond Good and Bad."

## Key Arguments

- The origin of "good" is sought in the wrong place by "English psychologists" (utility + forgetting + habit from the recipient's side). The aristocratic self-affirmation is the true homestead.
- Priestly vs. knightly-aristocratic values: the former (introspection, neurasthenia, clean/unclean sharpened) branches off and produces the dangerous intensification that enables the slave revolt.
- The "evil enemy" is a creation of resentful imagination. The aristocrat inter pares is considerate, loyal, proud, friendly; outside the circle a "beast of prey" (Roman, Viking, etc.). Popular morality invents a neutral "subject" behind the doing so that strength can be blamed for being strength ("the bird of prey is evil").
- The Jewish/Christian inversion: from the "trunk of that tree of revenge and hate" grows the "crown" — a new love (the Redeemer) as the most dangerous bait of revenge. "Me too made eternal hate" would be the truer inscription over the gate of the Christian Paradise than Dante's.
- "Good and evil" has triumphed; the "morality of the vulgar man" has triumphed. The "redemption" (from the masters) is progressing; everything is becoming "Judaised, or Christianised, or vulgarised." Napoleon as the "most unique and violent anachronism" and "incarnate problem of the aristocratic ideal in itself."
