# Geometrical Method - Rationalism (Spinoza)

> Spinoza presents the *Ethics* in the geometrical manner (*more geometrico*): a chain of definitions, axioms, propositions with demonstrations, scholia, and corollaries. This is not merely a style of presentation. Because the order of ideas is the same as the order of things (parallelism + 1a4), deducing consequences from clear definitions of essences reveals the actual causal and conceptual order of nature. Rationalism here means that reality is rationally ordered and that we can know its natures by analyzing the logical relations among our concepts.

## Core Description

The method is modeled on Euclid but applied to metaphysics and ethics (not abstract ideal figures). 

- **Definitions** pick out the “inmost essence” of a thing (see *Treatise on the Emendation of the Intellect*). From a perfect definition, all the essential properties of the thing can be deduced. They are not arbitrary stipulations but attempts to capture what makes a thing the thing that it is.
- **Axioms** are self-evident truths or common notions (e.g., 1a4: cognition of an effect depends upon cognition of its cause and involves it).
- **Propositions** are deduced from previous propositions plus definitions and axioms. Each has a proof.
- **Scholia** and corollaries provide commentary, alternative proofs, and implications.

Because Spinoza holds that conceptual dependence mirrors causal dependence in nature, the deductive order of the text is not just logical but ontological. The properties that follow from the definition of substance or of God are the same properties that actually follow from the nature of substance or of God in reality.

This is the heart of Spinoza’s rationalism: the universe is intelligible through and through; there is no brute fact or arbitrary divine will that could have made things otherwise. To understand a thing is to understand its causes (and ultimately God as the cause of all things) and to see how its properties necessarily follow from its essence.

The method has an ethical dimension. By tracing the consequences of our conceptions, we come to see the necessity of things (including our own affects and bondage) and thereby increase our power and freedom. The geometrical presentation itself is meant to produce in the reader the shift from imagination (inadequate ideas) to reason and ultimately to intuitive knowledge.

## Relevance to Vault

The geometrical method is the single best historical model for the vault’s own ideal of disciplined, cumulative, schema-grounded knowledge work.

- **Definitions as schema and core concepts**: The vault begins with clear, stable definitions (the schema in [[GROK]], the core concepts pages, the structure of the index). These function like Spinoza’s definitions: from them the rest of the system (practices, page formats, cross-linking rules, maintenance workflows) is supposed to follow with necessity.
- **Deductive chain**: Ingest → read fully → extract key information → integrate into existing pages + create new ones as needed → update index + log. Each step should be traceable back to the schema and to the sources, just as each proposition is traceable to prior propositions, definitions, and axioms.
- **Essences and properties**: A well-formed source summary or concept page should allow the reader (or future agent) to deduce many of its important implications and connections without having to re-read the entire raw source. The “essence” (the clear synthesis) contains the power to generate the “properties” (related links, contrasts, applications).
- **Consistency and non-contradiction**: In a geometrical system, contradictions are immediately visible as failures of deduction. In the vault, contradictions, orphans, stale claims, and missing cross-references are the equivalent of failed proofs; lint passes are the process of restoring deductive coherence.
- **Reader transformation**: Spinoza intended the geometrical presentation to change the reader (from confused imagination to adequate ideas and freedom). The vault is meant to do the same for its user and for the agent: repeated engagement with the clear, connected structure gradually increases power and reduces bondage to unexamined habits or raw data.
- **Contrast with other vault models**: Vs. Descartes’ meditative, first-person, doubt-and-reconstruct method (hyperbolic doubt clears the ground; then clear and distinct perceptions are secured). Spinoza’s method assumes from the start a rationally ordered reality that can be read off from correct definitions. Both are present in the vault and can be used in different contexts (doubt/lint when foundations feel shaky; geometrical deduction when the schema and prior work are strong).
- **Naturalism of method**: The method works because nature (including the “nature” of the knowledge base) is rationally ordered. There is no need for external authority or grace; the order is immanent and discoverable.
