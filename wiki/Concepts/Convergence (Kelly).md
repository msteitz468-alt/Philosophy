# Convergence (Kelly)

> Kevin Kelly's use of convergent evolution (recurring similar forms/solutions along independent paths) as evidence that the technium has "wants" and biases that produce recurring outcomes. Just as eyes, wings, and other complex adaptations evolved independently many times in biology, many technological inventions and forms recur across cultures and eras when the enabling conditions exist. There are "inevitable inventions" at the level of general solutions (e.g., writing, agriculture, the alphabet, the steam engine, the computer, the internet in broad outline). Convergence supports the view that "what technology wants" is not wholly arbitrary or culturally determined but shaped by deep physical, mathematical, and evolutionary constraints. The 12 forces are expressions of these convergent biases in the current digital technium.

## Summary (from the source)

In *What Technology Wants*, Kelly devotes significant attention to convergence as a signature of the technium's evolutionary nature. Biological examples: eyes evolved ~40-60 independent times; flight (wings) multiple times; intelligence-like capacities in distant lineages (cephalopods, corvids, cetaceans, primates). The "same" complex solution appears when the problem (seeing, flying, navigating complex environments) is posed under similar constraints.

Technological parallels: writing systems, agriculture, the wheel (in some forms), mathematics, printing, steam power, electricity, computers, networks — general forms recur or are "discovered" independently when the cultural/technical substrate allows. Kelly argues this is not coincidence or pure diffusion but evidence of attractors in the possibility space. The technium "wants" certain solutions because they are stable, evolvable, or high-utility under the physics and economics of tools and information.

Convergence implies that some high-level trajectories are robust: given enough time and the right preconditions (exotropy, self-organization, human curiosity + tool-making), certain technologies will appear along many paths. This grounds the "inevitable" in *The Inevitable*: the 12 forces (Becoming, Cognifying, etc.) are not one culture's invention but the convergent expression of digital bits, networks, and scalable intelligence. Specifics (which company, exact interface, etiquette) vary; the general direction (everything becoming a service in flux, intelligence added to everything, answers cheap and questions valuable) is over-determined by the nature of the technology.

Kelly is careful: convergence is at the level of forms and propensities, not exact duplicates. Culture, politics, economics, and chance still shape expression. But the bias is real and strong enough to be useful for long-term orientation ("listen to the technology").

## Key Claims / Positions

- **Recurring solutions**: Independent lineages (bio or tech) converge on analogous structures/functions when facing similar problems under similar constraints. Evidence for deep "wants" or attractors in evolutionary possibility space.
- **Inevitable inventions**: At the level of general technology (e.g., "a global communication network," "cheap ubiquitous copying," "scalable artificial cognition"), convergence makes certain outcomes highly likely once preconditions are met. Not every detail, but the family of solutions.
- **Support for technium wants**: Convergence is one of the strongest arguments that the technium has directionality independent of any single human plan. It "wants" eyes (in bio) and cognifying (in tech) because they solve persistent problems in evolvable ways.
- **Limits of convergence**: Not everything converges; path dependence, lock-in, and cultural choice matter. Some technologies are "chosen" more than inevitable (e.g., specific standards wars). The Amish example shows selective non-adoption or delayed adoption is possible for value reasons.
- **Link to exotropy and the 12 forces**: Convergence operates within the exotropic arc. The 12 forces are the current convergent biases of the digital technium (e.g., multiple cultures independently moving toward accessing/sharing/tracking/cognifying once bits and networks are cheap and global).
- **Methodological value**: To see where technology is going, study where it has come from and the recurring patterns (convergence) across its history. This orients "protopian" engagement rather than surprise or prohibition.

## Contradictions / Open Questions

- **Convergence vs. Orthogonality / Instrumental Convergence (Bostrom)**: Kelly's convergence is about similar *solutions* to similar problems (functional attractors). Bostrom's Instrumental Convergence is about similar *subgoals* (self-preservation, resource acquisition, cognitive enhancement) arising for *most* final goals, even arbitrary or anti-human ones. They can reinforce (a paperclip maximizer may still "converge" on powerful general AI and global infrastructure) but also diverge in moral valence. Kelly assumes life-like, choice-increasing, exotropic outcomes; Bostrom shows convergence can be dangerous. Cross to [[Sources/Superintelligence - Nick Bostrom (2014)]], [[Concepts/Instrumental Convergence (Bostrom)]], [[Concepts/Orthogonality Thesis (Bostrom)]].
- **Convergence vs. cultural/historical contingency (Lovejoy/Butterfield/Kuhn)**: Lovejoy's Chain assumes a fixed, gradated fullness; modern historiography (Butterfield on Scientific Revolution as mental shift; Kuhn on paradigms and incommensurability) emphasizes contingency, gestalt changes, and non-teleological history. Kelly's tech convergence reintroduces a form of directionality/teleology at the level of evolutionary attractors. Is it compatible with the "change in habitual mental operations" that overthrew older static diagrams? Cross to [[Sources/The Great Chain of Being - Arthur O. Lovejoy (1936)]], [[Sources/The Origins of Modern Science 1300-1800 - Herbert Butterfield (1949)]], [[Concepts/Scientific Revolution (Butterfield)]], [[Concepts/Incommensurability (Kuhn)]].
- **Convergence vs. Mitchell limits / Hofstadter "terror"**: If certain cognifying technologies are convergent and "inevitable," does that mean current DL (despite brittleness, lack of common sense/analogy per Mitchell) is on the path to robust understanding, or a local optimum that may require different architectures? Convergence at the high level (scalable intelligence) does not guarantee the current statistical pattern-matching route succeeds. Cross to [[Sources/Artificial Intelligence - Melanie Mitchell (2019)]], [[Sources/Gödel, Escher, Bach - Douglas Hofstadter (1979)]].
- **Convergence vs. choice (Amish, conviviality)**: If forms are convergent, how much real choice remains? Kelly presents Amish hackers as a working model of selective adoption that honors community values while using powerful tools on their own terms. Convergence sets the menu and the physics; culture chooses from the menu and shapes expression. Open: how scalable is the Amish stance to planetary technium governance?
- **With Kurzweil acceleration**: Both see strong directionality (convergent forces vs. law of accelerating returns + GNR), but Kelly emphasizes convergence across many paths and protopian messiness; Kurzweil more singular path to 2045 transcendence. See [[Sources/The Singularity Is Near - Ray Kurzweil (2005)]], [[Concepts/Law of Accelerating Returns (Kurzweil)]].
