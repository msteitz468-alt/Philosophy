# Expertise & Deliberate Practice

> The disciplined, intentional study and repetition required to achieve mastery, and the enduring role of human judgment/creativity that complements (rather than being replaced by) calculation, machines, or AI. Exemplified by Garry Kasparov (chess + Deep Blue) and the precision craft across culinary, music, writing, and performance classes in the 2026 MasterClass collection.

## Core Ideas
- **Deliberate practice over raw talent**: Kasparov class includes workbook, practice positions, PGN files for software analysis, chapter reviews with terminology, interactive move-following. Study of past games and patterns builds the calculation trees and intuition for complex positions.
- **Human creativity vs machine intelligence**: Kasparov's *Deep Thinking* and Deep Blue matches (1996 win, 1997 loss) frame the boundary — machines excel at brute-force search; humans bring creativity, preparation, long-term judgment, and the ability to find meaning beyond the board. The matches popularized chess and highlighted what remains irreplaceable.
- **Craft as sensory/precise/repetitive/generous**: BBQ notes (Brisket/Pulled Pork) and cooking classes (Keller fundamentals, Franklin whole-packer technique, Poilane bread, Ansel pastry) emphasize trim/inject/season/wrap/rest, feel for bark and tenderness, repetition until automatic. The same discipline appears in photography (Liebovitz), songwriting (St. Vincent), filmmaking (Scorsese, Cameron, Herzog), and music production (Deadmau5, Timbaland).
- **Preparation + presence under pressure**: Astronaut (Hadfield), mental strength (Arzon), poker (Ivey/Negreanu), skate (Hawk) — all stress systems, visualization, recovery, and showing up with full attention.
