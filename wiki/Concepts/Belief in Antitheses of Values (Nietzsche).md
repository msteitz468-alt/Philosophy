# Belief in Antitheses of Values (Nietzsche)

> One-sentence hook: Nietzsche's name for "**the fundamental faith of the metaphysicians**" — the unexamined assumption that the things of highest value must have a separate, pure origin (Being, the Thing-in-itself, God) *opposed* to the low and "evil" — when perhaps the high and the low are "insidiously related, knotted, and crocheted," even "essentially identical."

## Overview

The second aphorism of Chapter 1 of *Beyond Good and Evil* ("The Prejudices of Philosophers"), and for Nietzsche the *deepest* of those prejudices — "the typical prejudice of metaphysicians of all times." Metaphysicians "assume things of the highest value must have an origin of their own, in the lap of Being, the intransitory, the concealed God, the 'Thing-in-itself'—not in this transitory, seductive, illusory, paltry world of delusion and cupidity." Truth must come from truth, the good from the good, the selfless from the selfless; they cannot have grown out of their opposites.

Nietzsche challenges the *whole framework of opposed values* (truth/illusion, good/evil, selfless/selfish, being/becoming):

> "But perhaps the value of the 'good and respected things' consists precisely in their being insidiously related, knotted, and crocheted to the 'evil' ones—perhaps even essentially identical with them. **Perhaps!** But who wishes to concern himself with such dangerous 'Perhapses'!"

The supposed *antitheses* of value may be "only superficial estimates… provisional perspectives" ([[Concepts/Perspectivism (Nietzsche)]]) — surface valuations mistaken for metaphysical bedrock.

## Key Arguments

- **The metaphysician's "faith," not finding.** The belief in value-antitheses is *presupposed*, not proven; it is the hidden axiom that lets the metaphysician posit a "true world" of pure origins behind the apparent one. (Plato's "Pure Spirit and the Good in Itself" is the paradigm — "the worst, most tiresome, and most dangerous error hitherto"; Christianity is "Platonism for the 'people.'")
- **Possible identity of opposites.** The radical alternative: the "good" things may *be* the "evil" things, refined, sublimated, renamed — value grows out of its opposite. This is the seed of the *Genealogy*'s genealogical method (see [[Concepts/Master Morality and Slave Morality (Nietzsche)]], [[Concepts/Ressentiment (Nietzsche)]]): trace a value to its "grey," impure origin rather than assuming a pure one.
- **Untruth as a condition of life.** Dropping the antithesis truth/illusion: "The falseness of an opinion is not for us any objection to it. The question is how far an opinion is life-furthering… We are fundamentally inclined to maintain that the falsest opinions (including the synthetic judgments a priori) are the most indispensable to us; without… constant counterfeiting of the world by means of numbers, man could not live. **To recognize untruth as a condition of life**: that is certainly to impugn the traditional ideas of value in a dangerous manner, and a philosophy which ventures to do so has thereby alone placed itself **beyond good and evil**."
- **The task is for a new order of thinkers.** To question value-antitheses honestly "awaits a new order of philosophers" — see [[Concepts/Philosophers of the Dangerous "Perhaps" (Nietzsche)]].

## Contrasts / Lineage

- **Vs. [[Thinkers/Plato]]** ([[Concepts/Teachability of Virtue (Plato Protagoras Meno)|Forms]], the Good in Itself): the original and "worst" instance — the separate, pure realm of value Nietzsche most wants to dissolve.
- **Vs. [[Thinkers/Kant]]** ([[Concepts/Two Standpoints: Sensible World and Intelligible World (Kant)|the intelligible world]], the "Thing-in-itself"; [[Concepts/Categorical Imperative - Universal Law Formulation (Kant)|the categorical]]): the "Tartuffery of old Kant" who "entices us into the dialectic by-ways" toward a pure a priori moral law — value-antithesis in critical dress.
- **Vs. [[Thinkers/Spinoza]]** ([[Concepts/Geometrical Method - Rationalism (Spinoza)|the geometrical method]]): "hocus-pocus in mathematical form… mail and mask" — the appearance of pure, disinterested deduction concealing a value-laden starting point.
- **Generates → [[Concepts/Perspectivism (Nietzsche)]]** and **[[Concepts/Beyond Good and Evil (Nietzsche)]]**: once antitheses are seen as provisional perspectives, the way is open to "beyond good and evil" and the revaluation of values; closely allied with [[Concepts/Will to Power (Nietzsche)]] (values as impositions of power, not discoveries) and [[Concepts/Philosophy as Involuntary and Unconscious Autobiography (Nietzsche)]] (the "pure" claim hides the philosopher's heart's desire).
- **Vs. [[Thinkers/Bertrand Russell]] / [[Concepts/Correspondence Theory of Truth (Russell)]]**: a head-on collision. Russell holds truth is fixed by mind-independent fact and warns ("cosmic impiety") against exactly "untruth as a condition of life"; Nietzsche treats the truth/illusion antithesis itself as a life-serving prejudice. The vault holds both poles.

## Vault / AI / Personal Relevance

- **A master lint heuristic**: the sharpest formulation of "question the binary." When two vault pages (or a source) are organized around a clean opposition — accurate vs. useful, principled vs. pragmatic, signal vs. noise, honest vs. aggressive sales — ask whether the "high" term is "knotted and crocheted" to the "low" one, or grown from it. Resist treating a convenient antithesis as bedrock.
- **"Untruth as a condition of life" for models**: useful fictions, simplifications, and working approximations are indispensable, not failures — provided their role is owned (cf. the realist caveat from [[Concepts/Correspondence Theory of Truth (Russell)]] that they must still answer to facts). A productive tension to hold consciously, not resolve.
- **Provenance/bias**: a page that presents its values as self-evident "pure" findings is exhibiting the metaphysician's prejudice; honest maintenance marks the value-laden starting point (pairs with [[Concepts/Philosophy as Involuntary and Unconscious Autobiography (Nietzsche)]]).
- **Personal/career**: dissolves moralized binaries in professional life (e.g., "service vs. production," "relationship vs. revenue") that are often interdependent rather than opposed.
