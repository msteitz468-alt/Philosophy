# The Henads (Proclus)

> Proclus's divine Unities: a series of "ones" within the First Hypostasis, derived from and dependent on the One, serving as the Participated intermediaries through which lower realities touch the strictly unknowable First Principle — and as the philosophical home of the traditional gods.

## Summary

Formulated by Syrianus and systematized in [[Thinkers/Proclus]]'s *Elements of Theology*, the henads apply Iamblichus's Unparticipated/Participated distinction to the One itself: the One becomes the Unparticipated Monad, the henads its Participated terms (*ET* 116), arranged in hierarchical orders (Intelligible, Intelligible-Intellectual, Intellectual, Supercosmic, Intracosmic). Wallis assigns them three functions:

1. **Metaphysical** — bridging absolute Unity and Plurality without compromising the One's transcendence (one of the problems [[Thinkers/Plotinus]] bequeathed: how can the One produce what it does not contain?).
2. **Religious** — accommodating the gods of Hellenic religion within the system, each god a henad; providence operates "in virtue of the gods' very existence" (*autōi tōi einai*, *PT* I.15), automatically and without deliberation, grasping contingent events "in a single timeless vision."
3. **Epistemological** — the henads, unlike the One, are knowable by analogy from their effects.
