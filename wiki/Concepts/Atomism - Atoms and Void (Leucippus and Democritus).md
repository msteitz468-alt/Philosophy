# Atomism - Atoms and Void (Leucippus and Democritus)

> The physical and metaphysical doctrine that the universe is composed entirely of indivisible, solid particles (atoms) moving through empty space (the void).

## Summary

Ancient **Atomism** is the crowning synthesis of Pre-Socratic cosmology. Devised by Leucippus and fully developed by Democritus, it successfully reconciled Eleatic logic (which denied change and motion due to the impossibility of Non-Being) with everyday sensory experience. The atomists did this by proposing a bold physical hypothesis: that **Non-Being exists as a physical reality in the form of empty space (the void)**, allowing indivisible units of Being (atoms) to move, collide, and combine.

---

## Core Principles

**1. The Atoms (Units of Being)**:
- Matter is not infinitely divisible. If we divide matter, we eventually reach indivisible particles called **atoms** (*atomos*, literally "uncuttable").
- Atoms are solid, homogeneous, indestructible, and eternal. 
- They have no color, taste, temperature, or smell. They differ only in **shape, size, position, and arrangement**.

**2. The Void (Non-Being)**:
- The **void** is empty space. Contrary to Parmenides, the atomists asserted that *"what is not, is just as much as what is."*
- The void is infinite, providing the medium in which atoms move.

**3. Mechanical Necessity (No Teleology)**:
- Atoms have an inherent, eternal motion in the void. They collide, rebound, and occasionally hook together (due to their shapes, e.g., jagged, smooth, spherical).
- All physical objects and qualities are emergent properties of these atomic configurations.
- All change is simply the rearrangement of atoms. There is no divine design, no final causes, and no cosmic forces of attraction like Love and Strife; everything happens by mechanical necessity: *"Nothing occurs at random, but everything for a reason and by necessity."*

## Later Elaborations and Differences

- **Epicurus**:
  - Adopted Democritus's atomism but added the **clinamen (swerve)**—a slight, random deviation of atoms from their straight paths. Epicurus introduced the swerve to explain how atoms collide in an infinite void (where all otherwise fall at equal speeds) and to provide a physical basis for human **free will** (breaking the chain of strict Democritean determinism). See [[Concepts/Epicurean Atomism (Swerve)]].
- **Lucretius**:
  - Elaborated on the emergence of secondary qualities (e.g., color, taste) from quantitative atomic shapes, and defended the mortality of the soul (composed of smooth, round atoms that disperse upon death). See [[Sources/On the Nature of Things - Lucretius (Books II-III)]].

---

## Relevance to Later Science

- **Boyle and Newton**: The corpuscular philosophy of the Scientific Revolution was a direct revival of ancient atomism, treating the world as solid particles acting under mathematical laws.
- **Modern Chemistry**: Directly anticipates Dalton's atomic theory, which explains chemical compounds in terms of fixed combinations of indivisible atoms.
