# Sensation and Reflection as the Two Fountains of Ideas (Locke)

> All our ideas come from EXPERIENCE. The mind at birth is like white paper, void of all characters. There are two sources, or "fountains," from which the understanding is furnished with all its ideas: (1) SENSATION — the senses conveying perceptions from external sensible objects (ideas of sensible qualities like yellow, white, heat, cold, soft, hard, bitter, sweet); (2) REFLECTION — the mind's perception of its own internal operations (perception, thinking, doubting, believing, reasoning, knowing, willing, and the passions/satisfaction or uneasiness arising from thoughts). These are the only originals; there are no innate ideas.

## Key Text from Book II, Chapter I

" Let us then suppose the mind to be, as we say, white paper, void of all characters, without any ideas: — How comes it to be furnished? Whence comes it by that vast store which the busy and boundless fancy of man has painted on it with an almost endless variety? Whence has it all the MATERIALS of reason and knowledge? To this I answer, in one word, from EXPERIENCE. In that all our knowledge is founded; and from that it ultimately derives itself. Our observation employed either, about external sensible objects, or about the internal operations of our minds perceived and reflected on by ourselves, is that which supplies our understandings with all the MATERIALS of thinking. These two are the fountains of knowledge, from whence all the ideas we have, or can naturally have, do spring."

- Sensation: external objects affect senses → distinct perceptions/ideas of sensible qualities.
- Reflection: "the perception of the operations of our own mind within us" — "internal sense." Ideas such as perception, thinking, doubting, believing, reasoning, knowing, willing.
- "The understanding seems to me not to have the least glimmering of any ideas which it doth not receive from one of these two."

Evidence from children: ideas come by degrees as senses are furnished; reflection comes later because it requires attention (children look abroad first).

Against "soul always thinks" (prior to or independent of body/sensation): no evidence; consciousness is tied to perception/thinking; sleep without dreaming or memory of thoughts; leads to absurdities (two persons in one man, etc.). Ideas coeval with sensation/perception. The foetus or infant has little to no perception/thought until sensation provides matter.

The understanding is passive in the reception of these simple ideas ("materials of knowledge"); it cannot refuse them or create new simple ones from nothing.

## Relation to Simple/Complex and Further Development

Once furnished with these from the two fountains, the mind can repeat, compare, unite them into complex ideas (see [[Concepts/Simple Ideas (Locke)]] and future complex ideas concepts). But the originals are only sensation and reflection.

## Contrasts and Ties in the Vault

- **Foundation for Hume's Copy Principle**: Hume takes Locke's "from experience" (sensation → impressions lively; reflection too) and sharpens it: all simple ideas are copies of prior simple impressions. The "two fountains" become the basis for the meaning-test ("from what impression...?"). See [[Concepts/Impressions and Ideas - Copy Principle (Hume)]] — now directly linked to this primary source.
- **Vs. Descartes / innatists**: Explicit denial of any ideas not derived from these two (no trademark innate idea of God independent of experience; no ideas from pure intellect alone before sensation).
- **Vs. Plato**: No recollection of pre-existent ideas; all content enters via the two fountains.
- **Vs. Lucretius/atomism (secondary qualities)**: Locke's later distinction (primary qualities real/resemblant in bodies; secondary powers to produce sensations) builds on the experiential origin here. Lucretius has a more radical "secondary qualities entirely emergent, not in atoms" — vault now has both for comparison.
- **AI / grounding / agents**: Perfect model for multimodal/embodied learning. No "ideas" (representations) without sensory input (sensation) or internal processing (reflection on operations). Symbol grounding problem: ungrounded tokens have no ideas. Context engineering = deliberately furnishing the "cabinet" via high-quality "sensation" (sources) + reflection (maintenance, synthesis).
- **Wiki meta**: Raw/ sources = external sensation. Wiki pages, index, concepts, log = the mind's operations reflected upon and compounded. The "historical, plain method" of tracing every idea back to its experiential origin = the discipline of reading index + sources before editing/synthesizing.
