# Conditional Rendering and Quantum Indeterminacy (Virk)

> Virk's central physics claim: quantum indeterminacy is an optimization technique — "only render that which is being observed" is both the rule of video game engines and the rule of wave-function collapse.

## Summary

Video game rendering engines conserve resources by rendering only visible, non-occluded geometry within a player's field of view; "the rest of the world exists only as information unless it is being observed." Virk maps this **conditional rendering** onto quantum mechanics: particles exist as probability waves (unrendered information) until observation collapses them into definite states (rendered pixels). His formulation: "The basic rule of video game rendering engines is the same as what causes the collapse of the probability wave... only render that which is being observed." Musk's version: "Quantum Indeterminacy is really an optimization technique."

The supporting physics, per Virk:
- **Double slit / Copenhagen interpretation** — observation determines particle-vs-wave behavior; Heisenberg: "The path of the electron comes into existence only when we observe it."
- **Wheeler's delayed-choice experiment** — future measurement retroactively fixes past behavior; Wheeler's **participatory universe**: "one has to cross out the old word 'observer' and put in its place the new word 'participator.'"
- **Many Worlds (Everett/DeWitt)** — Virk reinterprets branching universes as *computed information branches* (game probability trees evaluated minimax-style, per Tom Campbell's "profitability function"), cheaper than physical duplication.
- **Quantized space-time** — Planck length (~1.6×10⁻³⁵ m) as pixel size, Planck time (~5.4×10⁻⁴⁴ s) as clock cycle; the speed of light as the engine's propagation constant; Zeno's paradox dissolves if space is discrete (cf. [[Thinkers/Zeno of Elea]]).
- **Entanglement** — two particles as two references to the same memory address, "consistent with simulation architecture, not with faster-than-light signaling."
- MMORPG synchronization as the model of shared reality: worlds render per-observer, synchronized by server state — no monolithic objective world required.

## Contradictions / Open Questions

- > [!warning] Virk's reading requires the *consciousness-causes-collapse* interpretation; mainstream decoherence theory makes any interaction, not an observer, sufficient — which would sever the game-engine analogy. The vault's Phil of Science pages (instrumentalism, underdetermination) apply: identical predictions, rival ontologies.
- Retrocausality readings of delayed choice are contested; standard QM explains the results without backward causation.
