# Toleration (Locke)

> In *A Letter Concerning Toleration* (and sequels, in the Complete Works): True religion is inward persuasion of the mind, not outward force. Magistrate's power is force (civil interests only: life, liberty, property); church is voluntary society for worship. No compulsion in religion. Limits: no toleration for atheists (no binding oaths/promises), those whose religion requires loyalty to foreign prince (e.g. some Catholic doctrines), or intolerant sects that would persecute others if empowered. Civil peace is the boundary.
