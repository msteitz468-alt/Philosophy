# Normal Science and Puzzle-Solving (Kuhn)

> **Normal science** is "research firmly based upon one or more past scientific achievements" — the long, cumulative periods in which a community works *within* a [[Concepts/Paradigm (Kuhn)|paradigm]], solving **puzzles** it defines. Its "most striking feature… is how little [its problems] aim to produce major novelties, conceptual or phenomenal." Far from testing the paradigm, normal science *presupposes* it.

## The Idea

Most science, most of the time, is normal science: a "mopping-up operation" that articulates and extends the reigning paradigm rather than questioning it. Kuhn classes its problems into three types:

1. **Determination of significant facts** — measuring/refining the quantities the paradigm deems important.
2. **Matching of facts with theory** — closing gaps between observation and paradigm predictions (and, when they don't tally, fixing the *theory's articulation* or impugning the *experiment*, not the paradigm).
3. **Articulation of theory** — drawing out what is implicit in the paradigm (often by mathematical analysis; Kuhn's term: **articulation**).

### Why "puzzle"?

A **puzzle** (crossword, jigsaw, chess problem) is something the framework *guarantees has a solution*, with the rules and criteria of success fixed in advance. The challenge is the solver's ingenuity, not the validity of the rules. So too in normal science: failure to solve a puzzle reflects on the **scientist**, not the paradigm. The scientist "is like the chess player who… tries out various alternative moves… These trial attempts… are trials only of themselves, not of the rules of the game." This is why **normal science is not a test of the paradigm**: "In so far as he is engaged in normal science, the research worker is a solver of puzzles, not a tester of paradigms."

Normal science is **highly cumulative** and "immensely efficient" — precisely because the shared paradigm relieves the community of re-arguing fundamentals, it can press deep into nature's detail. That very depth and precision is what eventually exposes the **anomaly** the paradigm cannot absorb (see [[Concepts/Anomaly and Crisis (Kuhn)]]). Normal science thus carries the seeds of its own disruption.

## Reception (Hacking's caveats)

- Many working scientists conceded "that is how it is" in their daily work — yet some found the picture deflating. Kuhn did **not** mean normal science was unimportant; he thought it the bulk and glory of science.
- Hacking notes a limitation: Kuhn (like Popper) **privileged theory**; the **experimental and instrumental** traditions (Galison) have their own substantial novelty that the "no major novelties" claim understates. Normal science can be full of experimental/technological novelty even when theoretically conservative (e.g. the Higgs search — vast novelty in service of *confirming* existing theory).

## Vault Meta / AI

Routine ingests, cross-linking, and lint are the wiki's **normal science**: puzzle-solving that articulates the schema (the paradigm) and is judged by it. This is efficient and right *most* of the time — but it cannot, by itself, produce a reframing; only a recognized anomaly forces that. For AI/agentic work: distinguish **puzzle-solving within a frame** (optimize, fill gaps) from **questioning the frame**; an agent stuck in "normal science" mode will tidy data to fit and miss the anomaly that should trigger a rethink.
