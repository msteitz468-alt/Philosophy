# Art and Truth (Heidegger)
> Art is not beauty added to a thing but "the truth of beings setting itself to work" — the strife of world and earth through which unconcealment happens.

## Summary

Heidegger's philosophy of art, developed primarily in *The Origin of the Work of Art* (*Der Ursprung des Kunstwerkes*, OWA, 1935/1950), is one of the most original contributions of the later Heidegger and a central expression of the [[Concepts/Historicality and the Turn (Heidegger)|Turn]]. Art is not an aesthetic luxury, not beauty laid over a substrate, not the expression of an artist's feelings. It is a **happening of truth** — the event in which beings are unconcealed and a historical world is opened.

### The Three Exhibits

Heidegger develops his account through three exhibits:

1. **Van Gogh's painting of peasant shoes**: The painting does not merely depict shoes; it reveals the **equipmentality of equipment** — "the equipmentality of equipment first genuinely arrives at its appearance through the work." The shoes in use are invisible (ready-to-hand); the painting makes their being show up.

2. **The Greek temple**: The temple at Paestum or Aegina does not represent anything. It **sets up a world** — the sacred, the political, the communal — and **sets forth earth** — rock, storm, light, the natural ground. The temple gathers: it opens the space within which a historical people encounters its gods, heroes, and destiny.

3. **Poetry** (*Dichtung*): Language is prior to all other arts because it is the medium of disclosure itself. "Language speaks, not man. Man only speaks when he fatefully answers to language." Poetry in the broad sense is **projective saying** that names things for the first time — "the opening up of the Open, and the clearing of beings, happens only as the openness is projected."

### World and Earth

The artwork involves two opposed forces:

- **World** (*Welt*): The human environment of values, tools, decisions, history — striving for openness, clarity, and articulation.
- **Earth** (*Erde*): The natural, concealing counterpart — the ground, materials, and resistance from which world must be wrested. Earth is essentially self-closing: it shelters and withholds.

The artwork is the site of their **strife** (*Streit*): "Setting up a world and setting forth the earth, the work is the fighting of the battle in which the unconcealedness of beings as a whole, or truth, is won" (OWA 55). The tension is composed into the work as **rift** (*Riss*) — a design or pattern that holds world and earth together precisely in their opposition.

### The Founding of Truth

Art founds truth in three senses:

1. **Bestowing** (*Schenken*): Truth arrives as a gift, an overflow — not as something produced by deliberate effort.
2. **Grounding** (*Gründen*): The work addresses a historical people *on their native earth* — it is not universal but rooted.
3. **Beginning** (*Anfang*): Art is an original leap (*Ursprung*) that contains the end latent within itself. A great beginning (Homer's epics, the Greek temple) is not a primitive start but an anticipation of everything that follows.

### Artist and Preservers

The artist is not a genius expressing inner states but a conduit through which truth sets itself to work. The work also requires **preservers** (*Bewahrende*) — those who dwell within the world the work opens, responding to its claim. Art without preservers is merely an object in a museum.

## Key Claims / Positions

### How Different Thinkers Approach This

- **[[Thinkers/Plato]]**: Art is twice removed from truth — the painter copies a bed, which is itself a copy of the Form of Bed (*Republic* X). Heidegger inverts this: art does not copy reality but *opens* it. The truth that happens in the Greek temple is more primordial than any propositional truth.
- **[[Thinkers/Arthur Schopenhauer]]**: Suggested that art directly depicts the Platonic Ideas, bypassing the phenomenal world of representation. Closer to Heidegger than Plato, but Schopenhauer still treats art as *depicting* something already there rather than as an *event* of unconcealment.
- **[[Thinkers/Hegel]]**: In his *Aesthetics*, Hegel declared that art is "a thing of the past" — spirit has moved beyond art to religion and philosophy. Heidegger leaves the question open but implies that in an age of technological enframing (*Gestell*), art may be the only site where truth still happens.
- **[[Thinkers/Nietzsche]]**: Art as the will to power's highest expression; the artist as creator of values. Heidegger: Nietzsche is right that art is essential, wrong that it is an expression of the *subject*'s will. The Turn moves the emphasis from Dasein's activity to Being's self-disclosure.

## Contradictions / Open Questions

- > [!warning] Heidegger's reading of the Van Gogh shoes has been famously challenged by Meyer Schapiro, who argued the shoes belong to Van Gogh himself (a city dweller), not a peasant woman. The philosophical point may survive the art-historical correction, but the example's authority is weakened.
- The claim that poetry is prior to all other arts privileges language in a way that composers, painters, and architects might contest.
- Whether Heidegger's account of art applies beyond the Western tradition (Greek temple, German poetry) is uncertain.
