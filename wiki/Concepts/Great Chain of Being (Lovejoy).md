# Great Chain of Being (Lovejoy)

> The conception of the universe as a "Great Chain of Being" (*scala naturae*): an immense (or infinite) series of links graded in hierarchical order of excellence from the barest existents up to the *ens perfectissimum* (or God), with every possible kind realized (plenitude), no gaps (continuity), and each differing from its neighbors by the least possible degree (gradation). Dominant presupposition of Western thought — philosophy, theology, science, poetry — from Plato/Aristotle through Neoplatonism, medieval scholasticism, Leibniz/Spinoza, and 18c Enlightenment (Pope's *Essay on Man* as popular expression) until its temporalizing in the late 18c and breakdown in the 19c. Arthur O. Lovejoy's 1936 *The Great Chain of Being* is the definitive history of this unit-idea complex and its "instructive negative outcome."

## Summary (synthesis across sources)

Lovejoy isolates the Chain as the fusion of three principles (see related concepts) that together implied a static, fully rational, "full" cosmos with no room for arbitrariness, gaps, or unfulfilled possibility. It answered "why is there diversity?" and "why this much?" via the nature of the Good/Perfect as necessarily generative and non-envious (Timaeus). It justified optimism/theodicy ("whatever is, is right"; partial evil is universal good because the full spectrum must exist). It shaped biology (classification, missing links), cosmology (even post-Copernicus often retained the presuppositions), and theology (Augustine, Pseudo-Dionysius, Aquinas: diversity as required by goodness; natural law within the graded order).

The idea carried internal tensions from the start: the otherworldly, self-sufficient Absolute (no need of creatures) vs. the this-worldly, fecund "good" that must overflow into all possibles ("two Gods" in much Western religion). The static, once-for-all completeness conflicted with temporality and change. In the 18c it reached popular apogee in optimism; by late 18c/early 19c, biology, geology, and Romantic sensibility "temporalized" it — converting the Chain into a progressive Becoming (pre-Darwinian evolutionism; Schelling: God not eternally complete but coming-to-be through the world-process). The inversion: the scheme now starts from below (insufficiency striving upward) rather than overflowing from above; the World of Ideas becomes mere possibility awaiting realization.

Lovejoy's moral (Ch. XI): the history of the Chain *in so far as it presupposed complete rational intelligibility of the world* (sufficient reason for the world's exact magnitude, pattern, every link, no arbitrariness or fortuity) "is the history of a failure." A temporal world of change cannot be deduced from or reconciled with a system of eternal necessary truths (arbitrary facts of beginning, duration, why *these* possibles and not others). Plenitude + continuity lead to infinities that baffle or negate reason (qualitative continuum is a contradiction; maximal diversity requires leaps). Complete rationality, when excluding all arbitrariness, "becomes itself a kind of irrationality." The world is contingent; "Will is prior to Intellect." (Yet the idea had many "curiously happy consequences," e.g., spurring the classificatory sciences.)

## Key Claims / How Different Thinkers Use This

- **Plato (Timaeus / Republic)**: Plenitude from the non-envious Good/Demiurge that desires all things to be as like itself as possible; all ideal possibilities must have sensible counterparts. Gradation and continuity more implicit/vague.
- **Aristotle**: Supplies explicit continuity (no leaps; zoophytes bridge plant/animal) and graded *scala* (powers of soul; offspring development as rank criterion). Rejects strict plenitude ("not necessary that everything possible should exist").
- **Neoplatonists (Plotinus et al.)**: First full systematic fusion via emanation (One → Intellect/Forms → Soul → matter); necessary overflow without loss; hierarchy of hypostases as the Chain in metaphysical form. "Non permisit manere Deum in seipso sine germine."
- **Medieval (Augustine, Pseudo-Dionysius, Aquinas)**: Christian transposition; diversity justified ("if all things were equal, all things would not be"); God's "love"/goodness as creative fecundity; Chain underpins natural law, beatific vision as apex, scala of creatures.
- **Leibniz / Spinoza (17c rationalism)**: Plenitude + sufficient reason (every detail has ultimate ground; this world the fullest compossible or the only one deducible from substance). Absolute determinism.
- **18c Enlightenment (Pope et al.)**: Popular ethical version — vast Chain from God to microscopic; "all that rises, rise in due degree"; optimism as theodicy. Biology: attempts to realize the "full" series in time (missing links).
- **Romantic / early 19c (Schelling, Oken)**: Temporalizing and inversion — God/Life-Force not eternally complete but becoming through progressive realization; "the more perfect has risen from its own less perfect condition"; plenitude now as flying goal of process, not permanent character. Pre-Darwinian evolutionism.
- **Lovejoy (1936)**: The complex as unit-idea whose career reveals the limits of the rationalist postulate. "The hypothesis of the absolute rationality of the cosmos [is] unbelievable."

## Contradictions / Open Questions

- Static rational completeness vs. empirical temporality/change (arbitrary facts of when/how the process begins or why this exact span/content).
- Plenitude (maximal diversity) vs. continuity (no leaps/gaps): a truly "full" universe of kinds must be full of qualitative discontinuities.
- Otherworldly self-sufficient God vs. this-worldly generative/productive God (the "two Gods" tension; resolved only by masking devices like "no envy").
- With vault Philosophy of Science: the Chain as the background static, hierarchical, teleological, fully intelligible cosmos that 20c post-positivism (Popper falsification/critical rationalism, Kuhn paradigms as non-cumulative, Feyerabend "anything goes," Hacking experimental creation of phenomena) reacts against once the rationalist hypothesis fails.
- With AI/CogSci: fixed unilinear hierarchy vs. emergent/distributed/tangled (Minsky society of mindless agents with no homunculus; Hofstadter strange loops/tangled hierarchies as mechanism of mind/I; Dennett multiple drafts/no Cartesian Theater; Pearl Ladder requires intervention/counterfactuals beyond static structure). The old optimism of rational mastery echoes in singularitarian visions (Kurzweil) that Mitchell and Bostrom critique.
