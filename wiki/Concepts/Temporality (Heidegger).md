# Temporality (Heidegger)
> Time is not a sequence of nows through which Dasein passes but the ecstatic structure of Dasein's own being — the horizon for the understanding of Being as such.

## Summary

Temporality (*Zeitlichkeit*) is the deepest layer of [[Concepts/Dasein (Heidegger)|Dasein]]'s being — the ground of [[Concepts/Care (Sorge) (Heidegger)|care]] and thus of everything that Dasein is. Heidegger's central ontological thesis is that "the central problematic of all ontology is rooted in the phenomenon of time" (BT 18). Time is more fundamental to Dasein than space: the date of one's birth limits possibilities in ways that place of birth does not; tradition is handed down over time, not across space; decisions and actions presuppose time more crucially than they presuppose location.

### The Three Ecstases

Temporality has three **ecstases** (from Greek *ek-stasis*, "standing outside") — Dasein "stands outside" itself toward each:

1. **Future** (*Zukunft*): Dasein projects itself toward its possibilities — ultimately toward death. The future is primary: it is because Dasein *runs ahead* that it can come back to what it has been and engage with what is present.

2. **Having-been-ness** (*Gewesenheit*): Not the dead past (*Vergangenheit*) but the living past that bears on the present. Dasein does not *have* a past that is over; it *is* its having-been. Thrownness is the temporal mode of facticity.

3. **Present** (*Gegenwart*): The authentic present is the **moment of vision** (*Augenblick*) — the instant in which resolute Dasein seizes a possibility. The inauthentic present is mere **making-present** (*Gegenwärtigen*) — absorption in what is currently at hand.

These ecstases map onto [[Concepts/Care (Sorge) (Heidegger)|care]]'s threefold structure: ahead-of-itself (future) / already-in-the-world (having-been-ness) / being-alongside entities (present).

### Four Levels of Time

*Being and Time* distinguishes four levels of time, each derived from the preceding:

1. **Primordial/authentic temporality**: The ecstatic unity of future, having-been-ness, and moment-of-vision in resolute Dasein.
2. **Inauthentic temporality**: The everyday mode in which Dasein disperses itself — expecting, forgetting, making-present without the focus of the Augenblick.
3. **World-time**: Time as datable ("now, when the lecture begins"), spanned ("from dawn to dusk"), public ("it is time for…"), and significant ("too late," "not yet"). This is the time of practical life, intermediate between ecstatic temporality and clock-time.
4. **"Vulgar" time / Now-time** (*Jetzt-zeit*): The homogeneous, infinite sequence of nows — [[Thinkers/Aristotle]]'s conception (time as the number of motion), perpetuated through Bergson. This is the most derivative level: an abstraction from world-time that strips away datability, spannedness, and significance. Now-time is infinite because of Dasein's "fleeing in the face of death" — inauthentic Dasein, refusing finitude, projects time as endless.

### Temporality and Transcendence

Dasein's **transcendence** — its stepping beyond every particular entity to the world as such — is grounded in ecstatic temporality. Each ecstasis has a **horizonal schema** (the pattern bounding it): "for the sake of itself" (future), "in the face of which" (past), "in order to" (present). Transcendence secures Dasein's **freedom**: freedom is not eliminated by causal grounds but presupposed by them, since asking "why?" — viewing entities as possibilities — requires Dasein's temporal openness. "Man is not primarily the no-sayer…, nor is he the yes-sayer; he is the why-asker" (GA xxvi. 280).

### Heidegger vs. the Greeks on Time

The Greeks viewed being as temporal **presence** (*ousia* / *parousia*) — being is what is present, what endures. Heidegger argues this privileges one temporal ecstasis (the present) and thereby distorts the question of Being. [[Thinkers/Plato]] ascribed true being only to the eternal Forms; [[Thinkers/Aristotle]] defined time as the number of motion with respect to before and after, yielding the homogeneous now-sequence. Heidegger contends that both miss the ecstatic, finite temporality of Dasein.

## Key Claims / Positions

### How Different Thinkers Approach This

- **[[Thinkers/Aristotle]]**: Time = the number of motion. An endless sequence of nows, with only the present now existing (the past is gone, the future not yet). Heidegger: this is the most derivative level of time, not its ground.
- **[[Thinkers/Kant]]**: Time as the pure form of inner sense — the a priori condition of experience. Heidegger reads Kant as closer to his own view than Kant realised: the transcendental imagination (in the first *Critique*) temporalises the categories. But Kant retreated from this in the second edition, subordinating imagination to understanding. Heidegger treats Kant's doctrine of the primacy of practical reason "more seriously, perhaps, than Kant did."
- **St Augustine**: "What then is time? If no one asks me, I know; if I wish to explain it to one that asks, I do not know." Augustine's *Confessions* XI anticipates Heidegger's awareness that time is intimately bound to the soul/Dasein but does not escape the now-sequence framework.
- **Husserl**: Phenomenology of time-consciousness — the primary model for Heidegger's analysis. But Husserl postulated a "third realm" of timeless meanings (*Sinn*), which Heidegger rejects: there are no supratemporal entities.
- **Bergson**: Named as the endpoint of the tradition viewing time as a homogeneous now-sequence, despite Bergson's own protests (*durée* as opposed to spatialised clock-time). Heidegger groups Bergson with Aristotle rather than with himself.
- **[[Thinkers/Hegel]]**: "The last and greatest of the Cartesians" — accepted Aristotle's view of time. Heidegger confronts Hegel at the end of BT to differentiate his own position.

## Contradictions / Open Questions

- > [!warning] Heidegger rejects all atemporal and supratemporal entities — no eternal Forms, no timeless God, no Fregean third realm. This puts him in tension with any philosophy or theology that posits necessary, non-temporal truths.
- The relationship between ecstatic temporality and the temporality of Being-itself (the announced but unwritten third section of BT) remains Heidegger's unfinished project.
- Galileo's experiment (as example of how science presupposes ecstatic temporality in projecting nature mathematically) is suggestive but underdeveloped.
