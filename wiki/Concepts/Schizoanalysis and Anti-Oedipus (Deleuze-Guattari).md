# Schizoanalysis and Anti-Oedipus (Deleuze-Guattari)
> Schizoanalysis is Deleuze and Guattari's alternative to psychoanalysis: instead of interpreting symptoms, it asks what lines an individual or group is on, what assemblages desire forms, what flows are coded or decoded.

## Summary

Schizoanalysis replaces interpretation with cartography. The psychoanalytic question is "what does this mean?" (Oedipal family drama, castration). The schizoanalytic question is "what does this do, and on which plane?" — a purely functional and pragmatic inquiry into assemblages.

**Three kinds of lines and their dangers (Essay 29):**
1. **Rigid segmentary/molar lines** — coded binary divisions (family/profession, male/female, citizen/foreigner). Danger: overcoding, repression, fascism.
2. **Supple molecular lines** — fluxes and micro-becomings that cross thresholds without coinciding with molar cuts. Danger: microfascisms, "black holes" of subjective absorption.
3. **Lines of flight/rupture** — carry subjects toward the imperceptible, pure becoming. Danger: becoming a machine of destruction, molar fascism.

"The question of schizoanalysis or pragmatics, micropolitics itself, never consists in interpreting, but merely in asking what are your lines, individual or group, and what are the dangers on each."

**Against the State model.** Schizoanalysis asks whether political organization can be constructed without being modeled on the State apparatus. The "war machine" (nomadic assemblage) is not the State's opposite (which would just be anti-State reaction) but a different mode of collective organization operating on lines of flight rather than molar segmentation.

**Capitalism and the axiomatic.** Capitalism is not a code (qualitative) but an **axiomatic** (functional, decoded): it operates by conjugating decoded flows of naked labor and abstract capital. Nation-states are not cancelled by capitalism but become its "models of realization" — isomorphic to the capitalist axiomatic even when formally diverse (democratic, totalitarian, socialist). "Even the so-called socialist States are isomorphic, to the extent that there is only one world market, the capitalist one."

Capitalism combines **social subjection** (the worker as user of technical machines) and **machinic enslavement** (humans as component pieces of cybernetic/informational machines). Television: one is subjected as viewer, enslaved as input/output.

## Key Claims

- "A Marxist can be quickly recognized when he says that a society contradicts itself... We would rather say that, in a society, everything flees and that a society is defined by its lines of flight."
- Politics is "active experimentation, since we do not know in advance which way a line is going to turn."
- Minority questions (linguistic, ethnic, sexual, youth) are not archaisms but "a new type of revolution... in the course of becoming possible."
- Schizoanalysis has no interpretive ambition — only pragmatic-cartographic.

## How Different Thinkers Use This

- **[[Thinkers/Karl Marx]]:** Marx sees capitalism defined by contradiction (use value / exchange value, labor / capital). Deleuze/Guattari: capitalism is defined by decoded flows and lines of flight — contradiction is the secondary, regulatory form, not the constitutive one. Marxist revolutionary politics models itself on the State; schizoanalysis seeks a war-machine alternative.
- **[[Thinkers/Nietzsche]] (Three Aspects of Culture):** Nietzsche's genealogy of morality — culture as training/selection to produce a sovereign individual capable of promising — is extended by Deleuze into a theory of reactive force capture. Historical culture perverts species-level training ("the domesticated man" as "sublime abortion").

## Contradictions / Open Questions

- > [!warning] Conflicts with [[Concepts/Collective Action and Free Riding (Olson)]]: Olson's analysis of collective action presupposes rational actors seeking determinate goods. Schizoanalysis rejects the subject/interest framework entirely — but then it is unclear how collective action gets organized at all on lines of flight.
- > [!warning] The claim that capitalism has no code (only an axiomatic) sits in tension with Deleuze/Guattari's own analysis of capitalist "recoding" through the family, the State, and commodity form. The boundary between axiomatic and code is never fully stabilized.
