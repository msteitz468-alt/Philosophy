# Entity Realism vs Theory Realism (Hacking)

> The central distinction in Ian Hacking's *Representing and Intervening*: **realism about entities** (the objects, processes, and structures postulated by theories really exist and have causal powers) is distinct from, and can be defended independently of, **realism about theories** (theories are or aim to be literally true or approximately true descriptions of the world). One can be anti-realist about theories while realist about the entities experimenters use as tools.

## The Distinction

- **Realism about theories**: Theories aim at the truth (or approximate truth/verisimilitude); when successful they correctly describe how the world is. Anti-realism about theories: theories are at best useful instruments for prediction and organization; they are not to be believed literally.
- **Realism about entities**: The theoretical entities (electrons, fields, quarks, etc.) mentioned in theories "should really exist." Anti-realism about entities: such entities are at best useful intellectual fictions or calculational devices.
- Hacking's claim: the two realisms are logically independent. Materialists (e.g., J.J.C. Smart) may be realist about entities as building blocks without requiring full theory-truth. Causal realists (e.g., Nancy Cartwright) assert existence of entities whose causal powers are well-known. Neither needs to be a full theory-realist.

## Hacking's Stance

Hacking defends a robust **entity realism** grounded in experimental practice while remaining largely anti- or non-committal about the literal truth of high-level theories. The "spray" argument (see [[Concepts/If You Can Spray Them Then They Are Real (Hacking)]]) shows that experimenters treat electrons etc. as real because they are used to intervene, regardless of whether the encompassing theory is taken as true.

This allows a form of scientific realism that survives the attacks on theory-comparison, incommensurability, and underdetermination that threaten theory-realism.

## Relation to Other Vault Content

- **Vs. positivism** (Ayer, Comte, Mach, van Fraassen): the classic anti-realism about both theories *and* entities. Hacking's experimental use criterion directly challenges the anti-entity half.
- **With Popper**: shares a realist orientation (Popper's "conjecturalism" / scientific realism against essentialism and instrumentalism — see [[Concepts/Three Views Concerning Human Knowledge (Popper)]]), but grounds entity realism in manipulation rather than (or alongside) severe testing and verisimilitude.
- **With/after Kuhn and Feyerabend**: incommensurability and "anything goes" make *theory* realism and representational comparison difficult; Hacking treats this as a reason to pivot the realism question to entities-via-intervention.
- **Parallel to Cartwright** (not yet primary): both defend selected entities while skeptical of full fundamental theory truth (different mechanisms).
- **Lakatos**: research programmes allow a surrogate realism/objectivity without requiring correspondence truth of theories — Hacking discusses this as one way to be (partly) realist after Kuhn.
