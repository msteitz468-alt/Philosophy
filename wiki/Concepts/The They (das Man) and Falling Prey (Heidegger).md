# The They (das Man) and Falling Prey (Heidegger)
> "Everyone is the other, and no one is himself" — the anonymous public "who" of everyday Dasein, and the movement by which Dasein falls away from itself into it.

## Summary

*Being and Time* Division One, chapters 4–5 (§§25–38). The "who" of everyday Dasein cannot be presupposed as the given "I" or subject: "the who of everyday Dasein is precisely not I myself" (§25). A worldless, isolated I is never given; the world is a **with-world** (*Mitwelt*), being-in is **being-with** (*Mitsein*), and others are encountered from the shared world of work — not by inference or "empathy." Being-alone is itself a deficient mode of being-with (§26). Concern for others (*Fürsorge*) spans two extremes: "leaping in" for the other (taking over his care, dominating) versus "leaping ahead" of him (freeing him for his own care).

### Das Man (§27)

Everyday being-with is governed by *distantiality*, *averageness*, and *leveling down*, which together constitute *publicness*. The who of everydayness is **das Man** (the they): "Everyone is the other, and no one is himself. The they … is the nobody to whom every Dasein has always surrendered itself, in its being-among-one-another." "In this inconspicuousness and unascertainability, the they unfolds its true dictatorship. We enjoy ourselves and have fun the way *they* enjoy themselves." The they *disburdens* Dasein of responsibility for its choices. Crucially, authentic selfhood is "an existentiell *modification* of the they," not detachment from it — the they is an existential, a positive constitution of Dasein.

### Falling Prey (Verfallen) and Its Everyday Faces (§§35–38)

The everyday disclosedness of the they operates through:

- **Idle talk** (*Gerede*): groundless passing-the-word-along that closes off rather than discloses — "Things are so because one says so … its initial lack of grounds to stand on increases to complete groundlessness" (§35).
- **Curiosity** (*Neugier*): the not-staying, distracted craving for the new — "everywhere and nowhere" (§36).
- **Ambiguity** (*Zweideutigkeit*): no one can any longer tell genuine understanding from its counterfeit (§37).

These constitute **falling prey** (*Verfallen*, Stambaugh: "entanglement") — explicitly *not* a value judgment and not a fall from some purer prior state: "As an authentic potentiality for being a self, Dasein has initially always already fallen away from itself and fallen prey to the 'world'" (§38). Its movement-characters are temptation, tranquillization, alienation, entanglement, and the "plunge"; falling intertwines with [[Concepts/Disclosedness — Attunement, Understanding, Discourse (Heidegger)|thrownness]], leaving Dasein "sucked into the turbulence of the they's inauthenticity." Falling prey is the third structural moment of [[Concepts/Care (Sorge) (Heidegger)|care]] and, in [[Concepts/Being-toward-Death and Authenticity (Heidegger)|being-toward-death]], powers the they's evasive "one dies."

## Key Claims / Positions

### How This Compares to Other Thinkers in the Wiki

- **[[Thinkers/Descartes]] / [[Thinkers/Edmund Husserl]]**: both start from an ego given prior to others; Heidegger inverts this — *Mitsein* is equiprimordial with being-in-the-world, and the everyday self is the they-self, so the "I" is an achievement, not a datum. Husserl's problem of [[Concepts/Intersubjectivity and the Life-World (Husserl)|intersubjectivity]] (constituting the other from the ego) is dissolved rather than solved.
- **[[Thinkers/Jean-Paul Sartre]]**: transposes das Man into [[Concepts/Bad Faith (Mauvaise Foi) (Sartre)|bad faith]] and [[Concepts/The Look and the Other (Sartre)|the Look]], but makes the relation to the Other fundamentally conflictual, where Heidegger's being-with is neutral and even the ground of solicitude.
- **[[Thinkers/Søren Kierkegaard]]** (where present in the vault): the analysis of "the public" and leveling anticipates das Man; Heidegger ontologizes what Kierkegaard moralized.

## Contradictions / Open Questions

- > [!warning] Heidegger insists *Verfallen* carries "no value judgment," yet the vocabulary (dictatorship, plunge, alienation) is unmistakably pejorative — a tension noted since the earliest reception; cf. the normative vacuum flagged in [[Concepts/Being-toward-Death and Authenticity (Heidegger)]].
- If authenticity is only an "existentiell modification of the they," the they is ineliminable — how then can resoluteness be more than a style of conformity?
