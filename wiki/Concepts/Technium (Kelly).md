# Technium (Kelly)

> Kevin Kelly's term for the "seventh kingdom" of life: the global, massively interconnected system of technology — not just hardware but culture, art, social institutions, intellectual creations, software, law, philosophical concepts, and the generative impulses of invention. The technium has its own evolutionary dynamics, a measure of autonomy (self-organization, self-repair, self-amplification on a continuum with life and mind), and inherent "wants" or propensities (to self-organize into hierarchies, to perpetuate and expand itself, to increase complexity/exotropy/order against entropy, to maximize choices, to converge on recurring forms/solutions). Technology coevolves with humanity; it is an extension and accelerant of life's trajectory. "What technology wants" is continuous with deep evolutionary and cosmic tendencies; humans both shape it and are shaped by it. The 12 forces in *The Inevitable* are specific, digital-era expressions of these deeper wants.

## Summary (synthesis across sources)

In *What Technology Wants* (2010), Kelly (building on *Out of Control*, 1994) defines the technium as the extended "body" and cumulative system of all technologies and their interactions, including the intangible and cultural. It is the latest phase of a cosmic arc of increasing order, information, and "mind" (exotropy) that began with the Big Bang, continued through the origins of life, and now accelerates via human invention and cultural evolution.

The technium behaves like life: it evolves (variation, inheritance/selection via use and copying), self-organizes, replicates given the chance, increases in complexity, diversity, energy density, sentience/efficiency, and "aliveness." It has autonomy on a continuum — networks self-repair, traffic flows self-steer, evolutionary algorithms self-improve, bits "whisper to themselves" unaccounted for by known nodes. It must obey not only human intentions but the laws of self-organization, life, and mind.

Key: "what technology wants" refers to unconscious tendencies/leanings/trajectories (propensities, biases, compulsions), analogous to how a bacterium "wants" nutrients or life repeatedly "wants" eyes (convergent evolution). Explicitly: it wants to sort into hierarchies, to perpetuate itself, to increase choices (von Foerster), to become more complex/exotropic.

Humans and the technium co-evolve and co-shape desires. Technology fulfills and amplifies what we deeply want rather than opposing or replacing human nature. The future of technology is the future of humanity.

In *The Inevitable* (2016), the technium thesis grounds the 12 forces: they are the current, digital-specific manifestations of these long-term wants/biases in the physics of bits, networks, and scalable cognition. The "inevitable" is momentum from the technium's nature; protopia and the Beginning (holos) are its near-term expressions.

## Key Claims / Positions

- **Seventh kingdom**: Life has six kingdoms (or more in modern taxonomy); technology is the seventh — an outgrowth of life with its own evolutionary logic, faster than biological evolution.
- **Autonomy on a continuum**: The technium exhibits self-repair, self-steering, self-reproduction (limited), self-improvement. It is "whispering to itself." Not fully independent of humans (we invented it), but not fully controllable by any single intention either.
- **Wants / propensities**: To hierarchical self-organization; self-perpetuation and expansion; increasing exotropy (order, information, complexity, mind); increasing number of choices/options; convergence on similar solutions along independent paths (like eyes/wings in biology; many tech inventions recur).
- **Exotropy as the arc**: The universe trends toward greater order/complexification/mind against entropy (Dyson: "the universe knew we were coming"; Sagan: "starstuff pondering the stars"). Life and technology are phases of this "ordained becoming."
- **Co-evolution and amplification**: We domesticated technology and it domesticated us (e.g., human physiology changed with tools/cooking). "What technology wants" is deeply continuous with "what we want" — it amplifies rather than alienates human nature.
- **Convergence**: Independent lineages (biological or technological) often arrive at analogous forms/solutions. Inevitable inventions exist at the level of general forms.
- **Human agency within givens**: We cannot demand technology obey us any more than we can demand life obey us. Sometimes surrender to its lead and abundance; sometimes bend its course. Choices remain (e.g., Amish selective adoption as a model of convivial, value-prioritizing use; steering rather than stopping).
- **Infinite game**: (Carse) Play to keep the game (of mind, choice, becoming) going and expanding, not to win/finish it. Technology extends this evolutionary play.

## Contradictions / Open Questions

- **Autonomy vs. control/steering**: The technium has real momentum and "wants" independent of us, yet we created it and retain agency over particulars. Boundary? How much is inevitable vs. chosen? Cross to [[Sources/Superintelligence - Nick Bostrom (2014)]] (Orthogonality Thesis: intelligence and final goals independent; Instrumental Convergence as "wants" that may be orthogonal to human flourishing) and [[Sources/The Human Use of Human Beings - Norbert Wiener (1950)]] (early recognition of technological momentum and need for human meaning/control).
- **Life-like amplification vs. orthogonality/hype/limits**: Kelly frames technium wants as life-continuous and amplifying of human values (protopia, holos, Beginning). Tensions with Bostrom (arbitrary goals possible, including paperclip maximizers indifferent to humans) and with [[Sources/Artificial Intelligence - Melanie Mitchell (2019)]] (current AI lacks common sense/analogy/understanding; hype cycles; pattern matching ≠ robust cognition). Kelly's convergence/co-evolution vs. Mitchell's corrective on brittleness.
- **Dynamic evolutionary "wants" vs. static Great Chain**: The technium/exotropy/becoming is the evolutionary/process successor that overthrows Lovejoy's static, finished, hierarchical "diagram" (plenitude/continuity/gradation in a complete, unchanging scala). Cross to [[Sources/The Great Chain of Being - Arthur O. Lovejoy (1936)]] and [[Concepts/Great Chain of Being (Lovejoy)]]; also Butterfield/Kuhn on the Scientific Revolution as the mental shift that began displacing such presuppositions.
- **Protopia/embrace vs. rupture or decisive risk**: Kelly's incremental becoming + new problems vs. Kurzweil Singularity (2045 rupture, nonbio dominance) or Bostrom decisive strategic advantage/singleton scenarios. See [[Sources/The Singularity Is Near - Ray Kurzweil (2005)]], [[Sources/The Inevitable - Kevin Kelly (2016)]].
- **Choices within inevitability**: Amish hackers (selective, community-prioritizing adoption) as positive model vs. total rejection (Unabomber critique in the book). How to "listen" to the technium while prioritizing human goods? Convivial tools (Illich influence).
