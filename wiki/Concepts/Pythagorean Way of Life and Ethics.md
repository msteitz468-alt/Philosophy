# Pythagorean Way of Life and Ethics
> The *bios pythagorikos* — the distinctive Pythagorean form of life — built on ritual precepts (*acusmata*/*symbola*), purity, friendship, and the principle that "human beings are the property of the gods." The practical core that the doctrinal labels (mystic, scientist) tend to miss.

## Summary
On M. Laura Gemelli Marciano's chapter in [[Sources/A History of Pythagoreanism - Carl A. Huffman (ed.) (2014)]], the *bios* cannot be separated from its religious dimension. She reads it through Deikmann's contrast of "instrumental" vs "receptive" consciousness, arguing that modern rationalist interpretations distort a life organized as *service* to the divine. This is a direct counter to Zhmud's deflationary, secular reading of the school (see [[Schools/Pythagoreanism]]) and to the claim that the ritual precepts are late inventions.

## The Acusmata as Practice, Not Doctrine
The ***acusmata*** ("things heard") or ***symbola*** — orally transmitted maxims — "form the original nucleus of the *bios pythagorikos*." Against Zhmud, Gemelli Marciano insists they were **genuinely practiced**, not late fabrications (see [[Concepts/Acusmata and the Two Sects (Acusmatici and Mathematici)]]). They are neither superstition nor "absurdities" but **transformative practice** orienting the practitioner toward purity and "attunement" with the divine — they were to be *enacted*, not explained. The bios rests on the principle that "human beings are the property of the gods, and as such must order their whole life towards the honor... of the divine" (cf. Plato, *Phaedo*).

## Affinities and Content
- **Egyptian priesthood parallels**: purity rules, linen, food taboos, and the "**scales**" maxim ("don't overstep the scales" — don't tip the balance in your own favor) closely parallel the Egyptian *Book of the Dead*, suggesting possible Egyptian influence on the form of the precepts.
- **Food rules varied and were probably less rigid early on** — the earliest Pythagoreans were likely *not* total vegetarians; strict vegetarianism intensified later (Empedocles). This matches Zhmud's view that sacrificial meat was permitted (see [[Thinkers/Pythagoras]]).
- ***Daimones*, heroes, and the dead** are integral and normal; many *acusmata* concern appeasing them (ringing sounds as their voices, silence in the dark, food fallen to the floor left for the heroes).
- **Silence** (*echemythia*) is both esoteric reticence and literal attentive *listening*; the *doctrine* of [[Concepts/Metempsychosis (Transmigration of Souls)|metempsychosis]] was "widely known," but the *experience* of incarnation and liberation is what is guarded.

## Friendship and Ethics
Pythagorean ethics centers on **friendship as "harmonious equality" (*enarmonion isotēta*)** arranged on a hierarchy of authority — exemplified by the legend of **Damon and Phintias** and the maxim "friends have all things in common." Women were admitted to teaching (Theano). This ethic of proportionate concord links directly to [[Thinkers/Archytas]]' claim (fr. 3) that calculation "increased concord" and to Plato's harmonized soul (see [[Concepts/Pythagorean Harmonics]], [[Thinkers/Plato]]).

> [!note] Scholarly tension. Gemelli Marciano (practice is early and genuinely religious) and Zhmud (the *acusmata*/sect machinery is a late fiction; the school was a secular *hetairia*) directly disagree — a live form of the broader [[Contradictions/Pythagoras the Scientist vs the Religious Charismatic]].
