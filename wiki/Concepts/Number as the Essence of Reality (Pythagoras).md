# Number as the Essence of Reality (Pythagoras)

> The Pythagorean metaphysical doctrine asserting that number is the primary substance and ordering principle of the cosmos.

## Summary

The doctrine that **"all things are numbers"** marks the transition from materialist monism (seeking a physical element as the *arche*) to formalist rationalism (seeking the structural, mathematical principles of the universe). Rather than asking what the world is *made of*, Pythagoras asked how the world is *structured*. The answer lay in the mathematical ratios and proportions that govern the natural world.

---

## Core Claims and Arguments

**The Musical Discovery**:
- Pythagoras discovered that the pitch of a vibrating string depends on its length, and that consonant musical intervals correspond to simple numerical ratios:
  - **Octave** = 2:1 ratio
  - **Fifth** = 3:2 ratio
  - **Fourth** = 4:3 ratio
- This was the first historical demonstration that a qualitative sensory experience (harmony in music) is governed by quantitative mathematical laws.

**Cosmic Order and Mathematics**:
- The Pythagoreans generalized this discovery: if music is mathematical, the entire cosmos must be structured according to mathematical proportions.
- They represented numbers geometrically as points or units (e.g., 1 is a point, 2 is a line, 3 is a plane, 4 is a solid). Thus, physical space and bodies are literally constructed out of numbers.

**The Table of Opposites**:
- The Pythagoreans organized reality into ten fundamental oppositions, led by **Limit** (associated with odd numbers, light, good, male, stability) and the **Unlimited** (associated with even numbers, darkness, bad, female, motion). 
- Harmony is the imposition of **Limit** upon the **Unlimited** through mathematical proportion.

---

## Relevance to Later Philosophy and Science

- **Plato's Forms**: Plato adapted this doctrine to argue that physical things are imperfect copies of immaterial, eternal realities (Forms)—which are best understood mathematically.
- **Modern Physics**: Directly anticipates the Galilean-Newtonian assumption that the book of nature is written in the language of mathematics, and the modern physics view of reality as mathematical relations (e.g., string theory, quantum wavefunctions).

## Contradictions / Open Questions
- > [!warning] The Paradox of the Irrational: The discovery of irrational numbers (such as $\sqrt{2}$, the length of the diagonal of a unit square) shattered the early Pythagorean assumption that all reality could be explained by simple whole-number ratios, exposing a deep gap between geometric reality and numerical rationalism.
