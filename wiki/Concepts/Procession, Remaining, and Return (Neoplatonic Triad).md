# Procession, Remaining, and Return (Neoplatonic Triad)

> *Monē – proodos – epistrophē*: every effect abides in its cause, proceeds from it, and reverts upon it — the universal three-beat law of reality that the Athenian School generalized from Plotinus's two-stage account of emanation.

## Summary

In [[Thinkers/Plotinus]], emanation already has two moments: a formless outflow of life (procession), and a turning-back on the source in contemplation by which the product receives form and order (reversion). [[Thinkers/Iamblichus]] and the Athenian School made this triadic and universal: Abiding (*monē*, the effect's remaining in its cause), Procession (*proodos*), Reversion (*epistrophē*), correlated with Limit, Infinity, and their Mixture and applied at every level of the hierarchy — alongside the parallel "vertical" triad Being–Life–Intelligence and the participation triad Unparticipated–Participated–Participant (Law of Mean Terms). [[Thinkers/Proclus]]'s *Elements of Theology* deduces the scheme systematically: all effects revert upon their causes; higher causes reach further down than lower ones; Theodorus of Asine's dictum "All things pray except the First" captures the universality of reversion. Wallis notes the triad solved Plotinian difficulties (e.g. about Intelligible Matter) at the price of an ever-multiplying scholastic hierarchy.

**How different thinkers use this:** Plotinus — implicit, two-stage, tied to the psychology of contemplation; Iamblichus — explicit structural law plus mythological mapping (Cronos–Rhea–Zeus); Proclus — geometric deduction, with the cycle also read as the structure of causation itself. Hegel's dialectical triad is the modern descendant Wallis gestures at ("Procline" Hegel vs. "Plotinian" Schelling) — cf. [[Concepts/Aufhebung (Sublation)]].
