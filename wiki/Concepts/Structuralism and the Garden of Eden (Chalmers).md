# Structuralism and the Garden of Eden (Chalmers)
> The foundations of *Reality+*: physics is structure, computation requires causal structure ("causal structure isn't cheap"), and the manifest image "has a structural core that isn't an illusion at all."

## Summary

*Reality+* chs. 21–23 supply the machinery behind [[Concepts/Simulation Realism (Chalmers)]].

**Computation is causal** (ch. 21): Egan's dust theory and the Putnam-rock/Searle-wall triviality arguments ("the wall behind my back is right now implementing the Wordstar program...") fail because implementation requires **counterfactual-supporting causal structure** — a prerecorded movie of the Game of Life doesn't implement Life. **Causal structuralism**: "a physical system implements a mathematical computation when the causal structure of the system mirrors the formal structure of the computation." Computers are "Causation Machines"; simulation and gradual uploading preserve causal structure. "Implementing a computation requires the right causal structure, and causal structure isn't cheap."

**Structural realism** (ch. 22): from Carnap's *Aufbau* ("a noble failure") and Russell, revived by Worrall — structure is what survives theory change, answering both the no-miracles argument and the pessimistic induction. Ontic vs. epistemic structural realism map onto pure it-from-structure vs. it-from-structure-from-X; the perfect simulation hypothesis is "highly congenial to epistemic structural realism." The Newman/numbers-to-physics problems are solved by causal powers and counterfactual-supporting laws. Core argument: physical theories are structural; Sim Universe shares Nonsim Universe's structure; so if we're simulated, our theories are true. Tegmark's Mathematical Universe Hypothesis is the "too extreme" pure limit. Kantian coda (via Langton's "Kantian Humility"): structure = appearances, the realizer = Ding an sich — "The relational properties of reality are knowable, but the intrinsic properties are unknowable."

**The Garden of Eden** (ch. 23): Eden is "a place where everything was exactly as it seemed to be" — capital-E Space, Time, Redness, Solidity, Free Will; eating from "the Tree of Science" was the Fall. Of Sellars's four strategies for reconciling the manifest and scientific images, **reconstruction** — Edenic primitivism → functionalism ("solidity is as solidity does"; "Distance is what there's less action at") — is "the most often correct." The anti-simulation intuition ("nothing is laid out in space as it seems") is an Edenic intuition, equally true of relativity and QM. **Imperfect realism**: "the manifest image is partly an illusion — but it has a structural core that isn't an illusion at all." Eden = Reality 0.0; ordinary reality = Reality 1.0; VR = Reality 2.0 — the latter two on a par. Consciousness alone resists structuralization (Mary's room), so it-from-bit-from-consciousness stays "on the table."

## How This Relates to Prior Thinkers

- **[[Thinkers/John Searle]] / Hilary Putnam**: their computation-triviality arguments rebutted (Chalmers's own 1994–96 papers); Egan's dust theory likewise — "patterns of cause and effect" are not optional.
- **[[Thinkers/Kant]]** ([[Sources/Phenomenology of Spirit - Hegel (1807)]] aside, the vault's Kant pages): read via Langton as proto-epistemic-structural-realist — simulation as naturalized noumenon.
- **[[Thinkers/Bertrand Russell]] / Rudolf Carnap**: direct ancestors; Newman's problem inherited and answered.
- **[[Thinkers/Hume]]**: the regularity view of causation is the dust theory's last defense; Chalmers replies that dust lacks even "the global regularities needed for genuine cause and effect."
- **Wilfrid Sellars / the Churchlands**: manifest-image framework adopted; eliminativism resisted.
- **[[Concepts/Number as the Essence of Reality (Pythagoras)]]** and [[Concepts/Conditional Rendering and Quantum Indeterminacy (Virk)]]: the vault's older mathematical-structure and computational-physics threads, now given their most careful formulation.
