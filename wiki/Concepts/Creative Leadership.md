# Creative Leadership

> Principles and practices for leading creative/high-output teams and organizations drawn from world-class practitioners (Anna Wintour, Howard Schultz, Sara Blakely, others in the 2026 MasterClass collection). Combines iron conviction/vision with systems, direct communication, delegation, personal discipline, and values that sustain long-term success.

## Core Lessons (from sampled classes)
- **Hire great, self-reliant, diverse teams and trust them** (Wintour: reads every word/approves every look but does not micromanage; empowers editors to decide on her/brand's behalf).
- **Give feedback quickly and be direct**. "Not everyone is going to agree with you." Strength of conviction + clarity is core to leadership.
- **Build systems for tracking work** and be thoughtful about meetings and why they happen. Hyper-organization enables responsiveness.
- **Personal routines as force multiplier**: Wintour 4-5am wake, read British/American papers (NYT/WSJ/Guardian), Twitter/Instagram, exercise (tennis), email, mentally map the day. Office 8-8:30.
- **Values and profits are not enemies** (Schultz): fiduciary duty to shareholders + moral duty to communities served. Employee dignity became a foundational Starbucks value because of the founder's father's experience being "tossed aside."
- **Jump in despite self-doubt**: List costs of leaping (money, family time) *and* costs of not leaping (regret, missed contribution). Today's environment (tech, capital, global consumers) makes it unusually accessible.
- **Consumer-pain origin + leverage** (Blakely): Spanx from cutting feet out of pantyhose for cream pants + $5k savings + no prior experience. Sent product to Oprah → Favorite Things → QVC scale. Personal story as foundation of brand and influence.
