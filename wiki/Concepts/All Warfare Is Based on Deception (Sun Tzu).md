# All Warfare Is Based on Deception (Sun Tzu)

> One-sentence hook: Sun Tzu's blunt axiom — "**All warfare is based on deception**" — that controlling the enemy's *perception* (appearing weak when strong, far when near, disordered when ordered) is the lever of victory, because action follows belief and the misled adversary brings about his own defeat.

## Overview

Chapter I ("Laying Plans"), ss. 18–25, immediately after the calculative five factors — Sun Tzu pairs cold analysis with active *misdirection*. "All warfare is based on deception" (Giles: "the truth of this pithy and profound saying will be admitted by every soldier"). The catalogue:

> "When able to attack, we must seem unable; when using our forces, we must seem inactive; **when we are near, we must make the enemy believe we are far away; when far away, we must make him believe we are near.** Hold out baits to entice the enemy. Feign disorder, and crush him… If your opponent is of choleric temper, seek to irritate him. Pretend to be weak, that he may grow arrogant… **Attack him where he is unprepared, appear where you are not expected.** These military devices, leading to victory, must not be divulged beforehand."

## Key Arguments

- **War is fought in the enemy's mind.** Every item manipulates the adversary's *model of you* — your capability, location, intentions, emotional state. Victory is engineered by making him act on a false picture: he defends the wrong point, attacks the bait, overextends in arrogance.
- **Deception serves [[Concepts/Winning Without Fighting (Sun Tzu)|economy of force]] and [[Concepts/Shih - Momentum, Formlessness, and Striking Weakness (Sun Tzu)|formlessness]].** "Appear where you are not expected" concentrates your strength against his weakness; "be subtle, even to formlessness" (Ch. VI) keeps him from concentrating against you. Deception is how you achieve favorable asymmetry without extra force.
- **Manipulate emotion, not just information.** "If of choleric temper, irritate him; pretend weakness, that he may grow arrogant" — the good tactician "plays with his adversary as a cat plays with a mouse" (Wang Tzu, via Giles). Provocation and flattery are deception aimed at the opponent's *judgment*.
- **Surprise and adaptation.** "These devices must not be divulged beforehand"; and "according as circumstances are favorable, modify your plans" — deception is improvised against *this* enemy's responses, not scripted.
- **The honest core under the deception.** Crucially, deception is *outward*-facing; internally the general must be ruthlessly truthful (real [[Concepts/Calculation and the Five Factors (Sun Tzu)|calculation]], real [[Concepts/Know the Enemy and Know Yourself (Sun Tzu)|self-knowledge]]). You deceive the enemy precisely *because* you see clearly yourself.

## Contrasts / Lineage

- **The ethics collision (flag, don't resolve)**: "all warfare is based on deception" is the head-on opposite of [[Concepts/Categorical Imperative - Universal Law Formulation (Kant)|Kant's]] prohibition on lying (the false-promise example) and [[Concepts/Formula of Humanity as End in Itself (Kant)|treating others never merely as means]]. It also tensions with [[Concepts/Toleration (Locke)|Lockean]] "inward persuasion" and the truthfulness prized by [[Concepts/Philosophy of Logical Analysis (Russell)|Russell]]. Sun Tzu's frame is the *exception* state of war (a last resort, Chs. II & XII), not ordinary ethical life — but the vault holds the tension openly.
- **Vs. [[Concepts/Power, Empire, and Revolt (Thucydides)]]**: Thucydides' actors deceive constantly (the Mytilenean and Sicilian episodes), but he narrates deception's *tragic* unraveling; Sun Tzu prescribes it as craft.
- **Resonance with [[Concepts/Perspectivism (Nietzsche)|Nietzsche]] / [[Concepts/Belief in Antitheses of Values (Nietzsche)|"untruth as a condition of life"]]**: both unsettle the simple truth/illusion antithesis — for Sun Tzu, *managed illusion* is a condition of victory. (Russell's "cosmic impiety" worry is the counterweight: deception as craft must not become contempt for truth as such.)
- **Game theory / signaling**: a pre-modern treatise on *costly signaling, bluffing, and information asymmetry* — directly cognate with poker ([[Sources/MasterClass Collection - 2026|Negreanu/Ivey]]), negotiation ([[Concepts/Persuasion & Sales Science|information asymmetry]]), and adversarial games.

## Vault / AI / Personal Relevance

- **Information asymmetry as the lever**: the strategic core transferable to non-violent contexts — control what your competitor/counterpart believes about your position, capacity, and intentions; concentrate where you are unexpected. (In sales/negotiation this reads as legitimate *discretion and positioning*, not fraud — conceal your reservation price, reveal strength selectively; cf. [[Concepts/Persuasion & Sales Science]].)
- **AI/adversarial reasoning**: directly relevant to game-playing agents, security (deception/honeypots, the "doomed/converted spy" ≈ disinformation), and red-teaming; also a caution — *robustness against deception* (an adversary manipulating your model of the world) is the defensive mirror, tied to [[Concepts/Foreknowledge and the Use of Spies (Sun Tzu)|verifying intelligence]].
- **The truthfulness boundary**: a useful line for the vault and for ethics generally — outward strategic discretion vs. inward honesty. The maintainer never deceives *the wiki* (provenance, calibrated claims) even while a strategist may conceal from a rival. Distinguish *discretion/positioning* (legitimate) from *fraud/manipulation* (not).
- **Personal/career**: read charitably as **discretion, timing, and positioning** — don't show your whole hand, choose when and where to compete, manage the impression you create — not as license for dishonesty.
