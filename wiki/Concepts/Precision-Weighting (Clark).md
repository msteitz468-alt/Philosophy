# Precision-Weighting (Clark)
> The brain's estimate of how reliable a prediction or sensory signal is — the "volume control" that decides whether your guesses or your senses win — and, on Clark's account, what attention *really is*.

## Summary

In [[Concepts/Predictive Processing (Clark)]], predictions and sensory prediction-errors must be combined, and everything turns on their relative weighting. **Precision** is the brain's best estimate of the reliability of a signal in a given context — "the estimated value of this information, in this context, for this task" — implemented physiologically as altered post-synaptic gain on neuronal populations. Only predictions (or sensory signals) estimated as *reliable* strongly shape experience.

Clark's boldest identification: **precision-weighting is attention.** "What we informally think of as 'attention' is implemented... by mechanisms that alter these precision-weightings." He calls precision "the single most powerful tool in the predictive processing toolkit" (the headlamp that determines what gets processed as significant). Crucially, precision effects can run **nonconsciously** — "the tip of the predictive iceberg" — as in subliminal face/shock pairing experiments.

## Key Claims / Positions — Pain, Placebo, and Computational Psychiatry

- **Pain is a construction, not a readout.** "Brains build human experience only by combining their own predictions with sensory evidence." Nociceptor firing is only *one source of evidence*: the construction worker who felt searing pain from a nail that (it turned out) passed harmlessly between his toes shows pain with no injury; other cases show no pain despite massive nociception. Pain is a *perception*, and chronic pain can become "a disease in its own right" — the third, "nociplastic," pain category.
- **Placebo and nocebo run deep** (down to the spinal cord), driven by expectations of relief or harm; Irene Tracey's fMRI work shows even religious imagery modulating pain.
- **Functional disorders** — genuine impairment (tremor, weakness, tubular visual fields, functional blindness) with no structural cause — are explained by *aberrant precision/attention*, not faking. Jon Stone's light-triggered functional blindness, Hoover's sign for functional leg weakness, and Rob McIntosh's numbness-weakness correlations are Clark's cases.
- **Autism**: better explained by *overweighted sensory precision* (enhanced reliability on incoming signal) than the older "weakened priors" theory — reduced McGurk and Hollow-Mask effects follow.
- **Psychosis / schizophrenia and PTSD**: aberrant *high-precision prediction-error* signals force radical revisions of the world-model (Peter Chadwick: "I had to make sense, any sense, out of all these uncanny coincidences... by radically changing my conception of reality"). Nonclinical voice-hearers detect hidden sine-wave speech far more often (75% vs. 47%).
- **No single correct balance.** Optimal precision-weighting is *relative to environment*; "computational psychiatry" reconceives disorders as differing balances in information processing rather than fixed symptom clusters. This is the mechanism later exploited in [[Concepts/Hacking the Prediction Machine (Clark)]].

## How This Relates to Prior Thinkers

- **[[Thinkers/Daniel Dennett]]**: attention-as-precision fits the Multiple Drafts picture in which no content is "definitively conscious" until it wins the competition for influence; precision is a candidate mechanism for that weighting.
- **[[Thinkers/Kant]]**: precision governs *how much* the mind's own contribution shapes a given experience — a dial on the Kantian point that mind co-constitutes appearance.
