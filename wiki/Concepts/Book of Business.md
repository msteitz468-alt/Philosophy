# Book of Business

> In insurance brokerage, the "book" an Account Executive owns and grows: the portfolio of client accounts under management, measured by total premium, number of accounts, retention rate, average account size, largest account, cross-sell ratio, and revenue generated (retention + new business + cross-sell). Central to compensation (salary + commission/bonus on book performance) and the core distinction between AM (service) and AE (ownership/growth) roles.

## Key Metrics (from sources)
- Total premium under management.
- # of commercial accounts (focus agribusiness/food processing).
- Retention rate (target 90%+ first-year in playbook).
- Average account size / largest account.
- Proactive growth examples (e.g. identified uncovered exposure, recommended/placed additional coverage adding $22K annual premium).
- Cross-sell ratio (target 2.5+ lines per client).
- New accounts bound (playbook target 10-15 in 12 months, $75-150K avg premium).

## In Career Context
- Personal promotion plan: Frame *everything* as "your book" even as AM (to demonstrate AE mindset). Use specific numbers in internal one-pager, interviews ("I currently manage a $XM premium book of N accounts... retention X%... added $Y via proactive...").
- Playbook execution: The entire cradle-to-grave strategy (prospecting → discovery → proposal → close → onboard → retain/cross-sell/renew) is designed to build and grow the book per the 12-month targets and action calendar.
- Self-assessment: "Book ownership mindset" is a scored competency (think retention/revenue/cross-sell, not just service tasks).
