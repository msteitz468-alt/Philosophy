# Superintelligence (Bostrom)

> Any intellect that greatly exceeds the cognitive performance of humans in virtually all domains of interest. Nick Bostrom (2014) analyzes its possible forms (speed, collective, quality), the paths by which it could be created, the kinetics of an intelligence explosion once a seed is achieved, and the strategic and control implications. Distinguished from narrow AI by its generality and from human-level AI by the magnitude of the advantage.

## Summary

Bostrom defines superintelligence as "any intellect that greatly exceeds the cognitive performance of humans in virtually all domains of interest."

Forms:
- **Speed superintelligence**: Same architecture as human or human-level AI, but running much faster (subjective time compressed; can think years in hours).
- **Collective superintelligence**: Large number of somewhat sub-human or human-level AIs coordinated at a scale and with communication/integration far beyond human groups, achieving effective superhuman performance on many tasks.
- **Quality superintelligence**: Fundamentally more capable architecture — better algorithms, deeper insights, novel concepts humans cannot grasp, etc.

The book emphasizes that once a system reaches human-level or slightly above in the key areas needed for recursive self-improvement (AI research, software engineering, hardware design, strategy), an intelligence explosion becomes possible. The speed and character of the takeoff depend on the "recalcitrance" (difficulty) of further gains versus the optimization power applied.

A superintelligent system would have decisive cognitive superpowers in areas such as hacking, social manipulation, technology R&D, strategy, and persuasion — making control and alignment extraordinarily high-stakes.

## Key Claims

- Superintelligence is not just "smarter humans"; the difference in capability and strategic position can be qualitative and civilization-determining.
- The first superintelligence (or the project that creates it) may obtain a decisive strategic advantage, with profound implications for global power structure (singletons vs. multipolar scenarios).
- Because we design the initial conditions, we have leverage — but the window may close rapidly.

## Relation to Vault

- **Turing**: Turing predicted machines would eventually surpass humans in intellectual fields and sketched a research program (learning machines) that could lead there. Bostrom provides the detailed analysis of the post-threshold dynamics.
- **Minsky, Dennett, Hofstadter**: Models of mind (agent societies, multiple drafts, strange loops) inform what "human-level" or "quality super" might look like architecturally and whether emulation (WBE path) or de novo design is more likely or controllable.
- **Wiener**: Early warnings about powerful machines and alignment scale up directly to the superintelligence case.
- **Pearl**: Causal mastery would be one of the first and most powerful advantages a superintelligence would have over current systems.
