# Anomaly and Crisis (Kuhn)

> An **anomaly** is a result that violates the expectations the [[Concepts/Paradigm (Kuhn)|paradigm]] sets up — "the recognition that nature has somehow violated the paradigm-induced expectations that govern normal science." When anomalies become numerous, central, and intractable, normal science enters a **crisis**: a period of "extraordinary research," debate over fundamentals, and the search for a new paradigm. Anomaly and crisis are the hinge between [[Concepts/Normal Science and Puzzle-Solving (Kuhn)|normal science]] and [[Concepts/Scientific Revolutions and Paradigm Shifts (Kuhn)|revolution]].

## Anomaly

- **Discovery comes from anomaly**: "Discovery commences with the awareness of anomaly" — when something runs counter to what the paradigm leads us to expect. Novelty emerges "with difficulty, manifested by resistance, against a background provided by expectation" (the famous **Bruner–Postman anomalous-playing-card experiment** Kuhn cites: subjects shown a red six of spades misperceive it as normal until, with effort, the anomaly is seen).
- **Anomaly is not bare refutation**: this is Kuhn's key amendment to Popper. We "see what we expect," so it often takes a long time for an anomaly to be *recognized* as such. And there are *always* discrepancies between theory and data — "the incompleteness and imperfection of the existing data-theory fit… define many of the puzzles" of normal science. Most discrepancies are absorbed as puzzles or set aside as curiosities (e.g. Brownian motion before molecular theory). The recognition of something as a **significant** anomaly "is itself a complex historical event," not a simple logical falsification.
- An anomaly becomes serious when it (a) calls a *fundamental* generalization into question, (b) blocks an application of practical importance, or (c) persists despite the best efforts of the ablest practitioners.

## Crisis

- Persistent, central anomalies that resist all attempts at "mopping-up" provoke a **crisis** — "the proliferation of competing articulations, the willingness to try anything, the expression of explicit discontent, the recourse to philosophy and to debate over fundamentals." Kuhn quotes Wolfgang Pauli on the eve of matrix mechanics: physics felt as though it were falling apart.
- Crisis is the **loosening** of the rules that makes genuine novelty psychologically and institutionally possible — it is "a necessary precondition for the emergence of novel theories."
- **Crisis does not by itself overthrow the paradigm.** Two Kuhnian points: (1) "**To reject one paradigm without simultaneously substituting another is to reject science itself**" — a faulty paradigm is retained until a replacement appears; and (2) scientists do not lose faith merely because of counterinstances ("there is no such thing as research without counterinstances"). The paradigm is abandoned only when a viable rival can take over.

## Relation to Other Vault Content

- **Vs. Popper's falsification** (central): Kuhn agrees the anomaly is what "evokes competitors for an existing paradigm," but insists anomalies are **not** falsifying instances that mandate immediate rejection — "if any and every failure to fit were ground for theory rejection, all theories ought to be rejected at all times." Where Popper says a corroborated theory should still be exposed to refutation at all times, Kuhn says the community rightly clings to the paradigm through anomalies until crisis + a successor arrive. See [[Contradictions/Popper vs Kuhn - Falsification vs Paradigms]] and [[Concepts/Falsifiability as Criterion of Demarcation (Popper)]].
- **Theory-ladenness**: "we see what we expect" aligns with Popper's and Hanson's rejection of the neutral "given" (cf. [[Concepts/Basic Statements and the Empirical Basis (Popper)]]) — agreement on the premise, opposite conclusions.
- Sits between [[Concepts/Normal Science and Puzzle-Solving (Kuhn)]] and [[Concepts/Scientific Revolutions and Paradigm Shifts (Kuhn)]].

## Vault Meta / AI

The wiki's **anomalies** are sources or contradictions that the current schema cannot cleanly absorb. Kuhn's discipline: don't smooth an anomaly away (the temptation of "normal science" maintenance), but also don't treat one anomaly as instantly refuting the whole structure — register it, and let a *pattern* of intractable anomalies justify a schema-level (paradigm) change. For AI: anomaly-detection is gated by the frame's expectations, so a model can be blind to data its paradigm doesn't predict; surfacing "this doesn't fit" is itself valuable work.
