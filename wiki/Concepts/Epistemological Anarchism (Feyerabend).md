# Epistemological Anarchism (Feyerabend)

> Feyerabend's thesis that **science has no fixed method** and needs none: every proposed universal rule of scientific procedure has been violated in the great advances — and *had to be*. "Science is an essentially anarchic enterprise." The slogan **"the only principle that does not inhibit progress is: anything goes"** is the ironic conclusion of this argument, not a positive rule.

## The Argument

Feyerabend offers two converging lines (*Against Method*, Introduction + ch. 1):

1. **Historical**: examine real episodes — above all the Copernican Revolution and [[Concepts/Natural Interpretations and the Galileo Case (Feyerabend)|Galileo]] — and you find that scientists succeeded only by breaking the very rules (consistency with established theory, agreement with the facts, no ad hoc hypotheses, no propaganda) that methodologists prescribe. "Copernicanism… survived only because reason was frequently overruled in [its] past."
2. **Abstract**: because "facts" are theory-laden (constituted by older ideologies) and because history is "richer… more many-sided" than any rulebook, for **any** methodological rule there are circumstances in which it is advisable to break it. No rule is universally good.

The result: *no* methodology is both universal and beneficial — not inductivism, not the positivists' verificationism, and **not Popper's [[Concepts/Falsifiability as Criterion of Demarcation (Popper)|falsificationism]]** ("Popper's critical rationalism fails for the same reasons"). What remains, as the only rule with no exceptions, is the empty "anything goes."

## "Anything Goes" — What It Is Not

Feyerabend is emphatic (Preface, 3rd ed.): "anything goes" is **not a principle I hold** — "I do not think that 'principles' can be used and fruitfully discussed outside the concrete research situation they are supposed to affect" — but "the **terrified exclamation of a rationalist** who takes a closer look at history." It is a *reductio*: if you insist on one universal rule, the only one that fits all of history is vacuous. So:
- It is **not** a licence for sloppiness or "do whatever you like."
- It is **not** relativism "for whom there are no truths as such" (Feyerabend explicitly disowns that label).
- It is **not** a new method to replace the old ones — he "warns the reader" he has no intention "of replacing 'old and dogmatic' principles by 'new and more libertarian ones'."

The positive corollaries are **pluralism** ([[Concepts/Proliferation and Tenacity (Feyerabend)|proliferation and tenacity]]) and **[[Concepts/Counterinduction (Feyerabend)|counterinduction]]** — context-bound heuristics, not laws.

## Relation to Other Vault Content

- **Vs. all rival methodologies**: the radical pole opposing Popper's falsification, the positivists' verification, and even Lakatos's research programmes (which Feyerabend calls "anarchism in disguise"). See [[Contradictions/Popper vs Kuhn - Falsification vs Paradigms]] for the full rationalism↔anarchism spectrum.
- **Vs. [[Concepts/Verisimilitude (Truthlikeness) (Popper)]] / [[Concepts/Correspondence Theory of Truth (Russell)]]**: rejecting a universal method goes with rejecting science's privileged route to truth (see [[Concepts/Separation of Science and State (Feyerabend)]]).
- **With [[Concepts/Confirmation Holism (Duhem-Quine Thesis)]]**: underdetermination and theory-ladenness supply the abstract backbone — any rule can be saved or broken by adjustments elsewhere — though Feyerabend's moral is anarchist, Quine's pragmatist.
- **Sceptical kin**: a methodological cousin of [[Concepts/Mitigated Scepticism (Hume)]] and [[Concepts/Epoché (Suspension of Judgment)]] — distrust of grand rules — but turned toward *proliferation* rather than suspension.

## Vault Meta / AI

A warning against method-worship: no single ingest rule, prompt pattern, or "scientific" workflow is always right, and rigid adherence can suppress the moves that produce breakthroughs. Read "anything goes" as *use judgment in the concrete situation; keep plural approaches alive* — **not** as an excuse to abandon the SCHEMA's discipline (which Feyerabend's own caution about liberalizing "fact" supports). For AI: beware presenting one fixed methodology as the uniquely rational one; the meta-rule is fallibilist humility about meta-rules.
