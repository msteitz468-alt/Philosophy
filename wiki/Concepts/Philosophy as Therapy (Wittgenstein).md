# Philosophy as Therapy (Wittgenstein)
> Philosophical problems are not questions to be answered by theory but confusions to be dissolved — knots in our thinking produced when language is wrenched from its ordinary use. "To shew the fly the way out of the fly-bottle."

## Summary

The later [[Thinkers/Ludwig Wittgenstein|Wittgenstein]] redefines what philosophy *is*. It is not a body of doctrine, not a search for hidden essences, not a super-science of the most general facts. Philosophical problems arise when language idles — "philosophical problems arise when language goes on holiday" (§38); "Philosophy is a battle against the bewitchment of our intelligence by means of language" (§109). The philosopher's task is therapeutic: to trace a confusion back to the misuse of words that generated it, and so to dissolve it. "What is your aim in philosophy?—To shew the fly the way out of the fly-bottle" (§309).

This metaphilosophy frames the whole *Investigations*. The treatments of [[Concepts/Meaning as Use (Wittgenstein)|meaning]], [[Concepts/Rule-Following (Wittgenstein)|rule-following]], and the [[Concepts/Private Language Argument (Wittgenstein)|inner]] are not rival theories but therapies — ways of loosening the grip of a misleading picture (above all the [[Concepts/Picture Theory of Language (Wittgenstein)|naming/picture model]] of his own earlier self).

## Core Mechanics

### 1. Description, not theory
"We may not advance any kind of theory. There must not be anything hypothetical in our considerations. We must do away with all explanation, and description alone must take its place" (§109). "Philosophy simply puts everything before us, and neither explains nor deduces anything" (§126). The aim is a "perspicuous representation" that lets us "see connexions" (§122) — not new facts but a rearrangement of what we already know.

### 2. Bringing words home
"What we do is to bring words back from their metaphysical to their everyday use" (§116). Confusion arises when a word ("know," "mean," "see," "I," "time") is detached from the language-game that is its "original home" and made to do metaphysical work it cannot bear. "The results of philosophy are the uncovering of one or another piece of plain nonsense and of bumps that the understanding has got by running its head up against the limits of language" (§119).

### 3. "Leaves everything as it is"
"Philosophy may in no way interfere with the actual use of language; it can in the end only describe it... It leaves everything as it is" (§124). It corrects no scientific result and founds no new science. It only removes misunderstandings — those "concerning the use of words, caused, among other things, by certain analogies between the forms of expression in different regions of language" (§90).

### 4. Many methods, like therapies
"There is not *a* philosophical method, though there are indeed methods, like different therapies" (§133). There is no single technique because there is no single disease — each knot is untied in its own way. "The work of the philosopher consists in assembling reminders for a particular purpose" (§127). And: "Back to the rough ground!" (§107) — abandon the craving for crystalline ideal purity and return to the friction of ordinary use.

## How Different Thinkers Use This

- **vs. the early Wittgenstein:** The *Tractatus* still aimed at a final doctrine — a single logical scaffolding of all sense, to be shown and then transcended ("throw away the ladder"). The *Investigations* keeps the dissolutive impulse but abandons the systematic theory: there is no hidden logical form to uncover. See [[Contradictions/Early vs Later Wittgenstein]].
- **vs. [[Thinkers/A.J. Ayer|Ayer]] and logical positivism:** Both want to deflate metaphysics, but the positivists do so with a *theory* (the [[Concepts/Verification Principle (Ayer)|verification principle]]) that condemns whole classes of utterance as meaningless. Wittgenstein offers no such criterion-weapon; he treats confusions case by case and "leaves everything as it is."
- **vs. systematic metaphysics ([[Thinkers/Aristotle|Aristotle]], [[Concepts/Being qua Being (Aristotle)|being qua being]]):** Where the tradition asks "What is X?" expecting an essence, Wittgenstein asks how the word "X" is used, and often finds the original question to be a grammatical illusion.
- **Contrast with [[Thinkers/Martin Heidegger|Heidegger]]:** Heidegger also diagnoses a forgetting embedded in ordinary language, but seeks to *deepen* metaphysics (the question of Being), whereas Wittgenstein seeks to *dissolve* metaphysical questions as confusions.
- **Therapeutic vs. Pragmatic Wittgensteinianism**: Richard Rorty (2010) distinguishes the **therapeutic** reading (philosophy is plain nonsense and aims solely to dissolve puzzles) from the **pragmatic** reading (Wittgenstein replaces representationalism with a positive, social-practice theory of meaning). See [[Concepts/Linguistic Turn (Rorty)]].
- **Paul Horwich's Refurbished Therapism**: Horwich argues that metaphysical theories are not literal nonsense but are **epistemically irrational**, arising from innate human tendencies toward simple, uniform theories and overstretched analogies (e.g. applying mechanistic models to immaterial thoughts). Therapy consists in describing open-to-view linguistic practices to eliminate these irrational overgeneralizations.

## Contradictions / Open Questions
- Is "philosophy leaves everything as it is" itself a substantive philosophical thesis (and so self-undermining)? Critics charge that the therapeutic conception smuggles in a theory of meaning; defenders read it as method, not doctrine.
