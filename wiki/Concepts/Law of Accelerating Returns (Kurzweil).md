# Law of Accelerating Returns (Kurzweil)

> Ray Kurzweil's (2005) theory that the rate of technological (and evolutionary) progress is itself accelerating exponentially, not linearly. Technology follows S-curves of paradigms (slow start, rapid growth, maturation), but overall progress compounds across paradigms into smooth exponential growth in price-performance and capability (e.g., computation, DNA sequencing, communications, miniaturization). This leads to the "knee of the curve" where change becomes explosive, underpinning the Singularity around 2045. Builds on Moore's Law as one example of the fifth paradigm; extends to information technology broadly.

## Summary

From *The Singularity Is Near* (Ch. 2 and Appendix):

"The key idea underlying the impending Singularity is that the pace of change of our human-created technology is accelerating and its powers are expanding at an exponential pace."

Exponential growth is deceptive: starts imperceptibly, then explodes. Parable of lily pads doubling: owner leaves when <1% covered, returns to full coverage after "only" 7 doublings (128x).

Examples: computation (mechanical -> electromechanical -> vacuum tubes -> transistors -> integrated circuits -> 3D molecular/quantum, etc.); DNA sequencing; communications; Internet; miniaturization. Moore's Law (one manifestation of the fifth paradigm) as self-fulfilling but part of broader trend.

Paradigm life cycle: precursor, invention, development, maturity, false pretenders (upstart fails), obsolescence, antiquity. But S-curves compound.

Leads to "knee of the curve" (noticeable acceleration) then vertical change by mid-century.

## Key Claims

- Progress is exponential (repeatedly multiplying by a constant) rather than linear (adding a constant).
- S-curves of individual paradigms create overall exponential when viewed across history.
- Information technology follows this; computation price-performance has doubled every ~1 year (or faster) for over a century.
- This acceleration is the driver of the Singularity and GNR revolutions.
