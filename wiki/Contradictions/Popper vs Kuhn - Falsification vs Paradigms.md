# Popper vs Kuhn - Falsification vs Paradigms

> How does science actually change, and does it progress toward the truth? A spectrum from **rationalism to anarchism**. Popper: by **permanent rational criticism** — bold conjectures perpetually exposed to attempted **falsification**, with the best-corroborated surviving and science approaching truth (**verisimilitude**). Kuhn: by **mostly-uncritical normal science** under a **paradigm**, punctuated by rare, conversion-like **revolutions** across which rivals are **incommensurable** and progress is **evolution *from*, not *toward*, a fixed truth**. Lakatos: a *mediation* (rational appraisal of progressive vs. degenerating research programmes). Feyerabend: the **radical limit** — there is **no method** at all ("anything goes"), and science is one tradition among many. Hacking (orthogonal but complementary): realism is better secured by experimental *intervention* ("if you can spray them...") and entity use than by representational accuracy or theory-comparison; experiment has a life independent of high theory. See [[Sources/Representing and Intervening - Ian Hacking (1983)]].

## The Tension

This is the defining dispute of 20th-century philosophy of science, and the central axis of the vault's Philosophy of Science leg. It is partly **normative vs. descriptive** (how science *ought* to proceed vs. how, historically, it *does*), but the two genuinely conflict: each makes claims about both. Kuhn engages Popper by name in *Structure* §XII; Popper and his school (Lakatos, Watkins) replied in the 1965 Bedford College colloquium (*Criticism and the Growth of Knowledge*).

## Position A — Popper: science is permanent critical revolution toward truth

- The mark of science is **refutability**; every genuine test is an attempt to **falsify** (see [[Concepts/Falsifiability as Criterion of Demarcation (Popper)]]). The supreme methodological rule is to protect *no* statement from falsification.
- **Dogmatism is a vice.** A theory that survives severe tests is only tentatively [[Concepts/Corroboration (Popper)|corroborated]], never established; it must remain exposed to criticism. Clinging to a framework through counter-evidence is the "conventionalist twist" Popper condemns.
- Science **progresses toward truth**: bold, high-content conjectures that survive testing gain [[Concepts/Verisimilitude (Truthlikeness) (Popper)|verisimilitude]], with Tarskian truth as a regulative ideal.
- Theory choice is rational: rival theories compete on a shared logical/empirical field; the more falsifiable, better-corroborated, more truthlike theory is preferred.
- Held in: [[Thinkers/Karl Popper]]. See [[Sources/The Logic of Scientific Discovery - Karl Popper (Routledge Classics)]] and [[Sources/Conjectures and Refutations - Karl Popper]].

## Position B — Kuhn: science is normal puzzle-solving punctuated by paradigm revolutions, going nowhere in particular

- Most science is **normal science** ([[Concepts/Normal Science and Puzzle-Solving (Kuhn)]]): conservative puzzle-solving that *presupposes* the [[Concepts/Paradigm (Kuhn)|paradigm]] and does **not** test it. The scientist is "a solver of puzzles, not a tester of paradigms."
- **There is no bare falsification.** "I doubt that [falsifying experiences] exist." Every theory always faces unsolved puzzles and imperfect fits, so "if any and every failure to fit were ground for theory rejection, all theories ought to be rejected at all times." [[Concepts/Anomaly and Crisis (Kuhn)|Anomalies]] are recognized only with difficulty and are tolerated until crisis.
- **No paradigm is abandoned without a successor**: "the decision to reject one paradigm is always simultaneously the decision to accept another." The dogged faith that "nature can be shoved into the box the paradigm provides" is not a vice but the *functional precondition* of the precise, deep work that eventually exposes the paradigm-breaking anomaly.
- Change comes by [[Concepts/Scientific Revolutions and Paradigm Shifts (Kuhn)|revolution]] — a **gestalt switch / conversion**, "not the sort of battle that can be resolved by proofs," because rivals are **[[Concepts/Incommensurability (Kuhn)|incommensurable]]** (shifted standards, problems, and meanings).
- **Progress has no goal**: science is "evolution *from* primitive beginnings… toward no goal" (Darwinian selection of the fittest practice), not approach to "some one full, objective, true account of nature."
- Held in: [[Thinkers/Thomas Kuhn]]. See [[Sources/The Structure of Scientific Revolutions - Kuhn (Chicago, 4th ed.)]].

## Position C — Feyerabend: there is no method at all (epistemological anarchism)

- **[[Concepts/Epistemological Anarchism (Feyerabend)|"Anything goes"]]**: *every* universal methodology — inductivism, verificationism, and explicitly "Popper's critical rationalism" — has been violated in the great advances and would have *strangled* science if obeyed. There is no fixed method, and none is needed.
- **The Galileo case**: the textbook hero of *method* in fact won by [[Concepts/Natural Interpretations and the Galileo Case (Feyerabend)|replacing theory-laden "natural interpretations"]], by rhetoric and propaganda, by an untrustworthy telescope, and by **[[Concepts/Counterinduction (Feyerabend)|counter-inductively]]** defying the evidence. "Copernicanism… survived only because reason was frequently overruled."
- **Pluralism, not testing**: [[Concepts/Proliferation and Tenacity (Feyerabend)|proliferate]] rival theories and *tenaciously* keep them through anomalies — the opposite of Popperian "drop the refuted" and broader than Kuhn's single-paradigm normal science.
- **Beyond the rationality question**: Feyerabend agrees with Kuhn's incommensurability and theory-ladenness but rejects Kuhn's "normal science" as one more law-and-order myth; and he goes past the whole debate to deny that **science or "Reason" is privileged at all** ([[Concepts/Separation of Science and State (Feyerabend)]]).
- Held in: [[Thinkers/Paul Feyerabend]]. See [[Sources/Against Method - Paul Feyerabend]].

## Hacking's Experimental Pivot (orthogonal to the rationality spectrum)

Ian Hacking's *Representing and Intervening* (1983) engages the same post-Kuhn crisis but relocates the realism question. Incommensurability and meaning-shift make *theory* realism and representational comparison look shaky (Hacking treats this as one root of irrealist pressure). Rather than adding another voice on method or progress-toward-truth, Hacking argues that experimental *intervention* provides a more direct ground for **entity realism**: we count as real the entities we can use as tools to affect other things (the "spray" criterion from the Stanford niobium-ball experiment using positrons/electrons). The experimenter must be realist about such entities even if anti-realist about overarching theories. Experiment has "a life of its own" — often independent of high theory — and many phenomena are *created* by refining apparatus rather than discovered ready-made. This does not resolve the Popper–Kuhn–Feyerabend disputes about rationality and method but supplies a practical, use-based realism that can survive weakened representational claims. Hacking also discusses Lakatos's research programmes as a "surrogate for truth" (objectivity without correspondence). See dedicated source + [[Thinkers/Ian Hacking]] + [[Concepts/Entity Realism vs Theory Realism (Hacking)]] and [[Concepts/If You Can Spray Them Then They Are Real (Hacking)]].

- Held in: [[Thinkers/Ian Hacking]]. See [[Sources/Representing and Intervening - Ian Hacking (1983)]] (and Hacking's introductory essay to the ingested Chicago 4th ed. of Kuhn).

## Kuhn's Direct Critique of Popper (Structure §XII)

Kuhn grants that Popper's anomaly/falsifying instance matters — it "evokes competitors for an existing paradigm" — but makes three moves:
1. **Falsification is misdescribed.** What Popper calls falsification is really the *later* triumph of a new paradigm over the old — a **two-stage, joint verification–falsification process**, not the instant rejection of a theory by a counterinstance.
2. **Severity reintroduces the problem Popper fled.** If only *severe* failures justify rejection, "the Popperians will require some criterion of 'improbability' or of 'degree of falsification'" — landing them back in the difficulties of probabilistic confirmation theory that Popper rejected.
3. **Normal science is real and rational.** The uncritical, paradigm-bound activity Popper would deplore is most of science and is what makes discovery possible.

(Notably, Kuhn uses the word "verisimilitude" approvingly in a *different*, non-Popperian sense — for the realism of his own two-stage account.)

## The Popperian Reply

- Kuhn's "normal science" is **uncritical** and, to Popper and Watkins, not science but technology or "puzzle-solving" unworthy of the name; the critical attitude is exactly what should be defended.
- Incommensurability + conversion-not-proof threatens to make theory change **irrational** ("mob psychology," Lakatos's gibe) — abandoning the rationality of science.
- **Lakatos's mediation**: the *methodology of scientific research programmes* keeps Popperian rational appraisal (progressive vs. degenerating programmes) while accommodating Kuhn's history (a "hard core" is protected by a "protective belt," so programmes are not abandoned on a single anomaly). **Feyerabend** pushed the other way to [[Concepts/Epistemological Anarchism (Feyerabend)|"anything goes"]] — and replied to the Popperians that Lakatos's own standards apply only with hindsight, so they dictate nothing in the present and his rationalism is "anarchism in disguise." (Feyerabend now primary-sourced via [[Sources/Against Method - Paul Feyerabend]]; Lakatos not yet in the vault.)

## Points of Agreement (often missed)

- **Theory-ladenness of observation**: both reject the positivist neutral "given" (cf. [[Concepts/Basic Statements and the Empirical Basis (Popper)]]). They draw opposite morals from the same premise.
- **No certain foundations / fallibilism**; both prize bold theory over cautious fact-collecting and both took physics as prototype.
- Both deny naive cumulative-verification positivism; the quarrel is over what replaces it.

## Why It Matters

- It is the live question of whether science is **rational and truth-directed** (Popper) or **sociological and goal-less** (Kuhn) — with stakes for how we judge any knowledge-producing enterprise. The two views even split on the [[Concepts/Problem of Induction (Hume)|problem of induction]]: Popper *dissolves* it methodologically (deductive criticism), while Kuhn suggests his "evolution-from" picture might make it "vanish."
- For the vault's realist axis, Popper sits with [[Concepts/Correspondence Theory of Truth (Russell)]] (truth as approach-able), while Kuhn drifts toward the constructivist pole of [[Concepts/Pragmatic Theory of Truth (James)]] / [[Concepts/Perspectivism (Nietzsche)]] — though Kuhn (per Hacking) resisted the relativist label and kept shared theory-choice values.

## Attempted Resolutions

- **Lakatos's research programmes** (the standard mediating position) — not yet in the vault.
- A common irenic reading: Popper is **normative** (how a rational community *should* hold its theories), Kuhn **descriptive** (how communities *do* behave); both can be partly right. But this understates the genuine clash over whether progress is toward truth and whether normal-science dogmatism is virtue or vice. **Left open** — flagged, not reconciled, per the vault's contradiction policy.
