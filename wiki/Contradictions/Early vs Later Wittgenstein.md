# Early vs Later Wittgenstein
> The rare case of a major philosopher who systematically refuted himself: the *Tractatus* (1922) and the *Philosophical Investigations* (1953) give opposed accounts of what language is and how it means.

This is the highest-value tension in the [[Thinkers/Ludwig Wittgenstein|Wittgenstein]] corpus, because it is not a disagreement *between* thinkers but one thinker's deliberate dismantling of his own earlier system. The *Investigations* explicitly says it should be read against "my old way of thinking" (Preface), and its opening pages target the very picture the *Tractatus* perfected.

---

## Position A — The *Tractatus* (early Wittgenstein)

Held in: [[Concepts/Picture Theory of Language (Wittgenstein)]], [[Sources/Tractatus Logico-Philosophicus - Ludwig Wittgenstein (1922)]].

- **One essence of language.** Beneath the surface of ordinary sentences lies a single hidden *logical form*. Every meaningful proposition is, when analysed, a truth-function of elementary propositions.
- **Meaning is reference / picturing.** A name means by standing for a simple object; a proposition has sense by being a logical *picture* of a possible state of affairs (isomorphism of structure).
- **Simple objects.** Analysis must terminate in absolutely simple, indestructible objects that are the ultimate meanings of names.
- **The limits of language.** What cannot be pictured cannot be said — ethics, the self, logic itself can only be *shown*. "Whereof one cannot speak, thereof one must be silent."
- **Philosophy clarifies once and for all**, then is transcended (throw away the ladder).

## Position B — The *Investigations* (later Wittgenstein)

Held in: [[Concepts/Language-Games (Wittgenstein)]], [[Concepts/Meaning as Use (Wittgenstein)]], [[Concepts/Family Resemblance (Wittgenstein)]], [[Concepts/Rule-Following (Wittgenstein)]], [[Concepts/Private Language Argument (Wittgenstein)]], [[Concepts/Forms of Life (Wittgenstein)]], [[Concepts/Philosophy as Therapy (Wittgenstein)]].

- **No single essence.** Language is an open multiplicity of [[Concepts/Language-Games (Wittgenstein)|language-games]] united only by [[Concepts/Family Resemblance (Wittgenstein)|family resemblance]]: "What we call 'sentence' and 'language'... is the family of structures more or less related to one another" (§108).
- **Meaning is use, not reference.** "The meaning of a word is its use in the language" (§43); names mean nothing apart from a prepared place in a practice; naming "is not yet a move in the language-game" (§49).
- **No absolute simples.** "Simple" and "composite" make sense only relative to a language-game (§47–48); the *Tractatus*'s demand for indestructible objects is a grammatical illusion.
- **The inner is public-facing.** The [[Concepts/Private Language Argument (Wittgenstein)|private language argument]] denies that meaning could rest on private inner ostension — overturning the early "limits of *my* language / my world" solipsism.
- **Philosophy is endless therapy, not final doctrine** ([[Concepts/Philosophy as Therapy (Wittgenstein)|therapeutic metaphilosophy]]): "Philosophy... leaves everything as it is" (§124).

---

## What stays constant

The break is real but not total. Both works are *anti-metaphysical* and treat many traditional problems as confusions about language rather than discoveries to be made; both deny that philosophy adds to scientific knowledge. The continuity is the conviction that the limits of sense are set by language; what changes is whether those limits are drawn by a single hidden logic (early) or by the motley of human practices (late).

## How the vault holds it

The two positions sit on either side of this page and are cross-linked from each concept. Downstream, [[Thinkers/A.J. Ayer|Ayer]] and the Vienna Circle built on Position A (a single meaning-criterion); [[Concepts/Meaning as Use (Wittgenstein)|ordinary-language philosophy]], Austin and [[Thinkers/John Searle|Searle]] built on Position B. This makes Wittgenstein the rare source upstream of *two opposed* movements — see [[Thinkers/Ludwig Wittgenstein]].

Robert Hanna (2010) characterizes the transition as a radical shift from **Tractarian transcendental solipsism** (reducing logic, language, and the world to the limits of the solipsistic mind) to an **anthropocentric metaphysics of the ordinary**. The *Investigations* fully embeds logic, language, and mind into the everyday practices of natural-language-using rational human animals in their ordinary world, redefining logic as grammar and meaning as use in action.
