# Pythagoras the Scientist vs the Religious Charismatic
> Was Pythagoras the founder of mathematical natural science, or a religious wonder-worker onto whom later ages projected a science he never possessed? The vault holds both pictures; they cannot both be right. The two most rigorous source-critical studies in the vault — **Burkert (1972)** and **Zhmud (2012)** — land on opposite sides, so the dispute is live among the experts, not merely between old and new scholarship.

## Position A — Pythagoras the Mathematician-Scientist
Held in its *traditional* form on [[Thinkers/Pythagoras]] and [[Concepts/Number as the Essence of Reality (Pythagoras)]], drawn from [[Sources/Early Greek Philosophy - John Burnet (1920)]] and [[Sources/A History of Western Philosophy - Bertrand Russell (1945)]]:
- Pythagoras discovered the numerical ratios of the musical concords and generalized them into the doctrine that "all things are numbers."
- He founded mathematics as demonstrative *theōria*, proved (or owned) the theorem that bears his name, and originated the astronomical/harmonic cosmology ("music of the spheres").
- On this view the religious brotherhood and the science form a **single** project: rigorous *mathēmata* are the highest *katharsis*, purifying the soul (Burnet, following Döring; Cornford). Russell: "what appears as Platonism is, when analysed, found to be in essence Pythagoreanism."

### Position A, modern form — Leonid Zhmud (2012)
[[Sources/Pythagoras and the Early Pythagoreans - Leonid Zhmud (2012)]] is the major *post-Burkert* defence of the scientist Pythagoras — a rigorous source-critical rehabilitation, not a return to naive tradition. Its moves:
- **Reject the "either/or."** Pythagoras was *both* a religious teacher of metempsychosis *and* a pioneer of the *mathēmata*; the early evidence shows a *sophos* "famous both for his religious teaching and for *historia* and *philosophia*." Aristotle fr. 191 captures the duality exactly: Pythagoras "first dedicated himself to mathematical sciences... but later could not refrain from the wonder-working of Pherecydes."
- **Privilege the fourth-century Peripatetics.** Aristoxenus, Dicaearchus, and **Eudemus** — who wrote the first histories of geometry, arithmetic, astronomy, and harmonics — credit Pythagoras and the early Pythagoreans with real science *more often* than the Academics do, undercutting the claim that the Academy invented the scientific Pythagoras.
- **The "myth-to-logos" growth runs the wrong way.** The *supernatural* element is *thinner* in 5th-c. testimony and *thicker* in 4th-c.-and-later writers; the legend grew over time. So a strict source-criticism deflates the *wonder-worker*, not the scientist.
- **Genuine early science is recoverable**: the numerical ratios of the concords (Xenocrates fr. 87); the first three proportions, the even/odd theory, and the theorem (Eudemus, Aristoxenus fr. 23); the discovery of incommensurability and the disc experiment by **[[Thinkers/Hippasus]]** (early–mid 5th c., *not* late); the planetary **order** (Eudemus fr. 146) and a *physical* [[Concepts/Harmony of the Spheres|harmony-of-the-spheres]] model; and a whole secular tradition of **[[Concepts/Pythagorean Medicine and the Life Sciences|medicine and life science]]** ([[Thinkers/Alcmaeon of Croton]]).
- **Against retrojection** (see Position B): there was no early "all things are number" doctrine *to* retroject, and Aristotle's anonymous "Pythagoreans" are his own polemical construct — so Burkert's "two-lines" theory (a Platonic line projecting, an Aristotelian line reporting) is "incorrect"; the One + indefinite dyad ascribed to Pythagoreans is **1st-c. BC neo-Pythagorean**, not Old Academy. (Zhmud thus splits from Position A's *number-metaphysics* even as he defends Pythagoras-the-scientist: he relocates "all is number" to a later misreading — see [[Concepts/Number as the Essence of Reality (Pythagoras)]].)
- **Not a blanket apologist.** Zhmud *concedes* the smithy/hammer legend is fiction and the tetractys/decad mysticism is late and Academic — showing his case is selective source-criticism, not credulity.

## Position B — Pythagoras the Religious Charismatic (Burkert)
Held by [[Sources/Lore and Science in Ancient Pythagoreanism - Walter Burkert (1972)]]:
- Apply source-criticism strictly (Zeller's law: the tradition grows *richer the further it lies from the events*). The **only** doctrines fixed by the earliest, hostile witnesses (Xenophanes, Aristotle, Dicaearchus) are religious: the immortal, transmigrating soul ([[Concepts/Metempsychosis (Transmigration of Souls)]]) and ritual taboo ([[Concepts/Acusmata and the Two Sects (Acusmatici and Mathematici)]]).
- The mark of Pythagoras in early evidence is the *shaman's* claim to "know more" beyond ordinary limits — **not** the scientist's inference from evidence (*tekmairesthai*).
- The number-cosmology is documented first for [[Thinkers/Philolaus]] (late 5th c.); the metaphysics of ideal numbers is the work of **Plato's Academy** (Speusippus, Xenocrates, Heraclides), read back onto Pythagoras.
- The science-bearing *mathematici* were a contested, junior strand (linked to Hippasus); Iamblichus had to *reverse* his source to make them primary.
- The "Pythagoras founded mathematics" claim, and even the attribution of the discovery of the **irrational** to him, collapse under analysis (the discovery of incommensurability is re-dated to the late fifth century).

## The Crux
The disagreement is not over isolated facts but over **historical method** — how much trust to place in late, rich testimony versus early, meagre testimony — and Burkert and Zhmud, applying the *same* source-critical discipline, reach opposite verdicts because they weight the evidence differently:
- **Direction of the legend.** Both agree the tradition changes with distance. Burkert (with Zeller): it grows *richer* — so the late science is accretion, and the early core is religion. Zhmud: what grows is the *supernatural/wonder-working* element, while a rational core is present from the earliest (hostile) witnesses — so the late accretion is the *myth*, not the science.
- **The privileged witnesses.** Burkert leans on Aristotle's "*so-called* Pythagoreans" and reads the *acusmatici/mathematici* split as the key (science = junior strand). Zhmud leans on the Peripatetic historians of science (Eudemus, Aristoxenus, Dicaearchus) and argues the acusmatici/mathematici split is itself a **late (Imperial) fiction** (Nicomachus → Iamblichus) that cannot bear that weight — see [[Concepts/Acusmata and the Two Sects (Acusmatici and Mathematici)]].
- **The number-metaphysics.** Burkert: genuine but *late/Academic*, retrojected onto Pythagoras. Zhmud: it *never existed* as an early doctrine at all (Philolaus' real principles are limit/unlimited/harmonia; "to have number" = to be *knowable*), and Aristotle's number-doctrine is his own construct — so there is nothing to retroject. They thus agree "all is number" is not Pythagoras', for *opposite* reasons.
- **A shared residue.** Notably, both now agree on two negatives: the **smithy/hammer experiment is fiction**, and the **decad/tetractys mysticism is late**. The live dispute is narrower than the headline suggests: it is over whether *genuine mathematics, harmonics, and astronomy* reach back to Pythagoras and his immediate circle (Zhmud: yes, via Hippasus and Eudemus' testimony) or begin only with the junior *mathematici* and Philolaus (Burkert).

## The Adjudicating Volume — Huffman (ed.) 2014
[[Sources/A History of Pythagoreanism - Carl A. Huffman (ed.) (2014)]], the current authoritative synthesis, frames this very dispute as the field's central problem and declines to resolve it. G.E.R. Lloyd's opening chapter judges that "the real Pythagoras is more or less totally inaccessible" and that *all* the modern category-labels (shaman, sage, mathematician, scientist) fail — a **deliberate third position** that brackets the persona question rather than choosing Burkert's or Zhmud's reconstruction. The volume's deeper move is to *disaggregate*: it largely vacates the global "scientist vs charismatic" question by handing each strand to a specialist —
- the genuine science to [[Concepts/Pythagorean Mathematics]] (Netz: a "network," with [[Thinkers/Archytas]] the hinge — and pointedly *not* called a Pythagorean by Aristotle) and [[Concepts/Pythagorean Harmonics]] (Barker: real ratio-theory, pioneered by [[Thinkers/Hippasus]]);
- the "number-doctrine" to Aristotle's own historiographical reconstruction of [[Thinkers/Philolaus]] (Primavesi), undercutting the premise that there is a single doctrine to assign;
- the religion and community to [[Concepts/Pythagorean Way of Life and Ethics]] and [[Concepts/Pythagoreanism and Orphism]] (where Gemelli Marciano defends the genuinely religious *bios* against Zhmud's deflation — a fresh front in the same war);
- and the whole "scientist" *image* to its makers in [[Schools/Neopythagoreanism]], the [[Concepts/Pseudo-Pythagorean Writings]], and [[Concepts/The Three Ancient Lives of Pythagoras]].
The net effect supports the deflationary consensus on *Pythagoras himself* (the hard mathematics is Philolaus' and Archytas') while keeping the *persona* irreducibly open.

## Status
**Genuinely contested, not settled.** Burkert's deflationary verdict became the late-20th-c. mainstream; Zhmud's 2012 study is the most substantial scholarly push-back, reopening the question on Burkert's own source-critical terms. The traditional picture also remains historically important (it is what Copernicus, Galileo, and Kepler inherited as the "rebirth of Pythagorean science"). **Flagged, not silently reconciled** — the cluster pages carry both readings, with warning callouts pointing here.
