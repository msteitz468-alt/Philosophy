# The Highest Good — Pleasure vs Virtue

> Is the highest good pleasure (the absence of pain and disturbance), or is virtue the only good, with pleasure at best indifferent?

## The Tension

All the Hellenistic schools agree the goal is [[Concepts/Eudaimonia|eudaimonia]] (flourishing) and that it issues in [[Concepts/Ataraxia|tranquility]] — but they radically disagree about its *content*. Is tranquility reached by rightly managing pleasure, or by making oneself indifferent to everything except virtue?

## Position A — Epicurus: pleasure is the highest good

- Pleasure (rightly understood as *ataraxia* — absence of bodily pain and mental disturbance — not sensual indulgence) is the beginning and end of the happy life; it is the natural measure of good.
- Virtues are instrumentally valuable because they *produce* tranquil pleasure, not good in themselves.
- Withdraw from competition and politics: [[Concepts/Live Unnoticed (Lathe Biosas)]].
- Held in: [[Thinkers/Epicurus]] (and [[Thinkers/Lucretius]]). See [[Sources/Letters and Sayings of Epicurus - Epicurus]].

## Position B — Stoics: virtue is the only good

- Only virtue is good; only vice is bad; pleasure, health, wealth, and life itself are "indifferents." Tranquility comes from this valuation plus the [[Concepts/Dichotomy of Control (Epictetus)|dichotomy of control]], not from securing pleasure.
- To make pleasure the good is, for the Stoic, to enslave the soul to externals.
- Held in: [[Thinkers/Seneca]], [[Thinkers/Epictetus]], [[Thinkers/Marcus Aurelius]]. See [[Sources/Seneca - Letters from a Stoic]].

## A Platonic Third Position

- In the *Philebus* the contest is reframed as **pleasure vs intelligence**, with Plato ranking the ordered, reasoning life above pleasure as such — see [[Concepts/Pleasure vs Intelligence as the Good (Philebus)]]. Neither Epicurean hedonism nor pure Stoic virtue, but a measured, intellect-governed mixture.

## Attempted Resolutions

- The schools were direct rivals in antiquity and the ingested material preserves the rivalry rather than resolving it. The shared *goal* (tranquility) masks an irreducible disagreement about its *source*.
