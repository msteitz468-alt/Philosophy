# Simulation Argument (Bostrom)

> Bostrom's 2003 trilemma: either civilizations never reach simulation capability, or they reach it and don't run ancestor simulations, or simulated beings vastly outnumber real ones — in which case we are probably simulated.

## Summary

As rendered by Virk (the vault's current source for the argument — Bostrom's 2003 paper itself is not yet ingested), **ancestor simulations** are detailed simulations of ancestral eras run by post-human civilizations: "One thing that later generations might do with their super-powerful computers is run detailed simulations of their forebears or of people like their forebears" (Bostrom, quoted in Virk).

**The trilemma** (Virk's formulation):
1. "A civilization never makes it to this point. Thus, ancestor simulations are not possible."
2. "A civilization makes it to this point, but ancestor simulations are banned or not allowed."
3. "A civilization makes it to this point, and it creates many ancestor simulations. This is seen as the most likely scenario, because most technologies developed by advanced civilizations end up being used."

**The statistical basis**: since creating new simulated beings only requires additional computing power, "for each posthuman civilization, the number of simulated beings will almost certainly vastly outnumber real physical beings" (A >> B). By indifference reasoning, the probability that any given experienced life — ours — is simulated approaches the ratio of simulated to real beings.

*(Note: Virk's popular rendering — "this will be more than 1!" — garbles the probability; Bostrom's actual claim is that the credence approaches A/(A+B), close to 1, not exceeding it. Flagged per never-invent rule: correction is mine, the quote is Virk's.)*

## Chalmers's Refinement (Reality+, ch. 5)

With *Reality+* ingested, the vault now has a philosophically rigorous treatment alongside Virk's popular one. Chalmers follows **Hans Moravec** (not Bostrom) as the argument's originator ("Pigs in Cyberspace," 1992) and quotes Bostrom's trilemma exactly: "(1) the human species is very likely to go extinct before reaching a 'posthuman' stage; (2) any posthuman civilization is extremely unlikely to run a significant number of simulations of their evolutionary history (or variations thereof); (3) we are almost certainly living in a computer simulation." His refinements:

- Rejects the focus on **ancestor simulations** ("I don't see good reason to believe that there will be exact ancestor simulations"), broadening to **humanlike sims**.
- Reorganizes objections into **sim blockers** ("it will never happen": conscious sims impossible, insufficient compute — answered via ~10^39 flops estimates and Lloyd's limits, extinction, choice not to simulate) vs. **sim/nonsim signs** ("we're special": living early in the universe is a sim sign; biological-only consciousness a nonsim sign).
- Preferred form: "1. If there are no sim blockers, most humanlike beings are sims. 2. If most humanlike beings are sims, we are probably sims [by an indifference principle]. 3. So: If there are no sim blockers, we are probably sims." Bostrom's trilemma omits at least five further sim blockers, yielding an eight-way conclusion.
- Numerical upshot: "the chance that we are sims is at least 25 percent or so."
- Anti-skeptical payoff: this probability defeats simplicity- and Moore-style refutations — "I conclude that we cannot know that we are not in a simulation" ([[Arguments/Cartesian Skepticism and the Simulation Riposte (Chalmers)]]).

## How Different Thinkers Use This

- **[[Thinkers/Nick Bostrom]]**: the argument is officially neutral among the three horns; Virk reads Bostrom as leaning toward the NPC version (we are simulated consciousnesses).
- **[[Thinkers/Rizwan Virk]]**: accepts the statistics but substitutes the RPG version — conscious players, simulated avatars ([[Concepts/Simulation Hypothesis (Virk)]]).
- **Elon Musk**: popularized horn 3 ("one in billions" that we're in base reality) from game-technology extrapolation ([[Concepts/Road to the Simulation Point (Virk)]]).

## Contradictions / Open Questions

- > [!warning] Horn 1 connects to Bostrom's own existential-risk work ([[Sources/Superintelligence - Nick Bostrom (2014)]]): civilizations may self-destruct before the Simulation Point — his two major arguments trade against each other.
- Whether simulated beings can be conscious at all (required for the anthropic step) is exactly the [[Contradictions/Hofstadter vs Searle - Strange Loops vs Chinese Room]] dispute.
- The indifference-principle step is contested in anthropic reasoning generally.
