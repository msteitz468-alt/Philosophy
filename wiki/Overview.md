# Overview

Welcome to **GrokVault** — your personal, LLM-maintained knowledge base.

This vault follows the "LLM Wiki" pattern: instead of simple RAG over raw files, Grok (the AI) incrementally builds and maintains a rich, interlinked collection of markdown pages here in `wiki/`. Knowledge compounds over time.

---

## How This Works

- **You** curate sources (articles, papers, notes, transcripts, images) and drop them into `raw/`.
- **Grok** reads them, extracts meaning, creates and updates structured pages across the wiki, maintains cross-references, notes contradictions, and keeps summaries current.
- **You** explore the results in Obsidian (graph view, links, Dataview queries, etc.) and ask questions or give direction.
- **Grok** files valuable answers and explorations back into the wiki so they don't disappear into chat history.

The result is a living "second brain" that gets *better* the more you use it, rather than requiring constant manual bookkeeping.

## Getting Started

1. **Add your first source**
   - Use Obsidian Web Clipper (or copy-paste / export) to save an article, paper, or note as markdown into the `raw/` folder.
   - (Recommended) After clipping, use Obsidian's "Download attachments for current file" hotkey if you configured it to pull images into `raw/assets/`.
   - Tell Grok: "Ingest the new file in raw/" or "Process the latest source".

2. **Explore & query**
   - Once content exists, ask questions in natural language. Grok will read the index + relevant pages and synthesize with citations (wikilinks).
   - Valuable outputs get saved back as wiki pages.

3. **Watch it grow**
   - Use Obsidian's graph view to see connections emerge.
   - Check the log to see exactly what Grok did.
   - Periodically ask Grok to "lint the wiki" or "health check" for gaps, orphans, and contradictions.
