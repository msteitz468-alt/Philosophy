# Sextus Empiricus

> Greek philosopher, physician, and Pyrrhonian Skeptic (fl. c. late 2nd–early 3rd century CE). Principal surviving ancient source for the "Skeptic Way." Author of the *Outlines of Pyrrhonism* (3 books, in Benson Mates trans.) and works against the Dogmatists (*Adversus Mathematicos*). Systematized Pyrrhonian practice: oppose any non-evident claim with an equipollent counter-claim to induce epoché (suspension of judgment) and ataraxia (tranquility). Explicitly frames the three philosophies of his day, using the followers of Aristotle, Epicurus, and the Stoics as paradigmatic "Dogmatists" who claim to have found the truth. The Skeptic continues to search without asserting even the skeptical slogans as dogma.

## Key Ideas from the *Outlines of Pyrrhonism*

**The Three Main Types of Philosophy** (Book I opening):

- Dogmatists (properly so called): e.g., followers of Aristotle and Epicurus, the Stoics, and certain others — "think that they have found [the truth]."
- Academics: "have asserted that it cannot be apprehended."
- Skeptics (Pyrrhonists): "continue to search."

**The Skeptic Way Defined**:

A disposition to oppose phenomena and noumena (or any statements) in any way whatever. Due to the resulting equipollence (isosthenia: equal credibility on both sides), one reaches first epoché, then ataraxia.

- **Epoché**: State of intellect in which we neither affirm nor deny (anything non-evident).
- **Ataraxia**: Untroubled, tranquil condition of the soul. Follows epoché "as a shadow follows the body" (famous Apelles painter/sponge anecdote: frustration at depicting froth leads to throwing the sponge — which produces the desired effect "by chance").

The goal re: belief is ataraxia; for unavoidable pathé (cold, thirst, etc.), moderate pathé (ordinary people suffer the feeling *plus* the extra belief that it is "by nature bad"; the Skeptic drops the second).

**Does Not Dogmatize; Reports Appearances**:

Assents involuntarily only to pathé forced by phantasia (e.g., "I seem hot"). No assent to non-evident matters investigated by the sciences. Even the famous slogans ("Nothing more," "I determine nothing," "To every argument an equal argument is opposed," etc.) are not asserted as true in nature; they are imprecise, self-applicable, or reports of current pathos "like a chronicler."

**Criterion for Action (Not for Truth of Non-Evident)**:

The appearance (phantasia). Fourfold ordinary life followed without belief: (1) nature's guidance (sensation and thought), (2) compulsion of the pathé (hunger drives to food), (3) laws and customs handed down (piety good), (4) instruction in arts/crafts. "We say all these things without belief."

**The Modes (How Epoché Is Reached)**:

- **Ten Modes** (older Skeptics, Aenesidemus): 1. Variety of animals (different phantasiai from same things — e.g., perfume pleasant to humans, intolerable to dung beetles; sea water poisonous to humans, potable to fish; the "humble" dog has sensation superior in smell, internal reasoning per Stoic Chrysippus, "dialectic," justice, and better recognition in some cases). 2. Differences among humans (body, humors, tolerances, choices, age, custom). 3. Sense organs (different senses disagree on same object; apple appears with multiple qualities or one appearing variably). 4. Circumstances (awake vs sleep, age, drunk vs sober, hungry vs sated, predispositions, fear vs courage, etc.). 5. Positions, distances, locations (tower round far/square near; oar broken in water/straight out). 6. Admixtures (nothing affects us alone). 7. Quantity/constitution. 8. Relativity (most generic). 9. Frequency/infrequence. 10. Ways of life, customs, laws, mythic beliefs, dogmatic opinions.

- **Five Modes** (later Skeptics): disagreement (unresolvable impasse), infinite regress, relativity, hypothesis (undemonstrated axiom), circularity. Any inquiry (sense or thought object) falls under one or more → epoché. Used in tandem with the ten.

Additional modes against causal explanations (Aenesidemus' eight).

**Slogans** (expressive, not dogmatic):

"Not more [this than that]," "Non-assertion" (aphasia), "Perhaps/It is possible/Maybe," "I withhold assent," "I determine nothing," "Everything is indeterminate/non-apprehensible," "To every argument an equal argument is opposed" (the core method).

**Critiques of Dogmatic "Philosophy" (Books II–III)**:

Applies the method to the criterion of truth, signs, proof, the true, god (as productive cause: no agreed conception of substance/form/location; if provident then either causes all evil or is weak/partial/malevolent — impious in any case; cannot be proved without regress/circularity), cause in general (relative to effect; circular to conceive cause before knowing effect as effect and vice versa; cannot precede, coincide, or follow the effect properly), material sources (total ancient disagreement: Thales water, Anaximenes air, Heraclitus/Hippasus fire, Democritus/Epicurus atoms, Aristotle's elements + revolving body, Pythagoreans numbers, etc.), bodies (definitions lead to problems with boundaries, touch, points/lines/surfaces), and further physical/ethical topics.

**Life and Context**:

Skepticism named Pyrrhonean after Pyrrho of Elis (c. 360–270 BCE), who applied himself vigorously to it. Sextus (also a physician, "Empiricus" likely from the Empiric medical school, which had skeptical leanings) presents the position as a way of life, not a set of doctrines. The Outlines is an "outline account" for those already familiar with the system.

## Relevance to Vault

**The explicit counter to the vault's Dogmatists**: Sextus names the followers of Aristotle, Epicurus, and the Stoics as the central examples of those who "claim to have found the truth." This provides a built-in ancient skeptical lens and counter-synthesis on precisely the philosophers most heavily represented in the classical cluster (Plato/Aristotle/Thucydides + Stoics + Epicurus + Plotinus/Neoplatonism as late synthesis). Perfect "third philosophy" addition that dialogues directly with existing pages.

**Alternative route to shared telos (ataraxia/equanimity)**: All major Hellenistic schools in the vault seek tranquility/peace of mind, but by different means:
- Epicurean: understand atomist physics, limit desires, "death is nothing," friendship, "live unnoticed."
- Stoic: dichotomy of control, proper use of impressions, play your role in divine order, endurance.
- Pyrrhonian (Sextus): refuse to resolve the fundamental anomaly of appearances vs. non-evident; suspend on all non-evident; ataraxia follows "by chance" as the reward of epoché. Moderate pathé for the unavoidable. "Live according to appearances + handed-down customs/laws without belief."

Directly enriches and contrasts [[Concepts/Ataraxia]], [[Concepts/Eudaimonia]], [[Concepts/Dichotomy of Control (Epictetus)]], [[Concepts/Epicurean Atomism (Swerve)]].

**Empiricism of appearances**: The criterion for living is the phantasia (what appears), not a full physical theory or metaphysical demonstration. "The honey appears sweet to us; we grant the appearance. Whether it is sweet [in nature] we question." Supports the vault's source-driven, evidence-based practice while cautioning against overclaiming.

**Method for wiki / agentic / personal knowledge work**:
- "Chronicler" stance: report what the sources appear to show, with wikilinks and provenance, without treating the synthesis as final truth.
- Equipollence / "to every argument an equal...": deliberate generation of counter-arguments, opposing sources, or cross-model views before integrating (cf. reasoning scaffolds, self-critique, lint for contradictions).
- The modes as practical diagnostics for when claims are underdetermined (disagreement, regress, relativity, hypothesis, circularity) — flag in "Contradictions / Open Questions" sections.
- Suspension as epistemic humility and ongoing search: "continue to search" maps to the never-finished nature of the LLM Wiki (new sources, lint, co-evolution of [[GROK]]).
- Non-assertion: keep frontmatter and claims grounded in current evidence; update when new data appears.

**Classical domain completion**: Maps the full range of ancient options on knowledge, reality, the good, and how to live. Ties to every prior source (critiques atoms, final causes, providence, Forms, power-justification rhetoric). "Continue to search" vs. the assertors or mystical unifiers.

**Personal / Career / AI parallels**: Focus on controllable appearances and customs in daily/professional life (what works in sales/risk consulting, preparation, response) vs. suspending on outcomes/luck/market "nature."
