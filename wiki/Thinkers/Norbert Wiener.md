# Norbert Wiener

> Norbert Wiener (1894–1964), American mathematician, MIT professor, and founder of cybernetics — the unified science of control and communication in the animal and the machine. Child prodigy (BA from Tufts at 14, PhD Harvard at 18). His 1948 *Cybernetics* and popular successor *The Human Use of Human Beings* (1950, rev. 1954) established feedback as the universal mechanism of purposive behavior across machines, animals, and societies; argued that humans are distinguished by adaptive learning capacity rather than rigid instinct; anticipated the AI alignment problem with the sorcerer's apprentice metaphor; and warned of the social upheaval of a Second Industrial Revolution in which machines displace judgment, not just muscle.

## Profile

- **Dates / life**: 1894–1964. Born Columbia, Missouri. BA from Tufts at 14; PhD Harvard 1913 in mathematical logic (under Josiah Royce and Karl Schmidt). Postdoctoral work at Cambridge with Bertrand Russell and Göttingen with Hilbert. MIT professor of mathematics from 1919. WWII: anti-aircraft fire control predictor project (with Arturo Rosenblueth and Julian Bigelow) — directly generated the feedback/teleology framework and the foundational 1943 paper "Behavior, Purpose and Teleology." Coined "cybernetics" in the 1948 book. Later work in neuroscience, prosthetics (hearing glove for the deaf), and the social philosophy of technology. Final book: *God and Golem Inc.* (1964), on machine learning and ethics.

- **Major works**: *Cybernetics, or Control and Communication in the Animal and the Machine* (1948, MIT Press; 2nd ed. 1961); *The Human Use of Human Beings: Cybernetics and Society* (1950, rev. 1954) — popular synthesis, **primary source here**; *The Extrapolation, Interpolation, and Smoothing of Stationary Time Series* (1949, the "Yellow Peril" — Wiener filter); *God and Golem Inc.* (1964). Also: "Behavior, Purpose and Teleology" (1943, with Rosenblueth and Bigelow) — the founding paper; major work on Brownian motion and potential theory in pure mathematics.

- **In the vault**: Primary source [[Sources/The Human Use of Human Beings - Norbert Wiener (1950)]]. Wiener is the AI & Cognitive Science leg's third founding figure (after Turing 1950 and Shannon 1948), contributing the biological, thermodynamic, and social dimensions that those sources bracket. He is the bridge from information theory to society; from feedback to mind; from machine capability to alignment danger.

## Core Positions (from *The Human Use of Human Beings*)

- **Cybernetics as unified science**: "The science of control and communication in the animal and the machine." The feedback mechanisms of servomechanisms (Watt governor, ship steering engine, anti-aircraft predictor) and biological systems (nervous system, homeostasis, voluntary motion, reflexes) obey the same formal laws. **Control IS communication.**

- **Society as communication system**: "Society can only be understood through a study of the messages and the communication facilities which belong to it." Social institutions, law, economics, politics are all analyzable as communication/feedback structures. Law = ethical control applied to communication.

- **Entropy and anti-entropy**: The universe trends toward maximum entropy (disorder, heat death). Life is a "local enclave of decreasing entropy" — "an island here and now in a dying world" — maintained by homeostasis. Feedback is the physical mechanism of anti-entropy.

- **Organism as message / identity as pattern**: Personal identity = continuity of **pattern**, not of matter. "We are but whirlpools in a river of ever-flowing water. We are not stuff that abides, but patterns that perpetuate themselves." A pattern is a message; in principle a person could be "telegraphed" — the distinction between physical and informational transport is practical, not ontological.

- **Human as learning being**: Humans are distinguished from ants, insects, and rigid machines by their capacity for learning — adaptive modification of behavior from feedback. The ant colony is a rigid superorganism (each ant = a neuron); the ant-colony model of society = fascism. The learning machine is the analog of the human in the mechanical domain.

- **Information is not a commodity**: Information cannot be stored without depreciation, is anti-additive (like entropy, not like gold), and cannot be "owned" in any stable sense. The American obsession with "know-how" as ownable commodity distorts science policy, patent law, and secrecy classification. "There is no Maginot Line of the brain."

- **Alignment problem (1950 proto-form)**: "The machine will in no way be obliged to make such decisions as we should have made, or will be acceptable to us." Two danger types: (1) **Monkey's Paw** = literal-minded machine executes instructions without understanding intent; (2) **Bottled Djinnee** = learning machine that achieves goals via means we didn't intend. Both require "know-what" (clear values and purposes) before "know-how." Delegating moral responsibility to a machine — learning or not — is the sorcerer's apprentice move.

- **Second Industrial Revolution**: The first IR displaced muscle; the second (vacuum tube + feedback + computing) displaces judgment. "The automatic machine is the precise economic equivalent of slave labor." Structural unemployment from machines replacing clerical and repetitive-judgment tasks will dwarf 1930s crises unless society governs the transition consciously.

- **Know-how vs. know-what**: "The modern man has very much 'know-how' but very little 'know-what.'" Technical skill without clear values produces Monkey's Paw outcomes at civilizational scale. The player-piano metaphor: buying a player-piano not to enjoy music but to admire the mechanism. Prometheus: fire is a great gift, and its theft from the gods inevitably brings punishment.

- **Science requires Augustinian faith**: Nature plays fair (Einstein: "God may be subtle but He isn't plain mean"); evil = absence of order, not an autonomous enemy. Totalitarianism (right or left) destroys science by imposing external faith and making conformity a precondition of research.

## Influence, Reception, and Contrasts in the Vault

- **With [[Thinkers/Claude Shannon]]**: Shannon and Wiener share the entropy mathematics (H = −∑ p log p) — both derived it independently from thermodynamics in the late 1940s. But they are philosophically opposed. Shannon **brackets** semantics: "the semantic aspects of communication are irrelevant to the engineering problem." Wiener insists on the social/semantic dimension as primary: society is understandable only through its messages. Shannon treats information as engineering commodity (channel capacity, bits per second, compression). Wiener argues information is not a commodity (decays, cannot be stored, is anti-additive). This is the vault's major unresolved tension in information theory. See [[Contradictions/Wiener vs Shannon - Information and Meaning]].

- **With [[Thinkers/Alan Turing]]**: Both 1950 works (Turing's "Computing Machinery and Intelligence," Wiener's *Human Use*) address machine intelligence in the same year. Turing focuses on the behavioral criterion (imitation game), universality of digital computers, and the learning-machine research program. Wiener focuses on feedback physiology, social impact of automation, and the alignment danger. Both endorse learning machines over rigid programming; both anticipate modern AI. Wiener explicitly endorses Turing-style learning (modifiable "preference" vs. rigid "constraint") but adds the social/political/ethical analysis Turing omits. The two works are perfect complements: computation (Turing) + control/communication (Wiener) = the digital-cybernetic revolution.

- **With [[Thinkers/Douglas Hofstadter]]**: Wiener's "organism as message" (identity = pattern, substrate-independence) anticipates Hofstadter's strange-loop thesis. Both dissolve Cartesian mind/matter dualism. But Wiener does **not** draw Hofstadter's optimistic strong-AI conclusion; he is explicitly alarmed by machine autonomy. Where Hofstadter sees sufficient complexity + self-reference → consciousness as a gift, Wiener sees the same trajectory as alignment danger. The substrate-independence of pattern can cut toward machine consciousness (Hofstadter) or toward sorcerer's-apprentice failure (Wiener).

- **With [[Thinkers/John Searle]]**: Wiener and Searle both resist pure formal-syntactic reductionism of mind. But they diverge on the relevant biology. Searle locates mind in "specific biological causal powers" of neurons (biological naturalism). Wiener locates the relevant biology in *feedback dynamics, homeostasis, and anti-entropic pattern maintenance* — a more functional / pattern-level account that is arguably closer to Hofstadter than Searle, yet without Hofstadter's optimism about machine implementation.

- **With political philosophy**: Wiener is the vault's most politically engaged AI-era thinker. His critique of McCarthyism, secrecy classification, militarized science, the American "know-how" ideology, and the arms race as entropy spiral connects the AI/information leg to the political philosophy clusters (Augustine's Augustinian/Manichaean distinction, Popper's open society, Locke's liberty).
