# Empedocles

> Greek philosopher, statesman, and poet (c. 490 – 430 BCE) from Acragas (Agrigento) in Sicily. A colorful figure who wrote in verse and was legendary for his scientific experiments and claims of divine power.

## Key Contributions

**The Four Roots (Elements)**:
- Reconciled Parmenides' denial of absolute change with the physical world of senses. He agreed that nothing can be created from nothing or destroyed.
- However, he proposed that instead of one changing primary substance, there are **four eternal, indestructible roots**: **Fire, Air, Water, and Earth**.
- Physical objects are formed by the mixing of these roots in various proportions; what we call "generation" and "death" are simply the combination and separation of these changeless roots.

**Love and Strife**:
- Postulated two active cosmic forces that move the elements:
  - **Love** (*Philia*): The attractive force that mixes the elements together into a unified whole.
  - **Strife** (*Neikos*): The repulsive force that separates the elements.
- The history of the cosmos is an endless, repeating cycle between the complete rule of Love (where elements are fused into a perfect sphere) and the complete rule of Strife (where elements are totally separated). Our current world exists in a transitional state between the two. See [[Concepts/Four Elements and Love and Strife (Empedocles)]].

**Evolution and Perception**:
- Theorized that in the early stages of the cosmic cycle, parts of animals (limbs, heads, organs) arose separately and floated around, occasionally combining into random monsters. Only those combinations that were well-suited to survive persisted (a primitive precursor to natural selection).
- Formulated the theory of sensation: **"like is known by like."** We perceive earth through the earth in ourselves, water through the water, and so on.

## Relevance to Vault

- **The Standard Chemistry Model**: Empedocles' four-element theory became the dominant chemical model of the Western world (adopted by Plato, Aristotle, and medieval science) for over two thousand years.
- **Pluralist Resolution**: Initiated the pluralist path in metaphysics—that change is a rearrangement of stable, fundamental components.
