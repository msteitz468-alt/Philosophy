# Gilles Deleuze
> French philosopher of difference, becoming, and productive desire; creator of concepts that replace Being with multiplicity and identity with transformation.

## Summary

Deleuze (1925–1995) is best characterized by three roles: philosopher (creator of concepts), "stutterer" (one who makes language begin to stammer), and thinker of "the outside" — the space where philosophy differs from itself rather than from other disciplines. His central project is to displace the primacy of identity, representation, and transcendence in Western philosophy by articulating a rigorous ontology of pure difference and pure becoming.

His career proceeds through two phases. First, a series of monographs on canonical thinkers — Hume, Nietzsche, Bergson, Spinoza, Kant, Leibniz — in which he reads each "by the middle," forcing their arguments to their "vanishing point" to extract a generative machine of problems rather than reproduce doctrines. Second, in collaboration with psychoanalyst Félix Guattari, the great constructive works: *Anti-Oedipus* (1972), *A Thousand Plateaus* (1980), and related texts, which develop schizoanalysis as an alternative to psychoanalysis, and an entire political philosophy of nomadic resistance.

## Core Philosophical Commitments

**Against Platonism.** The governing target of Deleuze's ontology is Platonism's privileging of the original over the copy, and the copy over the simulacrum. Deleuze's overturning of Platonism does not substitute a new hierarchy; it affirms the simulacrum and releases becoming from subordination to stable Forms. See [[Concepts/Rhizome vs Trees (Deleuze-Guattari)]].

**Difference and Repetition.** Being is not one and self-identical but intrinsically differential. Repetition is not the recycling of identical entities but a difference-making activity — the "selective test" (eternal return) that expels reactive forces and produces becoming-active. See [[Concepts/Becoming (Deleuze)]], [[Concepts/Eternal Return as Selective Ontology (Deleuze-Nietzsche)]].

**Event ontology (via the Stoics).** Following the Stoics, Deleuze distinguishes bodies/states-of-affairs (actual, present, causal) from incorporeal events (virtual, neither past nor future but eluding the present, quasi-causal). Ethics means being "worthy of what happens to us" — counteractualization, not passive endurance. See [[Concepts/Incorporeal Event (Deleuze-Stoics)]].

**Transcendental empiricism (via Hume).** The principle of empiricism is the externality of all relations: relations are never internal to their terms. This displaces the predicative IS with the connective AND — inclusive disjunction over categorical subsumption.

**Univocal Being (via Spinoza).** Being is said in one sense of everything that is; there are no vertical axes of participation or emanation. Ethics (typology of immanent modes of existence) replaces morality (judgment by transcendent values). See [[Concepts/Ethics Without Morality (Deleuze-Spinoza)]].

**Productive desire (with Guattari).** Desire is not lack (Freudian/Lacanian), not drive, not phantasm — it is positive, connective, machinic. It produces real by assembling a plane of immanence. Psychoanalysis systematically destroys desire by Oedipalizing its content. See [[Concepts/Desire and the Body without Organs (Deleuze-Guattari)]].

**Minor and nomadic politics.** Every domain (language, art, politics) has a major/minor distinction: the major extracts constants and enforces identity; the minor undergoes continuous variation and produces becoming. Resistance is never the capture of the State but the construction of war-machines — nomadic assemblages on lines of flight. See [[Concepts/Molar and Molecular Lines (Deleuze-Guattari)]], [[Concepts/Minor Literature and Language (Deleuze-Guattari)]].

## Key Influences and Departures

| Thinker | What Deleuze Takes | Where He Departs |
|---|---|---|
| [[Thinkers/Hume]] | Externality of relations; empiricist method | Pushes empiricism to "transcendental" — not what we have but what forces thought |
| [[Thinkers/Spinoza]] | Univocal Being; affects; immanent ethics | Radicalizes the plane of immanence; strips last residues of One-as-unity |
| [[Thinkers/Nietzsche]] | Eternal return; will to power; active/reactive forces | Reads eternal return as selective ontology, not cosmological cycle |
| [[Thinkers/Kant]] | Productive role of transcendental; Idea as problem | Replaces transcendental subject with plane of immanence; rejects categorical morality |
| [[Thinkers/Plato]] | The problem of simulacra | Overturns Platonism rather than inheriting it: affirms the simulacrum |
| [[Thinkers/Heidegger]] | Being-question; event; critique of presence | Replaces ontological difference with univocal difference; Being→becoming |

## Contradictions / Open Questions

- > [!warning] Deleuze's ontology of becoming appears to dissolve personal identity (no subject, only haecceities) — this sits in tension with his political writings, which presuppose agents capable of "choosing" lines and "drawing" war-machines. See [[Concepts/Molar and Molecular Lines (Deleuze-Guattari)]].
- > [!warning] His reading of Nietzsche's eternal return as selective ontology (expelling reactive forces) is arguably an extension rather than an exegesis — Nietzsche scholars dispute whether the cosmological reading is so easily dismissed.
