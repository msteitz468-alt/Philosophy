# Anaximander

> Milesian philosopher (c. 610 – 546 BCE), pupil and successor of Thales. Designer of the first map of the world and introducer of the gnomon to Greece.

## Key Contributions

**The Apeiron (The Boundless)**:
- Rejected Thales's water in favor of a new concept: the **Apeiron** (the Boundless, Indefinite, or Infinite) as the *arche* and primary substance.
- Argued that if any one element (like water or fire) were infinite, it would long ago have conquered and annihilated the other elements. Therefore, the primary source must be a neutral, indeterminate substance from which all opposites (hot, cold, wet, dry) separate out.
- Postulated that the Apeiron is eternal and ageless, surrounding all worlds and governing them. See [[Concepts/Apeiron (Anaximander)]].

**Cosmic Justice**:
- Surviving fragment: *"And into that from which things take their rise they must also pass away according to necessity, for they make reparation and satisfaction to one another for their injustice according to the ordering of time."*
- View of the cosmos as a moral balance: the encroachment of one opposite on another (e.g., heat in summer) is an "injustice" that must be repaid by the encroachment of its opposite (cold in winter).

**Equilibrium and Cosmology**:
- Declared that the earth floats unsupported in the center of the cosmos. It remains in place because it is equidistant from all things, so there is no reason for it to move in one direction rather than another (a proto-form of the Principle of Sufficient Reason).
- Described the earth as cylindrical (like a drum) with humans living on the flat top.

**Proto-Evolutionary Ideas**:
- Theorized that life originated in the wet slime under the heat of the sun.
- Argued that humans must have descended from other animals (specifically fish), since human infants are helpless at birth and could not have survived on their own in the primitive world.

## Relevance to Vault

- **Popper's Bold Conjecture**: Karl Popper ([[Thinkers/Karl Popper]]) famously called Anaximander's theory of the unsupported earth "one of the boldest, most revolutionary, and most portentous ideas in the whole history of human thought." It anticipated Newton's gravity and fields by showing that physical bodies can be held in place by immaterial forces/equilibrium rather than material pillars or floating on water.
- **Critical Legacy**: Anaximander's critique of Thales's water is the first historical example of scientific criticism and advancement.
