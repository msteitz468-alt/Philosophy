# Iamblichus

> Syrian Neoplatonist (from Chalcis, d. c. 326) who determined the framework of all later Greek Neoplatonism: he multiplied the hypostases into rigorous triadic hierarchies, insisted the human soul fully descends, and grounded salvation in theurgy — ritual "divine work" — rather than contemplation alone.

## Summary

Wallis presents Iamblichus as the counter-attack against [[Thinkers/Porphyry]]'s telescoping of the Plotinian system. His Syrian and Pergamene schools supplied the theology of the emperor Julian's pagan restoration (361–63), and his principles — not Plotinus's — fixed the "ever-increasing scholasticism" of the Athenian School: rigorous logical demonstration and strict adherence to the text of the classical sources.

## Key Positions (Wallis)

- **Metaphysical stratification.** Postulated an **Ineffable** beyond even the One; made **Logical Realism** systematic (logical distinctions entail ontological ones — Being prior to Life, Life prior to Intelligence); Forms as self-subsistent substances. The **Law of Mean Terms** (dissimilar extremes require an intermediary sharing both natures) justified the proliferation of hypostases and divine orders.
- **Unparticipated / Participated / Participant.** A triple scheme of participation safeguarding transcendence: the Unparticipated universal (e.g. Universal Soul apart from all bodies), its Participated immanent manifestation, and the material Participant. Inherited and systematized by [[Thinkers/Proclus]] (*ET* 23–24).
- **Principle of Correspondence.** "Everything is in everything, but in each thing appropriately to its nature" (from [[Thinkers/Numenius of Apamea]]; Proclus *ET* 103) — rigidified so that particulars contain only *logoi*, not the Forms themselves (contra Plotinus).
- **The fully descended soul.** Rejected Plotinus's unfallen soul: no part of us remains in eternal contemplation, so human effort alone cannot save — see [[Concepts/The Undescended Soul (Plotinus vs Iamblichus)]]. He also denied transmigration into animals and made Soul a genuinely separate hypostasis, neither "defective Intelligence" nor a full member of the Intelligible order. The doctrine of the *authypostatos* (self-constituted substance) defended the soul's intrinsic immortality.
- **Theurgy over theory.** In *De Mysteriis* (answering Porphyry's *Letter to Anebo*): "the vital union depends not on this [thought], but on performance of the appropriate ritual actions" (II.11). Union with the gods comes by their voluntary bestowal of power — divine grace — not by human reasoning. See [[Concepts/Theurgy (Iamblichus)]] and [[Contradictions/Plotinus vs Iamblichus - Contemplation vs Theurgy]].
- **Exegesis: the *skopos*.** Each Platonic dialogue has one dominant theme to which all its content must be related — replacing Porphyry's piecemeal, multiple-meaning approach and governing all later Neoplatonic commentary.
