# René Descartes (1596–1650)

> French philosopher, mathematician, and scientist. Author of the *Meditations on First Philosophy* (1641), *Discourse on the Method* (1637), *Principles of Philosophy* (1644), and *The Passions of the Soul* (1649). Founder of modern rationalism and foundational figure for epistemology, philosophy of mind (mind-body dualism), and the metaphysical grounding of the new mechanistic science. "I think, therefore I am" (*cogito ergo sum*) as first principle; clear and distinct perception as criterion of truth; proofs of God as guarantor of knowledge; real distinction between thinking substance (*res cogitans*) and extended substance (*res extensa*).

## Life and Context

Born in La Haye (Touraine), France, to a family of legal/administrative nobles. Educated at the Jesuit college of La Flèche (excellent but traditional Aristotelian-scholastic curriculum, which he later rejected). Law degree from Poitiers (1616); never practiced. Volunteer military service (Prince Maurice of Nassau in Netherlands; later Catholic forces in Germany). Met mathematician Isaac Beeckman (1618–19), decisive intellectual influence. Famous "stove-heated room" dreams in Neuburg (Nov 1619) associated with discovery of new method for truth. Travelled widely; settled in Dutch Republic 1628–1649 (intellectual freedom, avoided French controversies). Part of Mersenne circle (Marin Mersenne as key correspondent and organizer). Published *Discourse* (1637, French, with essays on optics/geometry/meteorology) after shelving *Le Monde* (mechanistic physics) due to Galileo's 1633 condemnation. *Meditations* (1641 Latin) with solicited Objections and Replies to demonstrate rigor and preempt criticism. Sought (and claimed) Sorbonne approval. Later works: *Principles* (textbook form), *Passions* (mind-body interaction/emotions). Invited to Sweden by Queen Christina (1649); died of pneumonia in Stockholm (1650).

Context: post-Reformation religious wars; revival of ancient skepticism (Montaigne); scientific revolution (Copernicus, Galileo, mechanistic corpuscular views); Jesuit education vs. desire for new foundations. Descartes presents his project as serving both science (certain foundations for physics) and religion (rational proofs of God and soul's immortality against unbelievers).

## Major Works

- *Discourse on the Method* (1637, French): intellectual autobiography + provisional morality + method rules + metaphysical sketch (cogito, God proofs, mind distinct from body) + essays.
- *Meditations on First Philosophy* (1641/42): the core metaphysical work (see dedicated source summary). Six Meditations + Objections/Replies.
- *Principles of Philosophy* (1644): systematic textbook version of metaphysics + physics.
- *The Passions of the Soul* (1649): mind-body union, emotions, virtue.
- Earlier unpublished: *Rules for the Direction of the Mind*, *Le Monde* (physics, suppressed).
- Correspondence (especially with Mersenne, Princess Elizabeth, etc.): invaluable for development and clarifications.

## Key Philosophical Positions (from the Meditations)

- **Method of Hyperbolic Doubt**: Systematic, universal doubt of all that can be doubted (senses, dream, even math if powerful deceiver possible) to reach bedrock. Not Pyrrhonian suspension for tranquility, but tool to demolish shaky foundations and rebuild on certainty. "Withdrawal of the mind from the senses" and prejudices.

- **Cogito**: "I am, I exist" is necessarily true whenever conceived or uttered by me (even if all else—including my body—is illusion produced by an evil demon). First indubitable truth.

- **Nature of the Self/Mind**: The 'I' is a *res cogitans*—a thinking thing. Essence: doubting, understanding, affirming, denying, willing, imagining, sensing (as modes of thought). Not the body or any subtle material "soul." Wax example shows that even bodies are known by intellect (clear and distinct idea of extension etc.), not senses.

- **Criterion of Truth**: Whatever I perceive very clearly and distinctly is true. (Initially provisional; fully secured only after proving God exists and is no deceiver.)

- **Proofs of God**:
  - Causal ("trademark"): The idea of an infinite, independent, supremely perfect, creative being has objective reality too great to be caused by finite me (or any finite thing). "At least as much reality in the cause as in the effect" (formal or eminent). Idea must be innate (trademark of the maker).
  - Ontological: Existence belongs to the essence of a supremely perfect being (inseparable, like triangle's properties or mountain/valley). Cannot conceive God without existence. Same necessity/certainty as mathematical truths.
  - Conservation = continuous creation: I depend for existence at each moment; cannot be self-caused (would possess all perfections); regress ends at God.

- **God is no deceiver**: Perfection excludes malice or cunning deception. Therefore clear and distinct perceptions are reliable; the external world (at least its mathematically knowable primary qualities) exists; memory of prior clear perceptions is trustworthy.

- **Error and Free Will**: Error is privation (will overextends beyond intellect's clear perceptions; "indifference"). Intellect limited/finite; will unbounded (image of God). Proper use of freedom: affirm only what intellect clearly and distinctly presents. Habit of suspension on unclear matters acquired by meditation.

- **Real Distinction Mind/Body + Union**: Clear and distinct ideas of mind (thinking, non-extended) and body (extended, non-thinking) → really distinct substances (God could create one without the other). Yet in the human being they are closely conjoined/fused into one entity (not pilot in ship). Explains why sensations/appetites are confused (not pure intellection) and guide the composite. Imagination requires body; pure intellection does not.

- **Essence of Material Things**: Extension (length, breadth, depth), shape, size, position, motion, number, duration. Primary qualities clearly known; secondary (colors, tastes, heat as felt) obscure/confused, do not resemble anything in bodies exactly. Mathematical truths have immutable essences (even if no instances exist).

- **Knowledge (Scientia)**: Perfect, stable knowledge of anything (including math) requires knowledge of God. Without it, even clear perceptions can later be doubted (memory of proof vs. current attention; dream hypothesis). With God as non-deceiver, true science possible.

## Influence and Reception

Enormous and contested. Founded rationalism (innate ideas, clear/distinct criterion, a priori). Sparked empiricist reactions (Locke, Berkeley, Hume on ideas, causation, self). Central to Kant ("Copernican revolution," synthetic a priori, critique of traditional proofs). Mind-body problem (interaction, union) dominated early modern philosophy; occasionalism (Malebranche), parallelism (Spinoza), pre-established harmony (Leibniz) as responses. Mechanistic physics influential (though vortices replaced by Newton). Religious controversies: accused of atheism/skepticism despite proofs and claims to serve faith; works placed on Index. "Cartesianism" in 17th–18th c. universities and salons. Objections and Replies remain model of philosophical exchange.

## Relevance to Vault

Pivotal early-modern capstone (or rupture) for the classical philosophy cluster. Engages and transforms every major prior thread while supplying the rationalist/foundationalist alternative to skepticism, empiricism, and scholasticism.

- **Vs. skepticism (Sextus, Montaigne)**: Uses doubt hyperbolically for certainty rather than epoché/ataraxia. "Continue to search" replaced by reconstruction on cogito + God. Direct counter-synthesis to vault's Pyrrhonian material.
- **Vs. Plato**: Innate ideas / recollection parallel; Forms-like immutable essences (math, God); interior turn for truth. But individual rational certainty vs. dialectic/Forms ascent; mechanistic bodies vs. teleological cosmos.
- **Vs. Aristotle**: Rejects "nothing in intellect not first in senses" (revives Augustinian/innatist epistemology); mechanistic extension vs. elements/qualities + final causes; clear/distinct vs. phronesis + habituation. Retains substance language but redefines.
- **Vs. Locke** (new primary source): the *Essay* Book I is the great empiricist reply to Cartesian innatism. Rejects the innate idea of God (trademark/causal proof), the substantial self known prior to sensation, and internal clear-and-distinct standards independent of experience. Tabula rasa / empty cabinet vs. innate characters; all ideas traced to sensation or reflection. The vault now has the explicit Lockean anti-innatist arguments grounding the Hume reaction already present.
- **Vs. Augustine**: Profound interiority ("I" as certain; memory/attention themes); God as light/truth/guarantor; restless search for certainty. But epistemic foundationalism + proofs for science vs. grace, confession, and beatific rest as telos. "Restless heart" finds rational rest in clear ideas + God; vault can contrast with Augustinian "rest in you."
- **Vs. Aquinas / scholasticism**: Proofs of God (causal + ontological) reworked from idea's reality and conservation rather than motion/essence/existence or Five Ways. "Grace perfects nature" vs. Descartes' rational reconstruction of nature/science independent of (but compatible with) faith. Hylomorphism challenged by real distinction + union. Anti-Averroist notes in vault's Aquinas pages now sit alongside Descartes' own engagement with the tradition.
- **Vs. Stoics/Epicureans/Plotinus**: Control of assent (will) central, but directed by intellect's clear perceptions (not just dichotomy or pleasure-calculation or emanative return). Mechanistic cosmos vs. divine order or swerve or One.
- **Vs. Turing / computationalism**: The *res cogitans* whose essence is thought, and the demand that the thinker *know* that it thinks (cogito), is directly challenged by Turing's 1950 mechanistic account: a physical discrete-state universal machine, programmed and educated, can produce outputs indistinguishable (under text interrogation) from a thinking thing, without a separate non-mechanical substance or guaranteed inner "feeling." The Imitation Game tests functional equivalence without privileged access to consciousness.
