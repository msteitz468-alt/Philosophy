# Ludwig Wittgenstein (1889–1951)

> Austrian-British philosopher. One of the most influential figures in 20th-century philosophy, who made foundational contributions to logic, the philosophy of language, and the philosophy of mind.

## Life and Context

Born to one of the wealthiest families in Europe in Vienna, Austria. Wittgenstein studied engineering in Berlin and Manchester before moving to Cambridge in 1911 to study mathematical logic under [[Thinkers/Bertrand Russell|Bertrand Russell]]. Russell quickly recognized him as a genius who would advance his own work.

During World War I, Wittgenstein served in the Austro-Hungarian army and wrote the manuscript of his first masterpiece, *Tractatus Logico-Philosophicus* (1922), while in the trenches and as a prisoner of war. Believing he had solved all the essential problems of philosophy, he abandoned the field for a decade, working as a schoolteacher in rural Austria, a gardener, and an architect.

He returned to Cambridge in 1929, received his PhD, and succeeded G.E. Moore as Professor of Philosophy. Over the next two decades, his views underwent a radical transformation, leading to his posthumously published second masterpiece, *Philosophical Investigations* (1953), which rejected much of the *Tractatus*. He spent his later years in reclusive writing and died in Cambridge.

---

## Major Works

- *Tractatus Logico-Philosophicus* (1922): A short, highly structured, aphoristic work presenting a logical-atomist picture of language and reality, defining the limits of what can be logically said.
- *Philosophical Investigations* (1953, posthumous): A critique of the *Tractatus* that shifts from logical representation to a pragmatic view of language as a set of social activities ("language-games") governed by rules.

---

## Key Philosophical Positions (Tractatus Era)

* **The World as Facts**: The world is not a collection of things, but of facts (*Tractatus* 1: *"The world is all that is the case"*). Facts are composed of atomic states of affairs (*Sachverhalte*), which are combinations of simple, indestructible objects.
* **Picture Theory of Language**: Propositions are logical pictures of facts. The elements of a proposition correspond to objects in the world, and their arrangement shares the same logical form as the state of affairs they represent.
* **Logical Demarcation**: Language has a strict limit. A proposition only has sense (*Sinn*) if it represents a possible state of affairs in the physical world.
* **Saying vs. Showing**: Logical form itself cannot be represented in language; it can only be shown. Similarly, the deepest matters—ethics, aesthetics, religion, and the meaning of life—cannot be spoken of. They lie outside the limits of logical language.
* **The Ladder Aphorism**: The propositions of the *Tractatus* itself are ultimately nonsensical attempts to say what can only be shown. The reader must use them as a ladder to see the world rightly, then throw the ladder away.
* **Closing Maxim**: *Tractatus* 7: *"Whereof one cannot speak, thereof one must be silent."*

## Influence and Legacy

Wittgenstein is unique in having inspired two major, opposing philosophical movements:
1. **Logical Positivism**: The Vienna Circle adopted the *Tractatus* as their bible, interpreting its verificationist tendencies and critique of metaphysics as a rejection of all non-scientific talk (though they misunderstood Wittgenstein's mystical respect for the "unsayable").
2. **Ordinary Language Philosophy**: His later work (*Investigations*) shifted Anglo-American philosophy toward the pragmatic analysis of everyday language, influencing speech act theory (Austin, Searle) and Wittgensteinian critiques of mentalism.

## Relevance to Vault

Wittgenstein anchors the linguistic and logical limits of representation:
- **Logical Positivism Link**: Sits directly upstream of the logical positivism of [[Thinkers/A.J. Ayer|A.J. Ayer]]. Ayer's verification principle is a direct adaptation of the Tractarian limit of sense.
- **AI and Symbolic Representation**: The Picture Theory of Language is the purest philosophical formulation of symbolic representation (mapping symbols to objects via isomorphism), which is the foundation of classical GOFAI (Good Old-Fashioned AI) and formal databases.
- **Tension with Emergent Meaning**: Can be contrasted with [[Thinkers/Douglas Hofstadter|Hofstadter]]'s view of self-referential systems: where Wittgenstein seeks to enforce strict limits to prevent nonsense, Hofstadter shows how self-referential loops generate meaning and consciousness.
