# Plato

> Athenian philosopher-dramatist (c.427 – 347 BCE), student of Socrates, founder of the Academy, author of ~25 surviving dialogues that define Western philosophy. Author of Timaeus (cosmogony) and Critias (Atlantis).

## Life and Context (from Timaeus & Critias intro)
- Born into wealthy prominent family during Athens' conflict with Peloponnesian states (431–404 BCE).
- Deeply affected by the trial and execution of Socrates (469–399 BCE) on charges of irreligion and corrupting the young.
- Turned from politics to philosophy; founded the Academy (early university model) in Athens for philosophical/mathematical enquiry and training "philosopher-kings".
- Visited southern Italy/Sicily ~age 40, influenced by Pythagoreans.
- Aristotle (384–322) studied at the Academy for the last 20 years of Plato's life.
- Earliest Western philosopher with complete works preserved.

## Works in the Vault (as of 2026-06-03)

The vault now contains summaries or excerpts from the following Platonic dialogues, spanning most of his career:

**Cosmology / Late Period**:
- *Timaeus* — teleological cosmogony; demiurge imposes order on chaos; geometric elements; world-soul; time as moving image of eternity; human body as microcosm. [[Sources/Timaeus and Critias - Plato (Oxford, 2008)]]
- *Critias* — Atlantis: concentric island empire, bull rituals, hubris, cataclysm (sole ancient source). Unfinished. [[Sources/Timaeus and Critias - Plato (Oxford, 2008)]]

**Political Philosophy**:
- *Republic* — ideal state; philosopher-kings; tripartite soul (reason/spirit/appetite); Forms; Allegory of the Cave; regime decay. [[Sources/The Republic - Plato (Cambridge, 2000)]]
- *Laws* (Book I, Pangle trans.) — law's true aim is virtue, not war; "victory over oneself is the highest good"; education as formation of desire; soul governed by pleasure, pain, and reason. Plato's last and longest dialogue.
- *Gorgias* — rhetoric vs. true philosophy; justice vs. power; Socrates refuses to flatter; critique of sophistry and "might is right."

**Ethics / Epistemology**:
- *Meno* — can virtue be taught? Recollection (*anamnesis*); the slave-boy geometry proof; hypothesis method; true belief vs. knowledge.
- *Phaedo* — soul immortality; Theory of Forms; philosophy as preparation for death; Socrates' final hours.
- *Protagoras* — teachability of virtue debate; Socratic intellectualism (all wrongdoing from ignorance); hedonism critique; virtue as measurement of pleasure.
- *Philebus* — pleasure (*hedone*) vs. intelligence (*phronesis*) as the good; indeterminate/determinant/mixture/cause ontology; intelligence as cause of good life.
- *Theaetetus* — what is knowledge? Perception, true belief, true belief + account; critiques of relativism.

**Early Socratic**:
- *Euthyphro* — definition of piety; dilemma (gods love the holy because it's holy, or it's holy because they love it?).
- *Apology* — Socrates' defense; the examined life; "the unexamined life is not worth living."
- *Crito* — civil obligation; Socrates refuses to escape; implicit contract with the laws.

**Metaphysics**:
- *Parmenides* — self-critique of the Theory of Forms; participation; the One and the Many.

**Other**:
- *Ion* — poetry as divine inspiration, not knowledge.
- *Hippias Major/Minor, Laches* — definition attempts (beauty, courage); elenchus as method.
- *Clitophon* — short; possibly a critique of Socratic method's incompleteness.

## Key Ideas Highlighted by Timaeus/Critias
- **Teleology & Demiurge**: First thoroughgoing teleological account of nature. A good craftsman-god (demiurge) imposes mathematical/geometric order on pre-existing chaos/receptacle because "order is in all ways better than chaos." Cosmos as living intelligent being made in likeness of eternal model.
- **Elements & Mathematics**: Earth, air, fire, water built from two kinds of right triangles; regular motions; Platonic solids implied.
- **World-Soul, Time, Reincarnation**: Universe ensouled; time as moving image of eternity; human souls share ingredients with world-soul; bodies as vehicles; reincarnation for the disordered.
- **Likely Account**: Epistemic humility — accounts of the generated world are "likely stories," not certain.
- **Atlantis**: Sole ancient literary source for the myth of advanced island empire beyond Gibraltar that sank after hubris/war with ancient virtuous Athens (~9,000 years before Solon). Detailed geography, concentric city, bull rituals, then abrupt end of text.
- **Link to Republic**: Dramatic framing — the "ideal citizens" of yesterday's conversation (echoing Republic) are given historical embodiment in the ancient Athenians of the tale.

## Influence
- Academy → Neoplatonism.
- Shaped Stoicism, early Christian theology (demiurge → creator; teleology in natural theology).
- Renaissance Neoplatonism took Timaeus as key text.
- Aristotle: influenced by but critical of the teleology and cosmology.
- Enduring cultural impact: Atlantis legend (no independent ancient sources), "likely story," mathematics/geometry as language of nature, aspiration to cosmic order.
