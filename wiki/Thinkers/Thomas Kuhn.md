# Thomas Kuhn

> Thomas Samuel Kuhn (1922–1996), American historian and philosopher of science. Author of *The Structure of Scientific Revolutions* (1962), the most cited work in 20th-century philosophy of science. Against both the logical positivists' cumulative-verification image and Popper's permanent-revolution-by-falsification image, Kuhn described science as alternating between long periods of **normal science** (puzzle-solving under a shared **paradigm**) and rare, discontinuous **scientific revolutions** (**paradigm shifts**) across which rival frameworks are **incommensurable** and progress is "evolution *from*," not "evolution *toward*," a fixed truth.

## Profile

- **Dates / life**: Born Cincinnati 1922; PhD in **physics** from Harvard (1949). Converted to history of science while preparing a science course for non-scientists (the famous "Aristotle experience" — realizing Aristotle's physics was coherent on its own terms, not just failed Newton). Taught at Harvard, **Berkeley** (where *Structure* was written, amid conversations with Stanley Cavell and Paul Feyerabend), Princeton, and **MIT** (Laurence Rockefeller Professor of philosophy and linguistics). Died 1996.
- **Major works**: *The Copernican Revolution* (1957); ***The Structure of Scientific Revolutions*** (1962; 2nd ed. 1970 with the Postscript); *The Essential Tension* (1977, essays — incl. "Logic of Discovery or Psychology of Research?", his direct reply to Popper, and "Second Thoughts on Paradigms"); *Black-Body Theory and the Quantum Discontinuity, 1894–1912* (1978). Late work (largely unpublished at his death) pursued incommensurability via a theory of scientific *language*.
- **In the vault**: Primary source [[Sources/The Structure of Scientific Revolutions - Kuhn (Chicago, 4th ed.)]] (Chicago 4th ed., with Ian Hacking's introductory essay and the Postscript—1969). The second pillar of the Philosophy of Science leg, deliberately ingested after the Popper pair to set up the major contrast.

## Core Positions (from *The Structure of Scientific Revolutions*)

- **Paradigm**: a concrete scientific achievement that for a time gives a community its model problems, methods, and standards — "universally recognized scientific achievements that for a time provide model problems and solutions to a community of practitioners." Later split (Postscript) into the broad **disciplinary matrix** (symbolic generalizations, models, values, exemplars) and the narrow, central **exemplar** (shared worked problem-solutions). → [[Concepts/Paradigm (Kuhn)]]

- **Normal science as puzzle-solving**: most science is conservative articulation of the reigning paradigm; "how little [its problems] aim to produce major novelties." The paradigm is *presupposed*, not tested (the scientist is a chess player trying moves within the rules). → [[Concepts/Normal Science and Puzzle-Solving (Kuhn)]]

- **Anomaly and crisis**: discovery begins when "something goes awry"; but anomalies are not bare refutations — they must be *recognized* as significant, and only an accumulation of intractable ones produces a **crisis** (extraordinary research, debate over fundamentals). → [[Concepts/Anomaly and Crisis (Kuhn)]]

- **Scientific revolution / paradigm shift**: crisis resolves only when a new paradigm replaces the old. "To reject one paradigm without simultaneously substituting another is to reject science itself." The shift is a **gestalt switch / conversion**, "all at once," not compelled by neutral logic; afterward scientists "work in a different world," and textbooks rewrite history to hide the discontinuity (**invisibility of revolutions**). → [[Concepts/Scientific Revolutions and Paradigm Shifts (Kuhn)]]

- **Incommensurability**: across the revolutionary divide the meanings of key terms change, rivals disagree about which problems and standards count, and communication is "inevitably partial"; paradigm choice rests on persuasion, future promise, shared values (accuracy, consistency, scope, simplicity, fruitfulness), and even aesthetics — never on proof. → [[Concepts/Incommensurability (Kuhn)]]

- **Progress without a goal**: science is cumulative *within* normal science but not *across* revolutions (problems and solutions can be lost). Progress is **evolution *from* primitive beginnings** by selection of the fittest practice — like Darwinian natural selection — "toward no goal," with no "permanent fixed scientific truth" that each stage approximates.

- **Theory-ladenness**: there is "no scientifically or empirically neutral system of language or concepts"; observation is shaped by the paradigm. (Shared with Popper/Hanson; opposed to positivist "protocol sentences.")

## Influence, Reception, and Contrasts in the Vault

- **Vs. [[Thinkers/Karl Popper]]** (the headline contrast): Kuhn replaces Popper's permanent critical revolution with mostly-dogmatic normal science punctuated by rare revolutions; denies bare falsification ("I doubt that [falsifying experiences] exist"); makes the scientist's stubborn faith in the paradigm a *functional virtue*; and rejects approach-to-truth (verisimilitude). Popperians replied that Kuhn's normal science is uncritical "puzzle-solving" unworthy of the name and risks relativism. See [[Contradictions/Popper vs Kuhn - Falsification vs Paradigms]].
- **Vs. logical positivism / Vienna Circle**: usually credited with "postpositivism," though Hacking notes Kuhn retained many positivist presuppositions (his late linguistic turn ~ "the logical syntax of the language of science").

- **With Quine**: Strong affinities with Quine's holism and critique of the dogmas. Quine's web of belief and underdetermination prefigure (and help explain) the non-cumulative, meaning-shifting character of revolutions and the role of pragmatic values in paradigm choice. Kuhn's "scientists work in a different world" and theory-laden observation have clear Quinean roots in the failure of reductionism and the analytic/synthetic distinction. Differences of emphasis: Quine more logical/pragmatist/naturalist; Kuhn historical/sociological. See [[Sources/Two Dogmas of Empiricism - W.V.O. Quine (1951)]] and [[Thinkers/W.V.O. Quine]].
- **[[Thinkers/Paul Feyerabend]]** (Berkeley colleague; now primary-sourced via [[Sources/Against Method - Paul Feyerabend]]): co-originated incommensurability and radicalized it into "anything goes," but rejected Kuhn's *normal science* as one more law-and-order myth and criticized the *uniformity* of Kuhn's paradigms. **Lakatos** (not yet in the vault): "methodology of scientific research programmes" tried to reconcile Popper's rationalism with Kuhn's history. See [[Contradictions/Popper vs Kuhn - Falsification vs Paradigms]].
- **Darwin / [[Concepts/Problem of Induction (Hume)]]**: Kuhn's anti-teleological "evolution-from" is explicitly modeled on Darwinian natural selection and is offered as a possible dissolution of the induction problem.
- **Nietzsche / James**: Kuhn's "different worlds" and conversion language sit near [[Concepts/Perspectivism (Nietzsche)]] and the constructivist pole of [[Concepts/Pragmatic Theory of Truth (James)]], but Kuhn (per Hacking) was "a fact lover and a truth seeker," resisting the relativist reading.
- **Ian Hacking** (*Representing and Intervening*, 1983; also author of the introductory essay to the ingested Chicago 4th ed.): accepts the force of incommensurability as a problem for representational realism and theory-comparison, but uses it to pivot the realism question to experimental intervention and entity use ("if you can spray them..."). Hacking's book is now primary-sourced; see [[Sources/Representing and Intervening - Ian Hacking (1983)]], [[Thinkers/Ian Hacking]], and [[Concepts/Incommensurability (Kuhn)]] (Hacking subsection).
- **Aristotle**: the term **paradigm** descends from Aristotle's *paradeigma* (argument by exemplar) in the [[Sources/Aristotle - Rhetoric (Book I)|Rhetoric]].

## Relevance to the Vault and Meta

- **Wiki as paradigm-governed practice**: the SCHEMA functions as a paradigm; most ingests are *normal science* (puzzle-solving that articulates the existing structure). A genuinely anomalous source can trigger a needed **paradigm shift** (restructuring the schema) that routine lint will not produce. Anomalies/contradictions should be surfaced, not smoothed — but, per Kuhn, not treated as instant refutations either.
- **AI/agentic**: incremental articulation vs. genuine reframing; theory-laden perception (an agent sees what its frame expects, hiding anomalies); **exemplars over explicit rules** (few-shot worked examples shape practice — a Kuhnian point for prompting/skill design).
- **Career/CPCU**: received market/underwriting playbooks are paradigms that work until anomalies accumulate; edge comes from recognizing the crisis and switching frames early.
