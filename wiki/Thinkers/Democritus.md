# Democritus

> Greek philosopher (c. 460 – 370 BCE) from Abdera in Thrace. Co-founder of ancient atomism alongside his teacher Leucippus. Known as the "Laughing Philosopher" due to his cheerful ethics.

## Key Contributions

**Ancient Atomism**:
- Developed a complete materialist system explaining the universe through **atoms** and **the void**.
- Asserted that the physical world consists of indivisible, solid, indestructible particles (atoms) moving through empty space (the void). See [[Concepts/Atomism - Atoms and Void (Leucippus and Democritus)]].
- Reconciled Eleatic monism with sensation by proposing that void (Non-Being) is a physical reality, allowing atoms to move and rearrange.

**Quantitative Materialism**:
- Held that atoms have no qualitative properties (no color, taste, temperature, or smell). 
- All sensory qualities are emergent properties created by the quantitative arrangements of atoms (their shape, size, position, and order).
- Famous remark: *"By convention sweet, by convention bitter, by convention hot, by convention cold, by convention color; but in reality atoms and void."*

**Mechanistic Determinism**:
- Asserted that all changes in the universe occur by necessity. Atoms collide, entangle, and separate according to purely mechanical laws.
- Rejected any concept of cosmic purpose, divine design, or teleology (contra Plato and Aristotle).

**Cheerful Ethics (Euthymia)**:
- Proposed the goal of life to be **cheerfulness** (*euthymia* or peace of mind), defined as a state in which the soul lives calmly and steadily, undisturbed by fear, superstition, or passion.
- Encouraged moderation, self-discipline, and intellectual pursuits to maintain this balance.

## Relevance to Vault

- **The Materialist Foundation**: Democritus is the direct founder of the materialist thread in Western philosophy. His system was adopted and modified by Epicurus and Lucretius, forming the main classical counterweight to Platonic-Aristotelian teleology. See [[Contradictions/Atomist Materialism vs Platonic-Aristotelian Teleology]].
- **Anticipation of Modern Science**: Amazingly anticipated modern atomic chemistry, physical determinism, and the distinction between primary qualities (shape, size) and secondary qualities (color, taste) formalized by Locke and Galileo.
