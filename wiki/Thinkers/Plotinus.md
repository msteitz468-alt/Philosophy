# Plotinus

> Greek philosopher (c. 204–270 CE), founder of Neoplatonism. Born in Egypt (or Alexandria), studied under Ammonius Saccas in Alexandria, taught in Rome from ~244 CE. His student Porphyry edited his treatises (written late in life) into the *Enneads* (6 groups of 9 = 54 treatises). Synthesized Plato's Forms and soul with Aristotelian Intellect and Stoic elements into a systematic, hierarchical metaphysics of emanation from the transcendent One, with the soul's ascent (return) through contemplation as the path to union (henosis) and true happiness. Deeply influenced later Western (Christian, Islamic, Renaissance) and esoteric thought. The Enneads cover ethics/psychology (I), physics/cosmology (II-III), and metaphysics/theology (IV-VI).

## Key Contributions (from Enneads title + classical context)

**Metaphysics — The Three Primary Hypostases (primarily Ennead V)**:
- **The One (To Hen / The Good)**: The ultimate, transcendent, ineffable source of all reality. Beyond being, intellect, description, or division. It "overflows" or emanates without diminishing itself or acting by will/intention (not a creator god). It is the Good itself; all things desire it.
- **Intellect (Nous)**: The second hypostasis. Perfect unity-in-multiplicity; the realm of the Platonic Forms/Ideas, which are its thoughts. Eternal, self-thinking thought that thinks the One (and itself). Bridge between the One and multiplicity.
- **Soul (Psyche)**: The third hypostasis. Mediates the intelligible and sensible worlds. Includes the World Soul and individual souls. Descends into bodies/matter (causing multiplicity, change, potential forgetfulness) but retains an innate memory of and orientation toward the higher. Capable of purification and ascent.

**Emanation (Proodos) and the Hierarchy of Reality**:
- Reality unfolds in a necessary, non-temporal "procession" or overflow: One → Intellect (Nous) → Soul → Nature/Matter (the sensible, material world as lowest image/reflection, privation or indeterminate substrate).
- Not creation ex nihilo, not by will or demiurge in Plato's sense, but spontaneous, undiminished giving from the prior principle. Each level is an image of the prior; matter is the last, furthest from unity (source of evil as privation, not positive force).
- Cosmos is a living, ensouled whole; time and eternity distinguished (Intellect eternal, Soul temporal).

**Return (Epistrophe), Virtue, and Contemplation**:
- The natural movement of all (especially Soul) is back toward the One — reversion, ascent, or "flight of the alone to the Alone."
- Path: purification (catharsis) from body/passions, virtue as "likeness to God" (assimilation), dialectic/philosophy, and ultimately mystical contemplation (theoria) leading to henosis (union).
- Ethics: true eudaimonia/happiness is the life of the intellect/contemplation, not action or externals. Virtue is both civic (lower) and purificatory (higher, separating soul from body). The philosopher's life is preparation for death (echoes Plato Phaedo).
- Beauty as a ladder: sensible beauty leads to intelligible, then to the One/Good.

**Other Themes (Enneads structure)**:
- Ennead I: ethical/psychological (virtue, happiness, the soul's nature and descent).
- Ennead II-III: physical/cosmological (providence, stars, time/eternity, nature of the world, matter).
- Ennead IV-VI: soul, Intellect, the One, beauty, the Good, freedom, the nature of evil (as privation).
- Critique of Gnostics (matter not evil by a malevolent creator); engagement with Aristotle (unmoved mover reinterpreted as Intellect, not highest); Stoic immanence vs. Plotinus' transcendence.

**Life and Reception**:
- Taught in Rome; circle included senators, women, and serious seekers. Lived ascetically, refused to sit for portrait (body as image). Died ~270 CE in Campania.
- Porphyry's edition/arrangement (and later Iamblichus, Proclus developments) shaped "Neoplatonism." Enormous influence: Augustine (via Plotinus on evil as privation, ascent/return, God as beyond being; explicit "conversion to Neo-Platonism" in *Confessions* Book VII as intellectual liberation from materialism — the "Platonists" taught him to conceive immaterial reality and the One, enabling his later Christian resolution; the garden ecstasy is real but insufficient without the Mediator and transforming grace), Islamic philosophy (al-Farabi, Avicenna, the One/Intellect/Soul), Christian mysticism (Pseudo-Dionysius, Eriugena), Renaissance (Ficino, Pico), Romantics, and modern idealism/esotericism. Often seen as the culmination of Greek philosophy ("Plato redivivus").

## From Wallis's *Neoplatonism* (1972)

Wallis's scholarly survey sharpens and partly corrects the picture above:

- **Life**: all factual information comes from Porphyry's reticent first-hand biography. Plotinus (204/5–270) studied under Ammonius Saccas for eleven years from age 28, taught orally in Rome for ten years before writing; Porphyry's thematic (not chronological) arrangement into six Enneads should not be over-read — most treatises span several topics. Porphyry reports Plotinus attained mystical union four times in their six years together (*V. Pl.* 23).
- **Consciousness innovation**: Plotinus's decisive move was identifying metaphysical realities with states of consciousness — the Hypostases are simultaneously objective beings and levels of inner awareness, the duality driving later Neoplatonism's deepest tensions.
- **Emanation mechanics**: "good diffuses itself"; perfect beings generate an external image of their internal activity (Enn. V.1.6, V.3.12); the One "creates without inclination, will or movement." Emanation has two stages — formless procession, then reversion in contemplation whereby the product receives form — later generalized as [[Concepts/Procession, Remaining, and Return (Neoplatonic Triad)]].
- **The undescended soul**: a level of every soul remains eternally in contemplation of Intelligence (IV.8.8) — see [[Concepts/The Undescended Soul (Plotinus vs Iamblichus)]]; the descent is both biological necessity (Universal Logos) and irrational audacity (*tolma*). Rejected by [[Thinkers/Iamblichus]] — the anthropological root of [[Contradictions/Plotinus vs Iamblichus - Contemplation vs Theurgy]].
- **Sympathy and magic**: cosmic sympathy (the world as one living organism, II.3.7, IV.4.32) explains astrology, prayer, and magic, but touches only body and lower soul; the philosopher transcends it by contemplation — the ground of his (and Porphyry's) resistance to [[Concepts/Theurgy (Iamblichus)]].
- **Problems bequeathed** (Wallis pp. 90–93): matter's status if divinely produced; how the One produces what it does not contain; the One's knowability; the internal order of the Intelligible world (Being–Life–Intelligence left in conflicting alternatives); the source of the soul's irrational impulse; whether the undescended soul collapses the Soul/Intelligence distinction. Wallis's thesis: later Neoplatonic metaphysics is "in many ways merely a series of footnotes to the Enneads," working out this list.
- **Successors**: [[Thinkers/Porphyry]] "telescoped" the Hypostases; [[Thinkers/Iamblichus]] stratified them and turned to theurgy; the Athenian School ([[Thinkers/Proclus]]) systematized — see [[Schools/Neoplatonism]].

## Relevance to Vault

This adds **Neoplatonism** as the capstone/synthesis to the classical philosophy domain (Plato idealism/teleology + Aristotle logic/ethics/rhetoric + Stoic practical ethics + Epicurus materialist empiricism + Thucydides power realism). Plotinus provides a dynamic, hierarchical, mystical metaphysics that "returns" to unity:

- **Metaphysics & Cosmology**: The One/Intellect/Soul + emanation deepens Plato's Forms/demiurge (Timaeus) and Aristotle's Intellect/unmoved mover while contrasting Epicurus' blind atomism (no hierarchy, chance + swerve) and Stoic immanent pneuma/logos (One is transcendent, not in the world). Matter as privation (not positive evil) offers a middle path. Multiple "levels" of reality model layered understanding (sensible vs. intelligible).
- **Ethics & the Soul**: Ascent to the One via contemplation/virtue as "likeness to the divine" parallels (and transcends) Aristotelian eudaimonia (activity of soul in virtue), Stoic equanimity/dichotomy (focus on what is "up to us" as preparation), Epicurean ataraxia (tranquility via withdrawal vs. Plotinus' active return/union). "Examined life" becomes the soul's journey home. Contemplation over mere action resonates with personal development and "higher goods" in career (e.g., Mack Steitz prioritizing professional excellence/virtue over externals in promotion strategy).
- **Epistemology & Method**: Dialectic + contemplation as path beyond senses (contra Epicurean strict empiricism). Recollection of the higher (echoes Plato Meno/Phaedo) + purification. The "return" as integration of scattered knowledge into unified vision.
