# Thales

> Athenian/Milesian philosopher and scientist (c. 624 – 546 BCE), recognized by Aristotle and the subsequent tradition as the first Western philosopher. Founder of the Milesian (Ionian) school.

## Key Contributions

**Water as Arche**:
- Declared **water** to be the *arche* (first principle or primary substance) of all things. 
- He reasoned that all nourishment is moist, seeds are moist, and when things die they dry up. Thus, water is the underlying substance that remains constant through changes of state.
- Asserted that the earth floats upon water like a log (explaining earthquakes as tremors in the cosmic ocean).

**All Things are Full of Gods**:
- Famously remarked that "all things are full of gods" and that "the magnet has a soul because it moves iron." 
- In Burnet's reading, this is not a return to mythology, but an early form of *hylozoism* (the belief that matter is alive and active, possessing inherent motive force) rather than requiring external divine intervention.

**Scientific and Mathematical Achievements**:
- Predicted the solar eclipse of May 28, 585 BCE, which stopped a battle between the Medes and Lydians (using Babylonian eclipse records).
- Introduced geometry to Greece from Egypt; credited with five geometric theorems (e.g., that an angle inscribed in a semicircle is a right angle—Thales's theorem).

## Relevance to Vault

- **The Critical Tradition**: Karl Popper ([[Thinkers/Karl Popper]]) identifies Thales as the initiator of the critical tradition: he did not demand dogmatic belief from his students, but rather put forward a bold hypothesis (all is water) and invited them to improve upon it.
- **The First Naturalist**: Shifted the search for explanations from the whim of the gods to the behavior of natural substances.
