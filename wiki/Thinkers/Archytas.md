# Archytas of Tarentum
> Pythagorean mathematician, harmonic theorist, and statesman of Tarentum (c. 435–360 BC); the friend of Plato and "the first and only Pythagorean who can be credibly associated with significant technical achievements in mathematics." The last great figure of the early school before it died out.

## Summary
On Malcolm Schofield's chapter in [[Sources/A History of Pythagoreanism - Carl A. Huffman (ed.) (2014)]], Archytas is the pivot where Pythagorean *mathēmata* become demonstrably first-rate mathematics. Unlike the unrecoverable "Pythagoras the mathematician," Archytas leaves genuine fragments and a securely attested technical achievement. He was simultaneously a working scientist and the dominant politician of Tarentum — elected *stratēgos* seven years running (the law normally forbidding re-election was waived for him) — making him the concrete proof that the early school fused the contemplative and the political life. His friendship with [[Thinkers/Plato]] (whom he reportedly helped rescue from Dionysius II of Syracuse) is the most important single channel by which Pythagorean mathematics entered the Academy.

## Genuine Achievements
- **The Delian problem (duplication of the cube).** Archytas solved the problem of finding two mean proportionals between two lines via a remarkable three-dimensional construction (the intersection of a cylinder, cone, and torus) — a tour de force of solid geometry preserved by Eudemus through Eutocius.
- **The "sibling sciences" (fr. B1, *On Mathematics*).** Archytas names the kindred mathematical sciences — "these sciences seem to be siblings" — an early statement of what becomes the **quadrivium** (arithmetic, geometry, astronomy, harmonics/music). This is the ancestor of the medieval four-fold mathematical curriculum (see the reception chapters).
- **The three means (fr. B2, *On Music*).** He defines the arithmetic, geometric, and "subcontrary" (harmonic) means — foundational to [[Concepts/Harmony of the Spheres|harmonic theory]]. The fragments' archaic style and the telling confusion of *pitch* with *loudness/volume* mark them as genuinely pre-Platonic.
- **Calculation as civic concord (fr. B3).** "Once calculation (*logismos*) was discovered, it caused civic conflict to cease and increased concord" — Archytas grounds political harmony in mathematical proportion, fusing his two careers.

## Archytas and Plato
Schofield reads Plato's *Timaeus* as "as much contrast as similarity" with Archytas — an *implicit foil*. Plato borrows the harmonic means for the construction of the World-Soul, but in doing so converts Archytas' empirical harmonics into cosmology and metaphysics. The famous Eudemus argument for an infinite universe (stand at the edge of the heavens and stretch out a staff — can you, or not?) is credited to Archytas and shows he genuinely philosophized, not merely calculated.

## What Is Spurious
Schofield is firmly source-critical (and somewhat more skeptical than Huffman's own monograph):
- **Most works under Archytas' name are pseudonymous.** *On the Whole System (On Categories)* is a forgery of roughly the late first century BC, exploiting the Imperial-era revival of Aristotle's *Categories*; *On Law and Justice* is "a mish-mash of mostly Platonic, Aristotelian... motifs" — see [[Concepts/Pseudo-Pythagorean Writings]].
- The attributions making Archytas the **founder of mechanics and optics**, and the stories of the self-propelled wooden **flying dove** and the children's **clapper (*platagē*)**, Schofield regards as doubtful.
