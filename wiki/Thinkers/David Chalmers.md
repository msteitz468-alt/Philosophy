# David Chalmers
> Australian philosopher of mind (b. 1966), coiner of the "hard problem of consciousness" and co-author of the extended-mind thesis, whose *Reality+* (2022) argues that virtual reality is genuine reality.

## Summary

Chalmers entered the vault indirectly — as the co-author of [[Concepts/Extended Mind (Clark and Chalmers)]] (with [[Thinkers/Andy Clark]], 1998) and as the named target of the deflationists' "hard problem" polemics ([[Concepts/Predictive Processing and the Hard Problem (Clark)]], [[Contradictions/Dennett vs Nagel - Multiple Drafts vs Subjective Experience]]). With the ingest of *Reality+* he becomes a primary voice. By his own account he began in mathematics, physics, and computer science, turned to philosophy "to go deeper," and spent most of his career on consciousness before virtual reality gave him "a new perspective" on Descartes's problem of the external world.

*Reality+* is a project in what he calls **technophilosophy**: "(1) asking philosophical questions about technology and (2) using technology to help answer traditional philosophical questions" — the name modeled on Patricia Churchland's *Neurophilosophy* (1987). Its central thesis: **"Virtual reality is genuine reality"** — virtual worlds are not illusions ([[Concepts/Simulation Realism (Chalmers)]]), virtual lives can be fully meaningful, and we cannot rule out being in a virtual world now ([[Concepts/Simulation Hypothesis (Chalmers)]]).

With the ingest of *The Conscious Mind* (1996), the consciousness work that made his name is now primary-source material. The book argues that phenomenal consciousness fails to supervene logically on the physical ([[Arguments/Zombie Argument (Chalmers)]], [[Concepts/Logical Supervenience and Reductive Explanation (Chalmers)]]), so materialism is false and [[Concepts/Naturalistic Dualism (Chalmers)]] — property dualism plus fundamental psychophysical laws — takes its place. The constructive side: [[Concepts/Coherence Between Consciousness and Awareness (Chalmers)]], [[Concepts/Organizational Invariance and the Qualia Arguments (Chalmers)]], and the speculative [[Concepts/Double-Aspect Theory of Information (Chalmers)]]. He honestly prices in the [[Concepts/Paradox of Phenomenal Judgment (Chalmers)]], defends machine consciousness against Searle ([[Concepts/Strong AI and Computational Sufficiency (Chalmers)]]), and closes with an Everett reading of QM ([[Concepts/Consciousness and the Everett Interpretation (Chalmers)]]). A self-described "temperamental materialist" driven to dualism: "If this is dualism, then we should learn to love dualism."

## Key Positions

- **Anti-skeptical strategy**: accept the Cartesian skeptic's premise that we can't know we're not simulated, deny that this undermines knowledge — because simulated objects are real, not illusory ([[Concepts/Cartesian Skepticism and the Simulation Riposte (Chalmers)]], [[Concepts/Simulation Realism (Chalmers)]]).
- **Probability of simulation**: refines [[Arguments/Simulation Argument (Bostrom)]] via sim blockers; estimates "the chance that we are sims is at least 25 percent or so."
- **The cogito, minimally**: "I am conscious, therefore I am" survives every skeptical scenario.
- **Objective reality**: against post-truth readings — "Reality exists, independently of us. The truth matters... Even in an age of multiple realities, I still believe in objective reality."
- **Consciousness**: *Reality+* holds its hard-problem commitments mostly offstage, but adds a "fourth plank": virtual and augmented minds are genuine minds. The full commitments are in *The Conscious Mind*: [[Concepts/Hard Problem of Consciousness (Chalmers)]], zombies, naturalistic dualism, and the information-based prototheory whose it-from-bit thread resurfaces in *Reality+* ([[Concepts/It-from-Bit Hypothesis (Chalmers)]]).

## Contradictions / Open Questions

- > [!warning] Chalmers's non-deflationism about consciousness conflicts with [[Thinkers/Daniel Dennett]] and [[Thinkers/Andy Clark]] — see [[Contradictions/Dennett vs Nagel - Multiple Drafts vs Subjective Experience]] and [[Concepts/Predictive Processing and the Hard Problem (Clark)]].
- > [!warning] His simulation realism inverts [[Concepts/Simulation Hypothesis (Virk)]]'s framing: where Virk treats simulation as a revelation that ordinary reality is game-like, Chalmers treats it as no threat to the reality of the ordinary.
