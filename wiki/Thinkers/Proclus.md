# Proclus

> Head of the Athenian Academy (412–85), the great systematizer of late Neoplatonism: the *Elements of Theology* deduces the whole scale of being in 211 geometric-style propositions, and his doctrine of the henads reconciles the ineffable One with the gods of pagan religion.

## Summary

Wallis stresses that Proclus was less an original thinker than the systematizer of the Athenian School founded by Plutarch of Athens (d. 432) — most doctrines were formulated by his teacher Syrianus (d. c. 437). His *Elements of Theology* and the huge *Platonic Theology* (a theological reading of the *Parmenides*' hypotheses, per Iamblichus's exegetic program) fix late Neoplatonic method: metaphysics as deductive science. The Athenian School prized speculative metaphysics, pagan observance, and theurgy, regarding the more scholarly, theologically neutral Alexandrians as "scholars rather than philosophers." Its succession ran to Damascius, the last head, under whom Justinian closed the Academy in 529; Damascius and Simplicius fled to Persia and returned under a treaty barring them from teaching — enforced leisure to which we owe Simplicius's preservation of the Presocratic fragments.

## Key Doctrines (Wallis)

- **[[Concepts/The Henads (Proclus)]]**: a series of divine Unities within the First Hypostasis, the Participated intermediaries between the unknowable One (now the Unparticipated Monad) and everything below — at once metaphysical bridge from Unity to Plurality, religious accommodation of the traditional gods, and epistemological handle (knowable by analogy from their effects).
- **[[Concepts/Procession, Remaining, and Return (Neoplatonic Triad)]]** (*monē–proodos–epistrophē*): every effect abides in, proceeds from, and reverts upon its cause — the universal structural law of reality, correlated with Limit, Infinity, and their Mixture.
- **Cyclic causation and reach of causes**: higher causes extend further down the hierarchy than lower ones (Being affects everything; Life only organisms; Intelligence only animals); Matter alone escapes Form, showing a "similarity of dissimilarity" to the One.
- **Evil as *parhypostasis***: a mere by-product, absence, or perversion of aspiration toward the Good with no genuine existence — correcting Plotinus's occasional identification of Matter with evil.
- **Providence "by virtue of the gods' very existence"** (*autōi tōi einai*, *PT* I.15): automatic, non-deliberative; the gods grasp temporal and contingent events "in a single timeless vision." Theodorus of Asine's dictum: "All things pray except the First" (*In Tim.* I.213).
- **Two vehicles (*ochēmata*) of the soul** — one immortal, one perishable — mediating the Porphyrian and Iamblichean positions on the soul's descent.
- **Faith, Truth, Love** (*pistis, alētheia, erōs*, from the *Chaldaean Oracles*) as theurgic virtues, with *erōs pronoētikos* — providential, descending love of the higher for its products — marking a stress on divine grace unknown to Plotinus.
- **Radical negative theology**: even the *Parmenides*' negations must finally be negated; the Law of Contradiction does not apply to the One (*In Parm.* VII).

## Legacy

The *Liber de Causis* (Arabic paraphrase of the *Elements*) circulated as Aristotle's until [[Thinkers/Thomas Aquinas]], using Moerbeke's 1268 translation of Proclus, exposed it. Pseudo-Dionysius put Athenian Neoplatonism in Christian dress; Hegel's dialectic is, in Wallis's shorthand, "Procline" where Schelling is Plotinian.
