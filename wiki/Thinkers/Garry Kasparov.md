# Garry Kasparov

> Legendary chess grandmaster (youngest World Champion at 22 in 1985, held highest rating until 2005 retirement). Known for deep calculation, preparation, and his public matches against IBM's Deep Blue supercomputer. Author of *Deep Thinking: Where Machine Intelligence Ends and Human Creativity Begins*. MasterClass on chess mastery, strategy, and the enduring role of human judgment vs machines/AI.

## Bio & Achievements (from class materials)
- Under-18 USSR champion at age 12; World Junior Champion at 17.
- 1984 World Championship match vs Anatoly Karpov controversially ended after 48 games (no clear victor at the time).
- Clinched the title in the 1985 rematch.
- Deep Blue matches (1996 win for Kasparov, 1997 loss) brought chess into mainstream consciousness and sparked global debate on human vs machine intelligence.
- Post-retirement: author, speaker, activist; extensive writing and research on the intersection of human creativity and artificial intelligence.

## Key Ideas from the MasterClass
- Chess as the ultimate laboratory for strategic thinking, calculation trees, opponent modeling, and long-term planning under uncertainty.
- Deliberate practice: study of past games, practice positions, workbook exercises, PGN files for software analysis.
- Human creativity and intuition remain decisive even when machines excel at brute-force calculation (Deep Blue reflections).
- The class includes interactive elements (follow moves in video), chapter reviews with advanced terminology, "Double Check" personal perspective sections, and extra practice materials.
