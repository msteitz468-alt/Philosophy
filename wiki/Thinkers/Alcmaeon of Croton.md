# Alcmaeon of Croton
> The Crotonian physician-philosopher in whom [[Sources/Pythagoras and the Early Pythagoreans - Leonid Zhmud (2012)|Zhmud]] locates the *empirical* heart of early Pythagoreanism — founder of physiology, the brain-centred theory of cognition, and a research programme of inference (*tekmairesthai*) rather than revelation.

## Summary
Alcmaeon (active late 6th / early 5th c. BC) was a younger contemporary of [[Thinkers/Pythagoras]] at Croton — the Greek world's foremost medical centre (Herodotus III.131). On Zhmud's reading he is the most consequential of the early Pythagorean *scientists*, and the figure who best refutes the picture of Pythagoreanism as pure number-mysticism or religious taboo: with Alcmaeon, "*medicina soror philosophiae*" — medicine shapes philosophy, not the reverse. See [[Concepts/Pythagorean Medicine and the Life Sciences]].

## Key Positions
**Health as *isonomia*** (DK B4):
- Health is the *isonomia* (equal balance) of opposed powers (*dynameis*) — wet/dry, hot/cold, bitter/sweet, etc.; disease is the *monarchia* (sole rule) of one over the others.
- Zhmud reads this as a *political* metaphor — balance vs. tyranny — reflecting the aristocratic ideology of the Pythagorean [[Schools/Pythagoreanism|hetairia]], and as the ancestor of the Hippocratic balance-of-humours theory. (It is also, via Simmias/Echecrates in the *Phaedo*, the likely real root of the "soul as attunement" doctrine — drawn from medicine, not from [[Thinkers/Philolaus]]' cosmic harmony.)

**Empirical epistemology** (DK B1):
- "Of the invisible, as of mortal things, the gods have certain knowledge; but to us, as men, only inference (*tekmairesthai*) is possible." This is the first explicit programme of *positive empirical research* — knowledge by inference from evidence — and the very faculty (τεκμαίρεσθαι) that Burkert contrasts with the shaman's claim to "know more." See [[Concepts/Metempsychosis (Transmigration of Souls)]].

**The brain and cognition**:
- The **brain** is the seat of thought *and* sensation, linked to the sense-organs by passages (*poroi*); only humans "understand," while animals merely perceive. This is the first rational neuro-cognitive theory — later taken up by Plato and Hippocratic medicine against the Aristotelian cardiocentric view.
- Zhmud judges Alcmaeon likely performed **dissection** (Chalcidius: "first dared to proceed to dissection"), discovering the optic nerves (*poroi* from eyes to brain).

**Embryology**: semen originates in the brain; both parents contribute seed; sex is fixed by the prevailing seed. (Hippon of Samos extended this — semen from the spinal marrow, with a supporting "experiment.")

**Astronomy & immortality**: Alcmaeon held the planets move W→E along the ecliptic, independently of the fixed stars (genuine astronomy — see [[Concepts/Harmony of the Spheres]]); and that the soul is **immortal** because, like the heavenly bodies, it is in *constant circular motion* — an argument later developed by Plato (*Phaedrus* 245c). Note: this is immortality **without transmigration** — distinct from Pythagorean [[Concepts/Metempsychosis (Transmigration of Souls)|metempsychosis]].

## Relevance to the Vault
- Alcmaeon is Zhmud's strongest single case that early Pythagoreanism contained rigorous, secular, empirical science. He widens [[Contradictions/Pythagoras the Scientist vs the Religious Charismatic]] beyond mathematics into medicine and the life sciences — a domain Burkert largely passed over.
