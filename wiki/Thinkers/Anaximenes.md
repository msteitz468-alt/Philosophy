# Anaximenes

> Milesian philosopher (c. 586 – 526 BCE), younger contemporary and associate of Anaximander. The last of the three great Milesian naturalists.

## Key Contributions

**Air (Aer) as Arche**:
- Declared **air** (*aer* or breath) to be the *arche* (primary substance).
- He linked the cosmos to the human soul: just as our soul, which is air, holds us together and guides us, so do breath and air encompass the whole world.

**Condensation and Rarefaction**:
- While Thales and Anaximander left the *mechanism* of change unexplained, Anaximenes introduced a physical mechanism: **condensation** and **rarefaction**.
- Rarefied air becomes **fire**. 
- Condensed air becomes successively **wind, clouds, water, earth**, and finally **stones**.
- All qualitative differences in the physical world are therefore reduced to quantitative differences in the density of a single material.

**Flat Earth Cosmology**:
- Held that the earth, sun, and stars are flat and ride upon the air like leaves.

## Relevance to Vault

- **First Physical Mechanism**: Anaximenes' explanation of change via density is the historical ancestor of physical state transitions (gas $\rightarrow$ liquid $\rightarrow$ solid). It represents the first attempt to explain qualitative change purely in terms of quantitative density, a foundational principle of mathematical physics.
- **Completion of Milesian Trilogy**: Rounds out the Milesian school's initial naturalism, providing a bridge to Pythagoras's mathematical quantities and Heraclitus's fire.
