# Paul Feyerabend

> Paul Karl Feyerabend (1924–1994), Austrian-born philosopher of science; the great provocateur ("the worst enemy of science," *Nature* called him) of the post-positivist debate. In *Against Method* (1975) he argues there is **no fixed scientific method**: every methodological rule has been productively broken, "**anything goes**," science is "an essentially anarchic enterprise," and — politically — science is one tradition among many that, in a free society, should be **separated from the state**.

## Profile

- **Dates / life**: Born Vienna 1924; served in the Wehrmacht in WWII (wounded, partly paralyzed). Studied in Vienna; gravitated to the **Popper** circle at the LSE (Popper was a major early influence and later chief target). Long career at **Berkeley** (from 1958), where he was a colleague of **Thomas Kuhn** (the two influenced each other on incommensurability) and a close friend of **Imre Lakatos**. Also held posts at Zurich and elsewhere. A famous, theatrical lecturer. Died 1994; autobiography *Killing Time* (1995).
- **Major works**: ***Against Method*** (1975; 3rd ed. 1993); *Science in a Free Society* (1978); *Farewell to Reason* (1987); *Philosophical Papers* (2 vols, 1981); the posthumous *Conquest of Abundance* (1999).
- **In the vault**: Primary source [[Sources/Against Method - Paul Feyerabend]] (3rd ed., which folds in *Science in a Free Society*). The fourth and most radical member of the vault's 20th-c. philosophy-of-science cluster (after [[Thinkers/Karl Popper]], [[Thinkers/Thomas Kuhn]], [[Thinkers/W.V.O. Quine]]).

## Core Positions (from *Against Method*)

- **Epistemological anarchism / "anything goes"**: No methodology is both universal and good; "anything goes" is the ironic residue once you see that every rule has rational exceptions — *not* a positive principle Feyerabend endorses. → [[Concepts/Epistemological Anarchism (Feyerabend)]]

- **Counterinduction**: Advance hypotheses that contradict well-confirmed theories and even well-established facts; alternatives are needed to expose the hidden assumptions buried in "the facts." → [[Concepts/Counterinduction (Feyerabend)]]

- **Natural interpretations & the Galileo case**: Observations are constituted by theory-laden "natural interpretations." Galileo advanced Copernicanism by *replacing* natural interpretations (introducing the relativity of motion and circular inertia, disguised as "reminiscence"), by "changing sensations" with an untrustworthy **telescope**, and by **rhetoric and propaganda** — violating every empiricist rule. → [[Concepts/Natural Interpretations and the Galileo Case (Feyerabend)]]

- **Proliferation and tenacity**: Multiply rival theories (proliferation) and cling to promising ones through anomalies (tenacity); both are rational. Drawn from **J. S. Mill's *On Liberty*** and Bohr's practice; uniformity "impairs [science's] critical power" and "endangers the free development of the individual." → [[Concepts/Proliferation and Tenacity (Feyerabend)]]

- **Incommensurability**: Co-originated (independently of Kuhn, 1962) and pushed further — major theory change alters "covert classifications," perception, even child-developmental and art-historical categories, so rival frameworks can be deeply non-comparable. (Discussed in [[Concepts/Incommensurability (Kuhn)]].)

- **Science is not privileged**: Neither science nor "Reason" is a universal measure of excellence; both are *particular traditions* unaware of their historical grounding. Science is "neither a single tradition, nor the best tradition there is." → the political thesis: **separation of science and state**, democratic control of research, science taught "as one view among many," and the right of non-scientific cultures "to stand on their own feet." → [[Concepts/Separation of Science and State (Feyerabend)]]

- **Humanitarian motive**: "My main reason for writing the book was humanitarian, not intellectual. I wanted to support people" — anger at the "wanton destruction of cultural achievements."

## Influence, Reception, and Contrasts in the Vault

- **Vs. [[Thinkers/Karl Popper]]**: the apostate student. Popper's [[Concepts/Falsifiability as Criterion of Demarcation (Popper)|falsifiability]] is just another rule that history breaks; strict critical rationalism "fails for the same reasons" as positivism and would have strangled Copernicanism. Feyerabend is the sharpest opponent of Popper's realist [[Concepts/Verisimilitude (Truthlikeness) (Popper)|verisimilitude]].
- **Vs. [[Thinkers/Thomas Kuhn]]**: ally and rival. Shares incommensurability and theory-ladenness, but rejects Kuhn's "normal science" as he rejects all law-and-order pictures, and reads Kuhn's monolithic "paradigm" as too uniform (his criticism of the uniformity of paradigms predates *Structure*'s reception).
- **Vs. Lakatos**: most intimate antagonist; *Against Method* is the unanswered half of their planned joint book. Feyerabend: Lakatos's "research programmes" are "anarchism in disguise" — their standards apply only in hindsight and so dictate nothing now.
- **With/against Ian Hacking** (*Representing and Intervening*, now primary-sourced): shares anti-"law and order" skepticism and the critique of fixed method; Hacking's detailed experimental cases (microscopes, phenomenon creation, spray criterion) provide concrete structure and a use-based entity realism that functions as a partial counterweight without restoring a universal methodology. Hacking also treats Feyerabend's flamboyance as having amplified the relativism charge against Kuhn more than the texts alone. See [[Sources/Representing and Intervening - Ian Hacking (1983)]], [[Thinkers/Ian Hacking]].
- **With [[Thinkers/W.V.O. Quine]]**: kindred in holism/underdetermination and theory-ladenness, but Feyerabend's moral is anarchist-pluralist where Quine's is naturalist-pragmatist.
- **Relativist pole**: aligns with [[Concepts/Perspectivism (Nietzsche)]], [[Concepts/Pragmatic Theory of Truth (James)]], and [[Concepts/Humanism - Reality as Malleable (Schiller and James)]], and with sceptical traditions ([[Concepts/Mitigated Scepticism (Hume)]], [[Concepts/Epoché (Suspension of Judgment)]]) — though he denied being "a relativist for whom there are no truths as such."
- **Liberty / pluralism**: his proliferation argument is explicitly Millian; connects to [[Concepts/Social Cohesion vs. Individual Liberty (Russell)]] and [[Concepts/Toleration (Locke)]].
- The full rationalism↔anarchism spectrum is laid out in [[Contradictions/Popper vs Kuhn - Falsification vs Paradigms]].

## Relevance to the Vault and Meta

- **Anti-method-worship**: a standing warning against fetishizing any one ingest rule, prompt template, or "scientific" workflow; proliferation, tenacity, and counterinduction are usable (bounded) heuristics, not new dogmas.
- **AI/agentic**: theory-ladenness means an agent's framing constitutes its "facts"; generating rival framings surfaces hidden assumptions. "Anything goes" ≠ sloppiness — it denies that any single rule is always right.
- **Career/strategy**: "best practice" and expert consensus are traditions, not the only road; advantage can lie in the counter-inductive move — tempered by his own caution that liberalizing "fact" has "grave repercussions."
